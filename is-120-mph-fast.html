12. what is the momentum of a 120 pound bicyclist that is traveling at 25 mph?​


Answer:

The momentum is 21,120 lb.ft/s

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the momentum of a traveling bicyclist. In momentum, this is possible when an object is moving.

For the formula, we will use:

p = mv

where

p     is the momentum of the object, unit is in kg.m/s (metric system) or

      lb.ft/s (British system)

m    is the mass of the object, unit is in kg or lb

v     is the velocity of the object, unit is in m/s or ft/s

For the given information

m = 120 lb

v = 25 mph or miles per hour

p = ?

Solving the problem

1. First, let us convert the velocity of the bicyclist to feet per second, we have:

[tex]v=120 \frac{mi}{h} x \frac{5280ft}{1mile} x\frac{1hour}{3600s}[/tex]

v = 176 ft/s

Now, we are ready to solve for the momentum.

2. Let us use the formula for momentum, then substitute the given information, we have:

p = mv

p = (120 lb)(176 ft/s)

p = 21,120 lb.ft/s          (unit in British system)

If you want the unit of momentum in metric system, just convert 120 lb to kg and 25 mph to m/s. Then substitute the converted units to the formula of momentum and the answer for momentum in metric unit is 609.47 kg.m/s.

Therefore, the momentum of the bicyclist is 21,120 lb.ft/s
