Rio: x

New amount of money with the ratio of 2:3.

Rio: x - 300   (gave 300 to Kim)
Kim: x + 300 (add 300 the amount from Rio)

Solution:

(x - 300) : (x + 300) = 2:3

Equate the product of means to the product of extremes:

2 (x+300) = 3 (x - 300)
2x + 600 = 3x - 900
3x - 2x = 600 + 900
x = 1,500

6. 1. Explain the meaning of the quotation by oscar wilde "to love onself is the beginning of a lifelong romance."-pa answer po pls​


Answer:

It is said that one of the kindest things you can do for yourself is accept yourself just the way you are. It is also said that one of the bravest things you can do is stretch yourself and reach for your dreams. So which is it, self-acceptance or self-improvement? The truth is both. Psychologically speaking, if we are to improve ourselves and be happy and peaceful along the way, we have to learn to navigate the fine line between self-acceptance and self-improvement.

Everything we do (including all the “bettering” of our lovely selves) must come from a place of self-acceptance and love if it is to be of any real lasting benefit. No good ever came from beating ourselves into being better. In fact, pushing, criticising, and punishing are practices that leave us feeling empty, inferior and ultimately unmotivated, goal reached or not!If we are to ever make sustainable positive change and maintain a sense of fun and perspective along the way, the change must come from a place of unconditional love and positive regard for ourselves. From this healthy platform, change becomes fun and adventurous.

After all, there is no doubt that we can achieve goals through pure grit and determination. The problem is we are at risk of feeling empty and fatigued if sheer determination is not balanced with great big doses of creativity, fun and self-encouragement.

21. Based on the poem above, why is the word "beloved" associated with the words 'tamed', 'wild' and 'love'? Analyze using the deconstructionist approach in literary criticism.​


Answer:

The deconstructionist approach in literary criticism suggests that meaning is not fixed and can be deconstructed or analyzed in multiple ways. In the poem above, the word "beloved" is associated with the words 'tamed', 'wild', and 'love' because of the multiple meanings and interpretations of these words.

The word "beloved" can be interpreted as a term of endearment or affection, suggesting a deep emotional connection between two individuals. The word "tamed" can be interpreted as the act of controlling or domesticating something or someone, suggesting a sense of ownership or possession. The word "wild" can be interpreted as untamed, free, and unrestrained, suggesting a sense of freedom or liberation. Finally, the word "love" can be interpreted as a complex emotion that can be both positive and negative, suggesting a sense of passion, desire, and attachment.

Therefore, the association between these words suggests a complex and nuanced relationship between the speaker and their beloved, one that involves both love and control, freedom and restraint. The deconstructionist approach highlights the multiple meanings and interpretations of words and how they can be used to create complex and layered meanings in poetry.
