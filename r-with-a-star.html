4. How do H-R diagrams help scientists compare new stars with the Sun? In your answer, name one type of data that H-R diagrams do not show.


Answer:

H-R diagrams are useful for comparing new stars with the Sun because they provide a visual representation of a star's temperature and luminosity. By plotting the temperature and luminosity of a new star on the H-R diagram, scientists can compare its position to the Sun and other known stars, and gain valuable insights into its properties and characteristics.

One type of data that H-R diagrams do not show is a star's chemical composition. While H-R diagrams provide important information about a star's temperature and luminosity, they do not provide information about the elements and compounds that make up a star's atmosphere and interior. To determine a star's chemical composition, scientists must use other methods, such as spectroscopy, which involves analyzing the light emitted by a star to identify its constituent elements.

30. This occurs in the main sequence of stars. A. Stellar NucleosynthesisB. Primordial NucleosynthesisC. R-ProcessD. Supernova Nucleosynthesis​


Answer:

A. Stellar Nucleosynthesis occurs in the main sequence of stars.

Explanation:

Stellar nucleosynthesis is the process by which elements are created within stars by combining the protons and neutrons together from the nuclei of lighter elements. All of the atoms in the universe began as hydrogen. Fusion inside stars transforms hydrogen into helium, heat, and radiation.

In the core of the Sun hydrogen is being converted into helium. This is called nuclear fusion. It takes four hydrogen atoms to fuse into each helium atom. During the process some of the mass is converted into energy.
