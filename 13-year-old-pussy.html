30. A is now 34 years old, and b is 4years old. In how many years will A be twice as old as b?


A = 34 yrs

B = 4 yrs

34 + x = 2 ( 4 + x)

34 + x = 8 + 2x

34 - 8 = 2x - x

26 = x

ANSWER: 26 years

Answer:

In how many years will A be twice as old as B? Hence, A is twice as old as B after 26 years. So, the correct answer is 26 or 60 years.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let the number of years be  x

A: In  x  years time, A will be  34 + x

B: In  x  years time, B will be  4 + x

At that time in the future: 2 x younger age = older age.

2 ( 4+ x ) = 34 + x

8 + 2 x = 34 + x

2 x − x = 34 − 8

x = 26

Check: In 26 years time:

A: In 26 years time, A will be 34+26 = 60

B: In 26 years time, B will be 4+26 = 30

2 x 30 = 60!!
