27. A wecking ball suspended from a crane Brinks loose and fail to the ground with an acceleration of 10m/s² - It hits the ground with a velocity of 30m/s.how long did it take to hit the ground.​


Explanation:

We can use the kinematic equation:

v = u + at

where:

v = final velocity = 30 m/s (since it hits the ground with a velocity of 30m/s)

u = initial velocity = 0 (since it starts from rest)

a = acceleration = 10 m/s^2

t = time

Solving for t:

t = (v - u) / a

t = (30 - 0) / 10

t = 3 seconds

Therefore, it took the wecking ball 3 seconds to hit the ground

Answer:We can use the kinematic equation:

v^2 = u^2 + 2as

where:v = final velocity = 30 m/s (the velocity at impact)u = initial velocity = 0 m/s (the wrecking ball starts from rest)a = acceleration = 10 m/s^2 (the acceleration due to gravity)s = displacement (the distance the ball falls)

We can rearrange this equation to solve for s:

s = (v^2 - u^2) / (2a)

s = (30^2 - 0^2) / (2 x 10)

s = 45 meters

Now, we can use another kinematic equation:

s = ut + (1/2)at^2

where:

t = time taken to fall

We can rearrange this equation to solve for t:

t = √(2s/a)

t = √(2 x 45 / 10)

t = 3 seconds

Therefore, it took the wrecking ball 3 seconds to hit the ground.
