4. A jogger is running eastward along a straight roadat 2m/s. If he accelerates at 0.1 m/s² in 20s.What is his new velocity?


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

A jogger is running eastward along a straight road  at 2 m/s. If he accelerates at 0.1 m/s² in 20 s.  What is his new velocity?

Given:

Vi (initial velocity) = 2 m/s

a (acceleration) = 0.1 m/s²

t (time)  =  20 s

Vf (final velocity) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve this problem using the acceleration formula (a = Vf-Vi / t). To find Vf,  we can use  (Vf = at + Vi ).

Vf = at + Vi

   = (0.1 m/s² × 20 s)  +  2 m/s

   = 2 m/s + 2 m/s

   = 4 m/s

Answer:

The final velocity or the new velocity of the jogger is 4 m/s

The jogger velocity from 1 m/s will increase by 0.1 m/s every second. So after 20 seconds, the jogger's new velocity is 4 m/s.
