7. What sum would yield an interest of Php. 36000 in 3 years at 3% per annum​


The principle sum Php. 400

Given,

Time (T) = 3 yearsRate (R) = 3% per annum

Per year, then, denotes that interest will be assessed or computed annually.

Therefore, 10 percent per annum signifies that 10 percent interest will be added to a loan's principle amount per year or every year.

and Simple Interest (SI) = Php. 36

Simple Interest (SI) = [tex]\frac{P x R x T}{100}[/tex]

[tex]36 = \frac{x.3.3}{100} \\x = \frac{3600}{9} = 400[/tex]

The base amount is 400 PHP.

With the premise that interest is compounded annually, the term "per annum interest rate" refers to the interest rate for a period of one year. For instance, a $10,000 loan with an annual interest rate of 5% would cost $500.

The genuine interest rate on an investment or loan is the effective yearly interest rate since it takes compounding into account.The rate increases with the number of compounding periods.Both a nominal interest rate and an effective yearly interest rate may be listed in the advertisements for a savings account or a loan.The rate that should be used to compare the returns on loans and investments is the effective annual interest rate.

If it is simple interest, SI = PRT/100

Where,

P is the principal amount,R is the rate of interest, andT is the time period.

If the rate of interest is 10% p.a, SI = P x 10 x 1/100 i.e., 0.1 PThe annual simple interest that must be paid or received is 10% of the principal sum plus the principal sum. The total sum to be paid or collected if the interest is compounded is [tex]A = P ( 1 + \frac{R}{100}) ^{T}[/tex]If the rate of interest is 10% p.a, [tex]A = P (1 + \frac{10}{100} )^{1}[/tex] i.e., 1.1P

Future value
