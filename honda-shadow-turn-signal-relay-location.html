14. These are locations of darker value that result in surrounding objects or surfaces.cast shadowb. core shadowc. highlight​


Answer

B. Core Shadow - It is the part of the object that is not being hit by a light source or is against the light. Core Shadow is also called Form Shadow. In 3D this is associated with Ambient Occlusion.

Explanation

A. Cast Shadow - a shadow created behind the object when a light source hits the front of the object. Meaning, the objects create a shadow by blocking the light source from hitting what is behind the object. Hence, the phrase "casting a shadow." The shadow is dark when it is closer to the object.

C. Highlight​ - the lightest part of an object being hit by a light source. Highlights can tell the viewer what direction the light source is coming from.
