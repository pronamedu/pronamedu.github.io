5. at a supermarket that sells fruit by the pound, one person bought 3 lb of oranges and 6 lb of grapefruit for a totalof $6, while a second person paid $6.40 for 5lb of oranges and 4lb of grapefruit. what is the price per pound of the oranges and the grapefruit?


The price per pound of oranges is $0.80 and the price per pound of grapefruit is $0.60.

The indicated problem is an example of word problem. The word problem gave the number of pounds of oranges and grapefruit that each person bought. The word problem also gave the price that each person paid. We are to find out the price per pound of oranges and grapefruit. Here are the steps in determining the price per pound of the oranges and the grapefruit:

Let x be the price per pound of oranges, and y be the price per pound of grapefruit. For the first person, the equation is 3x + 6y = 6 and for the second person, the equation is 5x + 4y = 6.4. We should subtract the two equations to cancel one variable (y) which is the second addend of the two equations. To be able to cancel, we should multiply a number to both equations to derive the least common multiple of the second addend of the two equations. The least common multiple of the second addends is 12. Therefore, let us multiple the first equation by 2, and the second equation by 3 to derive 12y's for both equations. The first equation then becomes 6x + 12y = 12, and the second equation then becomes 15x + 12y = 19.2. We should subtract the equations so the 12y of both equations will be cancelled. After subtraction, the resulting, remaining equation is 9x = 7.2. Therefore, x = 0.80. Substitute x = 0.80 the the two original equations to derive y. Using first equation: (3 x 0.8) + 6y = 6, this will give us 6y = 3.6. Therefore, y = 0.60. Therefore, the price per pound of oranges is $0.80 and the price per pound of grapefruit is $0.60.

Shape = CubeSide = 70 cm

Hence, the total capacity of the cooler is 343,000 cm^3

24. Twoidentical coolers are packed for a camping. Each cooler is packed withten-one-liter softdrinks and 1.5 Kg of ice. The softdrinks that were packed incooler Awere first put inside the refrigerator for 8 hours,while the softdrinksthat were packed in cooler Bwere justkept at room temperature for 8 hoursalso. When the campers opened the coolers after 4 hours later most of theice in cooler Aarestill ice, while almost all of the ice in cooler Bhave alreadymelted.Explainwhathappenedwiththetwocoolers.


The soft drinks in cooler B had higher thermal energy than the soft drinks in cooler A. Therefore, greater thermal energy flowed from the drinks in cooler B to ice, causing it to melt faster.

When the soft drinks are placed in ice, thermal energy flows from the object having a higher temperature to the object at a lower temperature until thermal equilibrium is achieved between the two bodies.

Note that the temperature difference between the soft drinks in cooler B and ice is much higher compared to the temperature difference between the soft drinks in cooler A and ice.

The reason behind this is that the soft drinks in cooler A were kept in a refrigerator for 8 hours while the soft drinks in cooler B were kept at room temperature for 8 hours.

When both groups of soft drinks were added to different coolers with the same 1.5 kg of ice, thermal equilibrium was attained in both cases.

The soft drinks in cooler B  transferred more energy to the ice in four hours causing it to melt faster compared to the amount of energy transferred to it by the soft drinks in cooler B. The energy between the soft drinks in cooler A and ice were minimal hence the soft drinks in cooler A still remained ice after 4 hours.
