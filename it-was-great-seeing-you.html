4. what similarities do you see between the two poems auld lang syne and i think one truly of those who were truly great and your own life what is it in them that reminds you of your own experience​


Answer:

Similarities and Differences Between Auld Lang Syne and I Think Continually of Those Who Were Truly Great

Auld Lang Syne is a popular traditional song being typically sung on New Year's Eve. It was a poem first transcribed by Robert Burns although, according to him, it is originally an ancient song. The meaning of Auld Lang Syne is 'For Old Time's Sake'. Concurrently, I Think Continually of Those Who Were Truly Great is a poem written by Stephen Spender.

Similarities

Some similarities of the two literary works include the following:

Remembering the days of old

Remembering a person or people from the past

Celebration of life

Differences

On the other hand, some of the differences between the two are listed below:

Auld Lang Syne

It is originally a song.

It was originated in Scotland.

The song is about and for old friends, hence, it is more personal.

I Think Continually of Those Who Were Truly Great

It is written as a poem.

Stephen Spender came from London.

The poem is about great people in the history, hence, broader and less personal.
