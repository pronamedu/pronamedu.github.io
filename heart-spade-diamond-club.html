8. if a card is drawn from a deck, what is the probability that a. it will be a flower b. an ace of spade c. not a diamond d. it will be a black heart e. the card drawn is a red card f. a diamond and a heart g. a flower or heart h. 4 and a heart


A deck of cards will always have 52 cards (13 each suit)

a.) Probability that it will be a flower

There are 4 suits in a deck of card (Heart, Diamond, Spade, and Flower)

Since the problem has not specified what should be drawn except that it should be a flower, there's [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] chance that you will get a flower.

b.) Probability that it will be an ace of spade

Since there's only one ace of spade in a deck of card, the probability that you will get it is [tex]\frac{1}{52}[/tex].

c.) Probability that it is not a diamond

If there are 4 suits in a deck of cards, and you do not want diamonds, this means that diamonds should be taken out of the choices, leaving us with 3 suits left. This means the probability is [tex]\frac{3}{4}[/tex]

d.) Probability that it will be a black heart

0. There's no such thing as a black heart. Hearts are red in a standard deck of cards.

e.) Probability that the card drawn is red.

In 4 suits, there are only 2 symbols that is colored red: Diamonds and Hearts. So if there are 4 suits and only 2 are red, the probability of getting a red card  is [tex]\frac{2}{4}[/tex] or [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] when reduced.

f.) Probability that the card is a diamond and a heart

The instruction tells us that only one card is drawn (if a card drawn[...]). Therefore, the probability is zero.

g.) Probability that the card drawn is a flower or a heart

There's 1/4 chance that you will draw a flower, same as the heart.

Since we used "or", and the events are "mutually exclusive", we will add instead of multiplying.

[tex]\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{4}[/tex] OR [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

h.) Probability that the card drawn is a 4 and a heart.

Since there's only 1 card that fits this description, the probability of this happening is [tex]\frac{1}{52}[/tex].

• Number of spades (black) = 13

Number of hearts (red) = 13

Number of diamonds (red) = 13

• Probability of an event (P) = Number of favourable outcomes / Total number of possible outcomes

(i) • Probability of drawing a black king (P1) = Number of black kings / Total number of cards in the pack

• Number of black kings = King of spades + King of clubs

= 1 + 1 = 2

• P1 = 2 / 52 = 1 / 26

(ii) • Probability of drawing either a black card or a king (P2) = (Number of black cards + Number of kings in red cards) / Total number of cards

=> P2 = (26 + 2) / 52

Or, P2 = 28 / 52

Or, P2 = 7 / 13

(iii) Probability of drawing a card that is black and a king (P3) = Number of black kings in the pack / Total number of cards in the pack

=> P3 = 2 / 52

Or, P3 = 1 / 26

(iv) Probability of drawing a jack or a queen or a king (P4) = (Number of jacks + Number of queens + Number of kings) / Total number of cards

=> P4 = (4 + 4 + 4) / 52

Or, P4 = 12 / 52

Or, P4 = 3 / 13

29. A deck of 54 cards has 2 jokers, and 13 cards of each of spades, hearts, clubs and diamonds. At least how many cards should be drawn at random so that there are at least 4 cards of the same suit? asap po


Answer:

15

Step-by-step explanation:

Consider the worst situation that we have 2 jokers and 3 cards from each suit to be  drawn.

There are in total 14 cards but still did not give us a suit with more than 4  cards.

And when one more card is drawn, we will have 4 cards for a certain suit,  by pigeon hole principle. Thus the answer is 15 cards.

There are 13 spades There are three 7's (7 spade is excluded since it's already in the set of spades) 13 + 3 = 16 Numerator = 16

Denominator:

Probability:

Numerator = 16 Denominator = 52 Probability = 16 ÷ 4 / 52 ÷ 4 Probability = 4/13
