5. 4NH3+3O2----> 2N2+6H2O 1. How many moles of NH3 are needed to react with 6 moles of O2? 2. How many grams of N2 are needed to react with 12 moles of H2O? 3. How many grams of O2 are needed to react with 250g of NH3? 4. How many grams of H2O are needed tp react with 100g of N2? With solution po sana. Thanks


Answer:

1) n NH3 = 8 mol

2) mass N2 = 112 g

3) mass O2 = 353 g

4) mass H2O = 193 g

Explanation:

1. How many moles of NH3 are needed to react with 6 moles of O2?

n NH3 = 6 mol O2 × (4 mol NH3 / 3 mol O2)

n NH3 = 8 mol

2. How many grams of N2 are needed to react with 12 moles of H2O?

mass N2 = 12 mol H2O × (2 mol N2 / 6 mol H2O) × (28 g N2 / 1 mol N2)

mass N2 = 112 g

3. How many grams of O2 are needed to react with 250g of NH3?

mass O2 = 250 g NH3 × (1 mol NH3 / 17 g NH3) × (3 mol O2 / 4 mol NH3) × (32 g O2 / 1 mol O2)

mass O2 = 353 g

4. How many grams of H2O are needed tp react with 100g of N2?

mass H2O = 100 g N2 × (1 mol N2 / 28 g N2) × (6 mol H2O / 2 mol N2) × (18 g H2O / 1 mol H2O)

mass H2O = 193 g

Answer:

To convert from grams to moles, we use the molar mass of water, which is 18.015 g/mol. So, 60.0 grams of water would be 60.0 g / 18.015 g/mol = 3.334 moles of water.To convert from moles to grams, we use the molar mass of sodium oxide, which is 39.9968 g/mol. So, 5.7 moles of sodium oxide would be 5.7 mol * 39.9968 g/mol = 227.69 g of sodium oxide.To convert from grams to molecules, we first need to determine the number of moles. We use the molar mass of sodium hydroxide, which is 40.0 g/mol. So, 50.0 grams of sodium hydroxide would be 50.0 g / 40.0 g/mol = 1.25 moles of sodium hydroxide. Then, we can use Avogadro's number to convert from moles to molecules. So, 1.25 moles of sodium hydroxide would be 1.25 mol * 6.022 x 10^23 molecules/mol = 7.53 x 10^23 molecules of sodium hydroxide.To calculate the number of sodium atoms in 0.22 mol of Na, we use Avogadro's number. So, 0.22 mol of Na would contain 0.22 mol * 6.022 x 10^23 atoms/mol = 1.32 x 10^23 sodium atoms.To calculate the mass in grams of 3.01 x 10^22 atoms of sulfur (S), we use the atomic mass of sulfur, which is 32.06 g/mol. So, 3.01 x 10^22 atoms of S would be 3.01 x 10^22 atoms * 32.06 g/mol / (6.022 x 10^23 atoms/mol) = 0.32 g of sulfur.
