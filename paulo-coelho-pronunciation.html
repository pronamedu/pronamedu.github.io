Paulo Coelho, (born August 24, 1947, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil), Brazilian novelist known for employing rich symbolism in his depictions of the often spiritually motivated journeys taken by his characters.

Paulo Coelho

Coelho was raised in Rio de Janeiro. He rebelled against the conventions of his Roman Catholic upbringing and, as a result, was temporarily committed to a psychiatric hospital by his parents. Coelho dropped out of law school in 1970 and traveled through South America, Mexico, North Africa, and Europe. In 1972 he returned home and began writing pop and rock music lyrics with Raul Seixas, a well-known Brazilian singer and songwriter. In 1974 Coelho was briefly imprisoned for alleged subversive activities against the Brazilian government.

After his release he worked for Polygram and CBS Records until 1980, when he embarked on new travels in Europe and Africa. It was during this trip that he walked the route of Santiago de Compostela, some 500 miles (800 km) of roads first used during the Middle Ages by pilgrims traveling from France to Spain. This extensive journey, inflected by a renewed interest in Catholicism, formed the basis of his first book, O diário de um mago (1987), which was published in English as The Diary of a Magus in 1992 and was reissued as The Pilgrimage in 1995.

In 1988 Coelho published O alquimista (The Alchemist), a mystical account of an Andalusian shepherd boy’s journey across North Africa in search of treasure. After being dropped by its first publisher, the book was reissued to great success in Brazil and—in translation—abroad. His memoir As Valkírias (1992; The Valkyries) recounts a quest to speak with angels, undertaken with his first wife during their youthful immersion in the countercultural milieu of the 1970s. He again turned to fiction with Na margem do rio Piedra eu sentei e chorei (1994; By the River Piedra I Sat Down and Wept), which concerns the spiritual reawakening of a young Spanish woman.

Coelho, who had recorded the trajectory of his return to the Catholic faith in The Pilgrimage and The Valkyries, continued to centre both his fiction and nonfiction works on questions of faith and spirituality. O monte cinco (1996; The Fifth Mountain) retells the story of the Biblical prophet Elijah, and Manual do guerreiro da luz (1997; Manual of the Warrior of Light) couches a selection of spiritual exhortations from well-known religious figures in a fictional framework. The Story of Pencil

The story of a pencil is simple at first but contains a lot of messages to get by. The following points can be learned from the story of a pencil by Paulo Coelho:

1. Make corrections after learning from your mistakes.

In daily life, a pencil is a helpful instrument that we use for both writing and drawing. Unintentionally making a mistake while using a pencil is possible, but there is an eraser on the pencil that you can use to correct the error and your written or drawn work. Because we are imperfect, humans make a lot of mistakes, but by learning from them, we can improve and gain wisdom. We must be able to start over after missteps and move on with our lives with a new outlook rather than letting them hold us back.

2. Life must be approached firmly but without too much pressure.

Whether writing or sketching, a pencil writes perfectly effectively when it is held firmly and directed. If the pencil is squeezed too hard, the tip may shatter or the pencil may split in half. The second thing we can learn from the pencil is that it's sage to approach life firmly with rules and disciplines in order to help us become valuable to both ourselves and the rest of the world. Self-imposed pressure might lead to your emotional, bodily, and spiritual collapse. As you build on the big idea, be patient and accomplish your goals one at a time.

3. You'll also need polishing and sharpening.

A pencil needs to be sharpened and polished in order to write clearly. Writing is improved with a sharp pointed pencil over a dull one. To create a clean, flawless drawing, an artist must ensure that their pencil is properly sharpened. As humans, we also need to be sharpened and polished to increase our value. We go through difficult times to strengthen us and get ready for bigger responsibilities in the future.

4. Looks can be deceiving.

Pencils are available in a range of colors and designs, depending on how the producer wants it to seem. The quality of the lead inside the pencil, not the branding, is what distinguishes it. Because humans have mastered the skill of faking, appearances can be misleading. So spend some time getting to know someone rather than a passing judgment based solely on appearance. To reach the right conclusion, understand their motivations and aspirations.

5. There is a use for each type of pencil.

For a variety of uses, numerous types of pencils are produced. There are pencils made for writing that write flawlessly, others made for drawing, and even another made for coloring. Each pencil performs well for its intended use.

Each of us was given a purpose when we were born as humans. Everyone possesses a quality about oneself that makes them unique. Our aim is to discover our purpose, develop as individuals, and serve the world. The article "Self Improvement is a Continuous Process" is another option.

6. Everything you do leaves a trace.

On a blank pad, writing with a pencil produces a mark that cannot be totally removed. It can be used to write things that unite people or things that divide them and make the world a better place. Every action we take while on this earth leaves a permanent imprint. When you treat someone kindly, it stays with them forever, just as when you treat someone badly, it stays with them forever. Our intention is to leave a fantastic lasting impression and foster happy memories.
