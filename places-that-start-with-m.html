Explanation:

4. Application 1. A car starts from rest and attains a speed of 40 km/h in 20 seconds. What is its acceleration in km/h/s? 2. A cyclist going up the Kawakawa Hill at a speed of 5 m/s slows down to 1 m/s in 8 seconds. Find his acceleration. 3. Which will have a higher speed after a minute of travel starting from rest - a car accelerating at 2 m/s² or a bus accelerating at 5 m/s²? 4. Give concrete examples of accelerating objects that can be seen in the following places: b. at an amusement park a. on the road

To find the acceleration of the car, we need to first convert the initial and final velocities to meters per second. 40 km/h = 11.11 m/s and 0 km/h = 0 m/s.

Using the formula, acceleration = (final velocity - initial velocity)/time taken, we get:

acceleration = (11.11 m/s - 0 m/s)/20 s = 0.5555 m/s²

To convert this to km/h/s, we multiply by 3600 (to convert m/s² to km/h/s) and get:

acceleration = 0.5555 m/s² × 3600/1000 = 2 km/h/s

Again, we need to convert the speeds to meters per second. 5 m/s and 1 m/s.

Using the same formula as before, we get:

acceleration = (1 m/s - 5 m/s)/8 s = -0.5 m/s²

Note that the acceleration is negative because the cyclist is slowing down.

To answer this question, we need to compare the final velocities of the two vehicles after one minute of travel.

For the car, we can use the formula:

final velocity = initial velocity + (acceleration × time taken)

Here, the initial velocity is 0 m/s, acceleration is 2 m/s² and time taken is 60 seconds. So,

final velocity = 0 m/s + (2 m/s² × 60 s) = 120 m/s

For the bus, using the same formula with acceleration = 5 m/s², we get:

final velocity = 0 m/s + (5 m/s² × 60 s) = 300 m/s

Therefore, the bus will have a higher speed after a minute of travel starting from rest.

b. At an amusement park: A roller coaster accelerating downwards from a high point, a swinging pirate ship accelerating back and forth, a freefall ride accelerating downwards before coming to a stop.
