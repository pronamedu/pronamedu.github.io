10. A pile of sand is in the form of a right circular cone of altitude 7 ft and slant height of 25 ft. what is the weight of the sand in lbs if its density is 100 lb/cu. ft?


Answer:

[tex]\therefore[/tex]The weight of sand is 422016lbs

Explanation:

To know the weight of the sand, first is to find the radius of the circular cone by using Pythagorean theorem. Then, find the volume of the cone by using the Volume of the Cone formula. Afterwards, multiply the volume of the cone and the density. In this way, we can find out what is the weight of the sand.

Solve for radius of the cone by using the Pythagorean theorem. Let [tex]b[/tex] be the cone's radius.

[tex]\boxed{\bold{c^2=a^2+b^2}} \\\\ \rm{25^2=7^2+b^2} \\\\ \rm{b^2=576} \\\\ \rm{b=\sqrt{576} = 24}[/tex]

Now, solve for the volume of the cone.

[tex]\boxed{\bold{V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2h}} \\\\ \rm{V = \frac{1}{3}(3.14)(24)^2(7)} \\\\ \rm{ = \frac{(3.14)(576)(7)}{3}} \\\\ \rm{=4220.16}[/tex]

Thus, solving for weight of the sand

[tex]\boxed{\bold{W_{Weight \ of \ the \ Sand} = V_{Volume \ of \ the \ Cone} \times D_{Density \ of \ the \ sand}}} \\\\ \rm{W = (4220.16)(100)} \\\\ \rm{W = 422016\ lbs}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex]The weight of sand is 422016lbs

16. sand is pouring from a spout at the rate of 1 cubic meter per min. it forms a cone whose height is always one-half the radius of the base. at what rate is the height increasing when the cone is 6m high


since h = 1/2 r then you'll have r as:
r = 2h
note that the formula in finding the volume of a cone is defined by:
[tex]V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h[/tex]
where r in that case is 2h. then you'll have it as:
[tex]V = \frac{1}{3} \pi (2h)^2 h[/tex]
[tex]V = \frac{1}{3} \pi (4h^2) h[/tex]
[tex]V = \frac{4}{3} \pi h^3[/tex]
since you are to find the rate at which the height is increasing then you will have to differentiate the derived equivalent formula.
[tex]V = \frac{4}{3} \pi h^3[/tex]
[tex] \frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{4}{3} \pi (3h^2) \frac{dh}{dt} [/tex]
[tex] \frac{dV}{dt} = 4 \pi h^2 \frac{dh}{dt} [/tex]
substituting the given you'll have:
Given: dV/dt = 1cu.m/min
                h = 6m
[tex] \frac{dV}{dt} = 4 \pi h^2 \frac{dh}{dt} [/tex]
[tex]1 = 4 \pi (6^2) \frac{dh}{dt} [/tex]
[tex] \frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{1}{144 \pi } [/tex]
or
[tex] \frac{dh}{dt} = 0.00221 \frac{m}{min} [/tex]

Surface Area of a Cone

A cone is a solid with a circular base. It has a curved surface which tapers (i.e. decreases in size) to a vertex at the top. The height of the cone is the perpendicular distance from the base to the vertex.

The net of a solid cone consists of a small circle and a sector of a larger circle. The arc of the sector has the same length as the circumference of the smaller circle.

The following figures show the formula for surface area of a cone. Scroll down the page if you need more examples and explanations.

surface area of cone

Surface area of cone = Area of sector + area of circle

= πrs + πr2

= πr(r + s)

21. Analyze the effect on Demand or/and Supply Curve sand the Equilibrium Price and Quantity of the Ice Cream Cones a)Hot weather b)Storm destroys sugarcane crops c)Tax increase for sugar-based products d)Mass lay-off of employees e)Price of the ice cream cones increase


Answer:

a) Hot weather - The effect on demand for ice cream cones will increase due to hot weather. This means that the demand curve will shift to the right, resulting in an increase in both equilibrium price and quantity of ice cream cones.

b) Storm destroys sugarcane crops - The effect on supply of sugar used in ice cream cones will decrease due to the storm's destruction of sugarcane crops. This means that the supply curve will shift to the left, resulting in an increase in the equilibrium price and decrease in the equilibrium quantity of ice cream cones.

c) Tax increase for sugar-based products - The effect on supply for ice cream cones will decrease due to the tax increase for sugar-based products. This means that the supply curve will shift to the left, resulting in an increase in the equilibrium price and decrease in the equilibrium quantity of ice cream cones.

d) Mass lay-off of employees - The effect on demand for ice cream cones will decrease due to mass lay-off of employees. This means that the demand curve will shift to the left, resulting in a decrease in both equilibrium price and quantity of ice cream cones.

e) Price of the ice cream cones increase - The effect on demand for ice cream cones will decrease due to an increase in price. This means that the demand curve will shift to the left, resulting in a decrease in equilibrium quantity of ice cream cones, while the equilibrium price increases.
