19. Innumerate the different cultivable species of mud crabs? ​


Different cultivable species of mud crabs

Mud crabs are classified into four species: Scylla serrata, S. S. tranquebarica S. and paramamosain olivacea, which are the focus of commercial fisheries and aquaculture production across their range. They are one of the most valuable crab species on the planet, with the majority of their commercial production being sent to market live. This is the first FAO aquaculture manual on this genus, and it covers everything from basic biology and aquaculture production to stock packaging and market readiness.

This guidebook brings together information on mud crab biology, hatchery and nursery technology, grow-out systems, disease management, processing, and packaging to provide a comprehensive approach to mud crab aquaculture production. When compared to other types of aquaculture, mud crab farming still has a wide range of options, including the use of wild-caught seedstock as well as seedstock produced in a hatchery; farming systems ranging from extensive to intensive, monoculture to polyculture; and farm sites ranging from mangrove forests to well-built aquaculture ponds or fattening cages. As an outcome, there is no one-size-fits-all approach to mud crab farming, but methodologies, technologies, and philosophies have been created that can be tailored to match the demands of farmers and governments
