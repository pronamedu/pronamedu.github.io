24. If a recipe requires 3 cups of flour to bake the cake, how many grams of flour is need assuming that its density is 0.5g/mL? (Show and upload the picture of your solution below)Use the assumption:1 cup flour = 236.59 mL*​


To convert cups to milliliters, we can use the conversion factor provided:

1 cup flour = 236.59 mL

To find the mass of flour needed, we can use the density provided:

density of flour = 0.5 g/mL

We can use the formula:

mass = volume x density

where the volume is in milliliters and the density is in grams per milliliter.

So for 3 cups of flour, the volume in milliliters is:

3 cups x 236.59 mL/cup = 709.77 mL

And the mass of flour in grams is:

mass = volume x density = 709.77 mL x 0.5 g/mL = 354.89 g

Therefore, 354.89 grams of flour is needed to bake the cake.
