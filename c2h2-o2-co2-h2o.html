7. balance the following equations ? CH4 + O2 CO2 + H2O CaCl2 + AgNO3- Ca(NO3)2 + AgCl C2H6O + O2 CO2 + H2O


Further explanation  

Equalization of chemical reactions (balanced chemical equations) can be done using variables. Steps in equalizing the reaction equation:  

1. gives a coefficient on substances involved in the equation of reaction such as a, b, or c, etc.  2. make an equation based on the similarity of the number of atoms where the number of atoms = coefficient × index (subscript) between reactant and product  3. Select the coefficient of the substance with the most complex chemical formula equal to 1  

1. CH₄ + O₂ ⇒ CO₂ + H₂O

CH₄ + aO₂ -----> bCO₂ + cH₂O  

C: left = 1, right = b -> b = 1  

H: left = 4, right = 2c ---> 4 = 2c ---> c = 2  

O: left = 2a, right = 2b + c ---> 2a = 2b + c ---> 2a = 2.1 + 2 -> 2a = 4 -> a = 2  

The equation becomes:  

CH₄ + 2 O₂ -----> CO₂ + 2 H₂O  

2. CaCl₂ + AgNO₃ ⇒Ca(NO₃)₂ + AgCl

aCaCl₂ + bAgNO₃ ⇒Ca(NO₃)₂ + cAgCl

Ca : left = a , right = 1 ⇒ a=1

Cl : left = 2, right = c ⇒ c=2

Ag : left = b, right = c  ⇒ b=c = 2

NO₃ : left = b, right = 2 ⇒ b=2

The equation becomes:  

CaCl₂ + 2AgNO₃ ⇒Ca(NO₃)₂ + 2AgCl

3. C₂H₆O + O₂ ⇒ CO₂ + H₂O

C₂H₆O + aO₂ ⇒ bCO₂ + cH₂O

C : left=2, right =b ⇒ b=2

H : left =6, right = 2c ⇒ 2c=6 ⇒c=3

O : left =2a+1, right=2b+c ⇒2a+1=2b+c⇒2a+1=2.2+3⇒2a=6⇒a=3

The equation becomes:  

C₂H₆O + 3O₂ ⇒ 2CO₂ + 3H₂O

(b) C(s) + O₂(g) → CO₂(g) ∆H = –394 kJ

(c) H₂(g) + 1/2O₂(g) → H₂O(l) ∆H = –286 kJ

The overall reaction is

2C(s) + H₂(g) → C₂H₂(g)

SOLUTION:

Looking at the overall reaction, we need 1 moles of C₂H₂ as a product so we reverse Equation (a) to get

(d) [tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & 2\text{CO}_2(g) & + & \text{H}_2\text{O}(l) & \rightarrow & \text{C}_2\text{H}_2(g) & + & \frac{5}{2}\text{O}_2(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -(-\text{1300. kJ}) \\ & & & & & & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = \text{1300. kJ} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

(e) [tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & 2\text{C}(s) & + & 2\text{O}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & 2\text{CO}_2(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = 2(-\text{394 kJ}) \\ & & & & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{788 kJ} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & 2\text{CO}_2(g) & + & \text{H}_2\text{O}(l) & \rightarrow & \text{C}_2\text{H}_2(g) & + & \frac{5}{2}\text{O}_2(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = \text{1300. kJ} \\ & 2\text{C}(s) & + & 2\text{O}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & 2\text{CO}_2(g) & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{788 kJ} \\ & \text{H}_2(g) & + & \frac{1}{2}\text{O}_2(g) & \rightarrow & \text{H}_2\text{O}(l) & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{286 kJ} \\ \hline & 2\text{C}(s) & + & \text{H}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & 2\text{C}_2\text{H}_2(g) & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = \text{226 kJ} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

23. . C2H2 undergoes combustion to produce carbon dioxide and water vapor.a. Write and balance the chemical equation of the reaction.b. If 75 L CO2 is produced, how many liters of C2H2 are required?c. What volume of H2O is produced?d. What volume of O2 is required?​


Question a:

Write and balance the chemical equation of the reaction.

Strategy:

Remember that the formula of an element or compound cannot be changed when balancing a chemical equation. The equation is balanced by placing the appropriate coefficients in front of the formulas. Follow the procedures in balancing a chemical equation.

Solution:

The unbalanced equation is

C₂H₂ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

In a balanced chemical equation, the number and types of atoms on each side of the equation must be the same. We see that there is one C atom on the products side and there are two C atoms on the reactants side.

C₂H₂ + O₂ → 2CO₂ + H₂O

At this stage, the C and H atoms are balanced, but the O atoms are not because there are 5 O atoms on the products side and only 2 O atoms on the reactants side. This inequality can be eliminated by writing [tex]\frac{5}{2}[/tex] in front of the O₂ on the reactants side:

C₂H₂ + [tex]\frac{5}{2}[/tex]O₂ → 2CO₂ + H₂O

This is a balanced equation. However, equations are normally balanced with the smallest set of whole number coefficients. Multiplying both sides of the equation by 2 gives whole number coefficients

2(C₂H₂ + [tex]\frac{5}{2}[/tex]O₂ → 2CO₂ + H₂O)

or

2C₂H₂ + 5O₂ → 4CO₂ + 2H₂O

Question b:

If 75 L CO₂ is produced, how many liters of C₂H₂ is required?

Strategy:

Let's assume that the temperature and pressure of C₂H₂ and CO₂ are the same. Which gas law do we need to relate the volume of the gases to the moles of gases?

Solution:

According to Avogadro's law, at the same temperature and pressure, the number of moles of gases are directly related to their volumes. From the equation, 2 mol C₂H₂ is equivalent to 4 mol CO₂; therefore, we can also write that 2 L C₂H₂ is equivalent to 4 L CO₂. The volume of C₂H₂ required is given by

[tex]volume \: of \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{2} = 75 \: L \: \text{C}\text{O}_{2} × \frac{2 \: L \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{2}}{4 \: L \: \text{C}\text{O}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{volume \: of \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{2} = 37.5 \: L}[/tex]

Question c:

What volume of H₂O is produced?

Strategy:

Let's assume that the temperature and pressure of H₂O and CO₂ are the same.

Solution:

From the equation, 2 mol H₂O is equivalent to 4 mol CO₂; therefore, we can also write that 2 L H₂O is equivalent to 4 L CO₂. The volume of H₂O produced is given by

[tex]volume \: of \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} = 75 \: L \: \text{C}\text{O}_{2} × \frac{2 \: L \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}{4 \: L \: \text{C}\text{O}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{volume \: of \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} = 37.5 \: L}[/tex]

Question d:

What volume of O₂ is required?

Strategy:

Let's assume that the temperature and pressure of O₂ and CO₂ are the same.

Solution:

From the equation, 5 mol O₂ is equivalent to 4 mol CO₂; therefore, we can also write that 2 L O₂ is equivalent to 4 L CO₂. The volume of O₂ required is given by

[tex]volume \: of \: \text{O}_{2} = 75 \: L \: \text{C}\text{O}_{2} × \frac{5 \: L \: \text{O}_{2}}{4 \: L \: \text{C}\text{O}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{volume \: of \: \text{O}_{2} = 93.75 \: L}[/tex]
