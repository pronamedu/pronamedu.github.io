To determine which compound contains the greatest mass of oxygen, we need to calculate the mass of oxygen in each compound and compare them.

0.75 mol of ethanol (C2H5OH)

The formula of ethanol is C2H5OH. To calculate the mass of oxygen, we need to find the number of oxygen atoms in one molecule of ethanol, which is one. Then, we can use the molar mass of oxygen (16.00 g/mol) to calculate the mass of oxygen in 0.75 mol of ethanol:

mass of oxygen = 1 oxygen atom x 16.00 g/mol x 0.75 mol

mass of oxygen = 12.00 g

0.60 mol of formic acid (HCO2H)

The formula of formic acid is HCO2H. To calculate the mass of oxygen, we need to find the number of oxygen atoms in one molecule of formic acid, which is two. Then, we can use the molar mass of oxygen (16.00 g/mol) to calculate the mass of oxygen in 0.60 mol of formic acid:

mass of oxygen = 2 oxygen atoms x 16.00 g/mol x 0.60 mol

mass of oxygen = 19.20 g

1.0 mol of water (H2O)

The formula of water is H2O. To calculate the mass of oxygen, we need to find the number of oxygen atoms in one molecule of water, which is one. Then, we can use the molar mass of oxygen (16.00 g/mol) to calculate the mass of oxygen in 1.0 mol of water:

mass of oxygen = 1 oxygen atom x 16.00 g/mol x 1.0 mol

mass of oxygen = 16.00 g

17. 2NH3 (g) + CO2 (g) S NH2CONH2 (aq) + H2O (0) The standard free energy change, AG°, at 25 °C equals –13.6 kJ. Find the value for the equilibrium constant, K. at 25 °C (298 K)​


Answer:

The value for the equilibrium constant, K, at 25°C is 0.96

Explanation:

The equilibrium constant (K) is the measure of the relative concentrations of the reactants and products at equilibrium, and can be calculated from the standard free energy change (ΔG°) of a reaction. The relationship between ΔG° and K is given by the equation:

ΔG° = -RTlnK

where R is the gas constant (8.31 J/mol·K) and T is the temperature in Kelvin (298 K for 25°C).

Given the ΔG° for the reaction:

2NH3 (g) + CO2 (g) S NH2CONH2 (aq) + H2O (0)

ΔG° = -13.6 kJ = -13600 J

Rearranging the equation to solve for K:

lnK = ΔG° / (-RT) = -13600 J / (-8.31 J/mol·K * 298 K) = -0.04095

So, K = e^(-0.04095) = 0.96

Therefore, the value for the equilibrium constant, K, at 25°C is 0.96. This means that at equilibrium, the concentration of the reactants is higher than the products, favoring the reactants.
