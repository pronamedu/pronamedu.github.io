10. Pa help poScienceTopic: Gay-Lussac's Law-A sample of nitrogen (N_{2}) occupies 300 mL at 200°C under a pressure of 3.0 atm. If the pressure is increased to 9.0 atm, by how much will the temperature increase to keep the volume constant at 300 mL?​


Answer:

Gay-Lussac's law states that the pressure and temperature of a gas are directly proportional when the volume is held constant. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

P1/T1 = P2/T2

where P1 and T1 are the initial pressure and temperature, respectively, and P2 and T2 are the final pressure and temperature, respectively.

In this problem, we are given:

P1 = 3.0 atm

T1 = 200°C + 273.15 = 473.15 K (temperature must be in Kelvin)

V1 = 300 mL

P2 = 9.0 atm

V2 = V1 (volume is held constant)

We can use the formula to solve for T2:

P1/T1 = P2/T2

3.0/473.15 = 9.0/T2

T2 = (3.0/473.15) x 9.0

T2 = 0.057 K

Therefore, the temperature will increase by 0.057 K to maintain a constant volume of 300 mL when the pressure is increased from 3.0 atm to 9.0 atm.

19. A popular household vinegar contains 5% by volume acetic acid , how much acetic acid is in 200 ml of the vinegar ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \%(v/v) = 5\% \\ & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{200 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solute (acetic acid).

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{\%(v/v)}{100\%} \times V_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{5\%}{100\%} \times \text{200 mL} \\ & = \boxed{\text{10 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of acetic acid in the vinegar is 10 mL.

Definition of Solution

Solutions are homogeneous mixtures formed by reactions between the solvent and the solute. The solvent is the substance that is present in the greatest amount, while the solute is the substance in which the amount is less.

Definition of Solution Concentration

The concentration of a solution is a quantity that shows the concentration of a solution through the ratio between the solvent and the solute. If there is a lot of solute, then the solution formed has a high concentration (concentration). Conversely, if the solute is small, the solution formed has a low concentration (dilute).

There are many units for the concentration of this solution, including mass percent, volume percent, mass percent per volume, molarity, molality, and mole fraction.
