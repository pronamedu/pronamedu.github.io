When force is applied, an object might change its size and shape. There are three conditions that will happen if a force is applied to an object. These are :

1. It will change the speed of the object.

2. It will change the direction of the object.

3. It will deform an object.

In the case when we drop a vase, it accelerates the speed of the vase dropping, the moment we applied force into it. Since vase are breakable, once it is dropped, it will break. The greater the force we applied to the vase, the greater its acceleration thus giving a greater impact to the vase.

In the case of pounding a styropore cup, when we exert too much force into the cup, the molecules of the cup aligned causing it to be deformed.

In the case of cutting a piece of cardboard, when we exert a force on the scissors, it will make the cardboard be cut into whatever shape we want it to be. This is due to the force applied to the scissor that makes it accelerates and move causing the scissors to cut the cardboard. This will also be the case of when we sharpen a pencil.

In the case of squeezing a lemon, the compressive force that we exert into the lemon will which squeezes the lemon causing it to change its size and shape.
