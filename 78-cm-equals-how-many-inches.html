11. How many moles are in 78 grams of CaCO3 ?


Answer:

To determine the number of moles in Calcium Carbonate (CaCO3) with a mass of 78 grams, we need to use its molar mass which is calculated by adding the atomic masses of its elements:

CaCO3 = 40.08 g/mol (Ca) + 12.01 g/mol (C) + 3(16.00 g/mol) (O)

CaCO3 = 40.08 g/mol + 12.01 g/mol + 48.00 g/mol

CaCO3 = 100.09 g/mol

Now we can use the formula:

moles = mass / molar mass

Substituting the values:

moles = 78 g / 100.09 g/mol

moles = 0.779 moles

Therefore, there are 0.779 moles in 78 grams of CaCO3.
