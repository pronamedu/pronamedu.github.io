12. find the area of the following examples circumference=6​


Answer:

The circumference of the circle is 6 units.

The radius of the circle is 6 / (2 × 3.14) ≈ 0.955 units.

The area of the circle is πr^2 = 3.14 × 0.955^2 ≈ 2.86 square units.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the area of a circle when given its circumference, we can use the formula:

C = 2πr

where C is the circumference, π (pi) is a constant approximately equal to 3.14, and r is the radius of the circle. Solving this formula for r, we get:

r = C / 2π

Now that we know the radius, we can use the formula for the area of a circle:

A = πr^2

Circle with circumference 6:

C = 6

r = C / 2π = 6 / (2 × 3.14) ≈ 0.955

A = πr^2 = 3.14 × 0.955^2 ≈ 2.86

Therefore, the area of the circle with circumference 6 is approximately 2.86 square units. Note that we rounded the values to two decimal places.
