7. what is the concentration of a solution with a 100ml of ethanol mixed with 300ml of water?​


What is the concentration of a solution with 100ml of ethanol mixed with 300ml of water?​

AnswerThis is the Formula for getting the concentration of a solution:

First, you get the total volume of the solution

Solution + Solvent = Total VolumeThen, you first divide the Solute by the Total Volume and then, multiply it by 100(Solvent / Total Volume) x 100Finally, convert your answer to percentage (%)Concentration = %

By the way, Solute the minor component in a solution, dissolved in the solvent. While Solvent is the substance (in larger amount) that dissolve the solvent.

So, for the answer:

Volume of solute (ethanol) = 100ml

Volume of solvent (water) = 300ml

Total volume= 100 + 300 = 400ml

Concentration = (100/400) ×100

                        = (.25) × 100

                        = 25%

26. Compute for the Molarity of a cesium bromide 300ml solution that contains 10.5 grams of CsBr.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{10.5 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{212.814 g/mol} \\ & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{300 mL = 0.300 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{10.5 g}}{\text{212.814 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.0493389 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} M & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0493389 mol}}{\text{0.300 L}} \\ & = \text{0.164 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{0.164 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of cesium bromide is 0.164 M.

28. What is the final temperature of a 180ml gas with a temperature of 47Â°c if the volume is increased to 300ml


Problem:

What is the final temperature of a 180 ml gas with a temperature of 47°c if the volume is increased to 300 ml.

Note:

Convert degree celsius to kelvin

k = °C + 273

Given Data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 180 ml

V₂ (final volume) = 300 ml

T₁ (initial pressure) =  47°c + 273  = 320 K        

T₂ (final pressure) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use Charles's Law which states that volume and temperature are directly proportional when the pressure and other variable are constant. when temperature increases, the volume of the gas also increases and vise versa.

T₂ =  V₂T₁ / V₁

    = 300 ml × 320 K  / 180 ml

    = 533.33 K

Answer:

the final temperature of the gas in Kelvin temperature is 533.33 K

30. the volume of gas at 3.5 atm is 300ml .if tlhe preasure is increased to 5atm.what will be the new volume?​


Good Day....

Problem:

the volume of gas at 3.5 atm is 300ml .if tlhe preasure is increased to 5atm.what will be the new volume?​

Given Data:

V1 (initial volume) = 300 ml                             V2 (final volume) = ? unknown

P1 (initial pressure) = 3.5 atm                           P2 (final pressure = 5 atm

Solution:

This problem can be solved using Boyle's Law which state that volume is inversely proportional to pressure when the temperature and the amount of gas are constant.

Boyle's Law formula is P1V1 = P2V2

To find V2 we can use V2 = P1V1 / P2

V2 = P1V1 / P2

      = 3.5 atm × 300 ml / 5 atm   (cancel the unit atm)

      = 1050 ml / 5

V2 = 210 ml

Answer:

The final volume is 210 ml
