8. At 20°C, a steel rod and an aluminum rod are both 1.25m long. (a) If both rods are heated to 100°C, which will be longer and by how much? (b) if both rods are cooled to -20°C, which will be longer and by how much? note that the coefficient of linear expansion of aluminum at 20°C is 25X10^-6/C° and coefficient of linear expansion of steel at 20°C is 12X10^-6/C°


Answer:

Given

Length (L) = 60 cm

Change in Length (ΔL) = 60 cm + 0.102 cm

Change of Temperature (ΔT) = 0 °C → 100 °C

Coeffiecient of Linear Expansion (α) = ?

α = ΔL / LΔT

α = (60 + 0.102) / (60)(100 - 0)

α = 60.102 / (60)(100)

α = 60.102 / 600

α = 1.0017 x 10⁻⁶ °C⁻¹
