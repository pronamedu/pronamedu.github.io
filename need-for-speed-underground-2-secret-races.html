17. in a race, Ted ran 9 kilometers in the first 1/2 hour. Then, he ran at anaverage speed of 6 kilometers per hour for the remaining distance.If Ted completed the race in 3 hours, what was his average speedfor the whole race?​


Answer:

8 kmph

Step-by-step explanation:

The misconception often is that average speed is computed as the average of the two values for speed. When in fact,

Average speed = Total Distance / Total Time

We can define the situation in slope-intercept form with slope as 6 to determine the total distance.

f(x) = 9 + 6(x - 1/2)

f(x) = 9 + 6x - 3

f(x) = 6 + 6x

f(3) = 6 + 6(3)

f(3) = 6 + 18

f(3) = 24

We can then substitute this value,

Average speed = 24/3 = 8 kmph

Therefore, his average speed is 8kmph.
