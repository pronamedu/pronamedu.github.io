1. 1.DESRIBE HOW PENILE ERECTIONS OCCURR WHAT DO EACH OF THE THREE MALE ACCESSORY GLANDS CONTRIBUTE TO THE SEMEN? 2.IDENTIFY THE IMPORTANCE OF TESTOSTERONE IN MALE REPRODUCTIVE FUNCTION.


Answer:

1. Penile erections occur as a result of increased blood flow to the penis, which causes the spongy tissue in the penis (corpora cavernosa) to become engorged with blood and become firm. This is a complex process involving multiple physiological factors, including the release of nitric oxide from nerve endings in the penis, which stimulates the production of cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP). cGMP relaxes the smooth muscles in the blood vessels of the penis, allowing blood to flow in and fill the spongy tissue. This results in an erection.

The three male accessory glands that contribute to semen production are the seminal vesicles, prostate gland, and bulbourethral glands. The seminal vesicles produce a fluid rich in fructose, which provides energy for sperm motility. The prostate gland produces a milky fluid that contains enzymes, citric acid, and prostate-specific antigen (PSA). This fluid helps to neutralize acidic vaginal secretions and provides a fluid medium for the sperm to travel in. The bulbourethral glands produce a clear, viscous fluid that lubricates the urethra and neutralizes any acidic urine that may be present.

2. Testosterone is a hormone produced by the testes in males, and it plays a critical role in male reproductive function. Testosterone is responsible for the development of male secondary sex characteristics, such as increased muscle mass, body hair, and deepening of the voice. It also plays a key role in the production of sperm, as it stimulates the Leydig cells in the testes to produce testosterone, which in turn stimulates the production of sperm by the Sertoli cells.

Testosterone also plays a role in maintaining sexual desire and libido in males. It stimulates the production of nitric oxide, which is necessary for the process of penile erection. Low levels of testosterone can lead to a decrease in libido, erectile dysfunction, and other sexual problems. Additionally, testosterone is important for overall health and well-being in males, as it is associated with bone density, muscle mass, and overall energy levels.
