4. 5. The surface area of a cylinder varies jointly as the radius and the sum of the radius and the height. A cylinder with a height of 8 cm and a radius of 4 cm has a surface area of 961. What is the surface area of a cylinder with a radius of 3 cm and a height of 10 cm?​

It is given that the area A of a cylinder varies jointly as the radius r and the sum of the radius and height [tex](r + h)[/tex] and [tex]A = 96\pi \: cm^2[/tex] when [tex]r = 4 \: cm[/tex] and [tex]h = 8 \: cm[/tex], therefore

[tex]A = kr(r + h)\\[/tex]

[tex]⇨ \: 96\pi = k \times 4 (4 + 8)\\[/tex]

[tex]⇨ \: 96\pi = k \times 4 \times 12[/tex]

[tex]⇨ \: 48k = 96\pi[/tex]

[tex]⇨ k = \frac{96\pi}{48} = 2\pi[/tex]

Thus, the general equation is [tex]A = 2\pi r(r + h)[/tex]

Since [tex]r = 3 \: cm[/tex] and [tex]h = 10 \: cm[/tex], therefore,

[tex]A = 2\pi r(r + h) = 2\pi \times 3(3 + 10) = 2\pi \times 3 \times 13 = 78\pi[/tex]

Hence, the surface area of the cylinder with radius [tex]3 \: cm[/tex] and height [tex]10 \: cm[/tex] is [tex]78\pi \: cm^2[/tex].
