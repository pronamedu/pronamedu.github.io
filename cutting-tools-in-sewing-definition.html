Answer:

1.The L-square is a two-piece ruler arranged in an L shape. The L-square is used to create scaled patterns from original sewing patterns.

2.Cutting Tools For Sewing Paper

Papercutting tools are an important tool in your sewing kit. You could use a regular pair of scissors, a special shorter blade scissor for patterns, or even a rotary cutter whose sole use is for cutting paper.

3.Thread clippers are small scissors used for nipping excess thread and parts of the fabric that have frayed or unraveled. ... These scissors also have notches on their blades, which allow you to make the buttonholes without cutting the edge of the fabric.

4.Marking tools are used to transfer the pattern symbols onto the fabric to accurately make the garment. ... The smooth edge makes a solid line marking and is used for delicate fabrics such as silk and chiffon.

5.Definition of tailor's chalk

: a thin flat piece of hard chalk or soapstone used by tailors and seamstresses for making temporary marks on cloth.

6.The importance of defining the tools and equipment in sewing is two-fold. First, it will give you a way to calculate startup costs and, secondly, in learning the functions of sewing tools and equipment, you will be able to define exactly what sort of duties a person in this industry performs.

7.Sewing pins are used to hold fabric together prior to sewing. They temporarily hold the fabric in place when attaching and cutting patterns, or while sewing. ... Sewing pins come in many lengths and thicknesses, and have different pinheads, materials, and tips, each with a specific purpose.

8.Pressing is an up and down movement when you need to open curves and flatten seams. Ironing is a side to side movement used purely to iron out creases.

9.A sleeve board helps you iron a shirt without making a crease in the sleeve. Sleeve boards are a great sewing tool and are useful for much more than just pressing sleeves. More examples. You can put a sleeve board on your ironing board to make the sleeves easier to iron.

27. measuring toolscutting tools marking tools sewing tools​


Answer:

Here are some examples of measuring tools:

Ruler: A flat, straight tool with marked increments used for measuring lengths.

Caliper: A tool with two adjustable arms used for measuring the dimensions of an object.

Tape measure: A flexible, retractable tool with marked increments used for measuring lengths and distances.

Protractor: A tool with a circular or semicircular head and marked degrees used for measuring angles.

Level: A tool with a bubble vial used for determining if a surface is horizontal or vertical.

Here are some examples of cutting tools:

Knife: A tool with a sharp blade used for cutting or slicing.

Scissors: A pair of cutting tools with two pivoted blades used for cutting paper or other materials.

Shears: A pair of cutting tools with long, curved blades used for cutting cloth or other materials.

Saw: A tool with a handle and a thin, sharp blade used for cutting wood or other materials.

Chisel: A tool with a handle and a sharp, flat blade used for carving or shaping wood or other materials.

Here are some examples of marking tools:

Pencil: A writing tool with a graphite core used for drawing or writing on paper.

Pen: A writing tool with a metal or plastic tip and a container for ink used for writing on paper.

Marker: A writing tool with a felt or foam tip and a container for ink or pigment used for making bold marks on paper or other surfaces.

Stencil: A template with cut-out shapes used for creating repeated patterns or designs.

Compass: A tool with a pointed end and a pivot used for drawing circles or arcs.

Here are some examples of sewing tools:

Needle: A thin, pointed tool with a hole at one end used for sewing.

Thread: A thin, flexible material used for sewing or weaving.

Thimble: A small, finger-shaped tool with a raised end used for pushing a needle through fabric.

Scissors: A pair of cutting tools with two pivoted blades used for cutting thread or fabric.

Sewing machine: A machine with a needle and a motor used for stitching fabric or other materials.
