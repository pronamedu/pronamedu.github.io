20. which of the following is the same as ( x-5)²​


Answer:

This question is a relatively standard way of presenting a problem, so to answer it shouldn’t take any rewriting. Let’s start by talking about what the squared sign actually means. When you square something, you take it, and multiply it by itself.

This means that if we have (x - 5)^2, we actually have (x - 5)(x - 5). The same applies to powers higher than two. If it were to the third power, we’d have (x - 5)(x - 5)(x - 5). But in our problem we only have to deal with the power of two. So expanding our equation lands us with an equation that looks like this:

(x - 5)(x - 5) = ?

The next step is to use a little tool called FOIL. FOIL is an acronym for the order you multiply numbers when multiplying parenthesis. It stands for First Outside Inside Last. In this case, the first numbers are both x’s, so the first part of our answer is x^2. The outside numbers are x and negative 5, so the second part of our answer is minus 5x. The inside numbers are negative 5 and x, so the third part of our equation is minus negative five again. Then finally the Last numbers are both negative fives, and since a negative times a negative is a positive, the last part of our answer is plus 25. This leaves us with the equation:

x^2 - 5x -5x +25

Simplifying it a little, we can turn this into:

x^2 – 10x + 25

Unless we are told what the equation is supposed to look like, this is about as far as we can go. If we put this into a graphing utility, we find it looks like this

Explain why the equation x + 2 = 5 and 2 + x = 5 have that same solution but the equation x - 2 = 5 and 2 - x = 5 do not.

-

Let's try to define Commutative Property:

Commutative Property is only applicable for addition and multiplication. This property means that even you swapped numbers from their positions, like x + 2 and 2 + x, the answer will be the same.

• Explanation:

• Based on observation, the first two equations have the same solution since both of them operates addition and in the commutative property.

Here's the solution:

[tex] \tt x + 2 = 5 \\ \tt x = 5 - 2 \\ \tt \: x= 3[/tex]

[tex] \tt 2 + x = 5 \\ \tt x = 5 - 2 \\ \tt \: x= 3[/tex]

• The same reason goes with the two remaining equations. The commutative property is not applicable to subtraction and even in division. That's why the answer will be different when you swapped the given quantities.

Here's solution:

[tex] \tt x - 2 = 5 \\ \tt x = 5 + 2 \\ \tt \: x= 7[/tex]

[tex] \tt 2 - x = 5 \\ \tt - x = 5 - 2 \\ \tt \: x= - 3[/tex]
