4. what is the moral lesson in the legend of the firefly


The Philippines have seen changes in their folklore and myth. During the early years of the 20th century, many writers began taking the traditional folklore characters and turning them into fairy tales which would be more known in the Philippine literature as "legends." These legends are very known in the Philippines. This have been part of the filipino culture as they are stories being told to the kids because they teach moral lessons.

The "Legend of the Firefly" is a heartbreaking legend came from Bataan, Philippines.

The legend goes like this, a long time ago, there was a handsome, well-built young man who is dreaming of marrying the most beautiful maiden he could lay his eyes on. He was boastful and arrogant. One day, he decided to go up to the mountain until he saw a beautiful and lovely maiden wearing the whitest dress. For him, she was the most beautiful maiden he ever saw. As he was getting close to her, the maiden suddenly disappeared. He went looking for her in the whole forest until he was tired and mad. And so he screamed: "You are not really beautiful! Did you hear me? You have a flat nose, you're a cross-eyed and you have the biggest ears I've ever seen." He, then, lied down under a tree and saw the beautiful maiden. He was so shocked, he knew that all the things he had said was just out of madness and are not true.

The maiden felt so insulted and hurt at the same time, and so she cursed the man to become a bug. He may only turn back into a human form when he has shown the maiden that will surpass the beauty of fairy. He flew near and far to find the maiden prettier than the fairy. Day and night, he didn't stop searching. He brought a light with him to help his search during the dark night, he was the only bug with a light, desperately searching so he could go back to normal again.

The moral lesson in the story is that there are consequences in the mistakes you do. Those consequences are not revenge, but it is for us to learn our lessons and that we will know better the next time. As for the bug, he has work hard in order to get back what he wants.

In real life, we do make mistakes, we hurt people and lose their trusts. We end up doing the consequences and try hard to earn their trusts just like the firefly in the story.

Most importantly, we have to be careful on what we're gonna say. We use our mouth to speak our mind but sometimes, we go overboard and hurt them with our words. In one song it says, "Words cut deeper than a knife." Let that be a reminder so we avoid hurting other people's feelings.

18. Characters with Description of The legend of firefly


Firefly: The main character of the story, Firefly is a small and nimble insect with a glowing abdomen. He is known for his courage, intelligence, and quick reflexes, which he uses to outsmart his enemies and save his friends.

Luna: A beautiful and wise moth who serves as Firefly's mentor and guide. Luna is known for her vast knowledge of the forest and her ability to navigate even in the darkest of nights.

Spider: A cunning and devious predator who preys on smaller insects like Firefly. Spider is known for his patience and his ability to spin intricate webs that ensnare his prey.

Owl: A wise and majestic bird who serves as a protector of the forest. Owl is known for his keen eyesight and his ability to fly silently through the night.

Beetle: A slow-moving but dependable insect who serves as Firefly's loyal friend and companion. Beetle is known for his strength and his unwavering loyalty to his friends.
