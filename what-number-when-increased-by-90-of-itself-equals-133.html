Answer:

The number is 70.

90% of 70 = 0.9 (70) = 63

70 + 63 = 133

133 = 133 (True)

16. a number increase by one-sixth of itself equals 14. what is its algebraic symbol?​


Answer:

Algebraic Expressions

The algebraic symbol is x + [tex]\frac{x}{6}[/tex] = 14.

Step-by-step explanation:

Expressing a statement into its algebraic form makes it easier to solve. In the statement above, it states that a number which increases by one-sixth of itself is equal to 14.

The first step is to find a symbol for the number. In algebra, x is the most common symbol used to denote a missing number. Then, the next part states that the number increases by one-sixth of itself. We can write that as + x/6 (we read it as plus x over 6, which is a fraction). Finally, just add = 14 at the end of the equation.

Therefore, our equation should be written as:

x + [tex]\frac{x}{6}[/tex] = 14.
