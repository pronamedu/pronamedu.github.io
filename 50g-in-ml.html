1. 1. If 50 mL of liquid has a mass of 50g, what is its density?​


Answer:

1.0 g/cm^3

In physics, density define as a degree of measure that describe how much space of an object or substance (volume) takes up in relation to the amount of matter in that object or substance (mass).

Formula for Finding Density

p= m/v

Where

p stands for Density

m stands for mass

v stands for volume

Given m = 50 mL of liquidv = 50 g of

ProblemFind the Density of the liquid

Solution

Unit for Density is can be gram per cubic centimeter

Convert mL to cm^3

1 mL = 1 cm^3

50 mL = 50 cm^3

Solve for Density

p = m/v

p = 50 g / 50 cm^3

p = 1.0 g/cm^3

Therefore the Density of the liquid is 1.0 g/cm^3.

7. How many moles of Xe are in 50g of Xe?​


Answer:

0.38 moles of Xe in 50 grams of Xe.

Explanation:

To find the number of moles of a substance, you can use the formula:

number of moles = mass (in grams) / molar mass (in g/mol)

The molar mass of Xe is 131.293 g/mol.

So, 50g of Xe is equal to:

number of moles = 50g / 131.293 g/mol = 0.38 moles.

Therefore, there are 0.38 moles of Xe in 50 grams of Xe.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 6) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 46.068 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of C₂H₅OH.

[tex]\text{moles of C₂H₅OH = 50 g C₂H₅OH} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₂H₅OH}}{\text{46.068 g C₂H₅OH}}[/tex]
