13. what is the meaning of the poem "as he pleases" by Rina Garcia Chua?​


As He Pleases

Rina Garcia Chua's poem "As He Pleases" explores the subject of love and the nature of relationships. The poem is written in free verse and has a nuanced, enigmatic meaning that can be interpreted in various ways.

Introduction

The speaker of the poem introduces a man who walks away from her at his whim. This implies that the speaker is reliant on the man and that he is in charge of the relationship. By calling the man's acts cruel, the speaker may be offended by them. The poem's main issue is the dynamics of power in interpersonal interactions.

InterpretationThe speaker implies that the man does have affection for her by saying "Turns to look at me / and I see love."

The poem's ambiguous conclusion only serves to further the notion that the speaker's relationship with the man is real.

"As He Pleases" covers the subject of love and the dynamics of power in relationships.

The poem highlights that love can be illogical and self-destructive.

Overall, the poem implies that emotional distance can be a source of power in a relationship and that the emotionally distant one may be in charge. The poem also examines how it might be challenging to distinguish the difference between genuine love and manipulative love.
