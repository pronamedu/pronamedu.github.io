• Problem:

James is twice as old as Peter, and Peter is four times as old as John. Express each of their ages in terms of x.

• Explanation:

Let the age of John be the independent variable, x.

[tex]\implies \tt John-x \: years \: old[/tex]

While for Peter, we need to multiply the age of John, x, by 4.

[tex]\implies \tt Peter-4x \: years \: old[/tex]

Then, for the age of James, it's two times the age of Peter.

[tex]\implies \tt James-2(4x) \: or \: 8x \: years \: old[/tex]

• Answer:

The ages of John, Peter and James are x, 4x and 8x years old, respectively.
