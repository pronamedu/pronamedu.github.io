8. Describe the badminton court,the shuttlecock, and the racket.


Answer:

Badminton Court;

The badminton court is 13.4m long and 6.1m wide. For singles the court is marked 5.18m wide. The lines marking out the court are easily distinguishable and coloured white or yellow. ... The diagonal full length of the full court is 14.366m.

Shuttlecock;

A shuttlecock (also called a bird or birdie) is a high-drag projectile used in the sport of badminton. It has an open conical shape formed by feathers (or a synthetic alternative) embedded into a rounded cork (or rubber) base. The shuttlecock's shape makes it extremely aerodynamically stable.

Racket;

A racket or racquet is a sports implement consisting of a handled frame with an open hoop across which a network of strings or catgut is stretched tightly. It is used for striking a ball or shuttlecock in games such as squash, tennis, racquetball, rackets, badminton and padel.

9. How many types of shuttlecock the sports badminton has?


There are two types of shuttlecock in badminton

Shuttlecock is one of the mandatory equipment in playing badminton. Its quality largely depends on its material, and different quality of the material will cost differently. In general, the two types of badminton shuttlecock are:

Shuttlecock made by feather. As the name suggests, feather shuttlecock is made from the feathers of geese or ducks. This type of shuttlecock is known to its low durability, hence it breaks often. This type is suitable for experts who often makes certain shots in badminton accordingly, so the shuttlecock rarely breaks. However, beginners are advised to avoid this type.Shuttlecock made by plastic or nylon. On the contrary to shuttlecock made by feather, plastic shuttlecock has a better durability and are widely used by newbie players in badminton, as even though beginners tend to play badminton roughly, the shuttlecock will not break easily (hence saving more money).

In general, it is not difficult to differentiate between those two type of shuttlecock. Nylon is more stiff in nature, while feather shuttlecock is more flexible. All in all, it is a given that each type of shuttlecock explained above has its own strength and weakness, so be sure to pick one that suits your needs.

Badminton, court or lawn game played with lightweight rackets and a shuttlecock.
