Answer:

Fluvial systems are dominated by rivers and streams. Stream erosion may be the most important geomporphic agent. 18. How can you help your community if you identify areas susceptible to fluvial/coastal erosion?​


Many coastal areas with an elevation of around 10 feet above sea level are prone to COASTAL EROSION, SUBMERSION and SALTWATER INTRUSION. When people settle around these places, their activities alter the natural environment. It is even aggravated when industries begin to locate near the ocean for the convenience of dumping their waste into the sea. Factories will need workers, so many people from the countryside move close to these factories causing an increase in population, this is why we see squatters situated around huge concrete factory walls. Soon natural coastal features such as saltwater marshes, estuaries, mangrove forests that slows down oceans waves are destroyed, which allows the water to gradually find its way inland into the streets and houses of people. People’s nonchalant view towards proper trash disposal is not helping at all.

The best example of this is the CITY OF MALABON which has all the changes and conditions mentioned above. The old-timers said that flooding was never a problem when they were younger, but now it is an annual event.

25. what is the extent of terrestrial, fluvial and aerial domain of the philippines? ​


Answer:

The national territory comprises the Philippine archipelago, with all the islands and waters embraced therein, and all other territories over which the Philippines has sovereignty or jurisdiction, consisting of its terrestrial, fluvial and aerial domains, including its territorial sea, the seabed, the subsoil, the insular shelves, and other submarine areas. The waters around, between, and connecting the islands of the archipelago, regardless of their breadth and dimensions, form part of the internal waters of the Philippines.

27. can you explain the three territorial domain, terrestrial domain,fluvial domain, and aerial domain.​


Answer:

The terrestrial domain refers to the land mass, which may be integrate or dismembered, or partly bound by water or consist of one whole island. It may also be composed of several islands, like the Philippines. It also includes all the resources attached to the land. AERIAL DOMAIN.

Fluvial Domain Refers to the water, this could be seas, rivers, oceans, lakes, canals, ports and harbor. *Internal or National Water.

aerial domain It includes the air directly above the state's terrestrial and fluvial domains, all the way up to where the outer space begins.
