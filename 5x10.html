8. What is the frequency of light waves with wave length of 5x10^-7?


Answer:

The frequency of the light wave is 6 x 10¹⁴ Hz

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to use the concept od wavelength to solve for the frequency of a light wave. To do this, we will be using the formula

λ = v/f

where

λ     is the wavelength, the unit is in meter (m)

v     is the speed of light, 3x10⁸ m/s

f      is the frequency, the unit is in Herts (Hz)

Given information

λ = 5x10⁻⁷ m (Let us assume that the given unit of wavelength is in meter)

f = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve the frequency using the given formula

λ = v/f

Then, substitute the given data

5x10⁻⁷ m = 3x10⁸ m/s / f

Cross-multiply

f = [tex]\frac{3x10^8m/s}{5x10^{-7}m}[/tex]

f = 6 x 10¹⁴ Hz

Therefore, the frequency of the light wave is 6 x 10¹⁴ Hz
