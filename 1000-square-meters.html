26. If a net force of 50 Newtons was applied to an object with a mass of 5 kg, what will the object's acceleration be? a. 10 meter per second squarecb. 100 meter per second squaredc. 1 meter per second squaredd. 1000 meter per second squared​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The acceleration of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{a = \frac{F}{m}}[/tex]

where:

a = acceleration

F = force

m = mass

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the acceleration

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{50 N}}{\text{5 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{10 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is a. 10 meter per second squared.
