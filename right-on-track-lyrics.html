26. 4. Do you think you are on the right track about your career goal? Why?​


Yes. You are on the right track as long as you are determined to pursue your desired career or goal. Being serious about planning our future paths can help us determine our long-term success.

The goal-setting process is consistent and can be summarized as follows:

Decide what you want to accomplish.Create a strategy for achieving it, including measurable goals and time frames.Put your strategy into action.

When developing goals, use SMART goal setting to ensure that you have created goals with an actionable plan for results.

Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, and TimeBound Career Goals

Are your career objectives SMART? Simply stating "I will start a new career" or "I want a promotion" is insufficient. Career goals are frequently established during the career planning process. You will complete self-assessments, research careers, and a skills gap analysis.

All of this information will help you write SMART career goals.

For example, if you want to work in health care, law enforcement, accounting, information technology, business, or another field, you should start by researching specific careers in that field. Once you've narrowed down your options, you can investigate the realities of those positions: What is needed? What is the job market like? What is the average yearly salary? You can start determining the next steps you'll need to take and set measurable goals within a time frame.

Here are a few examples of short-term goals that may assist you in achieving a long-term career goal:

Become a member of a professional organization.To discuss your career options, contact Career Services.Read journal articles about your industry to stay up to date on the latest trends and topics.Attend workshops and classes relevant to your field.Investigate companies and read job descriptions.Organize informational interviews.Look for ways to develop your skills, such as through volunteering.Investigate online networking and make new contacts.If you are currently employed, talk to your boss about professional development opportunities.
