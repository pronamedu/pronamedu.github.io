4. 3. Angelita has 7 big oranges and she decided to give them equally to her 14 friends. How much orange did each friend ate?​


PROBLEM SOLVING

[tex] \: [/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{P} {\large \mathrm {ROBLEM : }}}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm\bold \red{\#3.}[/tex] Angelita has 7 big oranges and she decided to give them equally to her 14 friends. How much orange did each friend ate?

[tex]{\tt{ANSWER:}}[/tex][tex]\red{\boxed{\boxed{\tt{\purple{\; \frac {1}{2} \;}}}}}[/tex]

[tex]======================[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{G} {\large \mathrm {IVENS : }}}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\begin{array}{c} \sf\red{Number \; of \; oranges =} 7 \\ \sf\red{Number \; of \; her \; friends =} 14 \end{array}}[/tex]

[tex]======================[/tex]

[tex]{\underline{\huge \mathbb{S} {\large \mathrm {OLUTION : }}}}[/tex]

[tex]{\Longrightarrow}[/tex] Since we're finding how many oranges does her 14 friends ate, we will divide the number of oranges to the number of her friends.

Equation:

Let N will be the representer of the unknown value. Thus;

[tex] \tt N \: = \: \frac{number \: of \: oranges}{number \: of \: her \: friends } [/tex]

[tex]\tt N \: = \: \frac{7}{14} [/tex]

[tex]\tt N \: = \: \red{\underline{\green{ 0.5 }}}[/tex]

[tex]{\Longrightarrow}[/tex] Since our answer is in decimal form, we will convert it to fraction form by taking the decimal number as a numerator and its place value as a denominator. Hence, the answer will be [tex] \sf \frac {5}{10}[/tex], but we can reduce it to its lowest term by finding the GCF of its numerator and denominator, and after that divide the Numerator and denominator by their GCF. Thus;

[tex] \tt \large\frac{5}{10} = \frac{5 \: \div \: \blue 5}{10 \: \div \: \blue 5 } = \small\purple{\boxed{\boxed{ \tt{ \blue{ \frac{1}{2} }}}}}[/tex]

[tex]{\Longrightarrow}[/tex] Thus, her friends ate [tex] \sf \frac {1}{2}[/tex] orange each.
