Horsepower

The term "horsepower" came about in the 1700's, when inventor James Watt was trying to explain the power of steam engines. He spent hours watching horses and calculating how much power they produced in a minute. Watt calculated that an average pony produced 22,000 foot-pounds of effort every minute.

Watt

The "watt" is named after James Watt who improved the Newcomen engine with his own steam engine in 1776. Watt's invention was fundamental for the Industrial Revolution.


Farad

The term "farad" was originally coined by Latimer Clark and Charles Bright in 1861, in honor of Michael Faraday, for a unit of charge amount, but by 1873, the farad had evolved into a unit of capacitance.

11. Cauculate the power output in watts and horsepower of an 80 kg that climbs a flight of stairs 3.8 m high in 4.0s


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for F

F = weight = mg

F = (80 kg)(9.8 m/s²)

F = 784 N

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (784 N)(3.8 m)

W = 2,979.2 J

Solving for P in watts

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{2,979.2 J}}{\text{4.0 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{744.8 W}}[/tex]

Solving for P in horsepower

[tex]P = \text{744.8 W} \times \dfrac{\text{1 hp}}{\text{746 W}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{0.9984 hp}}[/tex]

27. A 75K jogger runs up a long flight of stairs in 8.5 seconds. The vertical height of the stairs is 15 meters. What is the power output of the person in watts and horsepower? Show your solution. Someone, please help.


Answer:

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The joggers power output approximately 1298.36 Watts and in Horsepower is 1.74 HP

Explanation:

To find the jogger's power output, we have to use the formula below:

[tex]\boxed{\bold{P = \frac{Fd}{t}}}[/tex]

Let,

[tex]P[/tex] be the Jogger's power output [tex]F[/tex] be the mass of the Jogger.

Substitute the following mass, time taken, and displacement to the formula.

[tex]\rm{P = \frac{(75)(9.81)(15)}{8.5}} \\\\ \rm{P=\frac{11036.25}{8.5}} \\\\ \rm{P \approx 1298.38 \ Watts}[/tex]

Now, we convert the Watts to Horsepower. To convert it, we simply divide the given Watts by 745.69.

[tex]\rm{Horsepower = Watts \times \left(\frac{1}{ 745.69}\right) = 1298.38 \times \left(\frac{1}{745.69}\right) \approx 1.74 \ HP }[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The joggers power output approximately 1298.36 Watts and in Horsepower is 1.74 HP
