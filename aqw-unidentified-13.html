8. A 170.00 g sample of an unidentified compound contains 29.84 g sodium, 67.49 g chromium, and 72.67 g oxygen. Find the empirical formula.


Answer:

Na₂Cr₂O₇ or Sodium dichromate

Explanation:

Molar masses:

Na=23.1 g/mol

Cr=52

O=16

Solving for number of moles of each element in the compound:

Mol Na=29.84 g/23.1 g/mol=1.2918 mol

Mol Cr=67.29/52=1.2940 mol

Mol O=72.67/16=4.5419 mol

Now, we divide each element by the lowest number of moles which is 1.2918

Number of moles of Na=1.2918/1.2918=1

Number of moles of Cr=1.2940/1.2918=1

Number of moles of O=4.5419/1.2918=3.5

To make everything a whole number, we can multiply each mole by 2.

Na=1x2=2

Cr=1x2=2

O=3.5x2=7

Hence, the empirical formula is Na₂Cr₂O₇. This is a sodium dichromate compound.
