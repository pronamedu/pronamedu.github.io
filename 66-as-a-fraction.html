66 2/3 Percent as a Fraction

A fraction is a numerical value that can be defined as a part of a whole. Every fraction consists of a numerator and a denominator.

Answer: 66 ⅔% can be expressed as 2/3 in the form of a fraction.

Let us see how to convert a percentage to a fraction in the following section.

Explanation:

66 2/3 can be converted to an improper fraction and written as 200/3.

Now, percentage means out of 100.

66 2/3 % means 66 2/3 divided by 100.

Therefore,

66 2/3% = 66 2/3 ÷ 100

= 200/3 × 1/100

= 2/3

Hence, 66 ⅔% can be expressed as 2/3 in the form of a fraction.

29. Which fraction is smallest 15/75 ,9/72 ,11/66 ,13/39


To find which fraction is the smallest, simplify it first to lowest terms.

Divide both numerator and denominator by its common factor.


[tex] \frac{15}{75} \div \frac{15}{15} = \frac{1}{5} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{9}{72} \div \frac{9}{9} = \frac{1}{8} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{11}{66} \div \frac{11}{11} = \frac{1}{6} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{13}{39} \div \frac{13}{13} = \frac{1}{3} [/tex]


First we find the L.C.M. of the denominators of the fractions to make the denominators same.

 One way to find the least common multiple of the denominators is to first list the prime factors of each number.

5 = 1 × 5

8 = 2 × 2 × 2

6 = 2 × 3

3 = 1 × 3

Then multiply each factor the greatest number of times it occurs in either number. If the same factor occurs more than once in all numbers, you multiply the factor the greatest number of times it occurs.

LCM = 5 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 = 120

Now to make the fraction as like fractions divide the L.C.M. by the denominator of fractions, then multiply both the numerator and denominator of fraction with the number get after dividing L.C.M.

[tex] \frac{1}{5} [/tex]
[tex]120 \div 5 = 24[/tex]
[tex] \frac{1}{5} \times \frac{24}{24} = \frac{24}{120} [/tex]
[tex] \frac{1}{8} [/tex]
[tex]120 \div 8 = 15[/tex]
[tex] \frac{1}{8} \times \frac{15}{15} = \frac{15}{120} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{1}{6} [/tex]
[tex]120 \div 6 = 20[/tex]
[tex] \frac{1}{6} \times \frac{20}{20} = \frac{20}{120} [/tex]
[tex] \frac{1}{3} [/tex]
[tex]120 \div 3 = 40[/tex]
[tex] \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{40}{40} = \frac{40}{120} [/tex]

Now we compare the fractions 24/120, 15/120, 20/120 and 40/120.

Comparing numerators, we find that 15 < 20 < 24 < 40.

Therefore, the smallest fraction is 15/120.
