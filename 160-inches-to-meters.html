5. A trapezoid has an area of 160 square meters. One base is 5 meters long. The height measures 20 meters. What is the length of the other base?


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

The area of a trapezoid is equal to the average of the lengths of the bases multiplied by the height. Therefore, we can write the equation A = (b1 + b2) / 2 * h to find the area of a trapezoid, where A is the area, b1 and b2 are the lengths of the bases, and h is the height.

In the given problem, we are told that the area of the trapezoid is 160 square meters, the height is 20 meters, and one of the bases is 5 meters long. We can substitute these values into the equation above to get the following:

160 = (b1 + 5) / 2 * 20

We can use the properties of equality to isolate the variable b1 on one side of the equation, like this:

160 = (b1 + 5) / 2 * 20

160 = 20 * b1 / 2 + 100

160 = 10 * b1 + 100

60 = 10 * b1

6 = b1

Therefore, the length of the other base is 6 meters.
