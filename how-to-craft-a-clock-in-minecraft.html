4. an electric clock uses 3 watts of electricity. How many kilowatt-hours are used to run a clock for 1 week?​


Answer:

0.504kWh

Explanation:

Divide the number of watts by 1000 to convert the number from watts to kilowatts. 3W divided by 1000 results in 0.003kW. Multiply the number of kilowatts by the number of hours the device will be used. If the device will be used for 1 week (convert it into hours) so it'll be 168 hours in total, multiply 0.003kW by 168 hours to produce 0.504 kilowat-hours.

Or just simply multiply your watts (3watts) to the hours (1 week=168 hours), 3 watts × 168 hours = 504, then you need to divide it by 1000, so 504 divided by 1000, results to 0.504kWh.

21. a clock was reading the time accurately on Tuesday at noon. on Thursday the clock was running late by 612 seconds. on average, how many seconds did the clock skip every 30 minutes​


Step-by-step explanation:

If the clock was running late by 612 seconds on Thursday and it was reading the time accurately on Tuesday at noon, then it was 612 seconds late on Thursday compared to Tuesday at noon. If we assume that the clock was reading the time accurately on Tuesday at noon and Thursday at noon, then the clock was 612 seconds late on Thursday at noon compared to Tuesday at noon.

Since there are 60 minutes in an hour and the clock was 612 seconds late every 30 minutes, the clock was skipping 612/30 = <<612/30=20.4>>20.4 seconds per 30 minutes.

In other words, the clock was skipping 20.4 seconds every 30 minutes.
