6. Working together, Tom and Jerry painted a fence in 8 hours. Last year, Tom painted the fence by himself. The year before, Jerry painted it by himself, but took 12 hours less than Tom took. Howlong did tom and jerry take, when each was painting​


Answer:

Jerry takes 12 hours, and Tom takes 24 hours

Step-by-step explanation:

Jerry can do it in "j" hours, so he does 1/j per hour. Tom does it in "t" hours, so he does 1/t per hour. Then, working together, they can do 1/j + 1/t = 1/8 per hour, since they took 8 hours. I also know that Tom takes 12 hours longer than Jerry, so t = j + 12. Then I get 1/j + 1/(j + 12) =  1/8. That is:

hours to complete job:

  Jerry: j

  Tom: j + 12

  together: 8

completed per hour:

  Jerry: 1/j

  Tom: 1/(j + 12)

  together: 1/8

adding their labor:

1/j + 1/(j + 12) = 1/8

To solve for "j", I'll multiply through by 8j(j + 12) to get rid of all of the denominators:

8(j + 12) + 8(j) = (j) (j + 12) 

8j + 96 + 8j = j2 + 12j

16j + 96 = j2 + 12j

0 = j2 – 4j – 96 

(j – 12)(j + 8) = 0

Then j = 12 and j = –8. Of course, for the purposes of our problem, j must be positive, so we'll ignore the "j = –8" solution; it's "extraneous".

Then, since j = 12, then Jerry takes twelve hours. Tom takes twelve hours longer than Jerry.

8. tom's age is half the age of jerry. the age of tom 6 years ago is 1/3 jerry's present age. how old are they now.


Answer. Jerry's age now is 36. Tom's age now is 18.

Solution. Let a be the age of Jerry, then [tex] \frac{a}{2} [/tex] is Tom's age.

But they have different ages six years ago. To represent Jerry's age six years ago, we just have to subtract Jerry's age by 6 and Tom's age six years ago is given, resulting to


a - 6 Jerry's age six year ago

[tex] \frac{a}{3} [/tex] Tom's age six years ago (1)


But also without using the statement, representing Tom's age six years ago is

[tex] \frac{a}{2} [/tex] - 6 Tom's age six years ago (2)


Then we have to equate Tom's age six years ago (1) & (2)


[tex] \frac{a}{3} [/tex] = [tex] \frac{a}{2} [/tex] - 6


Multiply both sides by 6, since it is the least common multiple of the denominator.


2a = 3a - 36


Transpose 3a to the left


2a - 3a = -36

- a = -36

a = 36


Jerry's age now is 36.

Jerry's age six years ago is 30.

Tom's age now is half of 36 which is 18.

Tom's age six years ago is one-third of 36 which is 12.
