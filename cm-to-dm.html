20. 3. 2. Find the surface area of the following: O 11 dm r = 4.6 cm 1 = 12 cm r = 12 cm h = 16 dm r = 5 dm 20 cm 12 cm 6 cm​


Let's calculate the surface area of each object:

Sphere with radius 4.6 cm:
The formula for the surface area of a sphere is:

A = 4πr^2

where r is the radius.

Substituting the given value, we get:

A = 4π(4.6 cm)^2 ≈ 265.98 cm^2

Therefore, the surface area of the sphere is approximately 265.98 cm^2.

Cylinder with radius 12 cm and height 16 dm (1 dm = 10 cm):
The formula for the surface area of a cylinder is:

A = 2πr^2 + 2πrh

where r is the radius and h is the height.

Substituting the given values, we get:

A = 2π(12 cm)^2 + 2π(12 cm)(16 dm × 10 cm/dm)
= 2π(144 cm^2) + 2π(1920 cm^2)
≈ 12068.6 cm^2

Therefore, the surface area of the cylinder is approximately 12068.6 cm^2.

Rectangular prism with dimensions 20 cm, 12 cm, and 6 cm:
The formula for the surface area of a rectangular prism is:

A = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

where l, w, and h are the length, width, and height, respectively.

Substituting the given values, we get:

A = 2(20 cm × 12 cm) + 2(20 cm × 6 cm) + 2(12 cm × 6 cm)
= 480 cm^2 + 240 cm^2 + 144 cm^2
= 864 cm^2

Therefore, the surface area of the rectangular prism is 864 cm^2.

Overall, the surface areas of the three objects are approximately 265.98 cm^2, 12068.6 cm^2, and 864 cm^2, respectively.
