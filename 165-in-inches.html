19. A 4-stroke, 8 cylinder diesel engine with bore and stroke of 9 inch x 12 inch, respectively and speed of 1000 rpm has a brake mean effective pressure of 165 psi. determine the engine brake horse power


THE ENGINE BRAKE HORSEPOWER

The engine brake horsepower is approximately 170.92 BHP. To calculate the engine brake horsepower (BHP), we can use the following formula:

BHP = (BP x 2 x N) / 33000. Where BP is the brake power in ft-lb/s, N is the engine speed in RPM, and 33000 is a constant.

How to compute

First, let's calculate the brake power:

BP = (BMEP x L x A x N x NC) / 2Where BMEP is the brake mean effective pressure in psi, L is the stroke length in inches, A is the area of one cylinder in square inches (π/4 x bore^2), and NC is the number of cylinders.

Plugging in the given values:A = π/4 x 9^2 = 63.62 in^2NC = 8L = 12 inN = 1000 RPMBMEP = 165 psiBP = (165 x 12 x 63.62 x 1000 x 8) / 2 = 2,822,260 ft-lb/sNow we can calculate the engine brake horsepower:BHP = (BP x 2 x N) / 33000 = (2,822,260 x 2 x 1000) / 33000 = 170.92 BHP
