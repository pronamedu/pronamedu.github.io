18. calculate the number of equivalents in 220 grams of h3po4


Hello!

We have the following data:

m (solute mass) = 220 g

MM (molar mass of H3PO4)

H = 1 * 3 = 3

P = 1 * 31 = 31 u

O = 4 * 16 = 64 u

----------------------- --

MM (molar mass of H3PO4) = 3 + 31 + 64 = 98 g / mol

H3PO4 has the number of ionizable hydrogens H+ = 3

E (h3po4) =?

Ne (number of equivalents) =?

If, for the substance, we have:


[tex] E_{H_3PO_4} = \dfrac{molar\:mass}{number\;of\:H^+} [/tex]


[tex] E_{H_3PO_4} = \dfrac{98}{3} [/tex]


[tex] \boxed{E_{H_3PO_4} \approx 32,66\:g} [/tex]


Now, let's find the gram-equivalent, let's see:


[tex] N_e = \dfrac{m}{E_{H3PO_4}} [/tex]

19. Eunice bought 220 cups of dessert. 50 cups of which are salad.What percent of the cups of dessert are cups of salad?​


[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

PROBLEM:

Eunice bought 220 cups of dessert. 50 cups of which are salad. What percent of the cups of dessert are cups of salad?

[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

SOLUTION:

• The given problem can be solved by applying what you've learned from lesson on base, rate and percentage.

• The given quantities are 220 cups of dessert, in which 50 of them are salad. Thus, let 220 cups be our base and 50 as our percentage.

• Since base and percentage are known, you're tasked to find the rate. We'll use the formula: [tex]\rm Rate=Percentage÷Base.[/tex]

[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

[tex] \large\rm Rate=Percentage÷Base[/tex]

[tex] \large\rm R = 50 \div 220[/tex]

[tex] \large\rm R = 0.227[/tex]

• Thus, the rate is 0.23, when converted to percent, we'll have[tex] \rm 0.227 \times 100 = 22.7\%.[/tex]

[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

ANSWER:

Approximately, twenty-three percent of the cups of dessert are cups of salad.

28. a food label states that it has 220 calories and 12 grams fat. Compute for % fat calories​


Answer:

To calculate this, divide a food or drink's calories from fat by total calories (this information is on the product's food label) and then multiply by 100. For example, if a 300-calorie food has 60 calories from fat, divide 60 by 300 and then multiply by 100.

[tex]\purple{\underline{\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad}}[/tex]

[tex]\large{\underline{\sf{Problem:}}}[/tex]

A foot label states that it has 220 calories and 12 grams fat. Compute for % fat calories.

[tex]\large{\underline{\sf{Givens:}}}[/tex]

220 calories12 grams fat? fat calories

[tex]\large{\underline{\sf{Solution:}}}[/tex]

To calculate the fat calories, divide a food calories from grams fat then multiply to 100.

Formula:

[tex] \tt \blue{grams \: fat} \: \div \: \red{calories} \: \times \: \green{100}[/tex]

Solve:

(grams fat ÷ calories) × 100

Plugging the values

(12 ÷ 220) × 100

0.0545 × 100

5.45

Add the percentage sign

5.45%

[tex]\large{\underline{\sf{Final\: answer:}}}[/tex]

Thus, the fat calories of label food is 5.45%
