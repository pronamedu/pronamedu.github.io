9. What is inelastic collision.


A perfectly inelastic collision occurs when the maximum amount of kinetic energy of a system is lost. In a perfectly inelastic collision, i.e., a zero coefficient of restitution, the colliding particles stick together. In such a collision, kinetic energy is lost by bonding the two bodies together.


10. Inelasticity example


When there is a small change in demand when prices change a lot, the product is said to be inelastic. The most famous example of relatively inelastic demand is that for gasoline. As the price of gasoline increases, the quantity demanded doesn't decrease all that much. This is because there are very few good substitutes for gasoline and consumers are still willing to buy it even at relatively high prices.

14. Difference of elastic and inelastic


Elastic goods and services generally have plenty of substitutes. As an elastic service/good's price increases, the quantity demanded of that good can drop fast. Example of elastic goods and services include furniture, motor vehicles, instrument engineering products, professional services, and transportation services.

Inelastic goods are defined in economy as those for which the quantity demanded or supplied is unaffected when the price of that good (or service) changes. Some trafitional examples of these include gas, water, clothing,tobacco, food, and oil.

20. What Type Of Collision Resulted To The Formation Of Gravitational Waves Detected By LIGO?a. Elasticb. Inelasticc. Perfectly Inelasticd. Elastic Then Perfectly Inelastic ​


Answer:

B. Inelastic

Explanation:

The gravitational waves detected by LIGO were generated by the collision of two black holes, which is an example of an inelastic collision. In an inelastic collision, the objects involved stick together and lose some kinetic energy as they deform or break apart. In the case of the black holes, the collision was so violent that it created ripples in the fabric of spacetime, which were detected as gravitational waves by LIGO.
