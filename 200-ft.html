24. A cylindrical tank 5 ft. high is required to contain 200 gallons. What should be its diameter?


Diameter is 2.6 ft

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

h = 5 ft

V = 200 gal

1 ft^3 = 7.481 gal

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Diameter

Step 3: Solve for Diameter

Solution:

Convert gallons to cubic feet

[tex] = 200 \: gal \times \frac{1 {ft}^{3} }{7.481 \: gal} [/tex]

[tex] = 26.73 {ft}^{3} [/tex]

The formula for Volume of a cylinder is given by:

V = πr²h

where: V - volume

r - radius

h - height

Substituting the value of V and h in the formula,

[tex]26.73 {ft}^{3} = \pi {r}^{2} (5 \: ft)[/tex]

[tex] {r}^{2} = \frac{26.73 {ft}^{3}}{\pi(5 \: ft)} [/tex]

[tex]r = 1.3 \: ft[/tex]

diameter = 2 x radius

d = 2(1.3 ft)

d = 2.6 ft

29. 2. A 40 foot flagpole casts a shadow 25 ft. Find the length of the shadow casts by a nearby building 200 ft. tall kailangan ng agonsa guys ​


Asked: Find the length of the shadow casts by a nearby building.

Given:

The height of the flagpole = 40 feet

The length of the shadow cast by the flagpole = 25 feet

The height of the nearby building = 200 feet

Operation: We can use proportions to find the length of the shadow cast by the nearby building.

Numbers: Let x be the length of the shadow cast by the nearby building. We can set up the following proportion:

40/25 = 200/x

Solution:

To solve for x, we can cross-multiply and simplify the equation:

40x = 25(200)

40x = 5000

x = 125

Therefore, the length of the shadow cast by the nearby building is 125 feet.

Answer: The length of the shadow cast by the nearby building is 125 feet.
