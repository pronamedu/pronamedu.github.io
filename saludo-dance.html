22. the proper way of holding saludo with solo garrote​


Answer:

Saludo with Solo Garrote:

1) Feet together. 2) The right hand with the stick pointing upward over the left chest. 3) Left arm is at the side. 4) Body and head up straight and looking far.

Answer:

The collection you mentioned consists of several dance masterpieces created by Ramon Obusan, a renowned Filipino choreographer and folklorist. These dances showcase the diverse cultural traditions of the Philippines and other countries in the ASEAN region.

The first four series, "Unpublished Dances of the Philippines," Series I-IV, feature traditional dances from different regions of the Philippines that have not been previously published or performed. "Water, Fire and Life" and "Philippine Dances and Music-A Celebration of Life" are two more performances that showcase the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines through music and dance. "Saludo sa Sentenyal" is a dance piece that pays tribute to the 100th anniversary of the Philippine Revolution.

"Glimpses of ASEAN, Dances and Music of the ASEAN Member Countries" is a performance that highlights the cultural diversity of the Southeast Asian region by showcasing traditional dances and music from the different ASEAN member countries. Lastly, "Saplot (Ramon Obusan Folkloric Group): Philippines Costumes in Dance" is a performance that showcases the traditional clothing and costumes of the Philippines through dance.

Overall, Ramon Obusan's collection of dance masterpieces is a celebration of the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines and the ASEAN region as a whole, providing a glimpse into the traditions, music, and dances that have been passed down through generations.
