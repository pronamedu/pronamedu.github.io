18. A sports car moving at constant velocity travels 120 m in 5.0s. If it then brakes and comes to a stop in 4.0 s, what is the magnitude of its acceleration?


Answer:

The magnitude of the car's acceleration (deceleration in this case) is -6 m/s².

Explanation:

Uniformly Accelerated Motion

It is the state of the motion of an object when its acceleration is constant all throughout the traveled distance. In simple words, the acceleration of the object in this case is uniform.

Given:d = 120 mt₁ = 5 st₂ = 4sRequired:

acceleration (a)

Solution:

Solve for the velocity first. Velocity or speed is the distance per unit time or

v = d / tv = 120 m / 5 sv = 24 m/s

(We used the value of  t₁ for solving velocity because it is said in the problem that the car traveled 120 m in 5 seconds at constant velocity)

Solve for the acceleration. Acceleration is velocity per unit time or

a = v/ta = 24 m/s ÷ 4 sa = 6 m/s²a = -6 m/s²

We used t₂ for solving the acceleration because it is said that after hitting the breaks, the car stopped in 4 seconds. Also, since the car hit the break, it means that it will slow down, hence, it is deceleration and deceleration is the opposite of acceleration. So we need to put negative sign to the value we computed.

Therefore, the magnitude of the car's acceleration (deceleration in this case) is -6 m/s².
