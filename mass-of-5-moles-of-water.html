12. What is the mass of 5 moles of Fe2O3?


Given:

moles of Fe₂O₃ = 5 mol

Required:

mass of Fe₂O₃

Strategy:

The problem requires us to find the mass of 5 moles of Fe₂O₃. But how do we find it?

First, we will compute for the molar mass of Fe₂O₃ and then we will use dimensional analysis (factor-label method) to find the mass of Fe₂O₃.

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Fe₂O₃.

Based on the periodic table of elements, the atomic masses of Fe and O are 55.85 amu and 16.00 amu, respectively.

Note: The atomic mass is numerically equal to the molar mass.

The molar mass of Fe₂O₃ is

molar mass of Fe₂O₃ = (55.85 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass of Fe₂O₃ = 159.7 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Fe₂O₃.

Using dimensional analysis, the mass of Fe₂O₃ is

[tex]\text{mass of Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{5 mol Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} \times \dfrac{\text{159.7 g Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}{\text{1 mol Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{798.5 g}}[/tex]

Answer:

mass of Fe₂O₃ = 798.5 g

16. A solution is prepared by mixing 25.0g of water, H2O, and 25.0 g of ethanol, C2H5OH. Determine the mole fractions of each substance. Note:Molar mass of water is 18.015g/mole Molar mass of ethanol i 46.07 g/mole​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of ethanol and water

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{water}} = 25.0 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{18.015 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{1.39 mol} \\ & n_{\text{ethanol}} = 25.0 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{46.07 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{0.543 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the mole fractions of each substance.

• For water

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{water}} & = \frac{n_{\text{water}}}{n_{\text{water}} + n_{\text{ethanol}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{1.39 mol}}{\text{1.39 mol + 0.543 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.719} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For ethanol

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{ethanol}} & = \frac{n_{\text{ethanol}}}{n_{\text{water}} + n_{\text{ethanol}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.543 mol}}{\text{1.39 mol + 0.543 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.281} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fractions of water and ethanol are 0.719 and 0.281, respectively.

17. A solution of table sugar in water contains 5.25% by mass sucrose calculate the mole fraction of sucrose and water in 3.25% by mass socruse?​


Strategy:

The mole fraction is a dimensionless quantity that expresses the ratio of the number of moles of one component to the number of moles of all components present. In general, the mole fraction of component [tex]i[/tex] in a mixture or solution is given by

[tex]X_{i} = \frac{n_{i}}{n_{\text{T}}}[/tex]

where [tex]n_{i}[/tex] and [tex]n_{\text{T}}[/tex] are the number of moles of component [tex]i[/tex] and the total number of moles present, respectively.

Pointers to Remember:

Mole fraction is dimensionless, meaning, it has no unit of measurement.Mole fraction is always less than one.The sum of mole fractions of all components is always equal to one.If the given quantity is mass, convert it to moles using the molar mass of the component of interest.If the given quantity is percent by mass, always assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Then, determine the mass of each component.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components present.

Since the given is in terms of percent by mass, we assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Therefore, 5.25% aqueous sugar solution contains 5.25 g of sugar (solute) in 94.75 g of water (solvent).

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For sugar

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{sugar} =[/tex](12.01 g/mol × 12) + (1.008 g/mol × 22) + (16.00 g/mol × 11)

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{sugar} = \text{342.296 g/mol}[/tex]

For water

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{water} =[/tex](1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{water} = \text{18.016 g/mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For sugar

[tex]n_{sugar} = \frac{\text{mass}_{sugar}}{\text{molar mass}_{sugar}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{sugar} = \frac{\text{5.25 g}}{\text{342.296 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{sugar} = \text{0.0153376mol}[/tex]

For water

[tex]n_{water} = \frac{\text{mass}_{water}}{\text{molar mass}_{water}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{water} = \frac{\text{94.75 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{water} = \text{5.259214 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of each component.

For sugar

[tex]X_{sugar} = \frac{n_{sugar}}{n_{sugar} \: + \: n_{water}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{sugar} = \frac{\text{0.0153376 mol}}{\text{0.0153376 mol + 5.259214 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{sugar} = 0.00291}[/tex]

For water

[tex]X_{water} = \frac{n_{water}}{n_{sugar} \: + \: n_{water}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{water} = \frac{\text{5.259214 mol}}{\text{0.0153376 mol + 5.259214 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{water} = 0.9971}[/tex]
