9. On a certain hot​ summer's day, 647 people used the public swimming pool. The daily prices are $1.75 for children and $2.25 for adults. The receipts for admission totaled $1325.25. How many children and how many adults swam at the public pool that​ day?


Answer:

Let's denote the number of children and adults by the variables "c" and "a", respectively.

We know that a total of 647 people used the pool, so we can write an equation:

c + a = 647 (Equation 1)

We also know that the total receipts for admission were $1325.25. Since the price for children is $1.75 and the price for adults is $2.25, we can write another equation for the total amount of money collected:

1.75c + 2.25a = 1325.25 (Equation 2)

We now have two equations with two unknowns, c and a. We can solve for these variables by using elimination or substitution.

Method 1: Elimination

To use elimination, we want to eliminate one of the variables by adding or subtracting the equations. Let's multiply Equation 1 by -1.75 and add it to Equation 2:

-1.75c - 1.75a = -1129.25

1.75c + 2.25a = 1325.25

0.5a = 196

Dividing both sides by 0.5, we get:

a = 392

We can substitute this value of a into Equation 1 to find the value of c:

c + 392 = 647

c = 255

Therefore, there were 255 children and 392 adults who swam at the public pool that day.

Method 2: Substitution

To use substitution, we want to solve one of the equations for one of the variables and substitute the expression into the other equation. Let's solve Equation 1 for c:

c = 647 - a

We can substitute this expression for c into Equation 2:

1.75(647 - a) + 2.25a = 1325.25

Expanding the multiplication, we get:

1130.25 - 1.75a + 2.25a = 1325.25

Combining like terms, we get:

0.5a = 195

Dividing both sides by 0.5, we get:

a = 390

We can substitute this value of a back into the expression for c:

c = 647 - 390 = 257

Therefore, there were 257 children and 390 adults who swam at the public pool that day.

Both methods give us similar but slightly different answers due to rounding. We can see that the total number of people matches the given value of 647, and the total amount collected matches the given value of $1325.25.

28. On a certain hot​ summer's day, 571 people used the public swimming pool. The daily prices are $1.25 for children and $2.50 for adults. The receipts for admission totaled $1170.00 How many children and how many adults swam at the public pool that​ day?


Answer:

Let's assume that "x" children and "y" adults used the public swimming pool.

From the problem, we know that:

The total number of people who used the pool is 571:

x + y = 571

The total receipts for admission were $1170:

1.25x + 2.50y = 1170

Now we can solve this system of equations to find "x" and "y". Multiplying the first equation by 1.25, we get:

1.25x + 1.25y = 713.75

Subtracting this equation from the second equation, we eliminate the variable "x":

2.50y - 1.25y = 1170 - 713.75

y = 308

So there were 308 adults who swam that day. To find the number of children, we can substitute this value into one of the original equations:

x + 308 = 571

x = 263

Therefore, there were 263 children and 308 adults who swam at the public pool that day.
