12. steam enters the turbine causing the blade of the turbine to spin


Answer:

As the name implies, a steam turbine is powered by steam. As hot, gaseous steam flows past the turbine' spinning blades, steam expands and cools, giving off most of the energy it contains. This steam spins the blades continuously. The blades thus convert most of the steam's potential energy into kinetic energy.

Rotational energy from the turning turbine shaft is used directly to spin magnets inside a large coil and create electrical current. Mechanical braking is almost like an emergency brake for wind turbines. When the blades should no longer be spinning, the internal components will grind together to manually stop them instead of allowing the rotation to slowly die out as it does during pitch braking.


28. Water flows from a reservoir at the rate of 3000kg/min, to a turbine 120 m below. If the efficiency of the turbine is 80 percent, compute the horsepower output of the turbine. Neglect friction in the pipe and the small KE of the water leaving the turbine?


The horsepower output of the turbine : 63.056 Hp

Further explanation

Power is the work done every second. Can be formulated:

[tex]\rm P=\dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

P = watt = J / s

Work itself is a change in energy that occurs in objects, (changes in kinetic energy or potential energy)

[tex]\rm W=\Delta KE=\Delta PE\\\\\Delta PE=mg(h_2-h_1)\\\\\Delta KE=\dfrac{1}{2}m(v_2^2-v_1^2)[/tex]

Water flows from a reservoir at a rate of 3000 kg / min, to a turbine of 120 m below. the efficiency of the turbine is 80 percent

Then:

m = mass flow rate = 3000 kg/min = 50 kg/s

h = 120 m

Potential energy from water will be converted as input energy from the turbine. So the input power of the turbine:

[tex]\rm P_{in}=\dfrac{W}{t}\\\\P_{in}=\dfrac{\Delta PE}{t}\\\\P_{in}=\dfrac{m.g.(h_2-h_1)}{t}\Rightarrow h_1=0 ,t=every~second=s\\\\P_{in}=50\dfrac{kg}{s}.9.8~m/s^2.(120~m-0~m)\\\\P_{in}=58800~watt[/tex]

Because friction in the pipe and the small KE of the water leaving the turbine is ignored then the entire input power is transferred to the turbine output and considers its efficiency

[tex]\rm P_{out}=P_{in}\times efficiency\\\\P_{out}=58800\times 0.8\\\\P_{out}= \boxed{\bold{47040~watt}}[/tex]

Because 1 watt = 1/746 Hp

then

[tex]\rm P_{out}=\dfrac{47040}{746}=\boxed{\bold{63,056~Hp}}[/tex]
