23. table sugar melts at 185-degree celsius. what is the difference between the point of water and the melting point of the sugar in degree fahrenheit​


Temperature Differences

- Temperature difference, opposing the absolute temperature (Absolute temperature scales are Kelvin and Rankine. Absolute zero is the temperature at which a system is in the state of lowest possible (minimum) energy), is a measure of the relative amounts of internal energy within two bodies (e.g. table sugar and water). In simple terms, temperature is the measure of the how hot or how cold is an object.

Question : Table sugar melts at 185-degree Celsius. What is the difference between the boiling point of water and the melting point of the sugar in degree fahrenheit​? The Answer is 185 degrees Fahrenheit.

STEP-BY-STEP EXPLANATION

Given: Melting point temperature of table sugar is equal to 185 degrees Celsius.

Required: Temperature difference of two bodies ( Melting point of table sugar and the boiling point of water) in degrees Fahrenheit.

Solution: Assume the operating pressure is atmospheric (1 atmosphere); Therefore, the boiling point of water at Celsius scale is 100 degrees Celsius.

To get the temperature difference of the two bodies (table sugar and water), just subtract the melting point of the table sugar which is 185 degrees Celsius by the boiling point of water at one atmosphere, which is 100 degrees Celsius. Expressing mathematically, that would be:

185°C - 100°C = 85 degrees Celsius, but the required unit is in degrees Fahrenheit. So, convert 85 degrees Celsius to its equivalent value in fahrenheit.

Take note that 0 degree Celsius is 32 degree Fahrenheit, but there is correcting factor of 9/5 in converting in Celsius to Fahrenheit.

The formula is: ( T, °C x 9/5) + 32 = °F

In words, convert Celsius scale to Fahrenheit scale, multiply the correcting factor 9/5 to the temperature given in Celsius scale, then add 32.

Therefore, (85 x 9/5) + 32 = 185-degree FAHRENHEIT ⇒ ANSWER!
