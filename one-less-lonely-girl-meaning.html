9. Geometry of polar compound with one lone pair around the central atom.


Answer:

Geometry depends on the number of bonding pairs or coordination number.

Explanation:

Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion Theory (VSEPR)

The valence shell electron pair repulsion (VSEPR) theory is a model used to predict the shape or molecular geometry of molecules based on its bonding pairs and nonbonding pairs. It assumes that electron pairs arranged themselves to minimize repulsion effects from one another assuming that the nonbonding electron pair has the highest repulsion. Thus, electron pairs must be as much as possible far from one another.

We can't exactly predict the geometry of a polar compound with one lone pair around the central atom given that we don't know the exact molecule or the number of bonding pair or its coordination number. However, the list below identified the shape of a molecule with 2 to 5 bonding pairs and one lone pair.
