6. 1. Explain, using kinetic molecular theory, why a gas can be easily compressed but a liquid and a solidcannot.2. How do pressure, volume, and temperature relate to Kinetic Molecular Theory of gases?​


According to the kinetic molecular theory, gases are made up of particles (atoms or molecules) that are in constant motion and have a lot of empty space between them. When a gas is compressed, the volume of the container is reduced, which means that the particles are pushed closer together, but they still have a lot of empty space to move around in. This makes it easy to compress a gas.

On the other hand, liquids and solids are made up of particles that are tightly packed together and have less empty space between them. When you try to compress a liquid or a solid, the particles are already in close proximity to each other, so there is not much room for them to move closer together. This makes it difficult to compress a liquid or a solid.

According to the Kinetic Molecular Theory, the pressure of a gas is caused by the constant collisions of the gas particles with the walls of the container. As the volume of the container decreases, the gas particles will collide more frequently with the walls, resulting in an increase in pressure.

Similarly, as the temperature of the gas increases, the gas particles will move faster and collide more frequently with the walls, resulting in an increase in pressure. As the temperature of the gas decreases, the gas particles will move slower and collide less frequently with the walls, resulting in a decrease in pressure.

Finally, the volume of a gas is related to the Kinetic Molecular Theory through the idea that gas particles are in constant motion and have a lot of empty space between them. As the volume of the container increases, the gas particles will have more empty space to move around in, resulting in a decrease in pressure. As the volume of the container decreases, the gas particles will have less empty space to move around in, resulting in an increase in pressure.

1. The particle model of matter states that matter is made up of tiny particles (atoms and molecules) that are constantly in motion. In a solid, these particles are packed closely together and are not able to move around very much. This means that solids have a fixed shape and do not change shape easily. In contrast, the particles in a gas are much further apart and are able to move around freely. This means that gases will fill the shape of the container they are in, because the particles can move to fill the entire space.

2. In the particle model of matter, the particles in a gas are much further apart than the particles in a liquid. This means that the particles in a gas have more space to move around and can be compressed more easily. In contrast, the particles in a liquid are much closer together and do not have as much space to move around. This means that liquids are much more difficult to compress.

3. Powders such as cake flour are solids, not liquids. Although they can be poured, powders are not fluids, they are made of solid particles that are small and dry, they can be poured because they are dry and fine, but they still keep their solid properties, they can be shaped, they can be cut and they have a defined volume.
