6. When 50 mL of water is combined with 40 mL of ethanol, what is the total volume?


Answer: Approximately 86 ml

Reason: If you mix 50 ml of water with 40 ml of ethanol, the total volume actual equals less than 90 ml. The reason the mixture does not add up to 90 ml is because the molecules that make up ethanol are smaller than the molecules that make up water. So, the ethanol molecules cram themselves between the water molecules. As a result, the volume is less than expected. The volume adds up to approximately 86 ml. A great way to think of how this works it to think of a container of sand poured into a container of rocks. The sand fills in the empty spaces between the rocks.

24. The density of water at 40°c is 0.992 g/ml. what is the volume of 2.50 g of water at this temperature?


The volume of 2.50 g of water : 2.52 ml

Further explanation  

Density is a quantity derived from the mass and volume  

Density is the ratio of mass per unit volume  

The unit of density can be expressed in g/cm³ or kg/m³  

Density formula:  

 [tex]\tt \rho=\dfrac{m}{V}[/tex]

ρ = density  

m = mass  

V = volume  

The density of water at 40°C is 0.992 g/ml

The volume of 2.50 g of water :

[tex]\tt \rho=0.992~g/ml\\\\m=2.5~g\\\\V=\dfrac{m}{\rho}\\\\V=\dfrac{2.5}{0.992}\\\\V=\boxed{\bold{2.52~ml}}[/tex]

25. Calculate the percentage concentration of ethyl alcohol in a solution of 10 mL ethylalcohol in 40 mL water​


Given:

volume of solute = 10 mL

volume of solvent = 40 mL

solute: ethyl alcohol

solvent: water

Required:

percentage concentration

Solution:

[tex]\text{percentage concentration} = \dfrac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} \times 100\%[/tex]

[tex]\text{percentage concentration} = \dfrac{\text{10 mL}}{\text{10 mL + 40 mL}} \times 100\%[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percentage concentration} = 20\%}[/tex]

Answer:

percentage concentration = 20%

Calculating Percent Volume/Volume (% v/v)

A percent v/v solution is calculated by the following formula using the milliliter as the base measure of volume (v):

% v/v = mL of solute/100 mL of solution.

Example:

X % = 5.0 mL HCl/100 mL of solution.

X/100 = 5.0/100.

100X = 500.

X = 5.0% % v/v.
