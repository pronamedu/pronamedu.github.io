Below are balances and information taken from the records of Bulls Company for the last quarter of the current year: Inventories: October 1 Raw Materials Work in process Finished goods Cost of goods sold Manufacturing overhead P134,000 354,000 594,600 10,800,000 4,200,000 debit 4,600,000credit Supplementary data: (1) During the period, purchases of raw materials totaled P1,093,400 while physical count of raw materials revealed that P250,000 were unused. (2) 39,800 direct labor hours were utilized distributed as follows: (a) 25,000 hours worked on regular time at P67 per hr. (b) 14,000 hours worked at the late shift (c) 800 hours work on overtime, all on regular shift. (c) Overhead is charged to production at 80% of direct labor costs. (d) Actual overhead incurred were P1,420,000. Overhead variance is closed to all accounts with overhead elements only at the end of the year (e) At the end of the year, records show that work in process increased by P80,000 while Finished Goods decreased by P150,000. Required: 1. Determine the total factory payroll for the period, refer to PD 442 for the late shift and overtime premium. 2. Determine the total factory costs.


CONTROLLING LABOR COST

Controlling labor costs entails keeping tabs on the expense of labor. It  does not entail controlling the size of the payroll or that each employee's pay should be maintained as low as possible.

The balances and data below were derived from the Bulls Company's records for the year's fourth quarter.

The steps below can be used to calculate the total manufacturing payroll for the time:

1. The cost of the normal time work is calculated as follows: 25,000 hours * P67/hour = P1,675,000

2. Estimate the cost of the late shift labor as follows: 14,000 hours times P67 per hour multiplied by 1.25 (late shift premium) is P1,045,000.

3. Calculate the cost of the overtime work as follows: P74,000 (800 hours multiplied by P67/hour) * 1.25 (overtime premium)

4. The total manufacturing payroll is calculated by adding the costs of regular shift, late shift, and overtime labor: P1,675,000 + P1,045,000 + P74,000 = P2,794,000.

To determine the total factory costs, we can follow these steps:

1. Determine the cost of raw materials used: P1,093,400 - P250,000 = P843,400

2. Add the cost of raw materials used to the cost of direct labor to find the total direct materials and labor: P843,400 + P2,794,000 = P3,637,400

3. Add the total direct materials and labor to the manufacturing overhead to find the total factory costs: P3,637,400 + P4,200,000 = P7,837,400
