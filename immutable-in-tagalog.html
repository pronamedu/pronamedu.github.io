27. Explain the difference between Mutable and Immutable storage


Answer:

A mutable object can be mutated or changed. An immutable object cannot. For example, while you can add or remove objects from an NSMutableArray, you cannot do either with an NSArray. Mutable objects can have elements changed, added to, or removed, which cannot be achieved with immutable objects.


28. explain immutability, indefectibility and perennial

Immutability - not capable of or susceptible to change.

30. justify "God's love is immutable"​


Answer:

too many Christians have a view of God’s love like a girl picking petals off a daisy, saying, “He loves me, He loves me not!” If God’s love did change with our conduct and circumstances, then God would be riding a gut-wrenching roller-coaster of love. Nothing could be farther from the truth. If we are in Christ, then God’s love for us is unchanging. This is one facet of the sparkling diamond we call God’s immutability, which means God cannot change.

Answer:

The concept of God's love being immutable is rooted in the belief that God is unchanging and eternal. It means that God's love is constant and unchanging, regardless of any circumstances or actions on the part of humans. Here are some justifications for this belief:

1. God is perfect and unchanging: According to many religious traditions, God is perfect and unchanging. This means that God's nature and attributes, including his love, remain the same throughout time. Therefore, if God's love is part of his nature, then it must also be unchanging.

2. God's love is unconditional: Another aspect of God's love is that it is unconditional, meaning that it is not based on any external factors or actions on the part of humans. If God's love were conditional, then it would be subject to change based on human actions or circumstances, which would contradict the idea of an unchanging God.

3. The Bible teaches that God's love is immutable: The Bible, which is considered the authoritative text for many religious traditions, teaches that God's love is unchanging. For example, in the book of Malachi, it says, "For I the Lord do not change; therefore you, O children of Jacob, are not consumed" (Malachi 3:6). This verse implies that if God's love were to change, then humans would be consumed by his wrath.

4. Human experiences of God's love support the idea of its immutability: Many people report experiencing God's love in profound and transformative ways. These experiences often involve a sense of God's love being constant and unchanging, even in the midst of difficult circumstances or personal struggles. These experiences provide evidence for the idea that God's love is immutable.

In summary, the belief in God's immutable love is based on the idea of an unchanging and perfect God, whose love is unconditional and supported by biblical teachings and human experiences.
