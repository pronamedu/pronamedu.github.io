5. One nanometer is equal to 10-7 centimeter. If the diameter of a virus is 20 nanometers, what is its diameter in centimeter?​


Step-by-step explanation:

The answer would be 0.000002 centimeters.

Also, to convert from scientific notation to a regular mathematical number, you need to move the decimal place to the left depending on the amount of movements of the exponent. As a result, one nanometer = 0.0000001.

So, since 1 nanometer = 0.0000001, and the problem is asking what is the diameter of the virus when converting it from 20 nanometers to centimeters, we need to multiply:

0.0000001 x 20, which would result to 0.000002.

Hence, the diameter of the virus in centimeters is 0.000002.

20. what is micrometer caliper? ​


Micrometer, in full micrometer caliper, instrument for making precise linear measurements of dimensions such as diameters, thicknesses, and lengths of solid bodies; it consists of a C-shaped frame with a movable jaw operated by an integral screw. The fineness of the measurement that can be made depends on the lead of the screw—i.e., the amount the spindle moves toward or away from the anvil in one revolution and the means provided for indicating fractional parts of a revolution. The accuracy of the measurements depends on the accuracy of the screw-nut combination.

The nanometre or nanometer is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one billionth of a metre. One nanometre can be expressed in scientific notation as 1×10⁻⁹ m, in engineering notation as 1 E−9 m, and as simply 1/1000000000 metres
