5. creatinine is a substance found in blood. if an analysis of blood serum sample detected 0.58 mg of creatinine, how many micrograms we're present?


0.58 mg of creatinine = 580 mcg

Further explanation

Mass is one of the principal quantities, which is related to the matter in the object

The main mass unit consists of 7 units

Kilogram, kg Hectogram, hg Diagram, dag gram, g Decigram, dg centigram, cg milligrams, mg

downward multiplied by 10

upward divided by 10

Conversion of other mass units:

mg milligrams : 10⁻³ g

kg kilograms :  10³ g

µg micrograms (mcg) : 10⁻⁶ g

Mg megagrams (tons)  : 10⁶ g

ng nanograms  : 10⁻⁹ g

Gg gigagrams  : 10⁹ g

pg picogram :  10⁻¹² g

In the task , sample detected 0.58 mg of creatinine

So that, when converted to microgram (u), becomes:

1 mg = 10³ µg mcg, then for 0.58 mg :

[tex]\rm mcg=0.58\times 10^3=\boxed{580~mcg}}[/tex]
