21. Ryan took 15 to walk to school at an average speed of 70 m/min. His brother to walk the same distance. Find his brother's average speed.


Answer:

Ryan's brother's average speed is also 70 m/min.

Step-by-step explanation:

distance = speed x time

Since Ryan took 15 minutes to walk to school at an average speed of 70 m/min, we can calculate the distance he walked as:

distance = 70 m/min x 15 min

distance = 1050 meters

Now we can use the same formula to find his brother's average speed, given that he walked the same distance:

distance = speed x time

1050 meters = speed x time

Since we don't know the time his brother took, we can't solve for his speed directly. However, we can make an assumption that his brother took the same amount of time as Ryan did to cover the same distance.

So, we can write:

1050 meters = speed x 15 min

Solving for speed, we get:

speed = 1050 meters / 15 min

speed = 70 m/min

Therefore, Ryan's brother's average speed is also 70 m/min.
