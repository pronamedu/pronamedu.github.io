5. What is the empirical formula for a compound thatcontains 22.00 % N and 78.0% O?A. N2O5C. N203B. NO3D. NO2​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{N}} = \text{22.00 g} \\ & mass_{\text{O}} = \text{78.00 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{N}} = \text{22.00 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{14.01 g}} = \text{1.5703 mol} \\ & n_{\text{O}} = \text{78.00 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{16.00 g}} = \text{4.875 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{N}_x\text{O}_y[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the moles of each element by the least number of moles.

In this case, the least number of moles is 1.5703 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = \frac{\text{1.5703 mol}}{\text{1.5703 mol}} = 1 \\ & y = \frac{\text{4.875 mol}}{\text{1.5703 mol}} \approx 3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{NO}_3}[/tex]

Hence, the answer is B. NO₃.
