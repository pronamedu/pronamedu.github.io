8. 5 An air plane flies 1680 km in 1 hour 45 mins what is the average speed in kilometers in hour


Answer:

Airplane Average Speed: 960km/h

To find the average speed of the airplane in kilometers per hour, we need to convert the time from hours and minutes to just hours.

1 hour 45 minutes is equivalent to 1.75 hours (since there are 60 minutes in an hour and 45/60 = 0.75).

Now we can use the formula:

Average speed = Distance ÷ Time

Average speed = 1680 km ÷ 1.75 hours

Average speed = 960 km/hour (rounded to the nearest kilometer)

Therefore, the average speed of the airplane in kilometers per hour is 960 km/hour.

18. if Justine can run 3 km in an hour, how far can she run in 45 mins​


Velocity, distance, and time

Based on the case from the question.

If Justine's velocity is assumed, she can run with distance of 2.25 km in 45 minutes. constant

Let's review the previous statement.

According to the fundamentals of physics, velocity is defined as the amount of distance that an object travels in one unit of time. This velocity is a vector quantity with a direction and a value. Whereas the distance traveled is given in kilometers, the unit for determining speed is km/hour (km). While hours, minutes, and seconds are employed as units of time. On a global scale, the unit of measurement for velocity is generally M/second. Velocity can be measured in kilometer per hour, minutes, seconds, and so forth.

The following formula can be used to calculate the velocity value:

V = [tex]\frac{S}{T}[/tex]

where:

V: Velocity (km/h, km/m, km/s)

S: Distance (km/m)

T : Time (s/m/h)

Then, the following formula can be used to determine variations in cases where the distance or time value is unknown are:

S = V x T

T = [tex]\frac{S}{V}[/tex]

For the case mentioned above can be explained as follows:

[tex]S_{1}[/tex] = 3 kilometers

[tex]T_{1}[/tex] = 1 hour

V = ?

So,

V = [tex]\frac{S}{T}[/tex]

V  = [tex]\frac{3}{1}[/tex], which is  the the velocity of Justine is 3 km/hour.

If Justine traveled at the same velocity and finished the distance in 45 minutes, how far she can run?

V = 3 km/h

T = 45 minutes = ¾ hour

S = ?

As a result, if the question is asking for the value of distance (S),

then S = V x T

       S = 3 x ¾ = 9/4  = 2.25 km.

So, Justine run 2,25 km in 45 minutes.

We can also give an example of another case if the travel time is unknown

Example:

Justine rides a motorcycle at a speed of 60 km/h. Justine travels from school to her house which is 10 km away. So, the time taken is

V = 60 km/h

S = 10 km

T = [tex]\frac{10}{60}[/tex]

T = [tex]\frac{1}{6}[/tex] hours = [tex]\frac{1}{6}[/tex]    x 60 minutes = 10 minutes

So, Justine travel from school to her house in 10 minute by riding motorcycle with velocity 60 km/h
