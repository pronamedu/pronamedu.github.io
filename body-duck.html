6. Body Structure For Adaptation Of:FishCarabaoSnakeRoosterDuckCrabDolphinPogEagleCow​


Body Structure For AdaptationFish: Adapts to constant drinking and little urination. The goal is to maintain the amount of fluid in the body's cells. Salt that enters with water is actively excreted through the gills.Buffalo: Buffaloes are herbivorous animals that eat grass. Cows adapt to rolling in the mud on hot days to neutralize the heat of the sun.Snake: Snakes are reptiles. Snakes have a way of adaptation in which they secrete a liquid called "venom" which contains poison. The goal is to avoid enemies and predators.Rooster: Chickens have an adapted beak shape that allows them to eat grains and small animals. Chickens have specially shaped legs that they use to search the ground when foraging for food. Chickens have oily feathers to retain moisture in their skin and body.Ducks: Ducks are adapted by having membranes on their feet that make it easy to swim in water and walk in mud. This adaptation is called morphological adaptation or somatic adaptation.Crab: Crabs use their hard shells to survive and their claws to find food and protect themselves.Dolphins: Dolphins need behavioral adaptations to survive in salt water. Just as dolphins breathe with their lungs, adaptation occurs when they come to the surface regularly to obtain oxygen and release carbon dioxide into the air.Eagle: The form of adaptation of the eagle is a bent beak, a strong grip, the ability to fly quickly, as well as sharp eyesight.Cows: Cows have cellulose enzymes that are able to digest their food from grass which is a type of physiological adaptation.
