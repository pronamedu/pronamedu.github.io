15. Bonding between Sulfur and Hydrogen Sulfur and More electronegative element and value electronegative element and value Difference in electronegativity Bond Type​


Answer:

Bonding between Sulfur and Hydrogen Sulfur and More electronegative element and value electronegative element and value Difference in electronegativity Bond Type

The bonding between sulfur and hydrogen is a covalent bond, in which the two atoms share electrons in order to form a stable compound. In the case of sulfur and hydrogen, the sulfur atom is more electronegative than the hydrogen atom, meaning that it has a greater ability to attract electrons to itself. The difference in electronegativity between the two atoms determines the type of bond that is formed between them. If the difference in electronegativity is greater than 0.4, the bond is considered to be polar covalent, and if the difference is less than 0.4, the bond is considered to be nonpolar covalent. The value of the electronegativity of an element can vary depending on the specific reference scale that is used, but on the Pauling scale, the electronegativity of sulfur is 2.58 and the electronegativity of hydrogen is 2.20.

17. Electronegativity difference


Answer:

The absolute value of the difference in electronegativity (ΔEN) of two bonded atoms provides a rough measure of the polarity to be expected in the bond and, thus, the bond type. When the difference is very small or zero, the bond is covalent and nonpolar. When it is large, the bond is polar covalent or ionic.

21. solve for the electronegativity difference​


Answer:

Subtract the smaller electronegativity from the larger one to find the difference. For example, if we're looking at the molecule HF, we would subtract the electronegativity of hydrogen (2.1) from fluorine (4.0). 4.0 - 2.1 = 1.9. If the difference is below about 0.5, the bond is nonpolar covalent.
