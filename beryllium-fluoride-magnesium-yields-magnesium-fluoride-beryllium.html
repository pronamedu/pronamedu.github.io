[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of fluorine} & = 17.2 \: \cancel{\text{g Mg}} \times \frac{\text{1.563 g F}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{g Mg}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{26.9 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

6. Magnesium fluoride contains only magnesium and fluorine. what is the formula for this compound? answer


Answer:

The formula of magnesium fluoride is MgF₂.

Explanation:

Magnesium fluoride is an example of an ionic compound. Ionic compounds are compounds that consist of ions. It is composed of two elements; one element metal and the other is non-metal.

There is a method called criss cross method used for naming simple ionic compounds. In this method you need to know first the charges of the two elements. The charge of an atom is related to its valence electrons, which can be identified by looking at which group it belongs. This method is what we are going to use to know the formula of magnesium fluoride.

As mentioned earlier, we need to know first  the charges that magnesium and fluorine carries.

Let us start with magnesium. Magnesium is a metal, specifically an alkaline-earth metal, from Group IIA of the periodic table. Using this information, we now conclude that magnesium contains a positive 2 charge (+2).

For fluorine, fluorine is a non-metal from the halogen group, group 17. Elements from this group contains charge of negative 1 (-1).

Now we can do the criss-cross method. The positive 2 charge from magnesium will be the subscript of fluorine and the negative 1 charge from fluorine will be the subscript of magnesium. For clear visualization of this step, I provided an image on how to do the method.

From the method we used, the formula of the magnesium fluoride compound is MgF₂.

Understanding electron configuration

Electron configuration is an arrangement or picture that shows the placement of electrons in an atom. The arrangement of these electrons is based on shells or orbitals.

Electron configurations in atoms, can be expressed by the precipitation diagram and orbital diagram. Both can be useful for determining molecules and theories.

Theory underlying the electron configuration Bohr's atomic theory

Electron trajectories are also called electron shells, which are occupied by the maximum number of electrons.

In this theory, electron configuration is filling in electrons starting from the lowest energy level (shell), namely the first K shell, after the K shell is completely filled, proceed to the L shell, M shell, N shell, and so on.

The maximum number of electrons occupied by each electron shell can be found using the formula: 2.n2

The K shell (n = 1) holds a maximum of 2.12 = 2

Shell L (n = 2) holds a maximum of 2.22 = 8

Shell M (n = 3) holds a maximum of 2.23 = 16, so on.

To write down the electron configuration of an atom, that is by knowing the number of electrons of an atom indicated by the atomic number.

Theory of Quantum Mechanics

Electrons in atoms move around the nucleus in the atomic shells. Each atomic shell, composed of subshells, contains a quantum number (the set of s, p, d, and f orbitals).

To facilitate the determination of the value of the quantum number, an orbital diagram is used. The orbital diagram is denoted by a box.

Subshell s = 1 box

Subshell p = 3 squares

Subshell d = 5 boxes

Subshell f = 7 squares

20. what chemical equation of magnesium reacts with sodium fluoride to produce magnesium chloride and elemental sodium​


The chemical equation for the reaction between magnesium and sodium fluoride to produce magnesium chloride and elemental sodium is as follows:

2Mg + 2NaF → 2MgCl2 + 2Na

In this reaction, two atoms of magnesium react with two molecules of sodium fluoride to form two molecules of magnesium chloride and two atoms of elemental sodium. The reaction is a double displacement reaction, where the cations and anions of the reactants switch places to form the products. Magnesium chloride is an ionic compound that is highly soluble in water, while elemental sodium is a highly reactive metal that can easily ignite in air or react with water to produce sodium hydroxide and hydrogen gas. This reaction is often used in the production of certain chemicals and alloys, as well as in the extraction of magnesium metal from its ores.
