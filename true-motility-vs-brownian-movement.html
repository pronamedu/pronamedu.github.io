1. Examples for Brownian movement


Brownian motion is the random movement of particles in a fluid due to their collisions with other atoms or molecules.

Examples include, but not limited to, the motion of pollen grains on still water, movement of dust motes in a room (although largely affected by air currents), diffusion of pollutants in the air, diffusion of calcium through bones and movement of "holes" of electrical charge in semiconductors.
