Apart from animals, some human-made devices and technologies also use echolocation for various purposes. For example, submarines use echolocation to detect and locate other vessels or obstacles in the water. Similarly, some medical imaging technologies, such as ultrasound and echocardiography, use sound waves to create images of internal body structures. 16. Passengers on airplane are allowed a maximum of 66 lbs of luggage. Overweight luggage are charged P800 per kg excess. How much would a nurse going to Canada have to pay if her luggage weighs 215.65 kg? ​


Answer:

When travelling by aircraft, passengers have a maximum allowable weight for their luggage. They are then charged £10 for every kilogram overweight. If a passenger carrying 40 kg of luggage is charged £50, how much would a passenger carrying 80 kg be charged?

Solution

A £50 charge represents 5 kg overweight (5 times £10 = £50).
So 40 minus 5 = 35 kg must be the maximum allowable weight for baggage.

Hence 80 kg is 80 minus 35 = 45 kg overweight and the passenger would be charged 45 × £10 = £450.

18. a net force of 20newtons to the right acted upon a 5kg luggage for a period of 5 seconds.what is the acceleration of the luggage?​


Solving for Acceleration

Answer:

The acceleration of the luggage is 4  [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex] .

   

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

    F = 20 N

    m = 5 kg

    t = 5 s

Required: acceleration (a)

Formula:

We derive the formula of acceleration (a) from the formula of force (F), which is F = ma.

To derive the formula, we isolate the quantity on one side of the equation. To do this, we divide both sides of the equation by m:

    F = ma

    [tex]\frac{F}{m}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{ma}{m}[/tex]

    [tex]\frac{F}{m}[/tex] = a

Thus,

    a = F/m

Solution:

    a = 20 kg  [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex]  / 5 kg

    a = 4 [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex]

It is important to note that newton is a unit of force that is equivalent to kg  [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex] .
