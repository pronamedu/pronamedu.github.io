13. two sides of a triangle measures 16 m and 51 m. Its area is 234.32 m ². Find its perimeter Hint use A = 1/2 a x b x sinC


Answer:

May dalawang possible na sagot dito po! The teacher who wrote it probably intended Answer #1 but had overlooked something. :)

Answer #1: 105.999835897... meters

Answer #2: 131.7534771262... meters

[Notice how Answer #1 is suspiciously close to 106 meters? That's why I suspect the teacher meant that solution but forgot about the other one's existence!]

Step-by-step explanation:What's The Plan?

Two side lengths of the triangle are already given: [tex]a=16[/tex] and [tex]b=51[/tex].

If we knew the third side [tex]c[/tex], then we can get the perimeter by just adding all of the three sides. But, how would we figure out the third side [tex]c[/tex]?

Well, if we knew the angle opposite side [tex]c[/tex] (which is called the angle [tex]C[/tex]), then we can use the Law of Cosines [which guarantees [tex]c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos\left(C\right)[/tex]] to calculate [tex]c[/tex]. But how would we figure out the angle [tex]C[/tex]?

That's where the hint comes in! The area of any triangle can be found via [tex]A=\frac{1}{2}ab\sin\left(C\right)[/tex], and we know what the area is already! ([tex]A=234.32[/tex])

So, the plan is to use that information to solve for the angle [tex]C[/tex], then use the Law of Cosines to find the side [tex]c[/tex] which would finally allow us to find the perimeter.

Finding The Angle [tex]C[/tex] and The Teacher's Oversight

[tex]A=\frac{1}{2}ab\sin\left(C\right)[/tex]

Substituting [tex]A=234.32[/tex], [tex]a=16[/tex], and [tex]b=51[/tex] gives us this:

[tex]234.32=\frac{1}{2}\left(16\right)\left(51\right)\sin\left(C\right)[/tex]

[tex]234.32=408\sin\left(C\right)[/tex]

[tex]\sin\left(C\right)=\frac{234.32}{408}[/tex] [which is about 0.5743137...]

But as it turns out, this equation has infinitely many solutions. And there are actually TWO solutions which lie between 0 and 180 degrees. This is essentially why we had TWO possible triangles, and thus TWO possible perimeters.

Case #1 that the teacher may have assumed: [tex]C=\arcsin\left(\frac{234.32}{408} \right)[/tex]

[This would be about 35.051586... degrees; the [tex]\arcsin[/tex] function always outputs the solution that lies between [tex]-90^{\circ}[/tex] and [tex]90^{\circ}[/tex]]

Case #2 that the teacher may have overlooked: [tex]C=180^{\circ}-\arcsin\left(\frac{234.32}{408}\right)[/tex]

[This would be about 144.94841... degrees]

Finding The Side [tex]c[/tex] and The Perimeter

The Law of Cosines guarantees the following equality holds:

[tex]c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos\left(C\right)[/tex]

When we plug in the two different values for the angle [tex]C[/tex] into [tex]\cos\left(C\right)[/tex], we find that we get [tex]\cos\left(C\right)=0.818635294...[/tex] for Case #1 and [tex]\cos\left(C\right)=-0.818635294...[/tex] for Case #2.

The sign-change from positive in the first case to negative in the second case is what results in us getting TWO different values for the right-hand side after substitution.

Case #1: [tex]c^2=1520.98720006...[/tex]

Case #2: [tex]c^2=4193.01279993868...[/tex]

This, therefore, gives us TWO positive side lengths for the side [tex]c[/tex] and the perimeter.

Case #1: [tex]c=38.999835897...[/tex]

(The perimeter would be 105.999835897... meters in this case, and I suspect the teacher would've rounded it to 106 meters)

Case #2: [tex]c=64.7534771262...[/tex]

(Here, the perimeter would be 131.7534771262... meters, but the teacher may have overlooked this case)

I hope this helps! :)

15. Solve the following quadratic equations by Factoring.1. (m + 7) (m-5) = 02. 4m² - 20 m = 03. m² - 20 m + 51= 04. 6 m² - m = 15​


Answer:

Solving Quadratic Equations by Factoring

Dito maeencounter yung mga pinag-aralan niyo noong nasa 8th Grade ka, which is mga iba't ibang factoring techniques na ginagamit sa Math.

Solution

1. [tex] (m + 7) (m - 5) [/tex]

Mapapansin mo sa given is naka-factored out na siya. Meaning, wala ka na dapat galawin sa ganitong equation. So ang gagawin mo na lang is to use the ZERO PRODUCT PROPERTY na kung saan yung mga factors na nandyan sa given equation ay ieequate mo na lang sa zero.

[tex] (m + 7) = 0 [/tex]

[tex] (m - 5) = 0 [/tex]

Transpose yung mga number na nasa kaliwa papuntang kanan.

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = -7}}[/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 5}}[/tex]

Hence, ang sagot dito ay:

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = -7}}[/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 5}}[/tex]

2. Use CMF (Common Monomial Factor) Instead.

Factored form of 4m² - 20m = 0 is:

[tex] 4m (m - 4) = 0 [/tex]

So dahil nakuha na natin yung factored form, gaya nga sabi ko kanina, is equate natin ito sa zero.

[tex] 4m = 0 [/tex]

[tex] m - 4 = 0 [/tex]

First Solution: (4m = 0)

Divide both sides by 4

[tex] \frac {4m}{4} = \frac {0}{4} [/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 0}}[/tex]

Second solution: m - 4 = 0

Transpose the negative four to right side.

[tex] m = 4 [/tex]

Therefore, the solution set are:

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 0}}[/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 4}}[/tex]

3. [tex] m^2 - 20m + 51 = 0 [/tex]

Gagamit tayo ng Factoring Technique na: Sum-Product Method, na kung saan hahanap tayo ng two factors na pinag-add ay ang magiging sagot ay numerical coefficient ng second term, at kapag pinag multiply ang sagot nito ay ang third term.

Factors of 51

-17 • -3

Gumawa ng parenthesis, hanapin ang square root ni m² at ilagay kasama ng ating mga factors sa iisang parenthesis.

[tex] (m - 17) (m - 3) = 0 [/tex]

Equate the factors into zero.

[tex] (m - 17) = 0 [/tex]

[tex] (m - 3) = 0 [/tex]

Transpose.

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 17}}[/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = 3}}[/tex]

4th. 6m² - m = 15

Transform it first to the standard form.

Transpose 15 to the left side.

[tex] 6m² - m - 15 = 0 [/tex]

Hanapan ng factors.

[tex] (2m + 3) (3m - 5) [/tex]

Equate into zero.

[tex] 2m + 3 = 0 [/tex]

[tex] 3m - 5 = 0 [/tex]

Transpose yung mga dapat i-transpose.

[tex] 2m = -3 [/tex]

[tex] 3m = 5 [/tex]

Divide natin yung kasamang numerical coefficient ni m sa left side, both sides.

FIRST SOLUTION!

[tex] \frac{2m}{2} = \frac{-3}{2} [/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = -\frac{3}{2}}}[/tex]

SECOND SOLUTION!

[tex]\frac{3m}{3} = \frac{5}{3} [/tex]

[tex]\red{\boxed{m = \frac{5}{3}}}[/tex]
