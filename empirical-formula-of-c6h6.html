Therapeutic Epinephrine is the synthetic form of the naturally occurring sympathomimetic amine with vasoconstricting, intraocular pressure-reducing, and bronchodilating activities. By stimulating vascular alpha-adrenergic receptors, epinephrine causes vasoconstriction, thereby increasing vascular resistance and blood pressure. 22. The empirical formula of benzene i CH (it molecular formula i C6H6). If 10. 00 mg of benzene i ubjected to combution analyi, what ma of CO2 and H2O will be produced?.


Problem:

The empirical formula of benzene is CH (its molecular formula is C₆H₆). If 10.00 mg of benzene is subjected to combution analyisis, what mass of CO₂ and H₂O

will be produced?

Solution:

unbalanced chemical equation for combustion of benzene:

C₆H₆ + O₂ --> CO₂ + H₂O

1.) Balance the chemical equation before proceeding to the stoichiometric calculation.

C₆H₆ + ¹⁵/₂O₂ --> 6CO₂ + 3H₂O

Put 6 to balance the number of C. Put 3 to balance the number of H. Put 15/2 to balance the number of O.

Multiply the whole equation by 2 to eliminate the fraction.

(C₆H₆ + ¹⁵/₂O₂ --> 6CO₂ + 3H₂O)2

Balanced equation:

2C₆H₆ + 15O₂ --> 12CO₂ + 6H₂O

To check:

Reactants side:

12 carbon atoms

12 hydrogen atoms

30 oxygen atoms

Products side:

12 carbon atoms

12 hydrogen atoms

30 oxygen atoms

molar mass:

C₆H₆ = 6(12.011) + 6(1.008) = 78.114 g/mole

CO₂ = 12.011 + 2(15.999) = 44.009 g/mole

H₂O = 2(1.008) + 15.999 = 18.015 g/mole

2.) Do the stoichimetry. Use the balanced equation

a.) mass of CO₂

= 10.00 mg C₆H₆ × (1 g C₆H₆ /1000 mg C₆H₆) × (1 mole

C₆H₆/78.114 g C₆H₆) × (12 mol CO₂/2 mol C₆H₆) × (44.009 g CO₂/1 mol CO₂) × (1000 mg CO₂/1 g CO₂)

= 33.80 mg CO₂

b.) mass of H₂O

= 10.00 mg C₆H₆ × (1 g C₆H₆ /1000 mg C₆H₆) × (1 mole

C₆H₆/78.114 g C₆H₆) × (6 mol H₂O/2 mol C₆H₆) × (18.015 g H₂O/1 mol H₂O) × (1000 mg H₂O/1 g H₂O)

= 6.919 mg H₂O

Note: The answers are expressed in 4 significant figures because 10.00 has 4 significant figures.
