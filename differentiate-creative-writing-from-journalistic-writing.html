12. name and discuss 3 differences between journalistic writing and creative writing.​


Answer:

Journalistic writing and creative writing are two distinct forms of writing with different purposes, styles, and techniques. Here are three key differences between the two:

1. Purpose: One of the main differences between journalistic writing and creative writing is their purpose. Journalistic writing aims to inform, educate, and provide objective and factual information about current events, issues, and news. On the other hand, creative writing is focused on expressing the writer's imagination, emotions, ideas, and experiences through the use of literary devices such as plot, character, setting, and language.

2. Style: Another difference between journalistic writing and creative writing is their style. Journalistic writing is typically written in a straightforward, clear, and concise style with a focus on accuracy, objectivity, and impartiality. The language used in journalistic writing tends to be simple, direct, and free of flowery or poetic language. In contrast, creative writing often employs a more complex, figurative, and poetic style, with a focus on evoking emotions, creating vivid imagery, and expressing the writer's unique voice and perspective.

3. Techniques: Finally, the techniques used in journalistic writing and creative writing differ significantly. Journalistic writing relies heavily on research, interviews, and fact-checking to verify information and ensure accuracy. Journalists use a variety of techniques such as inverted pyramid structure, lead paragraphs, and attribution to convey information effectively. In contrast, creative writing often employs techniques such as foreshadowing, flashback, dialogue, and symbolism to create tension, suspense, and meaning. Creative writers also have more freedom to experiment with structure, form, and language to convey their message and express their creativity.

In summary, while both journalistic writing and creative writing involve writing and storytelling, they have different purposes, styles, and techniques. Journalistic writing is focused on informing and educating readers about factual information, while creative writing is focused on expressing the writer's imagination and emotions. The style and techniques used in each form of writing also differ significantly.
