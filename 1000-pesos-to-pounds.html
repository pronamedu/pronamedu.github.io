3. Isko wants to mix raisins worth 16 pesos per pound and nuts worth 22 pesos to make 24 pounds of a mixture worth 20 pesos per pound. How many pounds of raisins and how many pounds of nuts should he use?


There are many ways on how to solve the problem above, but we need to choose the best ways to solve.

We let x be the weight of the raisins in the mixture
      let y be the weight of the nuts in the mixture

For further understanding we need to create table;
__________________________________________________
     Type      I    Cost per Pound     I     Pounds     I    Total Cost
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raisins                  P16                          x                       16x
Nuts                      P22                           y                       22y
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mixture                  P20                         24                      480

We are looking for the weight of the raisins and nuts in the mixture.
From the table we can create an equations;
         [tex]x+y = 24[/tex]
         [tex]16x+22y=480[/tex]

Solve the equation by substitution;
Use equation 2;    [tex]16x+22y=480[/tex]
                             [tex]16(24-y)+22y =480[/tex]   substitute equation 1
                             [tex]384 - 16y + 22y = 480 [/tex]
                             [tex]6y = 480 -384 [/tex]
                             [tex]6y = 96[/tex]
                             [tex]y = 16[/tex]

So, there are 16 pounds of nuts in the mixture.

From equation 1; [tex]x + y = 24[/tex] substitute the weights of nuts to get the weights of the raisins.
                [tex]x + 16 = 24[/tex]
                [tex]x = 24-16[/tex]
                [tex]x = 8[/tex]

So, the weights of the raisins is 8 pounds.

The next thing to do is to check the answer if it does make sense.
16 pounds of nuts plus 8 pounds of raisins is 24 pounds.
If the mixtures cost 20 pesos per pound, then the total cost is 480.

Therefor we cab conclude that there are 16 pounds of nuts and 8 pounds of raisins in the mixture with a cost of 20 pesos.

9. Your favorite dog groomer charges according to your dog's weight, if your dog is 15 pounds and under the groomer charges 300 pesos. If your dog is between 15 and 40 pounds (including 40 pounds) she charges 400 pesos. If your dog is over 40 pounds, she charges 400 pesos plus andadditional 20 pesos for each pound over 40 pounds.A. write a piecewise function that describes what your dog groomer charges B. What would the groomer charge if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds ​


A. Write a piecewise function that describes what your dog groomer charges.

[tex]\sf f(x)= \begin{cases}\sf 300, &\sf if \: x \leq 15 \\\sf 400, &\sf if \: 15<x \leq 40 \\\sf 20x-400, &\sf if \: x > 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

B. What would the groomer charge if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds?

x=60, 60>40

[tex]\begin{aligned} &\sf f(x)=20x-400 \\&\sf f(60)=20(60)-400 \\&\sf f(60)=1200-400 \\&\sf f(60)=800 \end{aligned} [/tex]

The groomer would charge 800 pesos if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds.
