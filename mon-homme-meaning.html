1. Homme work • Write the meaning of the following elements of art. line- Shape- 1. 2. 3. color 4. Value- 5. fexture- 6. Space- 7. form-​


Answer:

katulad din iyan yung sagot ko

Answer:

1. A line is an identifiable path created by a point moving in space. It is one-dimensional and can vary in width, direction, and length. Lines often define the edges of a form.

2. Shape is a two-dimensional, flat, or limited to height and width.

3. Color is an element consisting of hues, of which there are three properties: hue, chroma or intensity, and value. Color is present when light strikes an object and it is reflected back into the eye, a reaction to a hue arising in the optic nerve.

4. Value defines how light or dark a given color or hue can be. Values are best understood when visualized as a scale or gradient, from dark to light. The more tonal variants in an image, the lower the contrast. When shades of similar value are used together, they also create a low contrast image.

5. Texture is the perceived surface quality of a work of art. It may be perceived physically, through the sense of touch, or visually, or both.

6. Space in a work of art refers to a feeling of depth or three dimensions. It can also refer to the artist's use of the area within the picture plane. The area around the primary objects in a work of art is known as negative space, while the space occupied by the primary objects is known as positive space.

7. Form has two meanings: it can refer to the overall form taken by the work – its physical nature; or within a work of art it can refer to the element of shape among the various elements that make up a work.

8. Line MQ intersects with line PN at point O. Also, ∠MON ≅ ∠POQ. If m∠NOQ = 30°, what is the measure of ∠MON?


Answer:

150°

Step-by-step explanation:

Since MON and POQ are congruent, then NOQ and MOP are also congruent. If MOQ is 30°, then MOP would also be 30°. So 30°+30°=60°.

The sum of all angles is always 360° so 360°-60° is 300°. Divide it by 2 since there are 2 congruent angles left. That would be 150°.
