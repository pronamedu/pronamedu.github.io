Answer:

d. y = 2x - 7

Step-by-step explanation:

Slope-Intercept Form of a Straight Line is:

[tex]y=mx+b[/tex] , where [tex]m[/tex] is the slope and [tex]b[/tex] is the y-intercept.

If [tex]slope=m=2[/tex], then [tex]y=2x+b[/tex].

Solve for [tex]b[/tex] by using point [tex](6,5)[/tex]:

[tex]y=2x+b\\5=2(6)+b\\5=12+b\\5-12=b\\-7=b\\b=-7[/tex]

With [tex]slope=m=2[/tex] and [tex]y-intercept=b=-7[/tex],

the equation of the line is [tex]y=2x-7[/tex].

28. factor 2x²+13x-6 = (2x+3) (x-2) ​


To factor 2x2+13x-6, we must first find two numbers that multiply to -6 and add to 13. The only numbers that meet these requirements are -3 and -2. These numbers can be used to factor the quadratic as follows:

2x² + 13x - 6 = 2x² + (-3x - 2x) - 6

= 2x(x-2) + (-3)(x-2) (x-2)

= (2x-3)(x-2) (x-2)

As a result, the factorization of 2x2+13x-6 is (2x-3) (x-2).
