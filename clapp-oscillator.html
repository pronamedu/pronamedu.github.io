1. Similarities between:• underdamped oscillation & overdamped oscillation• underdamped oscillation & critically damped • overdamped oscillation & critically damped ​


Answer:

overdamped moves slowly while underdamped moves quickly.

underdamped moves quickly while critically moves quickly as possible

overdamped moves slowly while critically moves quickly as possible

Explanation:

An overdamped system moves slowly toward equilibrium. An underdamped system moves quickly to equilibrium, but will oscillate about the equilibrium point as it does so.

A critically damped system moves as quickly as possible to toward equilibrium without oscillating about the equilibrium.

8. A mass-spring system makes 25 complete oscillations in 5 seconds. What is the period and frequency of the oscillations?​


Explanation:

The period of oscillation is the time it takes for the system to make one complete oscillation. In this case, the system makes 25 oscillations in 5 seconds, so the period is 5/25 = 0.2 seconds.

The frequency of oscillation is the number of oscillations that occur in a given period of time. It is the reciprocal of the period. In this case, the frequency is 1/0.2 = 5 oscillations per second

Simple Pendulum

Pendulums are nothing more than things that pivot and swing back and forth. Naturally, they operate in a gravitational field. The masses cancel as normal, and the only factors affecting the solution are gravity and the length of the rope. The period doubles and the oscillation frequency decreases by a factor of two as the length of the string is quadrupled.

The simple pendulum is a mechanical device that also oscillates. It consists of a point mass m hung from a fixed support by a light, inextensible string of length L.
