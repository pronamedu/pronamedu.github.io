7. True Or False1. Cash Method provides a better indicator of a business's profits or losses. 2. Accounting is the process of recording and classifying business financial transactions. 3. The only users of financial information are the owners of a business. 4. A calendar year is the accounting period used by most businesses. 5. Debit/Credit Rule- enter an amount in the normal balance side of an account to increase the balance and in the opposite side to decrease the balance. 6. Profit is the amount that a business's expenses exceed revenues. 7. A debit is an entry made to the right side of an account and always increases an account balance. 8. Whether a debit or a credit to an account increases the account's balance or decreases the account's balance depends on the type of account.9. Asset Accounts normally have a credit balance.10. Expense Accounts normally have a debit balance. ​


True Or False

TRUE 1. Cash Method provides a better indicator of a business's profits or losses.

Cash payments are payments using the country's currency in the form of notes or coins paid by the recipient of goods or services to the seller.

TRUE 2. Accounting is the process of recording and classifying business financial transactions.

Accounting is a process of recording, summarizing, classifying, processing and presenting transaction data, as well as various activities related to finance, so that this information can be used by someone who is an expert in their field and becomes material for making a decision.

TRUE 3. The only users of financial information are the owners of a business.

Business owner or business owner is a designation for shareholders or company founders. In contrast to ordinary employees, they do not manage service or production every day. On the other hand, the business owner's job is to determine the big picture and the steps that must be taken in business activities.

FALSE 4. A calendar year is the accounting period used by most businesses.

A calendar year begins on New Year's Day in each calendar system and ends one day before the next New Year's Day. A year can also start on any day in the calendar, and end one day before that day in the following year.

TRUE 5. Debit/Credit Rule- enter an amount in the normal balance side of an account to increase the balance and in the opposite side to decrease the balance.

Debits and credits are used in monitoring incoming and outgoing money in a business account. Simply put, debit is money coming into the account, while credit is money coming out of the account

TRUE 6. Profit is the amount that a business's expenses exceed revenues.

Profit can be defined in two ways, the first is profit in economics is the difference between income and total costs. Implicit costs include the opportunity costs that occur when firms choose to use certain factors of production

FALSE 7. A debit is an entry made to the right side of an account and always increases an account balance.

A debit is a fee or amount paid from one account to another. This will have an impact on increasing assets and decreasing liabilities or equity in the balance sheet. That way: Debiting the debtor's account means reducing debt.

FALSE 8. Whether a debit or a credit to an account increases the account's balance or decreases the account's balance depends on the type of account.

Debits and credits are used in monitoring incoming and outgoing money in a business account. Simply put, debit is money coming into the account, while credit is money coming out of the account

TRUE 9. Asset Accounts normally have a credit balance.

An asset account is an economic resource which also includes costs incurred as a result of previous transactions and has benefits in the future. Assets are the amount of wealth owned by a company to run its business.

TRUE 10. Expense Accounts normally have a debit balance.

Accounts included in the expense of accounting in the financial statements. Purchase of raw materials, depreciation of an asset such as operational vehicle depreciation, labor salary expense, and so on. All forms of costs that directly affect production activities are included in the expense category.
