10. ana collected 3 rubber bands on monday, and plans to collect twice as amany rubber bands the next day as the previous day. how may rubber bands should she collect the next monday?


Answer:

765 rubber bands

Step-by-step explanation:

In mathematics, there is what we call a SEQUENCE by which in definition is the order (in particular) of events related with each other following one another.

There are THREE MOST COMMON TYPES OF SEQUENCE we know, namely

1. Arithmetic Sequence

2. Geometric Sequence; and

3. Harmonic Sequence

Arithmetic sequence deals with a sequence by which the numbers differ with each other by a certain constant (d).

GEOMETRIC SEQUENCE, on the other hand, deals with numbers that have a constant ratio (r).

While harmonic sequence deals with numbers which when reciprocated, becomes arithmetic sequence.

Like the above problem given, since the number of rubber bands are just multiplied twice everyday, then we use geometric progression.

Note that the problem asks for the amount that Ana can collect on the following Monday. So in short, we need the total number of rubber bands she can collect by Monday.  

We are to use the FORMULA FOR SUM OF GEOMETRIC SEQUENCE

Sn = (a1)(1-r^n)/(1-r) where r is not equal to 1

We first create a representation for the sequence

Monday = 3

Tuesday = (2)(3) = 6

Wednesday = (2)(6) = 12

Thursday = (12)(2) = 24

Friday = (2)(24) = 48

Saturday = (2)(48) = 96

Sunday = (2)(96) = 192

Monday = (2)(192) = 384

So the sequence is  

3, 6, 12, 24, 48, 96, 192, 384

Therefore, by monday she should collect

= 3 + 6 + 12 + 24 + 48 + 96 + 192 + 384

= 765

Using the formula, we have

Sn = (a1)(1-r^n)/(1-r)

S8 = (3)(1-2^8)/(1-2)

S8 = 765

14. How would a magnet interact with rubber bands? ​


Answer:

If you hold two magnets the wrong way around, they push apart - they repel! In other words, if you hold two magnets together so that like-poles are close together (two norths OR two souths), they repel. Try it! ... 16. how a rubber band store potential energy


You input potential (stored) energy into the rubber band system when you stretched the rubber band back. Because it is an elastic system, this kind of potential energy is specifically called elastic potential energy. ... When the rubber band is released, the potential energy is quickly converted to kinetic (motion) energy.
