27. 9. What mass of O2 can be generated by the decomposition of 100.0 g of NaClO3? 2 NaClO3 → 2 NaCl + 3 O2 10. What mass of Li2O is needed to react with 1,060 g of CO2? Li2O+ CO2 → Li2CO3


✏️STOICHIOMETRY

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{9. \: SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

Note that the molar masses of O₂ and NaClO₃ are 32.00 g/mol and 106.44 g/mol, respectively. Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of NaClO₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 3 moles of O₂.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{O}_2 & = 100.0 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{NaClO}_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{NaClO}_3}}{106.44 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{NaClO}_3}} \times \frac{\text{3 mol} \: \cancel{\text{O}_2}}{2 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{NaClO}_3}} \times \frac{\text{32.00 g} \: \text{O}_2}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{O}_2}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{45.10 g} \: \text{O}_2} \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\large{\red{10. \: SOLUTION:}}}[/tex]

Note that the molar masses of Li₂O and CO₂ are 29.88 g/mol and 44.01 g/mol, respectively. Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Li₂O is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of CO₂.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Li}_2\text{O} & = 1,060 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{CO}_2} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{CO}_2}}{44.01 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{CO}_2}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \cancel{\text{Li}_2\text{O}}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{CO}_2}} \times \frac{\text{29.88 g} \: \text{Li}_2\text{O}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Li}_2\text{O}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{720. g} \: \text{Li}_2\text{O}} \end{aligned}[/tex]
