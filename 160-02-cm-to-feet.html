For 160cm

[tex]\[\begin{array}{l}A = \frac{{{{160}^2}}}{{4\pi }}\\\\A = \frac{{25600}}{{4(3.14159265359)}}\\\\A = \frac{{25600}}{{12.5663706144}}\\\\A = 2037.18c{m^2}\end{array}\][/tex]

[tex]\[\begin{array}{l}A = \frac{{{{120}^2}}}{{4\pi }}\\\\A = \frac{{14400}}{{4(3.14159265359)}}\\\\A = \frac{{14400}}{{12.5663706144}}\\\\A = 1145.92c{m^2}\end{array}\][/tex]

1. Mean (Average) Height:

The mean or average height can be calculated by adding all the heights and dividing the sum by the total number of heights:

Mean height = (160+158+158+159+160+160+162+165+166+167+170) / 11

= 166 / 11

= 15.09

So, the mean or average height of the players is approximately 159.09 cm.

2. Median Height:

To find the median height, we need to arrange the heights in ascending or descending order and then find the middle value. Since we have an odd number of values, the median is the middle value when the heights are arranged in order:

158, 158, 159, 160, 160, 160, 162, 165, 166, 167, 170.

So, the median height is 160 cm.

3. Mode Height:

The mode height is the most frequent value in the data set. In this case, the heights 160 cm is the most frequent since it occurs three times. Therefore, the mode height is 160 cm.

Range of Heights:

The range of heights is the difference between the highest and lowest value in the data set. In this case, the highest height is 170 cm and the lowest is 158 cm. Therefore, the range of heights is:

Range = highest height - lowest height

= 170 - 158

= 12 cm

So, the range of heights is 12 cm.

30. what is the standard division of 160 cm 166 cm 155 cm 158 cm 162 cm 170 cm 156 cm 153 cm


Answer:

5.78 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula:

[tex]\sf \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x_i - \mu)^2}{n-1} }[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{where} \begin{cases} \sf n = number \: of \: terms\\\sf \mu = mean\\\sf x_i = individual \: values \: of \: the \: terms \: on \: the \: sample\end{cases}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\sf \textsf{Given: } \{160,166,155,158,162,170,156,153\}[/tex]

[tex]\sf In \: this \: case, \; x_i = \{160,166,155,158,162,170,156,153\}[/tex]

[tex]\sf By \: counting \: the \: number \: of \: terms, \: n = 8[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{Solving for the mean }\sf \mu,[/tex]

[tex]\sf \mu = \frac{160+166+155+158+162+170+156+153}{8} = 160[/tex]

[tex]\therefore \sf (x_i - \mu)^2 = \begin{cases} \sf (160 - 160)^2 = 0\\ \sf (166 - 160)^2 = 36 \\ \sf (155 - 160)^2 = 25 \\ \sf (158 - 160)^2 = 4 \\ \sf (162 - 160)^2 = 4 \\ \sf (170 - 160)^2 = 100 \\ \sf (156 - 160)^2 = 16 \\ \sf (153 - 160)^2 = 49\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\sf It \: follows \: that[/tex]

[tex]\sf \sum(x_i-\mu)^2 = 0 + 36 + 25 + 4 + 4 + 100 + 16 + 49\\\text{} \quad \quad \quad \quad \ \ \ = 234[/tex]

[tex]\sf Substituting \: to \: the \: formula,[/tex]

[tex]\sf \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x_i - \mu)^2}{n-1} } = \sqrt{\frac{234}{8-1} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf \sigma = \sqrt{33.4285714286} \approx 5.78 \: cm[/tex]
