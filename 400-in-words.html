4. the importance of Data Backup 400 words​


Answer:

Data backup is the process of creating copies of data so that it can be restored in the event of data loss. Data backup is an essential part of any organization’s data management strategy and is a critical component of any disaster recovery plan.

Data loss can be caused by a variety of events, such as hardware failure, power outages, natural disasters, malicious attacks, or accidental deletion. Regardless of the cause, data loss can have a significant impact on an organization’s operations and financial performance.

Data backup helps to protect an organization’s data from loss by creating copies of the data that can be used to restore the system in the event of a disaster. Data backups can be stored onsite on local storage devices or offsite on cloud-based storage services.

Onsite backups are typically stored on local storage devices, such as external hard drives, tape drives, or RAID arrays. These backups are typically stored in secure locations and are designed to be quickly accessed in the event of a disaster.

Offsite backups are typically stored on cloud-based storage services, such as Amazon S3 or Microsoft Azure. These backups are stored in secure data centers and are designed to be accessed quickly in the event of a disaster.

Data backups are essential for protecting an organization’s data from loss. However, they are only effective if they are regularly updated and tested. Regularly updating data backups ensures that the most recent version of the data is available in the event of a disaster. Testing data backups ensures that the backups are functioning properly and that the data can be restored in the event of a disaster.

Data backups are also important for ensuring that data is available for regulatory compliance. Many organizations are required to maintain records for long periods of time and must be able to access these records in the event of an audit. Data backups ensure that the necessary records are available and can be quickly accessed if needed.

Data backups are also important for ensuring that data is available for data recovery. Data recovery is the process of restoring data from backups in the event of data loss. Data recovery can be a complex and time-consuming process, but data backups can make it much easier and more efficient.

In conclusion, data backups are an essential part of any organization’s data management strategy. Data backups help to protect an organization’s data from loss, ensure that data is available for regulatory compliance, and make data recovery much easier and more efficient. Regularly updating and testing data backups is essential for ensuring that the backups are effective and that the data can be restored in the event of a disaster.

Answer:

Foot binding, also known as "lotus feet," was a traditional practice in ancient China where young girls had their feet tightly bound to prevent them from growing properly. This practice was prevalent in China for over a thousand years and was considered a symbol of beauty and wealth despite being painful and harmful.

The process of foot binding typically began when a girl was around five or six years old. The feet were soaked in hot water to soften them, and then the toes were curled under and held in place with bandages. Over time, the bandages were tightened, and the feet were forced to become smaller and smaller. The process was extremely painful, and many girls suffered from infections and other foot-related health problems as a result.

Foot binding was a symbol of beauty and status in ancient China because it was believed that small feet were more attractive and feminine. Women with small feet were considered more desirable and were more likely to marry into wealthy families. Thus, foot binding became a way for families to improve their social status and ensure their daughters' future success.

However, foot binding was not just a symbol of beauty and status; it was also a way to control women's behavior. Women with bound feet were less mobile and less able to work outside the home, which made it easier for men to control their movements and keep them in the domestic sphere. Foot binding was thus considered a way to create obedient and submissive wives.

Despite its harmful effects, foot binding continued for over a thousand years in China, until it was finally banned in the early 20th century. The ban was part of a wider effort to modernize China and eliminate harmful traditional practices. Today, foot binding is no longer practiced in China, but the legacy of this harmful practice lives on. Many elderly women in China still suffer from the consequences of foot binding, including chronic pain and disability.

In conclusion, foot binding was a harmful practice that persisted in China for over a thousand years. It was a symbol of beauty and status, but it was also a way to control women's behavior and keep them in the domestic sphere. Today, foot binding is no longer practiced in China, but its legacy continues to affect many elderly women who suffered from this painful and damaging practice. It serves as a reminder of the harmful effects of traditional practices that prioritize beauty and status over health and well-being.

14. Explain 400 words what is accountant management, ​


Answer:

The process of creating organization goals by identifying, measuring, analyzing, interpreting and communicating information to managers is call management or managerial accounting.

Management accounting focuses on all accounting aimed at informing management about operational business metrics. It uses information relating to costs of products or services purchased by the company. Budgets are often used to quantify the decisions made in operational planning. Management accountants use performance reports to note variances between actual results from budgets.

The main difference between management accounting and financial accounting is financial accounting is the collection of accounting data to create financial statements, while management accounting is the internal processing used to account for business transactions.

How Poverty is Measured?

For measuring poverty United nations have devised two measures of poverty – Absolute & relative poverty. Absolute poverty is used to measure poverty in developing countries like India. Relative poverty is used to measure poverty in developed countries like the USA. In absolute poverty, a line based on the minimum level of income has been created & is called a poverty line. If per day income of a family is below this level, then it is poor or below the poverty line. If per day income of a family is above this level, then it is non-poor or above the poverty line. In India, the new poverty line is Rs 32 in rural areas and Rs 47 in urban areas.

There are various causes of poverty but the most important is population. Rising population is putting the burden on the resources & budget of countries. The other causes are- lack of education, war, natural disaster, lack of employment, lack of infrastructure, political instability, etc. For instance- lack of employment opportunities makes a person jobless & he is not able to earn enough to fulfill the basic necessities of his family & becomes poor.
