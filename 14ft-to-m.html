18. if a guy wire for a tree is 14ft long,making 45 degrees angle with tge ground how far is the base of the tree from the strake anchoring the wire? ​


TRIGONOMETRY==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Problem:}} [/tex] If a guy wire for a tree is 14ft long,making 45 degrees angle with the ground how far is the base of the tree from the strake anchoring the wire?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Solution:}} [/tex]

» Identify the sides of the right triangle by the referred angle of elevation:

• The guy wire is the hypotenuse, the distance between the tree and the strake anchoring the wire is the adjacent, and the trunk of the tree is its opposite. In this case where the given is hypotenuse and finding the adjacent, we will be using the cosine ratio.

» Solve:

[tex] \sf cos \: \theta = \frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse} [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: \theta = \frac{the \: ground}{the \: guy \: wire} [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: 45° = \frac{x}{14 \: ft} [/tex]

[tex] \sf x = 14 \:ft(cos \: 45°)[/tex]

[tex] \sf x = 9.9 \: ft [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \boxed{ \tt \purple{9.9 \: ft}}[/tex]
