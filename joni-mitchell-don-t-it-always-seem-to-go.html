The difference between the total path taken by Joni and the line connecting the starting and last points of the path is the displacement. The displacement is the shortest distance between the starting and ending points of Joni's path, while the total path taken includes any extra distance traveled due to changes in direction.

The distance covered by Joni can be found by adding up the lengths of the three legs of his path: 300 m + 400 m + 70 m = 770 m. However, his displacement is the distance between his starting and ending points, which can be found using the Pythagorean theorem:

displacement = √[(300 m)^2 + (400 m - 70 m)^2]

= √[(90000 m^2) + (96100 m^2)]

= √(18610000 m^2)

= 431.07 m (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, Joni covered a distance of 770 m, but his displacement is only 431.07 m.

If Joni goes back to his starting point, he will cover a distance of 770 m again (i.e., he will retrace his original path). However, his displacement will be zero because he will end up back where he started. This is because displacement only takes into account the change in position between the starting and ending points, regardless of the path taken in between.
