4. who are the characters in the soul of the great bell .... and what is the role


The Soul of the Great Bell

Answer:

The Soul of the Great Bell is a story about a young maiden who sacrificed her soul for the Great Bell. The Son of Heaven wanted to have a bell that was magnificent and can make a sound that will be heard in all of Pe-King City. He ordered a worthy official to take care of the preparations needed for the Bell to come into reality. Well-known Bell molders  were gathered and made plans, they intended to mix gold, brass and silver but to their dismay it didn’t.  

Characters in the Story:

Celestial August- Son of Heaven Kouan-Yu- Father of Ko-Ngai and the worthy official Ko-Ngai-the only daughter of the worthy official Ko-Ngai’s Lady in waiting- beside her when she jumped

Roles of each character:

Celestial August- was the one who ordered to make the bell and the one who threatened the worthy official for delaying the Great Bell. He served as the Antagonist or the opposing side of the story.  Kouan-Yu – the worthy official that was only doing his duty but almost caused him his own life. He has a role of a supporting character or one of the protagonist of the story. Ko-Ngai- the young maiden who sacrificed her own life in exchange of her father’s freedom and life. The main Protagonist of story. Ko-Ngai’s Lady in Waiting- is a supporting character, who managed to capture only one shoe while trying to stop Ko-Ngai from jumping in the cauldron

All Souls' Day, also known as the Commemoration of All the Faithful Departed and the Day of the Dead, is a day of prayer and remembrance for the souls of those who have died, which is observed by Latin Catholics and other Christian denominations annually on November 2. All Souls' Day is often celebrated in Western Christianity; Saturday of Souls is a related tradition more frequently observed in Eastern Christianity. Practitioners of All Souls' Day traditions often remember deceased loved ones in various ways on the day.[2][3] Beliefs and practices associated with All Souls' Day vary widely among Christian denominations.

The annual celebration is the last day of Allhallowtide, after All Saints' Day and its eve.[4] Prior to the standardization of Catholic observance on 2 November by St. Odlio of Cluny during the 10th century, many Catholic congregations celebrated All Souls Day on various dates during the Easter season as it is still observed in some Eastern Orthodox Churches and associated Eastern Catholic Churches.
