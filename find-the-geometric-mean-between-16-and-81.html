12. insert 3 geometric means between 16 and 81


Answer:

The three geometric means between 16 and 81 are 24, 36, 54

Step-by-step explanation:

Inserting 3 geometric means between 16 and 81, that is

16, __, __, __, 81

Step 1: Find the common ration (r) using the formula An = A1*r∧(n-1)

An = last term             r = common ration

A1 = 1st term               n = number of terms

Given:

An = 81       r = ?

A1 = 16       n = 5

Solution:

An = A1 * r ∧ (n-1)        Substitute the given

81   = 16 * r ∧ (5-1)       Simplify 5-1

81  = 16 * r∧4           Divide both sides by 16

16           16

81/16 = r ∧ 4                (Read as 81/16 equals r raised to the power of 4)

4√(81/16) = 4√(r∧4)     To solve for r, get the 4th root of both sides, 4√

Fourth root of 81 is 3, and fourth root of 16 is 2, therefore the common ration is

3/2   = r

Now that the common ratio is solved, multiply the common ratio 3/2 to the first term to get the 2nd term, then multiply the common ration to the 2nd term to get the third and so on, doing that the answers are

16, 24, 36, 54, 81

23. insert 3 geometric means between 16 and 81


Insert 3 geometric means between 16 and 81.

Before we are going to answer the problem we need to define first what is geometric mean?

Geometric mean is a type of mean or average, which indicates the central tendency or typical value of a set of numbers by using the product of their values. The geometric mean is defined as the nth root of the product of n numbers,

How to fine geometric mean?

Their are many ways on how to insert 3 geometric means between 16 and 81. Actually you can do trial and error, or you can do using the geometric sequence formula. 
               [tex] a_{n}= a_{1} r^{n-1} [/tex]

           where, [tex] a_{n} = [/tex] last term
                       [tex] a_{1} = [/tex] first term
                       [tex]r=[/tex] the common ratio
                       [tex]n=[/tex] number of terms

To know more about geometric sequence visit: https://brainly.ph/question/713116
 
For us to understand better, we need to write the sequence just like below.

                            16, ____, ____, ____, 81 

We noticed that 16 is the first term and 81 is the 5th and last term, because we need to insert 3 geometric means.

From the given geometric sequence formula, we need to substitute the values. 
                        [tex] a_{n}= a_{1} r^{n-1} [/tex]
                        [tex]81 = 16 r^{5-1} [/tex]
                        [tex]81=16 r^{4} [/tex]
                        [tex] \frac{81}{16}= r^{4} [/tex]
                        [tex] r^{4}= \frac{81}{16} [/tex]
                        [tex]r= \sqrt[4]{ \frac{81}{16} } [/tex]
                        [tex]r= \sqrt[4]{ \frac{ 3^{4} }{ 2^{4} } } [/tex]
                        [tex]r= \frac{3}{2} [/tex]

Therefore the common ratio is [tex] \frac{3}{2} [/tex]. To get the 3 geometric means you need to multiply the common ratio to the first term to get the 2nd term, multiply the common ratio to the second term to get the third term, multiply the common ratio to the third term to get the 4th term, and then multiply the common ratio to the 4th term to get the 5th term which is 81. The illustration follows;
                               16, 24, 36, 54, 81

Therefore the three geometric means are 24, 36 and 54.

Their are some examples that needs to insert only 1 geometric mean. Example, what is the geometric mean between 3 and 12? In this case we need to use ratio and proportion.
Let x be the geometric mean;
             [tex] x^{2}=3(12) [/tex]
             [tex] x^{2} = 36[/tex]
             [tex]x= \sqrt{36} [/tex]
             [tex]x=6[/tex]

You will get the geometric mean even without getting the common ratio.
