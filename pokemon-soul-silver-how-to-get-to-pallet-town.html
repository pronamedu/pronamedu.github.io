14. sterling silver is 92.5% pure silver. how many grams of sterling silver must be mixed to a 90% silver alloy to obtain 500 g of a 91% silver alloy? ​


Answer:

200 grams

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x and y be the weights, in grams, of sterling silver and of the 90% alloy to make the 500 grams at 91%.

Hence, x+y=500

The number of grams of pure silver in x plus the number of grams of pure silver in y is equal to the number of grams of pure silver in the 500 grams.

The pure silver is given in percentage forms.

Hence, 92.5% (x) + 90% (y) =91% (500)

Substitute y by 500−x in the last equation to write

92.5% (x) + 90% (500−x) =91% (500)

Simplify and solve

92.5x+45000−90x=45500

x=200 grams.

Hence, 200 grams of Sterling Silver is needed to make the 91% alloy.

So atomic number = number of protons = number of electrons  

Mass Number (A) is the sum of protons and neutrons  

Mass Number (A) = Number of protons + Number of Neutrons  

So that the relationship between atomic numbers and mass numbers can be formulated as follows:  

Atomic Number (Z) = Mass Number (A) - Number of Neutrons  

Number of protons = number of electrons found in the neutral element

Atomic number = 47

protons, electrons and the charge

number of protons : = 47
