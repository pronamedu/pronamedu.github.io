10. A ladder 15 meters long is leaning against a wall. The bottom of the ladder is pulled along the ground, away from the wall, at the rate of 4 cm/second. How fast is its height on the wall decreasing when the foot of the ladder is 12 meters away from the wall?​


Answer:

5.33 m/s

Step-by-step explanation:

We can solve this problem using related rates. Let x be the distance from the wall to the foot of the ladder, and let y be the height of the ladder on the wall. We are given that dx/dt = 4 cm/s (the rate at which the bottom of the ladder is being pulled away from the wall) and we want to find dy/dt (the rate at which the height of the ladder on the wall is decreasing) when x = 12 m.

Using the Pythagorean theorem, we know that:

x^2 + y^2 = 15^2

Differentiating both sides with respect to time, we get:

2x(dx/dt) + 2y(dy/dt) = 0

Simplifying this expression and plugging in the given values, we get:

2(12)(4) + 2y(dy/dt) = 0

Solving for dy/dt, we get:

dy/dt = -96/2y

We can now find the value of y when x = 12 m using the Pythagorean theorem:

12^2 + y^2 = 15^2

y^2 = 15^2 - 12^2

y^2 = 81

y = 9 m

Plugging this value of y into our expression for dy/dt, we get:

dy/dt = -96/2(9) = -5.33 m/s

Therefore, the height of the ladder on the wall is decreasing at a rate of approximately 5.33 m/s when the foot of the ladder is 12 meters away from the wall.

28. A ladder 15 meters long is leaning against a wall. The bottom of the ladder is pulled along the ground, away from the wall, at the rate of 4 cm/second. How fast is its height on the wall decreasing when the foot of the ladder is 12 meters away from the wall?​


Answer:

We can use the related rates formula to solve this problem:

d/dt (height of ladder on wall) = (d/dt distance of foot of ladder from wall) x (height of ladder on wall) / (distance of ladder from wall)

Let's plug in the given values:

distance of foot of ladder from wall = 12 meters = 1200 cm (since we're given the rate in cm/second)

d/dt (distance of foot of ladder from wall) = 4 cm/second

height of ladder on wall = ?

distance of ladder from wall = 15 meters = 1500 cm

We need to find d/dt (height of ladder on wall) when the foot of the ladder is 12 meters away from the wall.

To solve for the height of the ladder on the wall, we can use the Pythagorean theorem:

height^2 + distance^2 = ladder length^2

height^2 + 1500^2 = 15^2

height^2 = 15^2 - 1500^2

height = sqrt(15^2 - 1500^2) ≈ 75 cm

Now we can plug in the values to the related rates formula:

d/dt (height of ladder on wall) = (d/dt distance of foot of ladder from wall) x (height of ladder on wall) / (distance of ladder from wall)

d/dt (height of ladder on wall) = (4 cm/second) x (75 cm) / (1500 cm)

d/dt (height of ladder on wall) = 0.2 cm/second

Therefore, when the foot of the ladder is 12 meters away from the wall, the height of the ladder on the wall is decreasing at a rate of 0.2 cm/second.
