Persuasive - to convince the reader to accept a particular point of view or take a specific action. Argumentative - the purpose of it is always to persuade or persuade a particular audience to understand the other side of the argument in order to support a new belief idea. Structure of the essay Expository - consist of five paragraphs: An introduction, 3 body parts, and a conclusion Persuasive - made of 3 parts: Issue, Side, Argument. Argumentative - consist of an introduction, 2-3 body paragraphs, and a conclusion. Techniques and strategies used Expository - Choose the essay structure, start with an outline, verify pov requirements, focus on clarity, prepare the thesis statement, craft an appealing introduction, write the body parts, use transition between paragraphs, include supporting info and write an effective conclusion. Persuasive - pick a topic you're passionate about, know your audience, hook the reader's attention, research both sides, be empathetic, ask rhetorical questions, emphasize your point and repeat yourself. Argumentative - distinguish argumentation from persuasion, forming an opinion statement, appealing to the audience, connecting with anecdotes, answering objections, and avoiding logical fallacies.
