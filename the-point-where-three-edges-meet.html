3. the point where three edges meet​


The point where three edges meet is called a vertex. In geometry, a vertex is a point where two or more straight lines, rays, or edges intersect. In a three-dimensional figure, such as a cube or a pyramid, each point where three or more edges meet is a vertex. Vertices are important elements in geometry, and they help to define the shape and structure of a figure.
