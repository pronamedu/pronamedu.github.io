12. How many grams of CaCL2 (MM = 110 g/mol) should be dissolved in H2O to prepare a liter of 1.2M solution?


Given:

molarity = 1.2 M

molar mass of solute = 110 g/mol

volume of solution = 1 L

Required:

mass of solute

Solution:

mass of solute = molarity × molar mass of solute × volume of solution

mass of solute = 1.2 M × 110 g/mol × 1 L

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 132 g}}[/tex]

Therefore, 132 g of CaCl₂ should be dissolved in water.

19. if the molar mass of the compound in problem 1 is 110 grams/mole, what's the molecular formula?​


Answer:

To find the empirical formula, you find the moles of each element and then adjust so they are all whole numbers. To find the molecular formula, you need the empirical formula and the molar mass of the substance. This will be clear from the workings that follow.

1. Let us assume we have 100 g of substance.

65.5% C = 65.5 g C. Converted to moles, 65.5 g x 1 mole/12 g = 5.46 moles C

5.5% H = 5.5 g H. Converted to moles, 5.5 g x 1 mole/1 g = 5.5 moles H

29.0% O = 29.0 g O. Converted to moles, 29.0 g x 1 mole/16 g = 1.81 moles O

To get them all to be whole numbers, divide all by the smallest (1.81) to get

1 mole O

3 mole C

3 mole H

Thus, the empirical formula is C3H3O

2. Now to get the molecular formula, we need to determine how many empirical formulae fit into the molecular formula. The molar mass of the empirical formula is 3x12 + 3x1 + 1x16 = 55

Given the molar mass of the compound is 110 g/mole, that means that TWO OF THE EMPIRICAL FORMULA go into the molecular formula, i.e. 110/55 = 2. Thus the molecular formula is twice the empirical or C6H6O2

Explanation:

C6H6O2 is the answer
