11. How many atoms of cobalt are in a 0.39 mole sample of Co(C2H3O2)3?


Hello!

How many atoms of cobalt are in a 0.39 mole sample of Co(C2H3O2)3?

* Knowing that by Avogadro's Law for each mole of a substance we have 6.022 * 10²³ molecules.

1 mol ------------------ 6.022*10²³ molecules  

0.39 mol --------------------------- y molecules  

[tex]\dfrac{1}{0.39} = \dfrac{6.022*10^{23}}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes  

[tex]1*y = 0.39*6.022*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{y = 2.34858*10^{23}\:molecules\:of\:Co(C_2H_3O_2)_3}[/tex]

* We know that an molecule is formed by one cobalt atom, so we have:

1 molecule of Co(C2H3O2)3 --------------------------------- 1 atom of Co

2.34858*10²³ molecules of Co(C2H3O2)3 -------------- y atom of Co

[tex]\dfrac{1}{2.34858*10^{23}} = \dfrac{1}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes  

[tex]1*y = 1*2.34858*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]y = 2.34858*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{y \approx 2.35*10^{23}\:Atoms\:of\:Cobalt}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:  

Approximately 2.35*10²³ atoms of cobalt

24. properties of cobalt


Answer:

Cobalt is a brittle, hard, silver-grey transition metal with magnetic properties similar to those of iron (it is ferromagnetic). It has a high melting point and is hard-wearing even at high temperatures. Its alloys also possess useful properties and so it finds use in high speed steels and cutting tools for instance.

30. what is the significance of cobalt?​


Answer:

Cobalt salts have been used for centuries to produce brilliant blue colours in paint, porcelain, glass, pottery and enamels. Radioactive cobalt-60 is used to treat cancer and, in some countries, to irradiate food to preserve it. Cobalt is an essential trace element, and forms part of the active site of vitamin B12.
