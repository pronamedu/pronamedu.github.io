8. A square field near Valenzuela Town Center has an area of 2000 square feet. To the nearest foot, how much fencing would be needed to enclose the field?


Interesting question!

Now, see the figure.

The yellow region is 2000 sq. feet. Therefore, Area of square is 2000 sq feet.

Remember your formula:

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\blue{SIDE=\sqrt{AREA} }}}[/tex]

Here,

[tex]\to \sf Side=\sqrt{2000}[/tex]

[tex]\to \sf Side=20\sqrt{5} \ feet[/tex]

⊱ ──────  ✯  ────── ⊰

Now, to enclose the field, we need to cover the boundary of the field. In other words, We need to cover the perimeter of the field.

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\blue{Perimeter=4 \big(Side \big)}}}[/tex]

Here,

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter=4 \big(20\sqrt{5} \big)[/tex]

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter= \big(80\sqrt{5} \big) \ feet[/tex]

√5 = 2.2360

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter= \big(80 \times 2.2360 \big) \ feet[/tex]

[tex]\sf \to Perimeter \approx \big(178.88 \big) \ feet[/tex]

Approx value is 178.88 feet.

17. A rectangular plot requires 2000 ft of fencing to enclose it. If one of its dimensions is x (in feet), express its area y (in square feet) as a function of x


Let:

x = one of the dimension of the rectangle

y = area of the rectangle

Solution:

Perimeter = 2L + 2W

2000 = 2L + 2W

2W = 2000 - 2L

2W/2= (2000 - 2L)/2

W = 1,000 - L

• Since Area (y) = Wx, therefore,

y = Wx

y/W = Wx/W

x = y/W

• Substitute the value of "W" to the equation,

x = y/W

x = y/(1,000 - L)

• Express as a function of "x",

f(x) = y/(1000 - L)
