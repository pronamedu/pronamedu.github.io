19. It is the emotional high point of the story. It is also the most exciting part.


There are five elements of a short story: characters, setting, plot, conflict, and resolution. The plot is the series of events that happens in the story, and it can be plotted in a line starting with the exposition, in which the characters and the problems are introduced. Then, the rising action is when the problem intensifies. This is followed by the climax, which is the emotional high point of the story. It is also the most exciting part as it is the turning point. Then the falling action of a story is when the problem is starting to be resolved. Finally, there is the resolution when the story ends with the problem either solved or unsolved.  

The climax serves as a transition in the story towards the conclusion. It is used by writers to follow the basic plot structure of Freytag’s pyramid: exposition, inciting incident, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution, and dénouement.

Here are some examples of climaxes:

In Romeo and Juliet, the climax is the moment when Romeo kills Tybalt because this is when Romeo’s downfall begins. This downfall will lead to his death.  

In the novel Of Mice and Men, the climax is when Lennie kills Curley’s wife. Not only is this the saddest moment in the story, but it is also the action that leads to George making a decision about what to do with Lennie.  

In The Odyssey, the climax is when Odysseus was able to string the bow and stand before the suitors ready to attack. This serves as a transition towards Odysseus being able to regain his kingdom.  

Climax could also be used as a rhetorical device or stylistic device—the most important word or phrase in speech or poem, for instance. William Shakespeare’s “Sonnet 18” the lines “So long as men can breathe or eyes can see,/ So long lives this, and this gives life to thee” served as the climax to emphasize the theme that through art a person’s beauty can be immortal.
