21. If the mass of a sack of rice is double, how will it affect the strength of the gravitational pull between Earth and the sack of rice?


Given the formula of the universal law of gravitation:

Fg = G [(m1m2)/r²]

Where m1 = mass of the earth
m2 =mass of the sack of rice

2(m2) would lead to

Fg = G [(2m1m2)/r²]
Fg = 2G [(m1m2)/r²]


The gravitational pull between the earth and the sack of rice would double if the sack of rice doubles its mass.
