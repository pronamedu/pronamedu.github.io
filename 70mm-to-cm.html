3. The variable length of feathers in a shuttlecock. -variable length from 64mm to 70mm.


Answer:

The variable length of feathers in a shuttlecock is from 64mm to 70mm or 2 ½ inches to 2 ¾ inches.

Explanation:

Shuttlecock

The shuttlecock is one of the most important equipment in playing badminton. It may be made out of duck or goose feathers, synthetic or plastic.

General Design Rule of Shuttlecock

All shuttlecocks shall have 14 to 16 feathers fixed in the base.

The feathers can have a variable length from 64mm to 70mm or 2 ½ inches to 2 ¾ inches.

The feathers shall be fastened firmly with thread or other suitable material.

The tips of the feather shall form a circle with a diameter within a range of 58mm to 68mm.

The shuttle shall weigh from 4.74 to 5.50 grams.

12. The water pump fill the area of 150m² if it takes 2 hours to reach 70mm depth of water find the volume of the water realease with solution​


Answer:

To calculate the volume of water released by the water pump, we can use the formula:

Volume = Area x Depth

We know that the area of the area that the water pump fills is 150m², and the depth of water is 70mm. However, it is important to convert the depth from millimeters to meters, since the area is measured in square meters.

1mm = 0.001m

So, the depth of water in meters is:

70mm x 0.001m = 0.07m

Now we can use the formula to calculate the volume of water released:

Volume = 150m² x 0.07m = 10.5m³

This means that the water pump releases a volume of 10.5 cubic meters of water in 2 hours to reach a depth of 70mm

It's worth mentioning that the time it takes to reach a certain depth of water is not essential in calculating the volume of water released, the main factors are the area and the depth of water.
