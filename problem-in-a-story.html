Title of the story: Beauty and the Beast

Characters: Belle, The Beast, Enchantress, Maurice, Mrs. Potts, Lumière, Cogsworth, and Chip.

Problem in the story: An arrogant young prince and his castle's servants fall under the spell of a wicked enchantress, who turns him into the hideous Beast until he learns to love and be loved in return.

How the problem was solved: The spirited, headstrong village girl Belle enters the Beast's castle after he imprisons her father Maurice. With the help of his enchanted servants, including the matronly Mrs. Potts, Belle begins to draw the cold-hearted Beast out of his isolation.

Lesson learned from the story: The moral of Beauty and the Beast is that true beauty lies in one's character and not one's physical appearance. The story looks at inner beauty through various elements, such as the items each child asks of their father.
