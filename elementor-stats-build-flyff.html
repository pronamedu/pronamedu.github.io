A pie chart is a graphical presentation that uses sectors to show the comparison of the portion of each category with the whole.

The variance of Dan's gain is 2125. Note that the variance is expressed in squared units of the gain (i.e., P^2), so the standard deviation, which is the square root of the variance, is approximately P46.1.

Step-by-step explanation:

The expected value of Dan's gain, denoted by E(X), is given by the formula:

E(X) = Σ(x * P(X = x))

where x is the possible gain or loss on each toss, and P(X = x) is the probability of obtaining that gain or loss.

In this case, Dan can win P50 with probability 1/2 (since the coin is unbiased and each outcome is equally likely), and he can lose P40 with probability 1/2. So we have:

E(X) = (50 * 1/2) + (-40 * 1/2) = 5

So the expected value of Dan's gain is P5.

The variance of Dan's gain, denoted by Var(X), is given by the formula:

Var(X) = Σ[(x - E(X))^2 * P(X = x)]

In this case, we have:

Var(X) = [(50 - 5)^2 * 1/2] + [(-40 - 5)^2 * 1/2] = 2125

28. what are population and sample in Stat?


Population

Population refers to a set of people, animals, objects, measurements, and events involved in a statistical study wherein data are collected.

A population is the entire group that you want to draw conclusions about.

The members of the group selected from the population are known as the Sample.

Sample

A sample is the specific group that you will collect data from.

The size of the sample is always less than the total size of population.
