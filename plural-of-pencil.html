24. Athletics is plural are plural


To answer your question, "athletics is plural"  is the correct sentence if you are asking the correct verb to use in the sentence. It is incorrect to say "athletics are plural" because your subject (athletics) is just one word.

If you are asking whether the word "athletics" is singular or plural here is an explanation:

Athletics is a "non-count" or "uncountable" noun and should always be used with an "s". It involves different sports events such as running, jumping and throwing. In American English, it is called "Track and Field."

Examples:

She is going to attend an athletics meeting next week.Women are now getting more interested in participating in athletics.
