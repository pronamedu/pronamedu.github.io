22. Activity 3: The Floating Paper Clip Trick luissa What you need: bar magnet, stand and clamp, paper clip, thread, iron nail, index. card, plastic ruler, wooden ruler, coin​


Materials needed:

Bar magnet

Stand and clamp

Paper clip

Thread

Iron nail

Index card

Plastic ruler

Wooden ruler

Coin

Instructions:

Set up the stand and clamp so that the bar magnet is hanging from the clamp.

Tie a length of thread around the middle of the paper clip and then tie the other end of the thread to the clamp so that the paper clip is hanging vertically below the magnet.

Rub the bar magnet along the length of the iron nail about 20 times, always in the same direction. This will magnetize the iron nail.

Place the index card flat on a table and put the magnet on top of it so that the magnet is parallel to the table.

Place the magnetized iron nail on top of the magnet, so that it is also parallel to the table.

Place the plastic ruler and the wooden ruler on either side of the magnet and iron nail, so that they are also parallel to the table.

Place the coin on top of the index card, next to the magnet, and slowly move the coin towards the magnet.

As the coin gets closer to the magnet, the paper clip will begin to move towards the magnet as well. The paper clip will eventually start to float in mid-air next to the magnet, held up by the magnetic field.

Once the paper clip is floating, you can move the coin away from the magnet and the paper clip will continue to float there due to the magnetic field.

Note: This trick works because the magnetic field of the magnet and the magnetized iron nail creates a force that opposes the force of gravity on the paper clip, causing it to float. The plastic ruler and wooden ruler help to prevent the magnet from attracting the paper clip directly, while the coin helps to create a small gap between the magnet and the paper clip.
