Answer:

In Chinese mythology, Chang'e (嫦娥) is best known for stealing an elixir of immortality from her husband, the legendary archer Hou Yi (后羿), and escaping to become the goddess of the moon.

Explanation:

She either accidentally or purposely

swallowed the pill. This angered King Hou Yi, who went after his wife. Trying to flee, she jumped out the window of a chamber at the top of palace -- and, instead of falling, she floated into the sky toward the moon.

3. Goddess of the moon?


Many cultures, civilizations and religions have different deities, god or goddess, associated with or symbolizing the moon. However, the most known goddesses of the moon are from Greek and Roman mythology, as follows: 


a) Selene is the goddess of the moon based from Greek mythology. She is the dauther of Titan gods Hyperion and Theia, and sister of the sun-god Helios and Eos, who is the goddess of the dawn. She is often depicted as a goddess  who drives the moon chariot across the heavens, in attribution to how the moon passes across the sky from dawn to dusk. 

b) Luna is the Roman equivalent of the goddess of the moon. She is often presented in Roman art and literature as the female complement of the Sun (Sol).

18. sumeria's moon goddess


Answer:

Godly ancestry was important in Roman religion. According to Hesiod’s Theogony, Luna is said to be the daughter of Hyperion and Theia. Hyperion was the Titan god of heavenly light and one of the twelve Titan children of Gaia (Earth) and Uranus (Sky). Theia was his sister - a goddess of sight who endowed gold, silver and gems with their brilliance and intrinsic value. The Titans were the oldest gods and preceded the next generation, the Roman gods of Olympus. Luna was the sister of Sol (the male sun god) and of Aurora (the female goddess of dawn). She was one of the consorts of Jupiter, the god of sky, thunder and king of the Roman gods; their offspring included Pardeia, Herse and Nemeia.
