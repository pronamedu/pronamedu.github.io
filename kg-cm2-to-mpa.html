3. A steam turbine receives 5,000 kg/hr of steam at 5 mpa and 4000c and velocity of 25 m/sec. it leaves the turbine at 0.006 mpa and 85% quality and velocity of 20 m/sec. radiation is 10,000 kj/hr. find the kw developed.


To solve the problem, we need to use the First Law of Thermodynamics which states that the change in internal energy of a system is equal to the heat added to the system minus the work done by the system. Assuming steady state conditions and neglecting kinetic and potential energy changes, the energy balance for the steam turbine can be written as:

m_dot * (h_in - h_out) = W - Q

where m_dot is the mass flow rate of steam, h_in and h_out are the specific enthalpies at the inlet and outlet of the turbine, W is the work done by the turbine, and Q is the heat lost by radiation.

Using steam tables, we can find the specific enthalpies:

h_in = 3587 kJ/kg

h_out = 2440 kJ/kg

Substituting the given values, we get:

5000 kg/hr * (3587 - 2440) kJ/kg = W - 10000 kJ/hr

Simplifying and converting units, we get:

W = 7387 kW

Therefore, the power developed by the steam turbine is 7387 kW.

26. 1. A gas having a volume of 40 liters has a pressure of 0.24 MPa at 24°C. If the gas constant R = 212J/kg-K, calculate the mass of gas.2. What is the snecific gravity of​


Formulas:

Ideal gas law:

[tex]\boxed{PV=nRT}[/tex]

where

P = pressure

V = volume

n = number of moles

R = ideal gas constant

T = temperature

Depending on the units given, the formula could be

[tex]\boxed{PV=mRT}[/tex]

where

m = mass

density:

[tex]\boxed{\rho=\dfrac{m}{V}}[/tex]

ρ = density

m = mass

V = volume

specific gravity:

[tex]\boxed{\text{sp gr}=\dfrac{\rho_{\text{substance}}}{\rho_{\text{standard}}}}[/tex]

ρ(substance) = density of the substance

ρ(standard) = density of the standard (usually water is used having the density of about 1000 kg/m³ at 4°C)

Solution:

Given

[tex]V = 40\text{ L} = 0.040\text{ m}^3\\P = 0.24\times10^6\text{ Pa}\\T = 24^\circ\text{C}=297.15\text{ K}\\R = 212\text{ J/kg-K}[/tex]

Solving for the mass, we get

[tex]m=\dfrac{PV}{RT}=\dfrac{(0.24\times10^6\text{ Pa})(0.040\text{ m}^3)}{(212\text{ J/kg-K})(297.15\text{ K})} \\\\\boxed{m =0.1524\text{ kg}}[/tex]

Solving for the density, we get

[tex]\rho=\dfrac{m}{V}=\dfrac{0.1524\text{ kg}}{0.040\text{ m}^3}=3.81\text{ kg/m}^3[/tex]

Solving for the specific gravity, we get

[tex]\text{sp gr}=\dfrac{3.81\text{ kg/m}^3}{1000\text{ kg/m}^3}\\\boxed{\text{sp gr}=0.00381}[/tex]

What is Area of a Rectangle?

The area of a shape is the space occupied by the . In the given , the shaded space is the area of the rectangle.

area of the rectangle

Finding the area of a rectangular wall helps us in finding the amount of color required to paint it. Likewise, we can calculate the space occupied by a rectangular lawn in a park by finding the area of the rectangle. We measure the area in square units.

Learn with the Complete K-5 Math Learning Program

By finding the number of unit squares that are required to fill a shape, we can calculate its area. Since each unit square occupies one square unit space, so the total number of unit squares in the shape gives its area.

Consider a rectangle of length 6 in. and width 3 in. It can be filled with 3 rows and 6 columns of unit squares.

find the number of unit squares

The rectangle can be shown on top of a 10x10 grid with shaded inside the rectangle.

Each of these squares has an area of 1 square inch, and in the rectangle, there are 18 such squares. So, the area of the rectangle is 18 square inches. In other words, the area of a rectangle is the product of its length and width. That is, A = l x w where l is the length and w is the width of the rectangle.

For example, the area of a rectangle of length 35 m and width 25 m is 35 times 25 or 875 square meters.

example of area of the rectangle

A rectangular room has a length of 16 feet and a width of 18 feet. How much carpet is required to cover the entire room?

example of area of the rectangle 1

The area of the carpet is equal to the area of the room. We can find the area of the room by multiplying its length by width. That is, 16 ft times 18 ft or 288 square feet. Therefore, to cover the room, 288 square feet of carpet is required.

Fun Facts

The square is a special rectangle where length and width are the same. Hence the area of a square is given by multiplying the length of each side by itself.
