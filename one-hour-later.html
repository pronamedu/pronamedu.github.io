9. 3. A biologist is researching a newly- discovered species of bacteria. At time t = 0 hours, he puts one hundred bacteria into what he has determined to be a favorable growth medium. Four hours later, he found out that the total bacteria is 400. Give an exponential model for the situation. How many bacteria 12 hours?​


Answer:

An exponential model for the situation can be represented by the equation:

y = 100e^kt

where y is the number of bacteria at time t, 100 is the initial number of bacteria at t = 0, and k is the growth rate constant.

To find the value of k, we can use the information given:

At t = 4 hours, there are 400 bacteria:

400 = 100e^(4k)

Solving for k, we get:

k = ln(4) / 4

Using this value of k, we can find the number of bacteria at t = 12 hours:

y = 100e^(12k)

y = 100e^(12ln(4) / 4)

y = 100 * 4^3

y = 6400 bacteria

So there will be 6400 bacteria after 12 hours

The reason behind this is that the soft drinks in cooler A were kept in a refrigerator for 8 hours while the soft drinks in cooler B were kept at room temperature for 8 hours.

When both groups of soft drinks were added to different coolers with the same 1.5 kg of ice, thermal equilibrium was attained in both cases.

11. Two cars start out at the same point. One car starts out driving north at 30 mph. Two hours later the second car starts driving east at 25 mph. How long after the first car starts traveling does it take for the two cars to be 300 miles apart?​


To solve this problem, we can use the Pythagorean theorem, which states that for a right triangle, the sum of the squares of the lengths of the legs (the sides that meet at the right angle) is equal to the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle).

Let's consider the first car's journey. After traveling for t hours, it will have covered a distance of:

d1 = 30t

Now let's consider the second car's journey. It starts two hours later than the first car, so after t hours of driving, it will have covered a distance of:

d2 = 25(t - 2)

Notice that the second car starts driving two hours later, so we subtract 2 from t to account for the time difference.

The two cars will be 300 miles apart when the distance between them equals the length of the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs d1 and d2. Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can write:

d1^2 + d2^2 = (300)^2

Substituting the expressions for d1 and d2, we get:

(30t)^2 + [25(t - 2)]^2 = 90000

Simplifying and collecting like terms, we get:

225t^2 - 1200t + 1400 = 0

We can solve this quadratic equation using the quadratic formula:

t = [1200 ± sqrt((1200)^2 - 4(225)(1400))] / (2 * 225)

Simplifying, we get:

t = [1200 ± 300] / 450

t = 4 or t = 8/3

We can discard the negative solution, so the time it takes for the two cars to be 300 miles apart is:

t = 8/3 hours, or 2 hours and 40 minutes after the first car starts traveling.

15. Mr. Derbyshire makes a business trip from his house to Loganville in 2 hours. One hour later, he returns home in traffic at a rate 20 mph less than his rate going. If Mr. Derbyshire is gone a total of 6 hours, how fast did he travel on each leg of the trip?


Answer:

Answer: \boxed{80,60}.

Step-by-step explanation:

If Mr. Derbyshire is gone a total of 6 hours and he spends 2 hours going to Loganville, then he spends 6-2=<<6-2=4>>4 hours returning home.

Since he traveled 20 mph less on the return trip than he did on the trip to Loganville, let X be the speed he traveled to Loganville and X-20 be the speed he traveled on the return trip.

The total distance traveled to Loganville is X2=<<X2=2X>>2X miles.

The total distance traveled on the return trip is (X-20)*4=(4X-80) miles.

The total distance traveled on both legs of the trip is 2X+(4X-80)=6X-80=480 miles.

Solving for X, we find that X=80, so Mr. Derbyshire traveled at 80 mph on the trip to Loganville and at 60 mph on the return trip
