10. Suppose a ball is thrown downward from a tower within an initial velocity of 3.00 m/s. Determine the position and velocity of the ball after 1.00 s, 3.00 s, 5. 00 s.​


Explanation:

Assuming that the acceleration due to gravity is -9.81 m/s², we can use the following kinematic equations to determine the position and velocity of the ball:

Position (displacement):

Δy = vi*t + (1/2)*a*t²

Velocity:

vf = vi + a*t

where:

Δy = change in position (m)

vi = initial velocity (m/s)

vf = final velocity (m/s)

a = acceleration due to gravity (-9.81 m/s²)

t = time (s)

Using the given initial velocity of 3.00 m/s, we can solve for the position and velocity of the ball after 1.00 s, 3.00 s, and 5.00 s:

After 1.00 s:

Δy = (3.00 m/s)(1.00 s) + (1/2)(-9.81 m/s²)(1.00 s)² = -2.91 m (negative value means the ball is below the starting point)

vf = 3.00 m/s + (-9.81 m/s²)(1.00 s) = -6.81 m/s (negative value means the ball is moving downward)

After 3.00 s:

Δy = (3.00 m/s)(3.00 s) + (1/2)(-9.81 m/s²)(3.00 s)² = -44.1 m (negative value means the ball is below the starting point)

vf = 3.00 m/s + (-9.81 m/s²)(3.00 s) = -26.43 m/s (negative value means the ball is moving downward)

After 5.00 s:

Δy = (3.00 m/s)(5.00 s) + (1/2)(-9.81 m/s²)(5.00 s)² = -122.6 m (negative value means the ball is below the starting point)

vf = 3.00 m/s + (-9.81 m/s²)(5.00 s) = -48.05 m/s (negative value means the ball is moving downward)

Therefore, after 1.00 s, the ball is 2.91 m below the starting point and moving downward at 6.81 m/s. After 3.00 s, the ball is 44.1 m below the starting point and moving downward at 26.43 m/s. After 5.00 s, the ball is 122.6 m below the starting point and moving downward at 48.05 m/s.

17. if the ball is thrown with an initial velocity of 50 m/s what is the final velocity of the ball at a point at the same level as when it was thrown pa help po​


Answer:

Since it doesn’t really matter what the weight of the ball is, we can treat this as a kinematics problem.

For constant acceleration, we have a=(v-vo) /t, so that t= (v-vo) /a where vo is initial velocity.

So the time to peak is 0-(-50)/9.8 m/s^2 = 5.1 seconds, because v, the final velocity at peak, is 0.

Now, of course the trip is not over, and there needs to be whatever went up to come down. Is that the same time span? It would seem logical.

Prove it by the distance formula, p= 1/2 a*t^2+vo* t+po, where po is initial position=0.

Using t=5.1seconds, p=po+t*(v-vo) /2 , so p=t*(50/2)= 127.5 meters.

Now the distance back down is the same 127.5 meters. How long will it take to fall? Now the initial velocity, at the peak, is zero.

The acceleration is 9.8 m/s^2, and the distance is 127.5 m. So 127.5 =½at^2 = ½ 9.8 * t^2.

t=√(255/9.8)=5.1 seconds. So the total trip is 2×5.1=10.2 seconds reaching a height of 127.5 meters.

What goes up comes back down in the same time interval.

25. The ball is thrown downward at an initial speed of 21. 5m/s from the edge of the building. A) find the speed and velocity after 10s. B) how for did it travel and it's displacement? *With solution po sana*​​


Answer:

We can solve this problem using the kinematic equations of motion for constant acceleration. We assume that the acceleration due to gravity is constant at -9.8 m/s^2.

A) Finding the speed and velocity after 10s:

We can use the following equation to find the velocity of the ball after 10 seconds:

v = u + at

where:

v = final velocity

u = initial velocity

a = acceleration

t = time

Plugging in the values, we get:

v = 21.5 m/s - 9.8 m/s^2 × 10 s

v = -76.5 m/s

The negative sign indicates that the velocity is now in the opposite direction to the initial velocity, which means the ball is moving upwards.

To find the speed, we take the magnitude of the velocity:

speed = |v| = 76.5 m/s

B) Finding the distance traveled and the displacement:

To find the distance traveled by the ball, we can use the following equation:

s = ut + 1/2 at^2

where:

s = distance traveled

u = initial velocity

a = acceleration

t = time

Plugging in the values, we get:

s = 21.5 m/s × 10 s + 1/2 × (-9.8 m/s^2) × (10 s)^2

s = 107.5 m - 490 m

s = -382.5 m

The negative sign indicates that the distance traveled is downwards.

To find the displacement, we need to consider the direction of motion. The displacement is the change in position from the starting point to the ending point. Since the ball was thrown downwards from the building, its displacement after 10 seconds is the vertical distance between the edge of the building and the ball's final position.

The vertical distance traveled by the ball can be calculated using the following equation:

y = ut + 1/2 at^2

where:

y = vertical distance traveled

u = initial velocity in the vertical direction (which is zero)

a = acceleration in the vertical direction (which is -9.8 m/s^2)

t = time

Plugging in the values, we get:

y = 1/2 × (-9.8 m/s^2) × (10 s)^2

y = -490 m

The negative sign indicates that the ball is 490 meters below the edge of the building.

Therefore, the displacement is equal to the negative of the vertical distance traveled:

displacement = -(-490 m) = 490 m

The displacement is positive because the final position is below the starting point.


26. A ball is thrown up in the air with an initial velocity of 40 m/s. What is the ball's velocity after 2 seconds?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

The sign of g is negative because its direction is downward.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & v_0 = \text{40 m/s} \\ & g = -\text{9.8 m/s}^2 \\ & t = \text{2 s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final velocity.

[tex]\begin{aligned} v & = v_0 + gt \\ & = \text{40 m/s} + (-\text{9.8 m/s}^2)(\text{2 s}) \\ & = \text{40 m/s} - (\text{9.8 m/s}^2)(\text{2 s}) \\ & = \boxed{\text{20.4 m/s}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the ball's velocity after 2 seconds is 20.4 m/s.

Problem:

A ball is thrown downwards from the edge of a cliff with an initial velocity of 6 m/s.

a) How fast is it moving after 2 seconds?

Given:

Vi (initial velocity) = 6 m/s

t (time)  =  2 seconds

a (acceleration) = 9.8 m/s²

Vf (final velocity) = ? unknown

Formula for accleration is, a = Vf-Vi/t

to solve for Vf, we can use Vf = at + Vi

Vf = at + Vi

   = 9.8 m/s² × 2 s   +  6 m/s

   = 19.6 m/s   +  6 m/s

   = 25.6 m/s

Answer:

Final velocity is  25.6 m/s

t (time)  =  2 seconds

a (acceleration) = 9.8 m/s²

d (distance)  = ? unknown

d = Vit + 1/2at²

  = 6 m/s × 2 s   +   1/2(9.8 m/s²) × (2 s)²
