28. Describe the how the middle eastern cuisine evolves around the years. ​


Answer:

The Middle Eastern cuisine has evolved over the centuries, adapting to local ingredients, flavors, and spices to create dishes that are unique and delicious. Traditional dishes like hummus, tabouleh, and falafel have been around for thousands of years, but they have also been adapted to include a wide range of new ingredients. Lamb, beef, and poultry are still very popular, but modern Middle Eastern cuisine includes ingredients like eggplant, zucchini, and artichokes. Many dishes feature a variety of spices, such as saffron, cardamom, cumin, and turmeric. Regional variations have also emerged, with Persian, Lebanese, and Israeli cuisines featuring distinct flavors. Additionally, the introduction of fusion cuisine has allowed for the integration of flavors from other cultures, such as Indian, Mediterranean, and North African. The result is a vibrant and diverse cuisine that offers something for everyone.
