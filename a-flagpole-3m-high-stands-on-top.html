20. From a second storey school window keith Sees the top of the flagpole at an angle of elevation of 23. He is 24 feet above the ground and 42 feet from the flagpole . How high is the flagpole ?


Hi Johnsenn6370!

Your problem has something to do with trigonometric ratios. Trigonometric ratios are formed by getting the ratio of two sides of a right triangle. Based on a reference angle there will be a.) a hypotenuse (the side in front of the right angle) b.) an opposite side (the side in front of the angle) and c.) an adjacent side (the side other than the opposite side and the hypotenuse).

Furthermore there are six trigonometric ratios: SOH-CAH-TOA and CHO-SHA-CAO. These ratios are sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant and cotangent. With reference angle being denoted as θ the six trigonometric functions are: a.) sinθ = [tex]\frac{opposite side}{hypotenuse} [/tex], b.) cosθ = [tex]\frac{adjacent side}{hypotenuse} [/tex], c.) tanθ = [tex]\frac{opposite side}{adjacent side} [/tex], d.) cscθ = [tex]\frac{hypotenuse}{opposite side} [/tex], e.) secθ = [tex]\frac{hypotenuse}{adjacent side} [/tex] and f.) cotθ = [tex]\frac{adjacent side}{opposite side} [/tex].

As per your assignment, you need to visualize the problem by making illustrations. I'll provide you the illustration of the problem and a series of steps in answering the question: 
1.)  23°, the angle of elevation, becomes your reference angle. The partial height of the flagpole just in front of observer is the opposite side, i.e a part of the length of the flagpole, and the distance of the observer to the flagpole is the adjacent side. Notice that the opposite side is still unknown, so you have to compute for it using tangent ratio tanθ = [tex]\frac{opposite side}{adjacent side} [/tex]: and that will be tan23° = [tex]\frac{x}{42}[/tex]. Simplifying the equation will give you x ≈ 17.83. Remember that this is just a part of the length of the flagpole!
2.) The other remaining length of the flagpole is just the distance of the observer to the ground, which is 24 ft. So the height of the flagpole is x + 24. which is ≈41.83, the final answer.

21. A flagpole is at the top of a building. From a point 420 ft from the base of the building, an engineer finds that the angles of elevation on the top of the building and the top of the flagpole are 18° and 25°, respectively. Calculate the height of the flagpole.

h≈59.38ft

Step-by-step explanation:

The height of the building (x) is:

tan(18°)=x/420

x=420*tan(18°)

x≈136.47 ft

The height of the building plus height of the flagpole is:

tan(25°)=y/420

y=420*tan(25°)

y≈195.8 ft

Hence, the height of the flagpole is:

h=y-x

h=195.85-136.47

h≈59.38ft

24. There is a rope running from the top of a flagpole to a hook in the ground. The flagpole is 6 meters high,and the hook is 8 meters from its base. How long is the rope?​


PROBLEM:

There is a rope running from the top of a flagpole to a hook in the ground. The flagpole is 6 meters high,and the hook is 8 meters from its base. How long is the rope?

GIVEN:

height (shorter leg) = 6 meters

distance (longer leg) = 8 meters

length of the rope (hypotenuse) =?

SOLUTION:

We will use the pythagorean theorem c²=a²+b², where:

c is the hypotenuse, a is the shorter leg, and b is the longer leg

[tex] \large \tt {c}^{2} = {a}^{2} + {b}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt {c}^{2} = {6}^{2} + {8}^{2} \\ \\ \large \tt {c}^{2} = 36 + 64 \\ \\ \large \tt {c}^{2} = 100 \\ \\ \large \tt \sqrt{ {c}^{2} } = \sqrt{100} \\ \\ \large { \boxed{ \tt c = ±10}}[/tex]

Note: We will take the positive value of c since measurements are always positive.

CONCLUSION:

The rope is [tex]\bold\red{\tt{10\:meters}}[/tex] long.
