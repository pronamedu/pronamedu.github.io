7. definition of political aspect of agricultural policy​


Answer:

Agricultural policy describes a set of laws relating to domestic agriculture and imports of foreign agricultural products. Governments usually implement agricultural policies with the goal of achieving a specific outcome in the domestic agricultural product markets.

Agricultural policies use predetermined goals, objectives and pathways set by an individual or government for the purpose of achieving a specified outcome, for the benefit of the individual(s), society and the nations’ economy at large.[1] Agricultural policies take into consideration the primary, secondary and tertiary processes in agricultural production.[1] Outcomes can involve, for example, a guaranteed supply level, price stability, product quality, product selection, land use or employment.

Agriculture has large impacts on climate change, estimated to be contributing 20–25% of global annual emissions as of 2010.[2] Moreover, agriculture is highly vulnerable to the negative impacts of climate change, such as decreases in water access, geophysical processes such as ocean level rise and changing weather, and socioeconomic processes that affect farmers, many of whom are in subsistence economic conditions. In order for global climate change mitigation and adaptation to be effective a wide range of policies need to be implemented to reduce the risk of negative climate change impacts on agriculture[3][4] and greenhouse gas emissions from the agriculture sector.[5][6][7]

29. Writing Activity (Group) Specific Definition: Write a three- paragraph essay explaining the strand and track you are enrolled in. Emphasize the definition of important terms. Strand: TVL Track: Agriculture


Explanation:

The TVL (Technical Vocational Livelihood) strand in Agriculture is a senior high school program designed for students who are interested in pursuing a career in the agricultural industry. This strand provides students with practical skills and knowledge in the areas of crop production, animal husbandry, fisheries, and agribusiness management. The program is intended to prepare students for employment opportunities in agriculture or for further studies in college.

One of the important terms in the TVL (Agriculture) strand is agribusiness. Agribusiness is a term that refers to the business of agricultural production and the marketing of agricultural products. It involves a range of activities, including crop production, animal husbandry, processing, distribution, and marketing. Agribusiness management is an essential component of the TVL (Agriculture) strand, and students are taught how to apply business principles and practices to the agricultural sector.

Another important term in the TVL (Agriculture) strand is sustainable agriculture. Sustainable agriculture is an approach to farming that focuses on long-term environmental and economic sustainability. It involves practices that minimize the use of synthetic inputs, such as fertilizers and pesticides, and promote soil health, biodiversity, and ecosystem services. Students in the TVL (Agriculture) strand learn about sustainable agriculture practices and how to apply them in real-world farming situations.

In conclusion, the TVL (Agriculture) strand is a valuable senior high school program that provides students with practical skills and knowledge in the agricultural industry. It emphasizes the importance of agribusiness management and sustainable agriculture practices, which are essential for the long-term viability of the agricultural sector. By enrolling in this strand, students can prepare themselves for a career in agriculture or further studies in college.
