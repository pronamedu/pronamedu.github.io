2. The sum of the ages of Paul and Judy is 27 years.If Judy's age plus twice Paul's age is 43, what are their ages?​


Answer:

Paul's age is 16, while Judy's is 11.

Step-by-step explanation:

Using substitution, we do the following:

[tex]P +J = 27; 2P+ J =43\\J+P=27; J +2P=43\\J+P=27\\J+P+-P=27-P\\J+P-P=27-P\\J=27-P\\J+2P=43\\(27-P)+2P=43\\27+2P-P=43\\27+2P-1P=43\\27+1P=43\\27+P=43\\27+P-27=43-27\\P=16\\J=27-P\\J=27-16\\J=11[/tex].

Thus, Paul is 16 and Judy is 11.

of Marina’s age. The sum of their ages

is 38.

Let x be Marina’s age

Let _____ be Paul’s age

ANSWERPaul's age is ½x - 7

EXPLANATION

The first statement, says that Paul's age is 7 years younger than Marina's. Hence, we'll just subtract 7 years from Marina's age.

Since Marina's age is equal to x, the expression would be,

x - 7

The next statement says, "half of Marina's age", hence, we need to divide Marina's age, which is x, by 2.

Therefore, the expression would be,

x/2 - 7 or ½x - 7

17. When john was as old as paul is now, the sum if their ages was 51. When paul will be as old as john is now, the sum of thwir ages will be 103. John is older than paul by jow many years?


Explanation:

"When John was as old as Paul is now"

The key thing is to understand the set-up.

Say John is 30, and Paul is 20, the phrase above is the difference in their ages. J-P, or 10 years ago. . And both of them 'were' J-P (or here, 10) years younger. The 30/20 was an example.

J+P-2(J-P)= 51

Next is J-P years from now. So we add.

J+P+2(J-P)= 103

Next, simplify the first eq.

J+P-2J+2P=51

-J+3P = 51

And the second

J+P+2(J-P)= 103

3J-P = 103 (next, triple the first equation to add it)

-3J+9P=153

8P=256

P = 32 and J = 45

13 years ago (J-P), P was 19, J was 32, these add to 51.

In 13 years, P will be 45, J, 58, adding to 103.

You can also see that the way the question is set up, the sum of their ages changes (103-51) years, or by 52. From X years ago to X years hence, we can see that their age difference must be 52/4 or 13. 26 years pass from the point in past to point in future, times the 2 people. Once you see the 13 is true, the set-up can use 13 instead of J-P.

26. Mary’s age is 2 years less than thrice the age of Paul. The sum of Mary and Paul’s ages is 34. How old are they?​


Answer:

Mary is 25 years old, and Paul is 9.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's use M to represent Mary's age and P to represent Paul's age.

With the information provided, we can make two algebraic equations:

M = 3P - 2M + P = 34

Then, substitute the value of B in the 1st equation into the 2nd one.

⇒ M + P = 34 → 3P - 2 + P = 34

                               4P - 2 = 34

                                     4P = 34 + 2

                                     4P = 36

                                       P = 36 ÷ 4

                                       P = 9                         ∴ Paul is 9 years old

Now that we found out that Paul's age is 9, we can easily substitute the value into any of the equations. Let's use the 2nd one as it is easier.

⇒ M + P = 34

   M + 9 = 34

         M = 34 - 9

         M = 25                                                   ∴ Mary is 25 years old
