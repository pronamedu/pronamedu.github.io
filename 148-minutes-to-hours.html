PROBLEM:

A farmyard has pig and chickens. The owner said that his pigs and chickens had a total of 148 legs and 60 heads.

Which system of linear equation can be used to solve the number of pigs and chickens?

A. [tex] \begin{cases} \sf x + y = 148 \\ \sf 2x + 4y = 60 \end{cases} [/tex]B. [tex] \begin{cases} \sf x + y = 60 \\ \sf 2x + 4y = 148 \end{cases} [/tex]C. [tex] \begin{cases} \sf x + y = 60 \\ \sf 4x - 2y = 148 \end{cases} [/tex]D. [tex] \begin{cases} \sf x - y = 60 \\ \sf 4x + 2y = 148 \end{cases} [/tex]

ANSWER:

[tex] \quad {\Large{\boxed{\sf{ B.\:{\green{\begin{cases} \sf x + y = 60 \\ \sf 2x + 4y = 148 \end{cases} }}}}}} [/tex]

CLUE:

His pigs and chickens had a total of 148 legs and 60 heads.

» We know that a pig has four legs and a chicken has two. Both of them has one head.

So one equation to solve for the total number of heads is x + y = 60, where x is the number of chicken heads and y is the number of pig heads.

So the other equation will be 2x + 4y = 148, where x is the number of chicken legs and y is the number of pig legs.

Step-by-step explanation:

One integer A is divisible by another integer B, if after dividing them, A ÷ B, the remainder is zero.

148 is divisible by 8, if there is an integer 'n' such that:

148 = 'n' × 8.
