18. Which fraction is half-way b/n 3/7 and 4/9? a. 7/32 b. 7/16 c. 55/126 d. 8/15


In finding the halfway between two fractions, we will just add the two given fractions and divide it by 2.
[tex] \frac{3}{7}+ \frac{4}{9} [/tex]
In adding two dissimilar fractions, we will first change it to similar fractions.
We will find the Least Common Denominator of the two fractions which is 63
[tex]= \frac{(3)(9)}{63}+ \frac{(4)(7)}{63} [/tex]
[tex]= \frac{27}{63}+ \frac{28}{63} [/tex]
[tex]= \frac{55}{63} [/tex]
We will now divide the sum by 2.
55/63 ÷ 2 = 55/126

Therefore, the halfway between 3/7 and 4/9 is 55/126

21. A 1.00 g sample of 66 32 Ge decays to 0.87 g in 1.9 hr. What is the half-life of 66 32 Ge?


✏️HALF-LIFE OF AN ISOTOPE

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\tt{\huge{\green{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to solve for the half-life of the isotope. First, we need to identify the quantities to be used. These are

[tex]m_{0} =[/tex] original mass of the sample

m = final mass of the sample

t = time of decay

[tex]t_{\frac{1}{2}} =[/tex] half-life

k = rate constant

Second, we need to identify also the given values. These are

[tex]m_{0} =[/tex] 1.00 g

m = 0.87 g

t = 1.9 hr

Third, we need to calculate the rate constant. The rate constant is

[tex]k = -\dfrac{ln(\frac{m}{m_{0}})}{t}[/tex]

[tex]k = -\dfrac{ln(\frac{\text{0.87 g}}{\text{1.00 g}})}{\text{1.9 hr}}[/tex]

k = 0.073 /hr

Finally, we can now solve for the half-life. Therefore, the half-life of Ge-66 is

[tex]t_{\frac{1}{2}} = \dfrac{ln(2)}{k}[/tex]

[tex]t_{\frac{1}{2}} = \dfrac{ln(2)}{0.073 \: \text{/hr}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{t_{\frac{1}{2}} = \text{9.5 hr}}[/tex]
