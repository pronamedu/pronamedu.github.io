12. Select the corrected sentence from the choices below. He was not present when I was loaning games consequently he did not receive any. A. He was not present when I was loaning games; consequently he did not receive any. B. He was not present when I was loaning games; consequently, he did not receive any. C. He was not present when I was loaning games, consequently; he did not receive any. D. He was not present when I was loaning games, consequently, he did not receive any.


Answer:

B. He was not present when I was loaning games ; consequently, he did not receive any.

Explanation:

The use of a semicolon is to separate two closely related independent clauses but remove the pause that would be provided if a period was used instead. It allows a better flow even with the separation.

It is common to put a semicolon before the words: so, hence, then, for example, nevertheless, etc. In the given, it is the word consequently. Moreover, after the words stated beforehand, we separate it with a comma to continue the thought.

Hence, the corrected sentence would be:

B. He was not present when I was loaning games ; consequently, he did not receive any.

29. 1440g of honey were divided among 3 people. I received 5/12 of the total amount and my brother received 3/7 of the remainder. My sister received the rest.1. How many grams of honey did I receive? ( Show your work. )2. How many grams of honey did my sister receive? ( Show your work. )​


Let's break down the problem step by step.

First, we need to find the total amount of honey that my brother and sister received combined. We know that I received 5/12 of the total amount, so my brother and sister received 1 - 5/12 = 7/12 of the total amount.

Next, we know that my brother received 3/7 of the remainder. To find the remainder, we can multiply the total amount by 7/12 (since that's how much my brother and sister received combined) and subtract the amount my brother received. This gives us:

remainder = 1440g * 7/12 - (1440g * 5/12) = 560g

So my brother received 3/7 * 560g = 240g.

Finally, we can find how much my sister received by subtracting the amount my brother received from the remainder:

sister = 560g - 240g = 320g

Therefore, the answers to the questions are:

1. I received 5/12 * 1440g = 600g of honey.

2. My sister received 320g of honey.
