4. Was T-Rex a scavenger or predator? A predator will hunt living animals. A scavenger will look for other sources of food. Predators have eyes on the front of their heads. Scavengers' eyes tend to be more to the side. T-Rex probably did both.​


Answer:

T A T-REX IS BOTH A SCAVENGER AND A PREDATOR

Explanation:

T. rex, like the majority of living predators, is both a predator and a scavenger. Traces show that big tyrannosaurs were capable of foraging for food, but T. rex was also capable of hunting, or at least tried to. Tyrannosaurs have left scars or even teeth in their prey after unsuccessful predation attempts, according to paleontologists. In the Cretaceous, a duck-billed hadrosaur (Edmontosaurus annectens), a medium-sized plant-eating dinosaur, had a bite mark that severed part of its tail. There is evidence of bone regrowth around the T. rex teeth puncture scars, indicating that the animal was able to escape. A hadrosaur vertebra from South Dakota's Hell Creek Formation contains a tyrannosaur tooth preserved within the bone, which has since healed and grown around it as another example of unsuccessful predation.

When dinosaurs roamed North America, T. rex was the most dangerous predator. Paleontologists have learned a lot about T. rex behavior through studying the dinosaur's fossils. We know they possessed the greatest biting force ever by analyzing the strength of their skulls. As with other big predators, the T. rex was also capable of scavenging when the situation called for it.

Scavengers play an important role in the ecosystem by consuming dead animal and plant material.

17. VULTURES??!! Is it a scavenger or not??


Yes, vultures are scavengers.

Vultures are large birds of preys. Birds of prey are types of birds that hunt for live animals for food, or sometimes for game. Some types of vultures will even prey on mid-sized animals such as sheep, goats, and even bigger wild animals.

However, vultures are more known for scavenging and may even be considered as mother nature's best scavenging bird. They are attracted to fresh carcass that have just been killed or just left by other wild animals. This is why vultures are often depicted are circling around dead animals such as zebras, antelopes, elephants, and giraffes.

One must remember, though, that scavengers and predators are not at all bad. They help maintain the balance in the ecosystem and they often weed out the weak animals from the herd. Vultures are especially very helpful since they are known to feed on carcasses leaving the bones clean. This prevents the spread of bacteria or any disease that may develop from decay.

30. Activity No. 4: Scavengers Hunt! Directions: Aside from Music, what other art forms are popular during the Musical Periods in the History of Music. Research to make your own trivia corner.​


The Musical periods in the history of music are Medieval (1150-1400), Renaissance (1400-1600), Baroque (1600-1750), Classical (1750-1820), Romantic (1820-1900), and 20th century and 21st century (1900-current). Other popular art forms during the Musical Periods in the History of Music are sculpture, painting, architecture, and literature.

Medieval period

Various artistic media, including sculpture, illuminated manuscripts, stained glass, tapestries, mosaics, and metalworks, were used to create medieval art. These many forms were used to create a large number of works of art, which afterwards had a higher rate of survival than other formats like fresco wall paintings.

Renaissance period

Renaissance art, literature, architecture, and sculpture were created in Europe throughout the 14th, 15th, and 16th centuries as a result of a combination of factors including a heightened awareness of nature, a resurgence of classical learning, and a more individualized vision of man.

Baroque period

Some of the traits most frequently associated with the Baroque are grandeur, drama, energy, movement, tension, emotional exuberance, and a tendency to conflate differences between the many arts.

Classical period

The foundation of Western civilization, classical art spans the cultures of Greece and Rome. While pursuing ideas of beauty, harmony, and proportion across the centuries, Classical Art made advancements in painting, sculpture, decorative arts, and architecture.

Romantic period

Romantic artists thought that order and reason were only secondary to feelings and the senses in terms of how we experience and comprehend the universe.

Modern period

Modern painters experimented with brand-new modes of perception as well as novel notions regarding the nature of media and the purposes of art.
