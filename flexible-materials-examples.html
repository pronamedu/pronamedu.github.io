Explanation:

Materials have different properties. These properties can be physical or chemical properties. Physical properties are characteristics that can be observed without changing the identity of the materials. Examples of these properties are hardness, transparency, elasticity, solubility, malleability, ductility, and many more. Chemical properties are those characteristics that are observed when the identity of the materials is changed. Examples of chemical properties are oxidation, reactivity, and flammability. These properties are essential to identify the uses of these materials. Like for instance, malleability is the ability of the material to be hammered into sheets. This property can be used in making jewelry, roofing, and other materials that we use.
