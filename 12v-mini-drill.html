29. It is used for boring hole(s) in plastic or metal chassis with the used of drill bits having sizes from 1/6" to approximately 1/4". A. Hammer B. 12v mini hand drill C. Portable electric drill D. Soldering iron​


With drill bits that range in size from 1/6" to about 1/4", a (C) portable electric drill is used to make holes in plastic or metal chassis.

Caution:

To keep a tool from twisting when the torque is high, it takes a lot of force. Before using a drill, make sure the chuck jaw is tight and the drill bit is in place. To do a good job of boring, both the material to be bored and the drill bit must be at a 90-degree angle.

Hammer

A hammer is a tool, usually a hand tool, with a heavy "head" that is attached to a long handle and can be swung to hit a small area of an object. This could be to drive nails into wood, shape metal (like with a forge), or crush rock, for example.

12v Mini Hand Drill

Mini hand drill for DIY, DC12V motor with 10 twist drill bits Pin vise hand drills are great for making holes in plastic, wood, acrylic, pearl, paper, thin aluminum board, PCB circuit plate board, bodhi, walnut, and nut shells that are less than 1/4" thick. The size of the motor shaft is 2.3 mm.

Soldering iron​

Soldering is done by hand with a tool called a soldering iron. It gives off heat to melt the solder so that it can flow between two pieces of work.
