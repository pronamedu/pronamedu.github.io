There are 13 hearts in a deck of cards (one for each face value), and there are 52 cards in total. Therefore, the probability of drawing a heart is:

P(heart) = 13/52 = 1/4

2. Probability of drawing all faces:

There are 12 face cards in a deck of cards (four jacks, four queens, and four kings), and there are 52 cards in total. Therefore, the probability of drawing all faces is:

P(all faces) = (12/52) x (11/51) x (10/50) x (9/49) x (8/48) x (7/47) = 0.000105

Note that we used the multiplication rule of probability here, which states that the probability of two independent events occurring together is the product of their individual probabilities.

3. Probability of drawing a red suit:

There are two red suits in a deck of cards (hearts and diamonds), and each suit contains 26 cards. Therefore, the probability of drawing a red suit is:

P(red suit) = 26/52 = 1/2

4. Probability of drawing all queens:

There are four queens in a deck of cards (one for each suit), and there are 52 cards in total. Therefore, the probability of drawing all queens is:

P(all queens) = (4/52) x (3/51) x (2/50) x (1/49) = 0.000006

Note that we used the multiplication rule of probability again here, since the events are independent.

Hence, n(S)= 4/52 or 1/13

D. a black face card

There are 13 Spades & 13 Clubs which are BLACK, out of 52 cards. (13 +13=26)

Hence, n(S)= 26/52 or 1/2
