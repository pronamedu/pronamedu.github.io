17. It is the weakening or killing of our loving relationship with God. a mortal sin b venial sin c personal sin d original sin


Answer:

a) mortal sin

Explanation:

Mortal sin is typically defined as a grave or serious offense against God that is committed with full knowledge and deliberate intent, resulting in the complete separation of the soul from God's grace. It is often considered to be the most severe form of sin because it destroys the state of sanctifying grace in the soul, which is necessary for maintaining a close and loving relationship with God. Mortal sin is believed to sever the bond of charity between the soul and God, and therefore weakens or kills the loving relationship with God. In many Christian traditions, mortal sin is considered to be a deliberate rejection of God's love and a willful turning away from God, resulting in severe spiritual consequences.

23. Sin weakens the love relationship that we have with God. a personal sin b venial sin c original sin d mortal sin


Answer:

The correct answer is letter A

Explanation:

The statement "Sin weakens the love relationship that we have with God" applies to different types of sin, but the closest match to this statement is option A, "personal sin."

Personal sin is an individual's deliberate choice to act in a way that is contrary to God's will, which weakens the individual's relationship with God. Personal sin can be either venial (a less serious offense) or mortal (a grave offense that completely severs the individual's relationship with God).


24. A condition of disharmony into which all human beings are born. A personal sin B Mortal sin c original sin d venial sin


Answer:

C. Original Sin

Explanation:

The condition of disharmony into which all human beings are born is called original sin. According to Christian theology, original sin is the state of sin inherited by all human beings as a result of the disobedience of Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden.

A personal sin is any deliberate act, word or thought that goes against God's law. It is committed by an individual and may be either a mortal or venial sin, depending on its gravity.

Mortal sin is a grave offense against God that involves a deliberate and complete rejection of his love and grace. Mortal sin cuts off the sinner from God's life and merits eternal punishment in hell.

Venial sin is a less serious offense against God that does not result in the complete separation of the sinner from God's life. However, venial sin still damages the relationship between the sinner and God and requires repentance and forgiveness.
