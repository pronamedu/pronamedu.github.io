Finding the LCM Using Factor Tree

LCM stands for the least common multiple. It is the smallest multiple that the given numbers have in common. Factor tree method is one of the ways used to determine the LCM of the numbers. Factor tree refers to a diagram that determines the prime factors of a number.

Steps to Find the LCM Using Factor Tree

Break down the number until you end up with its prime factors.

List the prime factors of each number.

Multiply each factor with the greatest number of times it occurs in either number.

14. 1) 2x4, 2x7, 2x5 what is the lcm of each number​


Least Common Multiple:

The LCM of 2 × 4, 2 × 7, and 2 × 5 is 280. To get this, simply get the product of 2 × 4 × 7 × 5 whereas, the product of 2 and 4 is 8 and the product of 7 and 5 is 35. 8 times 35 is equals to 280.

Solutions:

List the given numbers.

          2 × 4

          2       × 7

          2            × 5

_______________

LCM:  2 × 4 × 7 × 5 = 280

Therefore, the LCM of 2 × 4, 2 × 7, and 2 × 5 is 280.

Remember:

Least Common Multiple (LCM) refers to the smallest possible number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

Examples:

8 is the LCM of 2, 4, and 832 is the LCM of 16 and 32.
