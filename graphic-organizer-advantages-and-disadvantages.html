In a series circuit, all the components are arranged in a single line. In a parallel circuit, all the components are arranged parallel to each other. If more than one resistor is connected in series, the voltage across each resistor is not the same though the current flow is the same throughout the circuit.

Similarities

Aside from the fact that they are both used to connect electrical components, such as diodes, resistors, switches and so on, together, there are few similarities between serial and parallel circuits. Series circuits are designed so that the current through each component is the same, whereas parallel circuits are designed so that the voltage through each component is the same.

Answer:

DIFFERENCES AND SIMILARITIES OF SERIES AND PARALLEL CIRCUIT:

*DIFFERENCES:

(SERIES CIRCUIT)

•One pathway

•Current same anywhere in the circuit

•Voltage shared in ratio to resistance

•Resistance adds up; draws less current; battery lasts longer

•One bulb fuses; circuit incomplete

•Brightness of bulbs less

(PARALLEL CIRCUIT)

•Two or more pathways

•Current splits-passes through pathways-adds up again

•Voltage across each pathway equals supply of voltage

•Total resistance less than the least resistance; current drawn is less; battery life short

•One bulb fuses; the others still go

•Brightness of bulbs more

*SIMILARITIES

(SERIES AND PARALLEL CIRCUIT)

•Both the circuits, either parallel or series have the same of converting electrical energy to light, heat, sound, etc.

29. Write 5 advantages and disadvantages of graphic organizers in your studies​


Answer:

Comprehension

Graphic organizers can help students to understand the relationship between various pieces of information that they may have otherwise failed to identify. Such organizers are particularly useful in helping students grasp abstract concepts.

Knowledge Evaluation

Asking students to complete graphic organizers allows instructors to evaluate the student's prior knowledge of a subject. As a result, teachers can make strategic decisions regarding how to most effectively present to students additional information relating to the subject at hand.

Impact on Notetaking

In some cases, notetaking decreases when students learn material with the help of graphic representations. As a result, the lack of a comprehensive guide that students can refer to when revising material may affect their performance.

Impact on Feedback

Some instructors find creating an answer key for a graphic organizer to be time-consuming. They use organizers to encourage discussion but do not correct each student's organizer upon completion; as a result, students may miss out on receiving valuable feedback.

What is a theory?

A theory is a logical abstraction of a phenomenon or the inferences drawn from the abstraction. Logical and contemplative thinking are typically associated with techniques like investigation or observational study. A theory is a carefully thought-out explanation for observations of the natural world that has been established through the scientific method and incorporates various facts and assumptions.

What is a scientific theory?

An explanation for an event in the natural world or the cosmos that has undergone significant testing and verification using accepted techniques for observation, measurement, and result evaluation is referred to as a scientific theory.

Examples of Scientific Theory:

The Plate Tectonic TheoryThe Big Bang TheoryThe Theory of GravityThe Theory of Evolution by Natural SelectionThe Modern Atomic TheoryThe Cell Theory

What is a philosophical theory?

The principles of human knowledge, the evolution of science and the foundations of scientific knowledge, the principles of cognition, argumentation, and communication, the nature of language and awareness, metaphysics, and the history of the field itself are all studied in theoretical philosophy.

Examples of Philosophical Theory:

EmpiricismStoicismNihilismAbsurdismExistentialismUtilitarianismRationalismConfucianismDeterminismObjectivism
