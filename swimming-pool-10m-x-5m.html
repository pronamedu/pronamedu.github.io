you have to multiply 10 and 100 and then add there quificient

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula for Area of a rectangle

Area = length × width

Let:

A = Area

l = length

w = width

Given:

Area = 100 m²

length = 10 m longer than its width

length = 10 + w

l × w = Area >substitute given values

(10 + w) × w = 100 m >distribute w

10w + w² = 100 >completing the square

w² + 10w = 100

w² + 10w +(10/2•1)² = 100 +(10/2•1)²

w² + 10w +(5)² = 100 +(5)²

w² + 10w + 25 = 100 + 25

w² + 10w + 25 = 125

(w + 5)² = 125 >take the square root

√(w + 5)² = √125

w + 5 = ±5√5

w + 5 = 5√5

w + 5 - 5 = 5√5 - 5

w = 6.18 m

w + 5 = -5√5

w + 5 - 5 = -5√5 - 5

w = -16.18 m

We cannot have negative dimension, so neglect the negative value of w

l × w = A

(l × 6.18m)/6.18m = 100 m²/6.18 m

l = 100 m²/6.18 m

l = 16.18

Does the length 16.18, 10m longer than the width? Yes. it is.
