How do cracks affect weathering?

Physical weathering acts to widen these rock fractures. Frost wedging happens when water filling a crack freezes and expands (as it freezes, water expands 8 to 11% in volume over liquid water).

How does physical weathering occur?

Physical weathering is caused by the effects of changing temperature on rocks, causing the rock to break apart. The process is sometimes assisted by water. ... Freeze-thaw occurs when water continually seeps into cracks, freezes and expands, eventually breaking the rock apart.

What type of weathering makes cracks?

Mechanical weathering, also called physical weathering and disaggregation, causes rocks to crumble. Water, in either liquid or solid form, is often a key agent of mechanical weathering. For instance, liquid water can seep into cracks and crevices in rock. If temperatures drop low enough, the water will freeze.

Physical Weathering Caused by Water

Option #1

Cracking of an egg is a physical change since the egg and the stuff inside does not change but the shape or appearance of the shell changes.

A physical change does not change the identity of the substance, while a chemical change changes the identity and composition of the substance. It’s an example of a chemical change because the composition of the egg is changing.
