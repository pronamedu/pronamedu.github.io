Why Practice Community Engagement?Advocates of community engagement assert that it improves health promotion and health research. However, the processes, costs, and benefits of community engagement are still a relatively new field of study. In 2004, AHRQ brought attention to the importance of empirical work in this area and greatly advanced our knowledge through a synthesis of the research, much of which indicated that community engagement strengthened the conduct of research (Viswanathan et al., 2004).

Explanation:

The nine areas and the corresponding benefits were as follows:

●Agenda—Engagement changes the choice and focus of projects, how they are initiated, and their potential to obtain funding.

●Design and delivery—Improvements to study design, tools, interventions, representation/participation, data collection and analysis, communication, and dissemination can be implemented.

●Implementation and change—Improvements can be made in the way research findings are used to bring about change (e.g., through new or improved services, policy or funding changes, or transformation of professional practices), and capacity for change and the maintenance of long-term partnerships can be expanded.

●Ethics—Engagement creates opportunities to improve the consent process, identify ethical pitfalls, and create processes for resolving ethical problems when they arise.

●The public involved in the project—The knowledge and skills of the public involved in the project can be enhanced, and their contributions can be recognized (possibly through financial rewards).

●Academic partners—Academic partners can gain enhanced understanding of the issue under study and appreciation of the role and value of community involvement, which sometimes result in direct career benefits.

●Individual research participants—Improvements in the way studies are carried out can make it easier to participate in them and bring benefits to participants.

●Community organizations—These organizations can gain enhanced knowledge, a higher profile in the community。

●The general public—The general public is likely to be more receptive to the research and reap greater benefits from it.

15. explain how does management evolve and how they evolve the aspect?​


Explanation:

Management is the process of leading and directing a group or organization to achieve goals. It evolves by adapting to changes in technology, workforce, and organizational structure. The most prominent aspect that evolves is the management style, which adapts to the current situation.

Explanation:

Management has evolved over time as a way to organize and coordinate resources to achieve goals and objectives. The evolution of management can be broken down into several key stages:

The Classical Management era: This period, which lasted from the late 1800s to the early 1900s, saw the development of the scientific management approach by Frederick Taylor and Henry Fayol. This approach focused on increasing efficiency and productivity through the use of time and motion studies, standardization, and the separation of planning and execution.

The Behavioral Management era: During the 1920s and 1930s, a new approach to management emerged, known as the behavioral approach. This approach focused on the human aspects of management, such as motivation, communication, and leadership, and was developed by researchers such as Elton Mayo and Abraham Maslow.

The Quantitative Management era: In the 1950s and 1960s, management science and operations research techniques, such as mathematical modeling and decision-making tools, were applied to management problems. This era saw the development of quantitative management techniques such as linear programming and decision theory.

The Contingency Management era: In the 1970s and 1980s, the contingency approach to management emerged, which emphasized the need for management to adapt to different situations. This approach recognized that different problems and situations require different management approaches, and that there is no one best way to manage.

The Systematic Management era: In the 1990s and 2000s, management started to adopt a more holistic and systemic approach. This era saw the development of the Total Quality Management (TQM) approach, which emphasized the need for continuous improvement and the participation of all employees in the management process.

Nowadays, management is in a constant evolution, with new technologies, new management styles, and new ways of thinking, it also includes the aspect of sustainability, digitalization, and adaptability to changes in the environment

22. How does Human evolve?​


Over time, genetic change can alter a species' overall way of life, such as what it eats, how it grows, and where it can live.

Human evolution took place as new genetic variations in early ancestor populations favored new abilities to adapt to environmental change and so altered the human way of life.

Answer:

Human evolution is the lengthy process of change by which people originated from apelike ancestors. Scientific evidence shows that the physical and behavioral traits shared by all people originated from apelike ancestors and evolved over a period of approximately six million years.

Explanation:

What causes human to evolve?

Five different forces have influenced human evolution: natural selection, random genetic drift, mutation, population mating structure, and culture. All evolutionary biologists agree on the first three of these forces, although there have been disputes at times about the relative importance of each force.
