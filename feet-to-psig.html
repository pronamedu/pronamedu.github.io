4. A tank is filled with fresh water until there is a depth of 35 ft. Determine the pressure, psig, at the bottom of the tank​


Answer:

The pressure at the bottom of the tank is 15.17 psig

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept of atmospheric pressure to solve the requirement and use psig as its unit of measurement.

Formula to use

P = ρgh

where

P     is the pressure, the given unit should be in psig (pounds per square

      inch, gauge)

ρ      is the density of freshwater

g      is the gravitational constant, 9.8 m/s²

Given information

h = 35 ft = 10.67 meters

ρ = 1000 kg/m³   (density of freshwater)

P = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve the atmospheric pressure using the given formula

P = ρgh

P = (1000 kg/m³)(9.8 m/s²)(10.67 m)

P = 104,566 Pascal (Pa)

Now, let us convert pascal to psi using the conversion 1 Pa = 0.000145038 psig, we have:

P = 15.17 psig

Therefore, the pressure at the bottom of the tank is 15.17 psig

6. A 4.9 ft3 of water are compressed to 5000 psig. What is the volume decrease if the temperature is 60F? Assume the average bulk modulus of elasticity is 311,000 psi.


Answer:

The volume decrease would be 0.024%.

Explanation:

At standard temperature and pressure, water has a density of 1 gram per cubic centimeter. This means that one liter (1000 cubic centimeters) of water weighs 1 kilogram.

If we apply a force to compress water, its density will increase. For example, if we compress 4.9 ft3 of water to 5000 psig, its density will increase to 1.0002 grams per cubic centimeter.

The volume of the water will decrease by 0.024%. This is because the water is now more dense, so it takes up less space.
