19. A time of 10 milliseconds is required for the current on a series RL dc circuit to reach 90% of its final steady-state value. What is the time constant in seconds for the circuit? Answer: 4.34 ms Show solution.


Answer:

The time constant (represented by the symbol τ) of an RL circuit is defined as the product of the resistance (R) and the inductance (L) of the circuit, i.e., τ = L/R.

In this problem, we are given that it takes 10 milliseconds for the current on a series RL dc circuit to reach 90% of its final steady-state value. We can use this information to determine the time constant of the circuit.

Let's use the following formula to calculate the value of time constant:

t = τ * ln(1/0.1)

where t is the time required for the current to reach 90% of its final steady-state value, ln is the natural logarithm function, and 1/0.1 is the ratio of the final steady-state value of the current to the value of the current at time t.

Substituting the given values, we get:

10 ms = τ * ln(1/0.1)

Rearranging this equation to solve for τ, we have:

τ = 10 ms / ln(1/0.1)

Using a calculator to evaluate the natural logarithm and perform the division, we get:

τ ≈ 4.34 ms

Therefore, the time constant of the RL circuit is approximately 4.34 milliseconds.
