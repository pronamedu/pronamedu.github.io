11. Two positive numbers differ by 5 and the square of their sum is 97​


Answer:

4 and 9

Step-by-step explanation:

Two positive numbers differ by 5 and the sum of their square is 97. Find the numbers.?

Let x be one number...

Let y be the other number..

"two positive numbers differ by 5"...so x - y = 5.

"sum of their square is 97"...so x^2 + y^2 = 97...

Therefore we have our two equations and we will solve the system.

x - y = 5

x^2 + y^2 = 97

Isolate one variable in the first equation and then plug in for that variable in the second...so I will isolate x in the first equation by adding y both sides..

x - y + y = 5 + y

x^2 + y^2 = 97

x = 5 + y

x^2 + y^2 = 97

Now substituting 5 + y for x in the second and solving for y...

(5 + y)^2 + y^2 = 97

Use FOIL to expand (5 + y)^2..

(5 + y)(5 + y) + y^2 = 97

(5)(5) + (5)(y) + (y)(5) + (y)(y) + y^2 = 97

25 + 5y + 5y + y^2 + y^2 = 97

Now combine your like terms.

25 + 10y + 2y^2 = 97

Now set equation equal to 0 by subtracting 97 to both sides..to move it over to the other side..

2y^2 + 10y + 25 - 97 = 97 - 97

2y^2 + 10y - 72 = 0

Factor...start by factoring out common factor of 2...

2 (y^2 + 5y - 36) = 0

Now this can be solved by factoring indeed (you could also solve by quadratic formula or completing the square)...look for two factors that multiply to -36 and adds to 5 and set them into (x + a)(x + b)...as those two factors are -4 and 9...so..

2 (y - 4)(y + 9) = 0

By the zero product property...set each factor equal to 0 and solve for y..

y - 4 = 0

y - 4 + 4 = 0 + 4

y = 4

y + 9 = 0

y + 9 - 9 = 0 - 9

y = -9

So y = 4 or y = -9...since it says two *POSITIVE* numbers...we reject y = -9.

So y = 4, now substitute 4 for y in one of the two original equations and solve for x.

x - y = 5

x = 5 + y

x = 5 + 4

x = 9
