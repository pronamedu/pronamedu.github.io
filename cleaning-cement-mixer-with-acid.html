2. A cement mixer can be rented for $100 for three days but every excess day to this will have an additional cost of $45 per day. Represent the cost of renting a cement mixer as a piecewise function of the number of days d it is rented.


PIECEWISE FUNCTION WORD PROBLEM

Answer: We can represent the cost of renting a cement mixer as the piecewise function of the number of days it rented is:

[tex]F(d) = \left \{ {{100, 0<d\leq 3} \atop {100+45(d-3),d>3}} \right.[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

A piecewise function is a function that can be divided into several parts or lines depending on the value of the input or the independent variable. If that input satisfies that specific line of the piecewise function, therefore, we will use it to determine the value of our output.

In this word problem, we have to analyze the given information. So let us represent our function as F(d) and we will represent our independent variable as the variable d which varies as the number of days. Since for the first three days of rent, the cost of the rent is fixed at 100 dollars. So we will represent this as F(d) = 100, 0 < d ≤ 3. This means that from the first day up to the third day of renting, the payment for the rent is still the same.

However, if the duration of the rent exceeded 3 days, for every day exceeded will cost an extra charge of 45 dollars. We can also represent this as: F(d) = 100 + 45(d-3), d > 3.

But remember that these functions are still part of a piecewise so we can merge them to get:

[tex]F(d) = \left \{ {{100, 0<d\leq 3} \atop {100+45(d-3),d>3}} \right.[/tex]

Let us take this scenario as an example. Assuming that the person who rented the cement mixer returned it after 5 days. So it exceeded the 3-day limit for it to cost 100 dollars only. This means that an additional charge will be added the total cost of rent. Using the function we constructed earlier, we will use the second line since the number of days is 5 which is greater than 3 so:

F(d) = 100 + 45(d-3)

F(d) = 100 + 45(5-3)

F(d) = 100 + 45(2)

F(d) = 100 + 90

F(d) = 190

Therefore, the person who rented the cement mixer will pay 190 dollars after 5 days.

29. perform the steps on how to properly clean the mixer​


Answer:

INSTRUCTIONS

1.Unplug the appliance. Always unplug the appliance before cleaning.

2.Hand-wash attachments. Wash beaters, hooks, and pouring shield in warm, soapy water. Always dry attachments completely before storing or use.

3.Clean mixing bowl. Wash mixing bowl in hot, soapy water or add it to a dishwasher load. If your mixer has a permanent home on the countertop, take the extra time to apply stainless steel cleaner to the bowl to remove water spots or blemishes.

4.Remove spills, splatters, and food residue. After each use, wipe down the stand with a damp cloth or mild cleaner. Be careful not to leak liquids into ventilation ports or openings around knobs and levers. Pay special attention to areas near the motor head, including speed control knob and 5.locking lever — ingredients collect here when used regularly.

6.Clean tough areas with baking soda. 7.Using a small brush and a water-baking soda mixture, scrub grime or food residue, paying attention to particularly stubborn areas like the speed control strip or bowl clamp plate.

8.Wipe inside attachment hub. Unscrew the attachment hub plate and wipe interior.

9.Get under the motor head. To clean areas under the motor head (including the beater shaft), tilt the motor head back and lock into place.

10.Clean the beater shaft thoroughly. The shaft and clip surrounding the shaft both gather a lot of fine ingredients and need to be cleaned well. Use a paper towel or small brush to remove flour and other ingredients.

Pay attention to build-up. The rim on the back of the motor head casing collects dust and grime that may go unnoticed. The lipped area underneath the motor head is a prime spot for splatters and food residue, as is the rim of the bowl clamping plate and any exposed screws. Use a Q-tip or small brush to remove grime and residue.

Remove crumbs and dust from the ventilation shaft. Use a toothpick, specialty brush, or vacuum to remove crumbs and dust from ventilation shaft. This area should always be clear to provide the best performance of your mixer.

11.Dust and clean underneath. Lay mixer on its side to wipe down the underside of appliance. Use a small brush or paper towel and mild cleanser to clean appliance foot pads.
