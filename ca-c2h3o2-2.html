8. How many atoms of cobalt are in a 0.39 mole sample of Co(C2H3O2)3?


Hello!

How many atoms of cobalt are in a 0.39 mole sample of Co(C2H3O2)3?

* Knowing that by Avogadro's Law for each mole of a substance we have 6.022 * 10²³ molecules.

1 mol ------------------ 6.022*10²³ molecules  

0.39 mol --------------------------- y molecules  

[tex]\dfrac{1}{0.39} = \dfrac{6.022*10^{23}}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes  

[tex]1*y = 0.39*6.022*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{y = 2.34858*10^{23}\:molecules\:of\:Co(C_2H_3O_2)_3}[/tex]

* We know that an molecule is formed by one cobalt atom, so we have:

1 molecule of Co(C2H3O2)3 --------------------------------- 1 atom of Co

2.34858*10²³ molecules of Co(C2H3O2)3 -------------- y atom of Co

[tex]\dfrac{1}{2.34858*10^{23}} = \dfrac{1}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes  

[tex]1*y = 1*2.34858*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]y = 2.34858*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{y \approx 2.35*10^{23}\:Atoms\:of\:Cobalt}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:  

Approximately 2.35*10²³ atoms of cobalt

Hello!

How much calcium is present in the amount of Ca(NO3)2 that contains 20 g of nitrogen?  

(a) 36.0 g Ca  

(b) 48.5 g Ca  

(c) 28.6 g Ca  

(d) 32.4 g Ca

[ First step ]  Let's find the number of moles of Nitrogen, let's see:

[tex]n_N\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:nitrogen) =\:?[/tex]

[tex]m_N\:(mass\:of\:the\:nitrogen) = 20\:g[/tex]

[tex]MM_N\:(nitrogen\:molar\:mass) = 14\:g/mol[/tex]

[tex]n_N = \dfrac{m_N}{MM_N}[/tex]

[tex]n_N = \dfrac{20\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{14\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{n_N \approx 1.429\:mol}[/tex]

[ Second step ] Let's find the number of mols of Calcium, let's see:

[tex]n_Ca\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:Calcium) =\:?[/tex]

[tex]n_N\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:nitrogen) =1.429\:mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{Ca} = \dfrac{n_N}{2}[/tex]

[tex]n_{Ca} = \dfrac{1.429}{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{n_{Ca} = 0.7145\:mol}[/tex]

[Step Three] Let's find the mass of Calcium in grams, let's see:

[tex]m_{Ca}\:(mass\:of\:the\:Calcium) =\:?\:(en:g)[/tex]

[tex]n_Ca\:(number\:of\:mol\:of\:Calcium) = 0.7145\:mol[/tex]

[tex]MM_{Ca}\:(Calcium\:molar\:mass) = 40\:g/mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{Ca} = \dfrac{m_{Ca}}{MM_{Ca}}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]m_{Ca} = n_{Ca}*MM_{Ca}[/tex]

[tex]m_{Ca} = 0.7145\:mol\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\dfrac{\hspace{0.6cm}}{~}*40\:g/mol\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\dfrac{\hspace{0.6cm}}{~}[/tex]

[tex]m_{Ca} = 28.58\to \boxed{\boxed{m_{Ca} \approx 28.6\:g}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

(c) 28.6 g Ca

The volume of CO₂ = 29.975 L

Further explanation

Given

T=77 F=25 C = 298 K

P = 765 mmHg= 1.00658 atm

mass Ca(HCO₃)₂ = 0.1 kg = 100 g

Reaction(balanced)

2CO₂  +  Ca(OH)₂ ⇒Ca(HCO₃)₂

Required

The volume of CO₂

Solution

Find mol Ca(HCO₃)₂(MW=162,1146 g/mol) :

[tex]\tt mol=\dfrac{100~g}{162,1146~g/mol}=0.617[/tex]

Find mol of CO₂

From the equation,mol ratio CO₂ : Ca(HCO₃)₂ = 2 : 1, so mol CO₂ :

[tex]\tt 2\times 0.617=1.234[/tex]

Find volume of CO₂

Use ideal gas law to find volume

[tex]\tt V=\dfrac{nRT}{P}=\dfrac{1.234\times 0.08205\times 298}{1.00658}=29.975~L[/tex]

30. Find the grams in 1.26 x 10 -4 mol of c2h3o2.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass.

The molar masses of C, H and O are 12.01 g, 1.008 g and 16.00 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass} & = \text{2(12.01 g) + 3(1.008 g) + 2(16.00 g)} \\ & = \text{59.044 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_3\text{O}_2 & = 1.26 \times 10^{-4} \: \text{mol} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_3\text{O}_2 \times \frac{\text{59.044 g} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_3\text{O}_2}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_3\text{O}_2} \\ & = \boxed{7.44 \times 10^{-3} \: \text{g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 7.44 × 10⁻³ g in 1.26 × 10⁻⁴ moles of C₂H₃O₂.
