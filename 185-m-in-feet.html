11. How many mL of 3.00 M phosphoric acid (H2PO.) can be made from 95.0 mL of 5.00 M H3PO4?a. 57 mLb. 75 mLc. 158 mLd. 185 mL​


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{95.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]M_{1} = \text{5.00 M}[/tex]

[tex]M_{2} = \text{3.00 M}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]n_{1} = n_{2}[/tex]

[tex]M_{1}V_{1} = M_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{M_{1}}{M_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{95.0 mL} × \frac{\text{5.00 M}}{\text{3.00 M}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{158 mL}}[/tex]

Answer:

c. 158 mL

16. An airplane flies eastward through still air with a velocity of 185 km/hr. Express the velocity in m/s.


An airplane is flying east through still air at a speed of 185 km/h. The speed of the airplane in m/s is 51.39 m/s.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

An airplane flies eastward through still air with a velocity of 185 km/hr.

Question:

Express the velocity in m/s.

​Asked:

To solve this problem, we must understand the units of velocity.

Average speed is the average distance traveled per unit time.

Average speed formula:

Average speed = distance traveled : travel time

Distance traveled = Speed ​​x time

Time = Distance : Speed

Distance unit conversion: 1 km = 1000 meters

Time unit conversion: 1 hour = 3600 seconds

185 km/hr = 185 x 1000 / 1 x 3600 m/s

                  = 185000/3600 m/s

                  = 51.39 m/s

An airplane is flying east through still air at a speed of 185 km/h. The speed of the airplane in m/s is 51.39 m/s.

26. How many grams of KNO3 are present in 185 mL of a 2.50 M solution?


Answer:

[tex]molarity = \frac{n}{v_{s}} [/tex]

n = miles of solute

Vs = volume of solute in L = 0.185L (1L=1,000mL)

[tex]no. of moles = Molarity \times Volume in L[/tex]

[tex]no. of moles of KNO_{3} = 2.50M \times 0.185L = 0.46[/tex]

[tex]moles \times molar \: \: mass = 0.46 \times 101.1g/mol
