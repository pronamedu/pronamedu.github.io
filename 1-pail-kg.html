The boy has a mass of 45 kg and is running at a speed of 8m/s. What is the boy's KE?

GIVEN:M = 45 kgV = 8 m/sKE = ?Formula: KE = ½ mv²

ANSWER:KE = 1,440 J

SOLUTION:PE = 5 kg × 9.8 m/s² × 0.5 mPE = 49 kg. m/s² × 0.5 mPE = 24.5 J

ANSWER:PE = 24.5 JProblem:1.) The boy has a mass of 45 kg and is running at a speed of 8m/s. What is the boy's KE?

Problem:2.) If a student lifts a 5 kg pail full of water at the height of 0.5m. How much potential energy does the pail possess?

The formula for kinetic energy is KE = ½ mv² where KE is kinetic energy, m is mass and v is the velocity. The unit for kinetic energy is the joule.

1 kg.m²/s² = 1 joule

22. 6 L of water is poured into 2 empty pails. After pouring, 1 pail contains 800 ml more water than the other pail. How much water is there in the other pail?


Answer:

One pail contains 3.4 L of water while the other pail contains 2.6 L of water.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

- 6 L of water is poured into 2 pails:

x + y = 6000

(1 liter = 1000 mililiters)

- One pail (x) contains 800 mL more water than the other pail (y):

x = y + 800

- How much water is there in the other pail? (solve for y)


Solution:

1. Solve for x in the first equation:

x + y = 6000

x = 6000 - y

2. Solve for y:

6000 - y = y + 800

6000 - 800 = y + y

5200 = 2y

y = 2600

There are 2,600 mL or 2.6 L of water in the other pail.


Proof:

- Insert the value of y in the second equation to find x.

x = 2600 + 800

x = 3400

- Insert the values of x and y in the first equation.

3400 + 2600 = 6000

6000 = 6000
