27. After shopping, it took Tyrone 30 seconds to push a shopping cart directly to his car. In one-third of that time, Tyrone had moved 20 meters at a constant velocity. What was the cart's velocity?​


Let's use the equation:

velocity = distance / time

We know that Tyrone moved 20 meters in one-third of the time it took him to push the cart to his car. So the time he took to cover 20 meters is:

(1/3) x 30 seconds = 10 seconds

Now, we can use the equation to find the velocity of the cart:

velocity = distance / time = 20 meters / 10 seconds = 2 meters per second

Therefore, the cart's velocity was 2 meters per second.
