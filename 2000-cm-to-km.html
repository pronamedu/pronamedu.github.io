7. The mean life of a tire is 30,000 km. the standard deviation is 2000 km. then, 68% of all tires will have a life between ___________ km and __________ km.


The Standard Deviation

The standard deviation is a statistic that expresses how much variance or dispersion there is in a group of numbers. While a high standard deviation suggests that the values are dispersed over a wider range, a low standard deviation suggests that the values tend to be close to the mean of the value of the collection.

Problem:

The mean life of a tire is 30,000 km. the standard deviation is 2000 km. then, 68% of all tires will have a life between ___________ km and __________ km.

Solution:

Let X represent the lifespan of a tire and assume that it has a normal distribution:

X  ̴  N (µ, σ²)

Assuming µ = 30 000 km, and σ = 2 000 km, respectively.

X  ̴  N  (30 000,2 000²)

(a) Per the empirical rule, 68% of a normal distribution's area is covered between its mean and mean plus one standard deviation.

P (μ – σ < X < μ + σ) = 68%

P(30 000 – 2 000 < X < 30 000 + 2 000) = 68%

P(28 000 < X < 32 000) = 68%

Therefore, the lifespan of 68% of all tires will be between 28,000 and 32,000 km.

The empirical rule is used in probability and statistics theory to determine the area covered by the normal curve using only the normal curve. It claims that roughly 95% of the space is covered by the normal curve between the mean plus or minus two standard deviations.
