13. What's the meaning of positive messages and negative messages?​


Answer:

Negative persuasive arguments illustrate the bad things that may happen if people do not follow a particular course of action or if they do the wrong thing. Positive persuasive messages, however, point out the good things that can happen if people follow a course of action or if they follow the wrong path.

A short communication in the form of textsent or received by a portable electronic device, esp. a cell phone. A brief electronic message sent between mobile phones.

There are three purposes for a message: inform, persuade or goodwill. The first purpose is to inform where a message can be used to communicate routine, repetitive, daily tasks, instructions, codes, steps and procedures in the workplace.

A target audience is the intended audience or readership of a publication, advertisement, or other message catered specifically to said intended audience.

24. what are the messages in the dna considered as messages in the proteins?


Answer:

A genetic message might be an instruction to assemble amino acids into a sequence to make a protein. These genetic messages are often thought of as blueprints for the cell. The genetic messages are found on DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid), and the DNA segments that carry these messages are called genes

How is DNA used to make proteins?

First, enzymes read the information in a DNA molecule and transcribe it into an intermediary molecule called messenger ribonucleic acid, or mRNA. Next, the information contained in the mRNA molecule is translated into the "language" of amino acids, which are the building blocks of proteins.
