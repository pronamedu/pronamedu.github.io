26. in your own idea discuss the hospitality industry of the Philippines ​


Answer:

As friendly as they are known for, the hospitality of Filipinos is a common trait that can be recognized through their convivial and heartwarming character. This can typically be observed in how Filipinos pleasantly welcome their guests, whether locals or foreigners. They put their best foot forward to make them feel comfortable in their country and homes (to the extent that they may go out of their way just to assist their visitors).

Part of the Filipino culture is how they receive their guests with hospitality and respect. This is best exemplified by welcoming and letting them in their homes as soon as guest/s arrive. “Tulóy ka/kayó” is a Filipino warm welcoming expression that shows kind accommodations to their visitors. “Tulóy” means “to go on” or “to enter.” For this context, the expressions “tulóy ka/kayo” can be defined as “You may come in” or “Please come in.”

28. Enumerate the provisios in the Philippines constitution relevant to tourism and hospitality industry​


There are several provisions in the Philippine Constitution that are relevant to the tourism and hospitality industry. These provisions include:

Protection of the Environment - Article II, Section 16 of the Philippine Constitution mandates the state to protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthful ecology. This provision is relevant to the tourism and hospitality industry because it requires the government to ensure that tourism activities are sustainable and do not cause harm to the environment.

Promotion of Tourism - Article XIV, Section 15 of the Philippine Constitution provides that the state shall promote the development of a tourist industry that is environmentally and socially responsible. This provision is relevant to the tourism and hospitality industry because it recognizes the importance of tourism to the country's economy and encourages the development of a sustainable tourism industry.

Protection of Cultural Heritage - Article XIV, Section 19 of the Philippine Constitution mandates the state to promote and protect the nation's historical and cultural heritage. This provision is relevant to the tourism and hospitality industry because it recognizes the importance of preserving the country's cultural and historical sites, which are major tourist attractions.

Protection of Workers' Rights - Article III, Section 18 of the Philippine Constitution provides that the state shall ensure the protection of workers' rights and promote their welfare. This provision is relevant to the tourism and hospitality industry because it recognizes the importance of protecting the rights and welfare of workers in the industry.

Promotion of Small Businesses - Article XII, Section 1 of the Philippine Constitution mandates the state to promote the development of small and medium-sized enterprises. This provision is relevant to the tourism and hospitality industry because it encourages the development of small businesses in the industry, which can contribute to the growth of the tourism sector and the economy as a whole.
