Step-by-step explanation:

The distance around the wheel of a truck is known as the circumference of the wheel. The formula for the circumference of a circle is:

C = 2πr

where C is the circumference of the circle, π is a mathematical constant (approximately equal to 3.14), and r is the radius of the circle.

In this problem, we are given that the circumference of the wheel is 9.42 feet. Therefore, we can set up an equation:

9.42 = 2πr

To solve for the radius, we can divide both sides by 2π:

r = 9.42 / (2π)

Using a calculator to approximate π to two decimal places, we get:

r ≈ 1.50 feet

Finally, we can find the diameter of the wheel by doubling the radius:

d = 2r ≈ 3.00 feet

Therefore, the diameter of the wheel is approximately 3.00 feet.
