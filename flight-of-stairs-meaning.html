11. A 52kg student runs up a flight of stairs of vertical height 3.0m in 4.7s. Calculate the power output


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The power of an object doing a work W in a given time t is given by

[tex]\boxed{P = \frac{W}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = power

W = work

t = time

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for F

F = weight = mg

F = (52 kg)(9.8 m/s²)

F = 509.6 N

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (509.6 N)(3.0 m)

W = 1,528.8 J

Solving for P

[tex]P = \dfrac{W}{t}[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{\text{1,528.8 J}}{\text{4.7 s}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P = \text{325.28 W}}[/tex]

Therefore, the power output is 325.28 W.

29. How much work does if 75.0 kg firefighter climbs a flight of stairs 28.0 m high


Work is defined as the product of force and distance. In this case, the force acting on the firefighter is his weight, which is equal to his mass multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (g). The distance is the height of the stairs that the firefighter climbs.

To calculate the work done by the firefighter, we can use the formula:

Work = force x distance

The weight of the firefighter is given by the formula:

Weight = mass x g

where mass is 75.0 kg and g is 9.8 m/s^2 (acceleration due to gravity)

So the weight of the firefighter is 75.0 kg x 9.8 m/s^2 = 735 N

Now we can use the work formula to calculate the work done by the firefighter

Work = force x distance

Work = 735 N x 28.0 m = 20580 J (Joules)

Therefore, the firefighter does 20580 Joules of work when climbing the flight of stairs 28.0 m high.
