The zygote is called an embryo. This is because after fertilization occurs and the zygote is formed, it begins the process of cell division and differentiation, which leads to the development of different structures and organs in the body. The embryo stage in human development is generally considered to last from fertilization until the end of the eighth week. After this point, the developing organism is referred to as a fetus, as it continues to grow and mature before birth.
