19. What is rolling resistance in a wheel and axle? How does it affect the wheel and axle? What is the wheel radius and axle radius on a steering wheel in a normal-sized car? What about a wooden ship?


Answer:

Rolling resistance is the force that resists the motion of a wheel or an axle when it is rolling on a surface. It is caused by the deformation of the surface that the wheel or axle is rolling on and the internal friction in the materials of the wheel and axle.

Rolling resistance can affect the wheel and axle in several ways. It can cause a reduction in the speed and efficiency of the vehicle or machine that uses the wheel and axle. It can also cause heat buildup, which can lead to mechanical failure of the wheel or axle.

The wheel radius and axle radius on a steering wheel in a normal-sized car can vary depending on the make and model of the car. However, in general, the radius of the wheel is typically between 12-18 inches (30-45 cm), while the radius of the axle is usually less than 2 inches (5 cm).

In the case of a wooden ship, the wheel and axle are typically much larger in size than those in a car. The wheel, also known as the ship's helm, can have a radius of several feet or more, while the axle, which is connected to the helm and the rudder, can have a diameter of several inches or more, depending on the size of the ship. Rolling resistance can be a significant factor for a ship's helm and rudder, especially in heavy seas, and can affect the ship's maneuverability and stability.

28. Discuss the difference between manual steering and power steering.​


Manual steering is the string system where steering is connected to a rod that turns the wheels as per the steering direction. This is called as Rack and pinion steering. Rack means rod and pinion means gear. A gear is attached to the steering rod. When the driver turn the steering wheel, it turns the gear in circular motion and then moves the steering rod which can be seen behind the clutch, brake and accelerator pedal. Here the rotational motion of steering wheels is converting into linear motion. This linear motion turns the wheels. Hence the steering is heavy one the car is idle and as soon as the tires start moving the kinetic energy reduces the weight of steering. Higher the speed lighter the steering.

On the other hand, in power steering or hydraulic steering a pump (motor) is attached to the steering column which uses oil to create hydraulic pressure to lighten the steering. One the engine is on the steering pump is on and the steering becomes light. This is also rack and pinion system with a motor. Steering is super heavy when the engine is off.
