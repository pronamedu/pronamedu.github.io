Answer:

Doctor Baisa can visit based on the following:

- location of patients: from the first floor up to the last floor;

-  case of patients: from delicate up to less harm;

- social status or hospital status: from private to ward cases.

Please be noted that you are asking for a narrative answer. I'm thinking that the scenario you have given is just telling the ways to visit the patients. But there is no provided problem such as the time constraint.

 

Step-by-step explanation:

In a hospital setting, an attending physician usually visits his/her patients only once a day maybe in a morning or any time within a day.

8 patients for average of 10-20 mins for each visit. This is changing based on the situation.

Minimum: 8 patients x 10 mins= 80 mins or 1 hr and 20 mins

Maximum: 8 patients x 20 mins= 160 mins or 2 hrs and 40 mins

In any case, patient's visit has changes time to time based on urgency and availability of doctor. Sometimes, the information relayed by nurse is really enough to make doctor's command. In other situation, the attending physician has assistant doctor like the resident doctor to watch the patients in general. This is normal and professional.

14. Use the fundamental counting principle in finding the number of possible combinations given the following situations. 1. How many three-letter combinations can be made from R, P, and A? 2. A restaurant's menu includes pancakes, waffles, and home fries. For drinks, they have coffee, hot chocolate, and tea. How many different choices of food and drinks can a customer have? 3. Camille has 7 blouses, 2 skirts, 3 pairs of short pants, and 5 pairs of jeans. How many different outfits can she wear, assuming that she always wears a belt? 4. Doctor Baisa is going to make his rounds in the hospital with his 8 patients. How many different ways can he make his rounds if he visits each patient only once? 5. The class of 5 - Masikap will be having their field trip. They are going to a museum, zoo, resort, bread factory, and a toy factory. How many possible itineraries are there? 6. A cinema sells 3 sizes of popcorns ( small, medium, and large) with 3 choices of toppings ( cheese, butter, barbeque). How many possible ways can a bag of popcorn be purchased? 7. On a math test, a student must write two problem-solving items with reasonable answers. For the first item, the student must select from topics A, B, and C. For the second item, the student must select from topics 1, 2, 3, and 4. How many different ways can the student select two problem-solving topics? 8. Neil needs to choose a four-digit PIN number, Each digit can be chosen from 0 to 9. How many different possible numbers can Neil choose? 4.


1. Since there are only three letters, we can use 3!, which is 3x2x1 or 6

2. There are 3 foods and 3 drinks. Simply multiply those together to get 9 different choices

3. Multiply every number you see in the problem. The last statement is a red herring since short pants, skirts, and jeans can be worn with a belt. 7x2x3x5 should give us 210.

4. Same as number one, we can use the factorial notation. This means it will be expressed as 8! or 40320 ways.

5. Since there are 5 choices, we can express this in factorial notation, This means it will be expressed as 5! or 120 ways.

6. Since there are 3 sizes of popcorn that comes with 3 choices of toppings, multiply the two values together to get 9 possible ways

7. Since there are 4 choices for number 1 and there are 4 choices for number 2, multiply the two values together to get 16 possible ways.

8.) Assuming digits in a PIN number can be repeated, there will be always 10 digits to choose from, and since it is required to be a four-digit number, multiply 10 four times or [tex]10^{4}[/tex] which is 10,000 possible ways
