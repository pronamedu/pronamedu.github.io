17. The radius and length of a steel cylinder are 60mm and 120mm respectively. If the mass density of steel is 7850kg/m^3, determine the weight of the cylinder?


The weight of the cylinder : 10.644 kg

Further explanation

A steel cylinder is a tube that has a circular cross-section and height

So the dimensions of a cylinder or tube are expressed by the radius/diameter of the cross-section of the tube and the height of the tube

Tube/cylinder volume formula:

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{V=\pi r^2.h}}[/tex]

Whereas Density is a quantity derived from the mass and volume

Density is the ratio of mass per unit volume

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\rho ~ = ~ \frac {m} {V}}}}[/tex]

ρ = density

m = mass

v = volume

Volume of the steel

The radius and length of a steel cylinder are 60 mm and 120 mm respectively, so :

r = 60 mm

h = 120 mm

density of steel = ρ = 7850 kg/m³

then volume of the steel

[tex]\rm V=\pi.60^2.120=1356480~mm^3=1.356.10^{-3}~m^3[/tex]

The weight of the cylinder

[tex]m=\rho\times V\\\\m=7850\times 1.356.10^{-3}\\\\m=\boxed{\bold{10.644~kg}}[/tex]
