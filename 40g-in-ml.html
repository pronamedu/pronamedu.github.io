8. 2. Calculate the molal concentration of a solution that contains 18 g of sodium hydroxide (NaOH) in 100 mL of water. (molar mass of NaOH = 40g/mol)​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute (NaOH).

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 18 g NaOH} × \frac{\text{1 mol NaOH}}{\text{40 g NaOH}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.45 mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Determine the mass of solvent (H₂O).

mass of solvent = density of solvent × volume of solvent

mass of solvent = 1 g/mL × 100 mL

mass of solvent = 100 g = 0.100 kg

Step 3: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.45 mol}}{\text{0.100 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 4.5 \: m}[/tex]

28. What is the molarity of a solution that contains 30 g of NaOH in 500 mL of solution (molar mass of NaOH = 40g/mol)? A. 0.0015 M C. 0.375 M B. 0.06 M D. 1.5 M


Molarity (M)Discussion:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}}[/tex]

For example, a 1.46 [tex]M[/tex] glucose solution contains 1.46 mol of the solute (glucose) in 1 L of the solution. Of course, we do not always work with solution volumes of 1 L. Thus, a 500-mL solution containing 0.730 mol of glucose also has a concentration of 1.46 [tex]M.[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

What is the molarity of a solution that contains 30 g of NaOH in 500 mL of solution (molar mass of NaOH = 40g/mol)?

A. 0.0015 M

C. 0.375 M

B. 0.06 M

D. 1.5 M

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 30 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution = 500 mL = 0.500 L}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute = NaOH}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{molarity}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{30 g}}{\text{40 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.75 mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.75 mol}}{\text{0.500 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 1.5 \: M}[/tex]

Answer:

D. 1.5 M
