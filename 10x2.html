Steps in Factoring by grouping:

Always check for the GCF or greatest common factorFind the product of "a" and "c" : General Formula [tex]ax^{n}+bx+c[/tex] Find two new factors of a•c that add up to bSplit the middle term into two terms using the sum of the two new factors, including the proper signsGroup the four terms to form two pairs. Be careful of the signsFactor each pair by finding the common factors and Factor out the common binomial

In factoring, there are six methods or techniques. The six methods are as follows:

Greatest Common Factor (GCF) -  When factoring in general this will also be the first thing that we should try as it will often simplify the problem.  Grouping Method - To get the "factoring by grouping", the process will start by looking for two numbers that multiply to ac, and whose sum is equal to b. Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Number of desks:  (2x + 1)Cost per desk: (5x - 3)

Total Cost = (number of desks) × (costs per desk)

Find the total cost:

Total cost = (2x + 1) (5x - 3)

= (2x)(5x) + (2x)(-3) + (1)(5x) + (1)(-3)

= 10x² -6x + 5x - 3

= 10x² - x - 3
