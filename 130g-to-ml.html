1. A force of 6500 dyne is applied to a body with a mass of 130g. What is the acceleration?​


Answer:

a =  0.5 [tex]\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex]

Explanation:

Given:

F = 6500 dyne

m = 130g

Required:

Acceleration ( a) = ?

Solution:

The formula for force is

F = mass x acceleration

F = m x a

F = 6500 dyne  by converting it to Newton (N) we will get

F = 6500 dyne x [tex]\frac{0.001N}{100dyne}[/tex]

F = 0. 065N

Convert g to kg

m = 130g x [tex]\frac{1kg}{1000g}[/tex]

m = 0.13kg

0.065N = 0.13kg x a

a = [tex]\frac{0.065N}{0.13g}[/tex]

a =  0.5 [tex]\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex]

C. 23.5%

• What is the percent by mass of a solution made by dissolving 40.0 grams of sugar into 130 grams of water?

Equation:

% by mass = (mass of solute/mass of sol.)•100%

Note:

mass of sol. = mass of solute + mass of solvent

Solution:

% by mass = (40.0g/40.0g+130g)•100%

% by mass = (40.0g/170.0g)•100%

% by mass = 0.23529 x 100%

% by mass = 23.5%
