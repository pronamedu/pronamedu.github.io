16. A 900-kg compact car moving at 20 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?​


KINETIC ENERGY

» A 900-kg compact car moving at 20 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?

Given:m (mass) = 900 kg v (velocity) = 20 m/s

Formula:

- To find the kinetic energy, use formula:

[tex] \sf{ \color{brown}{K.E = \frac{1}{2}mv ^{2} }}[/tex]

Solution:

KE = ½mv²

KE = ½ × (900) × (20)²

KE = ½ × (360,000)

KE = 180,000 J

Answer:180,000 J (joules)

30. how much force is needed to accelerate a 900-kg car at a rate of 3m/s2?​


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

how much force is needed to accelerate a 900-kg car at a rate of 3m/s2?

Note:

The formula of Force is Force = mass × acceleration ( F = m × a ).

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 900 kg

a ( acceleration ) = 3 m/s²

F ( Force ) = ? unknown

Solution:

The formula of Force is Force = mass × acceleration ( F = m × a ). We can use this formula in order to solve the problem:

F = m × a  

  = 900 kg  × 3 m/s²

  = 2, 700 kg·m/s²  

  = 2700 N

Answer:

The needed force to accelerate the car is equal to 2700 N or 2, 700 kg·m/s²
