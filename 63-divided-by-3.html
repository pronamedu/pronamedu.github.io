Check the examples below :

*Remember in the examples below whatever the sign of the number: 63 is the dividend, 7 is the divisor and 9 is the quotient.

Rules in dividing sign numbers or numbers sign :

+63 read as positive 63 is the same as 63

+7 read as positive 7 is the same as 7

*Positive dividend divided by positive divisor = positively sign quotient

Ex.

[tex]63 \div 7 = 9[/tex]

*Negative dividend divided by positive divisor = negatively sign quotient

Ex.

[tex]( - 63) \div 7 = - 9[/tex]

*Positive dividend divided by negative divisor = negatively sign quotient

Ex.

[tex]63 \div ( - 7) = - 9[/tex]

*Negative dividend divided by negative divisor = positively sign quotient

Ex.

[tex]( - 63) \div ( - 7) = 9[/tex]

In addition to the explanation above, to check whether the result of your division (quotient) is correct, multiply the quotient to the divisor and you will get the result of the dividend

RULES IN MULTIPLYING NUMBER SIGNS

*Positive x Positive = Positive

Ex. 7 x 9 = 63

*Positive x Negative = Negative

Ex. 7 x (- 9) = - 63

*Negative x Negative = Positive

Ex. (-7) x (-9) = 63
