22. how much force must be exerted at A to support the 850kg car at B? The piston at A has a diameter of 17mm and the piston at B a diameter of 300mm need po asap​


The solving for the force at A gives a value of approximately 176 kg to support the 850kg car at B.

FORCE A AND B

In order to determine the force that must be exerted at point A, we need to know the distance between points A and B and the angle at which the force is applied.

Assuming that the force is applied perpendicular to the pistons, the force at A can be calculated using the following equation:

Force at A = (Force at B) x (Area of the piston at A ÷ Area of the piston at B)

The area of a piston is calculated by taking the diameter of the piston and squaring it, then multiplying that value by pi (3.14). So the force at A can be calculated as follows:

Force at A = (850 kg) × [(17 mm)² × 3.14 ÷ (300 mm)² × 3.14]

                 = 176 kg

Solving for the force at A gives a value of approximately 176 kg. This is the force that must be exerted at point A in order to support the 850 kg car at point B.

It's important to note that this calculation assumes that the pistons are perfect cylinders and that the force is applied perpendicular to the pistons. If either of these assumptions is not true, the actual force required at point A could be different.
