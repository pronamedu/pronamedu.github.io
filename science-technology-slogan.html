11. slogan for science THEME: Integrated Approach in Science and Technology for Sustainable Future


The slogan is needed today, especially in the age of technology.

 

Science, often known as knowledge, is an attempt by humans to comprehend and preserve natural facts and events. Technology is an attempt by humans to apply science for the good of all.

Sustainable development is a method of human-facilitated development that makes the most of the available natural resources. In general, progress in a nation's social, economic, and political institutions is seen as a result of sustainable development.

In addition, sustainable development can also help improve people's welfare and facilitate the fulfillment of their needs without hindering the development of the next generation to do the same.

Therefore, the theme "Integrated Approaches of Science and Technology for a Sustainable Future" has the aim of aligning the development of science and knowledge with the needs of sustainability in life. A simple example is the application of STEM-based learning, namely learning based on science, technology, the environment, and mathematics. This approach has the aim of ensuring that learning in the classroom is in line with the needs outside the classroom, so that students can get used to and adapt to the times.

15. Slogan about integrated approach in science and technology for sustainable future


“Through Science, A Green Future.”

The key element of slogans that makes them effective is their simplicity. The general rule of thumb is “Less Is More”. The use of the word “green” is an effective shorthand to imply an environmentally-friendly image.

Steps In Making An Effective Slogan

This is the list of rules on how to make a slogan. This is not a rule set in stone, break it if you feel like it would make your slogan more effective.

Simplicity: The word choice should not make people who read it need to grab a dictionary. The goal is that normal people should be able to understand it.Make it Concise: The shorter the slogan, the better. Think of famous slogans used by many corporations. For example, “Just Do It,” by Nike, and “Happiest Place on Earth,” by Disneyland.Use of Literary Devices: Slogans become more memorable if you employ literary devices like using words that rhyme or alliteration or metaphors.
