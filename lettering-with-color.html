Let be the number of the first letter of the chosen color. 1) What is the sample space? 2) What is the probability of each color to be chosen? 3) What are the possible outcomes in the event when the letter of the color chosen is constant? 4) What is the probability that the chosen letter of color is vowel? 5) What is the probability that the chosen letter of color is constant?​


Step-by-step explanation:

1) What is the sample space?

The sample space is the set of all possible colors in the rainbow: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.

2) What is the probability of each color to be chosen?

Since all colors have an equal chance of being chosen, the probability of each color being chosen is 1/7.

3)What are the possible outcomes in the event when the letter of the color chosen is constant?

The possible outcomes in the event when the letter of the color chosen is constant are: yellow, green, blue.

4) What is the probability that the chosen letter of color is vowel?

The probability that the chosen letter of color is a vowel is 3/7 (red, orange, and indigo)

5) What is the probability that the chosen letter of color is constant?

The probability that the chosen letter of color is a constant is 4/7 (yellow, green, blue, and violet).

29. the color of an object that absorbs all colors except red THREE LETTERS​


Answer:

The simple answer to your question is red. If red is the only color the object doesn’t absorb then that means it reflects red light back out into the environment for our eyes to perceive.

It sounds like you could be seeking a different answer though: What color would an object be if it was every color except red? (meaning it absorbed only red light.) In that case the resulting color would be cyan, or something similar to it (it’s a bright combination of blue and green).
