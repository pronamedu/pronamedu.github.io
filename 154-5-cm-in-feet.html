4. How long is a leg of an isosceles trapezoid if the bases are 68 cm and 20cm, and it's perimeter is 154 cm?a. 33b. 34c. 35d. 36​


Answer:

Option a. 33

Step-by-step explanation:

An isosceles trapezoid have two congruent legs, let each leg be x , so :

[tex]154 = 68 + x + 20 + x[/tex]

[tex]154 - 68 - 20 = 2x[/tex]

[tex]2x = 66[/tex]

[tex]x = \frac{66}{2} [/tex]

[tex]{\boxed{{{x = 33}}}}[/tex]

Hence, leg of the trapezoid is 33m.

Answer:

B) 51 feet

Step-by-step explanation:

Trigonometry because right angle:

1. Angle of depression is 30

2. Adjacent to this angle is the light house which is 89 feet tall  

3. we need to find the opposite which is the distance from the boat to the light house.

therefore tan = opposite / Adjacent

= tan(30) * 89

= 51 feet

19. 2. How many centimeters of ribbon will Ana need to decorate the sides of a bed sheet which measures 154 cm by 88 cm.​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To find out how many centimeters of ribbon Ana will need to decorate the sides of a bed sheet, we need to calculate the perimeter of the bed sheet.

The perimeter of a rectangle is given by the formula:

P = 2l + 2w

where P is the perimeter, l is the length, and w is the width.

In this case, the length (l) of the bed sheet is 154 cm and the width (w) is 88 cm. So we can plug these values into the formula to get:

P = 2(154) + 2(88)

P = 308 + 176

P = 484

Therefore, the perimeter of the bed sheet is 484 cm.

To decorate the sides of the bed sheet, Ana will need to cover both the top and the bottom sides. This means she will need twice the length of ribbon as the perimeter of the bed sheet. So we can multiply the perimeter by 2 to get:

2 x 484 = 968

Therefore, Ana will need 968 centimeters of ribbon to decorate the sides of the bed sheet
