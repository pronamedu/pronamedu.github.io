Next, we are told that she walks another 750 meters, but we are not given the direction. Without this information, we cannot calculate her displacement from her friend's house to her final destination. However, we can calculate the total distance she walks, which is:

Further explanation

Electric power is defined as electrical energy per unit time

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{P=\dfrac{W}{t}}}[/tex]

P = watt

W = electrical energy, J

t = time, s

or it could be

P = V.I

P = I².R

P = V² / R

 power unit conversion

1 watt = 1 J / s

1 J = 1 watt. Second

1 kw = 1000 w

1 kwh = 1 kilowatt hour

1 kwh = 1000 watt hour

Given

P = 750 watt

t = 15 min = 15 x 60 s = 900 s

so the energy :

[tex]\rm W = P\times t\\\\W=750\times 900\\\\W=675000~watt.s=67500\times \dfrac{1~hr}{3600~s}\times \dfrac{10^{-3~kw}}{watt}=\boxed{\bold{0.1875~kwh}}[/tex]
