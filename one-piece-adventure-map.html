    Our forefathers had their own literature long before the Spaniards and other foreigners landed or set foot on Philippine shores. Our ancient literature depicts our daily customs and traditions through folk tales, old plays, and short stories. Whatever records our forefathers left were either burned by Spaniard friars who believed they were works of the devil, or written on materials that easily expired, such as tree bark, dried leaves, and bamboo cylinders, which could not have survived even if efforts were taken to preserve them. Other albums that remained had folk tunes that demonstrated the existence of a truly indigenous culture. Some of them were passed down by word of mouth until they reached the hands of publishers or printers interested in publishing ancient Filipino manuscripts. The Spaniards who came to the Philippines attempted to demonstrate that our forefathers were truly fond of poetry, songs, stories, riddles, and proverbs, which we still enjoy today and which serve to teach future generations about our true culture.

LEGENDS- Legends are a type of literature in which the common theme is the origin of a thing, place, region, or name.

FOLK TALES - Folk tales are stories about life, adventure, love, horror, and humor from which one might learn life lessons. These are beneficial to us since they help us enjoy our surroundings, analyze our personalities, and improve our life perspectives. THE MOON AND THE STARS is an example of this THE SUNLIGHT.
