15. If arc CE = 110 , angle D =


Answer:

B. 70

Step-by-step explanation:

Apply the outside angle theorem which says the angle formed by two tangents outside a circle is equal to half of the difference of the intercepted arcs.

The intercepted arcs are:

m(CE) = 110°

m(CBE) = 360° - 110° = 250°

thus:

m<CDE = 1/2(m(CBE) - m(CE))

Substitute:

m<CDE = 1/2(250° - 110°)

m<CDE = 1/2 (140°)

m<CDE = 70°

25. The measure of the two angles of a quadrilateral are 110° and 98° what is the measure of each angle​


Answer:

The sum of the angles in a quadrilateral is always 360 degrees. So, let the measure of the other two angles in the quadrilateral be x and y.

Thus, we can write the equation:

110° + 98° + x + y = 360°

Simplifying the equation, we get:

x + y = 360° - 110° - 98°

x + y = 152°

Now we can use the equation we derived to solve for x or y. Let's say we solve for x:

x = 152° - y

Substituting this value of x into the original equation, we get:

110° + 98° + (152° - y) + y = 360°

Simplifying this equation, we get:

360° - 110° - 98° = y + 152°

y = 360° - 110° - 98° - 152°

y = 360° - 360°

y = 0°

Therefore, one of the angles in the quadrilateral has a measure of 110°, one has a measure of 98°, and the other two angles both have a measure of 0°. However, we know that a quadrilateral cannot have an angle with a measure of 0°, so this means that the original problem was not solvable or there may have been a mistake in the given information.
