18. DIRECTIONS:HOW CROPS ARE ARRANGED IN ROW1.Crops are planted in rows or straight lines?2.Row planting arrangement?3.Multiple row planting arrangement?4.Spatial arrangement in intercroppin?​


Answer:

1.Crops are planted in rows or straight lines, either singly or in multiple rows, mainly to enhance maximu6m yields as well as for convenience. An east-west row orientation is preferred to maximize light absorption, but this is not always possible.

2.Row-planted crops are either arranged in equidistant single rows or in multiple rows. Planting in single rows is most common in monocropping or sole cropping, the growing of a single crop.

3.Multiple row planting is a system of growing crops in blocks or strips of 2 or more rows. The adjacent blocks are separated by a space which may remain vacant or planted to other crops. This planting arrangement is common in multiple cropping in which two or more crops are grown in the same piece of land.

4.Spatial arrangement is the systematic apportioning of the farm area or any growing surface for crop production. Planting of the intercrop between two adjacent hills within the same row of the main crop allows interrow cultivation but the intercrop has limited exposure to sunlight.
