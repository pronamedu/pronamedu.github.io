5. what are the similarities of politics and governance?​


Answer:

This answer of mine to another question is relevant here.

Politics is a reflection of the interests of different groups of people. A political party may reflect the view of a wide section of the society. That is good. This is different from governance. We may think that the interest of the political party (or the section of society which it represents) would influence the governance when it is in power. That is not completely true. When two political parties compete, each party wants to win, and that will change its focus.

Let us the take the following example. There are 11 voters in a country (it can be any number but we take 11 for the convenience). We may arrange these voters in the decreasing order of income. 1 is the Richest and 11th is the poorest. Let us take that there are two parties - one is Poor Person’s Party (PPP) and the other is Rich Person’s Party (RPP). When RPP competes with PPP, voters 1, 2, 3.. may vote for RPP and voters 11, 10, 9,.. may vote for PPP. However for RPP or PPP to win they should get not only the votes of their loyal supporters but also people at the middle, that is 5, 6, 7 or 6 specifically. That would mean that the competition would make both RPP and PPP to focus on 6 in shaping their agenda.

To a great extent, the nature of this voter can make a lot of difference. If 6 is poor, he/she wants more populism. If this voter is middle-class, the governance would be much more careful in having a good tax-public services combinations, and reduced corruption. On the other hand poor and rich do not have the incentive to demand good governance and to reduce corruption. In many countries, the median voter is part of the poor and that is an issue. Wherever, the median voter is part of the middle-class, governance would be careful and balanced.

Hence addressing poverty and enabling a major part of the poor to be part of the middle-class (through human development) are important to improve governance.

2.Political governance is the process of decision-making to formulate policy. Administrative governance is the system of policy implementation. Encompassing all three, good governance defines the processes and structures that guide political and socio-economic relationships.

3.The Barangay Local Government Unit: Barangays, the smallest local government unit, developed in the Philippines well before the Spanish colonial era. The first barangay developed around groups of 30-100 households with most limited to kinship principles.

In highly populated cities, a barangay can be as small as a cluster of a few blocks. The term barangay gains its popular usage during the time of Ferdinand Marcos.

5.What is the form of government during the pre-colonial government?

The barangay was the Filipino's earliest form of government. It was an independent settlement consisting of thirty to one hundred families usually situated along a river bank or at the mouth of a river spilling out to the sea. The term barangay was derived from the Malay word barangay or balangay, which means sailboat.

21. what the similarities of politics and government​


Answer:

Politics and government are closely related, and they share several similarities. Here are some of the key similarities:

Both involve decision-making: Politics and government both involve making decisions that affect individuals and society as a whole. In politics, these decisions are typically made by elected officials or other leaders who represent the interests of their constituents. In government, decisions are made by officials and bureaucrats who are responsible for implementing and enforcing laws and policies.

Both are concerned with power and authority: Politics and government are concerned with the allocation and exercise of power and authority. In politics, individuals and groups compete for power and influence, while in government, officials exercise power and authority through the implementation of laws and policies.

Both have a role in shaping society: Politics and government both have a significant impact on the way that society is structured and operates. Through the creation and implementation of laws and policies, governments can help to shape social and economic conditions, while political movements can influence the political and cultural landscape.

Both involve conflict and negotiation: Politics and government both involve conflict and negotiation, as individuals and groups with different interests and values compete for influence and resources. Negotiation and compromise are essential to both politics and government, as they allow for the resolution of conflicts and the creation of policies that reflect the interests of different groups.

Overall, politics and government are closely linked and share many similarities, as both involve decision-making, power and authority, shaping society, and conflict and negotiation.
