18. Which Of The Following Occupies More Space A Ball With Radius Of 5cm Or a Cube With An Edge Of 80mm?


Hi  VinceAlexis12,

It is really confusing if we are going to look just into the units of measurement. You can say "Hey! millimeter is smaller than centimeter". But if you look closely, there is a little trick there. Here's how,

Let us first convert the units and agree that we will be only using one type of unit only (for our example let's use centimeter)

Now, if we are using centimeter, we need to convert the millimeter edge of the cube (80 mm) to centimeter.

We can do this by dividing 80 mm by 10 (since 1 cm = 10 mm)

[tex]\frac{80}{10}[/tex]=8 cm

Therefore the edge of the cube is 8 cm.

Now let's use the volume formula for both the ball and the cube

For the ball we will be using this:

[tex]V=\frac{4}{3}\pi  r^{3}[/tex]

While for the cube we will be using this:

[tex]V=s^{3}[/tex]

Finding the volume of the ball we have:

[tex]V=\frac{4}{3}\pi  r^{3} = [tex]523.6 cm^{3}[/tex]

Finding the volume of the cube we have:

[tex]V=s^{3}[/tex] = [tex]512 cm^{3}[/tex]

From that point we can conclude that the ball occupies a lot of space than the cube
