2. The sum of the minuend,the subtrahend and the difference is 388. If the subtrahend is greater than the difference by 16,find the subtrahend.GIVE THE ANSWER AND THE SOLUTION.......ASAP



Minuend: x

Subtrahend: y

Difference: x

Subtraction liiteral equation:  x - y = z

y = x - z  (Equation 1)

Sum of minuend, subtrahend and difference is 388:  x + y + z = 388

y = 388 - x - z  (Equation 2)

The subtrahend is greater than the difference by 16:

y = z + 16  (Equation 3)

Equate y ⇒  Equations 1 and 2.  Find x, the minuend:

x - z  = 388 - x - z

x + x = 388 - z + z

2x/2 = 388/2

x = 194  ⇒   the minuend

Find z, the difference by equating y (Equations 2 and 3):

388 - x - z  =  z + 16

388 - 194 = z + z + 16

2z = 388 - 194 - 16

2z/2 = 178/2

z = 89  ⇒  the difference

Find the subtrahend, y:

y = z + 16

y = 89 + 16

y = 105  ⇒  the subtrahendANSWER: The subtrahend is 105.

Check:

x - y = z

194 - 105 = 89

89 = 89  (True)

x + y + z = 388

194 + 105 + 89 = 388

388 = 388 (True)

The subtrahend is greater than the difference by 16:

y = z + 16

105 = 89 + 16

105 = 105  (True)

6. in integers, is the subtrahend always the second number? ​


Answer:

Integers are the numbers made from the set of whole numbers and their opposites.

Example:

2 and -2

The difference is the answer for a subtraction problem.

Example:

A - B = C

C is the difference or answer for the subtraction problem.

The minuend is the number from which other number subtracted.

Example:

9 - 6 = 3

The minuend is the number 9.

The subtrahend is the number that is to be deducted from another number.

Example:

9 - 6 = 3

The subtrahend is the number 6.

The difference between two positive integers can be either positive, negative or zero.

When the first positive integer (or minuend) is larger than the second positive integer (or subtrahend) in a subtraction problem, the difference will be positive.

Example #1:

5 - 4 = 1

Note: A plus symbol is not necessary to represent a positive number. The positive number is assumed. Therefore, 3 is a positive number.

When the second positive integer (or subtrahend) is larger than the first positive integer (or minuend) in a subtraction problem, the difference will be negative.

Example #2:

3 - 7 = -4

When both integers have the same value, the difference is zero.

Example #3:

8 - 8 = 0

The difference between a positive and a negative integer can be positive or negative.

When you subtract a negative integer from a positive integer, the difference is always positive.

Example #1:

4 - (-2) = 6

Example #2:

4 - (-4) = 8

Note: When you subtract a negative integer, the integer becomes positive. Another way to think about it is that two negatives equal a positive. The negative of a negative is the opposite positive number.

That is, for integers: - (- a) = + a

When you subtract a positive integer from a negative integer, the difference is always negative.

Example #3:

-7 - 3 = -10

Note: A plus symbol is not necessary to represent a positive number. The positive number is assumed. Therefore, 3 is a positive number.

The difference between two negative integers can be positive, negative or zero depending upon the values of the integers.

When you subtract two negative integers and the first integer (or minuend) is smaller than the second integer (or subtrahend), the difference is negative.

Example #1:

-8 - (-2) = -6

When you subtract two negative integers and the first integer (or minuend) is larger than the second integer (or subtrahend), the difference is positive.
