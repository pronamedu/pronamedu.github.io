8. the water tank of the school is a right circular cylinder with a diameter of 36 inches and a height 6 ft. what is the volume of the water in (a) in cubic feet (b) cubic meter (c) liters


Given:
dm = 36 in.
h = 6 ft.

Required:
V @

a.) cu. ft
b.) cu. m
c.) L

Solution:
The volume of a right circular-base cylinder is:

V = π•r^2•h
(In terms of radius)

V = π (d / 2)^2 (h)
(in terms of diameter)

Convert the dm first:
dm = 36 in (1 ft / 12 in) = 3 ft

Proceed on solving the V:
V = π (3 ft / 2)^2 (6 ft)
V = 42.41 cu. ft (final answer)

In solving for b and c; you can either convert the dm into their respective units to find the required. Or just convert the answer to cu. m and liters directly.

V = 42.41 cu. ft (0.305 m / 1 ft)^3
V = 1.20 cu. m (final answer)

V = 42.41 cu. ft (30.48 cm / 1 ft)^3 (1 L / 1000 cc)
V = 1,200.92 L (final answer)

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

The length = (3x - 2) ft

The width = (2x + 1) ft

The height = (x + 1) ft

Question:

Find the width of the cake if its volume is 60 cubic feet!

Asked:

Block Volume Formula

The formula for the volume of a block is the length times the width times the height.

Volume =  the length × the width × the height

Our first step is to find the value of x.

Volume =  the length × the width × the height

[tex]60 = (3x - 2) (2x + 1) (x + 1)\\60 = (6x^2 + 3x - 4x - 2) (x + 1)\\60 = (6x^2 - x - 2) (x + 1)\\60 = 6x^3 - x^2 - 2x + 6x^2 - x - 2\\60 = 6x^3 + 5x^2 - 3x - 2\\6x^3 + 5x^2 - 3x - 2 - 60 = 0\\6x^3 + 5x^2 - 3x - 62 = 0\\(x - 2)(6x^2 + 17x + 31) = 0\\x = 2[/tex]

The second step, substitute the value of x into the equation for length, width and height.

Substitute the x values ​​to get the length, the width, and the height.

The length

[tex]= (3x - 2) ft\\\\ = (3 (2) - 2) ft\\\\ = (6 - 2) ft\\\\ = 4 ft[/tex]

The width

[tex]= (2x + 1) ft\\ \\= (2*2+1) ft\\ \\= (4 + 1) ft\\\\= 5 ft[/tex]

The height

[tex](x + 1) ft\\ = (2 + 1) ft\\ = 3 ft[/tex]

So, the cake has the length is 4 ft, the width is 5 ft, and the height is 3 ft.
