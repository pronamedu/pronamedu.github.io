Direct Proportion

Two quantities are said to be in direct proportion if an increase in one also leads to an increase in the other quantity, and vice-versa. For example, if a ∝ b, this implies if 'a' increases, 'b' will also increase, and if 'a' decreases, 'b' will also decrease.

29. Can you give me examples of Partitive Proportion word problems with solution?


Sure, Aestheticallypl...

For example:

I gave $780 to Marinette and Alya. They shared the money in a ratio of 6:4 respectively. How much will Marinette get in the share? How about Alya?

Steps:
1. Add the 2 quantities of the ratio.
2. Divide the total amount by the sum of the 2 quantities.
3. Multiply the quotient to the quantities.
Solution:
1) 6+4= 10
2) $780 / 10= $78
3) Marinette: $78 * 6= $468
Alya: $78 * 4= $312
