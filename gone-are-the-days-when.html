11. what would be the effect of these non renewable resources when they are gone​


WHAT WOULD BE THE EFFECT OF THESE NON-RENEWABLE RESOURCES WHEN THEY ARE GONE?

If by chance, non-renewable resources are gone then that only means we, humans are in big trouble. Almost all of the Antarctic ice will melt if we burn all of the remaining fossil fuels on Earth, according to a new study published today in Science Advances, potentially causing sea levels to rise by up to 200 feet, enough to flood most major cities on the planet.

When natural resources, both renewable and non-renewable, become scarce because they are used up faster than they can be replenished, resource depletion occurs. Water, fossil fuel consumption, forests, and fishing are all typically related to the term "resource depletion."

Long before the worst effects of global warming, the depletion of scarce resources including oil, gas, drinking water, and minerals are likely to have an impact on global GDP. In combination, these two factors are projected to severely limit global food supply, particularly in nations with high population densities or limited fertile areas.
