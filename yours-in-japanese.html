1. t B. Japanese ArtsWhat was your impression of Japanese art and culture?​


Answer:

What was your impression of Japanese art and culture?

By ‘was’, I presume you mean is… Unless you are asking about an impression I had in the past at some time? The impression upon me as a western person, has always been that traditional ethnic Japanese social behavioral traits, customs and rules were, throughout the country’s history. and to a diminished degree, are, uniquely quaint and rigid, bound by formality, a strict class structure and almost robotic recognition of deference and obedience at the price of hypocrisy and life itself. (by this I mean. for example, acts such as bowing to someone you are about to kill etc.) This cultural standard is reflected in the arts of japan, such as No theatre, and the visual arts, until the representational imagery, epitomized by wood-block prints etc, gave way to western style realism. For much of its history Japan geographically, and its social mentality were largely in a self-imposed state of ‘lockdown’ until the last quarter of the 19th century, although certain innovations like guns and guarded acceptance of a few foreign travellers had taken place since the 17th century. I see this relatively sudden propulsion, of what was virtually a medieval culture, into a modern global co- existence as an explanation for what are seen as both negative wartime behaviour and the remarkably positive advances made within such a short space of time.
