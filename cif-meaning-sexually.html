Answer:

C = 140°

C + D = 180°

Step-by-step explanation:

Supplementary angles are when the sum of two angles is equal to 180°.

So since angle C and D are supplementary angles, their sum should be equals to 180°.

We get 140° by subtracting 40° to 180° (180° - 40° = 140°)

A bond distance (or bond length) is the distance between the nuclei of two bonded atoms along the straight line joining the nuclei. Bond distances are measured in Ångstroms (1 Å = 10–10 m) or picometers (1 pm = 10–12 m, 100 pm = 1 Å).
