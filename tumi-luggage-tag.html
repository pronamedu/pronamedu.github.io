13. a net force of 20newtons to the right acted upon a 5kg luggage for a period of 5 seconds.what is the acceleration of the luggage?​


Solving for Acceleration

Answer:

The acceleration of the luggage is 4  [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex] .

   

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

    F = 20 N

    m = 5 kg

    t = 5 s

Required: acceleration (a)

Formula:

We derive the formula of acceleration (a) from the formula of force (F), which is F = ma.

To derive the formula, we isolate the quantity on one side of the equation. To do this, we divide both sides of the equation by m:

    F = ma

    [tex]\frac{F}{m}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{ma}{m}[/tex]

    [tex]\frac{F}{m}[/tex] = a

Thus,

    a = F/m

Solution:

    a = 20 kg  [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex]  / 5 kg

    a = 4 [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex]

It is important to note that newton is a unit of force that is equivalent to kg  [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex] .
