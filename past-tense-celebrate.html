PAST PERFECT TENSE

- A verb tense used to talk about actions that were completed before some point in the past.

- Is for talking about something that happened before something else.

PRESENT PERFECT TENSE

- A verb tense that is traditionally formed in English with have and a past participle and that expresses an action or state begun in the past and completed at the time of speaking or continuing in the present.

FUTURE PERFECT TENSE

- A verb tense or construction used to describe an event that is expected or planned to happen before a time of reference in the future.

PAST PROGRESSIVE TENSE

- Indicates continuing action, something that was happening, going on, at some point in the past.

PRESENT PROGRESSIVE TENSE

- Indicates continuing action, something going on now. This tense is formed with the helping verb, in the present tense, plus the present participle of the verb.
