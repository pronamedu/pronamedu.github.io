11. A sample of sulfur dioxide occupies a volume of 652 mL at 40oC and 720 mm Hg. What volume will the sulfur dioxide occupy at 760 mm Hg and zero degrees Celsius?


Explanation:

This is a combined gas law. Therefore, the equation is  [tex]\frac{P_{1} V_{1} }{T_{1} }[/tex]= [tex]\frac{P_{2}V_{2} }{T_{2} }[/tex]

And your given is:

P1 = 720 mmHg

V1 = 652 mL (convert to liters) -> 652 mL / 1000 = 0.652L

T1 = 40 degrees Celsius (convert to Kelvin) -> 40 + 273.15 = 313.15 K

P2 = 760 mmHg

V2 = ???

T2 = 0 degrees Celsius (convert to Kelvin) -> 0 + 273.15 = 273.15 K

Now, let's derive the formula so that V2 is isolated so that it's easier to solve.

[tex](\frac{P_{1} V_{1} }{T_{1} })(\frac{T_{2} }{P_{2} })[/tex] = [tex]\frac{P_{2}V_{2} }{T_{2} } (\frac{T_{2}}{P_{2}} )[/tex]

V2 = [tex]\frac{P_{1} V_{1}T_{2} }{T_{1} P_{2} }[/tex]

Then, substitute all the given values

V2 = (720mmHg * 0.652L * 273.15K) / (760mmHg *313.15K)

and calculate

V2 = 0.539 L is the volume :)

~ (round to the lowest significant number) 0.5L

16. a gas measures 10 ml at 27 degrees celsius and 760 mmhg what will be the volume of the gas at 15 degrees celsius and 750 mm Hg?​


Answer:

10mL

Explanation:

When working with gas laws, the temperature is always in Kelvins. To get Kelvins from a Celsius temperature, add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature.

Given:

P1 = 760 mmHg

V1 = 10mL

T1 = 27°C + 273.15 = 300 K

P2 = 750 mmHg

T2 = 15°C + 273.15 = 288 K

Missing:

V2 = ?

Solution:

Rearrange the equation to isolate V2. Substitute the known values into the equation and solve.

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

V2 = P1V1T2/T1P2

= 760mmHg × 10mL × 288 K/300 K × 750mmHg

V2 = 2,188,800 mL / 225,000

V2 = 9.7 or 10mL

Area of the circle as we know is A= π r²
knowing that the unshaded region is 1/4 of it then we can say that that the shaded region is 3/4 of the area of the circle. then you'll have it as:
A=3/4 π r²
r is given as 28mm. substituting the given you'll have:
A=3/4 π (28mm)²
