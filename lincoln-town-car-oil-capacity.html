23. When Max heats 0.025kg of oil in the pan , the oil temperature increases by 44° say the boiler specific heat capacity of 1670j/kg Calculate the energy transferred to the oil


Answer:

Explanation:

The formula to calculate energy transferred is:

energy transferred = mass * specific heat capacity * temperature change

Given:

mass (m) = 0.025 kg

specific heat capacity (c) = 1670 J/kg

temperature change (ΔT) = 44 °C = 44 K (since 1 °C = 1 K)

Using the formula:

energy transferred = m * c * ΔT

energy transferred = 0.025 kg * 1670 J/kg * 44 K

energy transferred = 1837.5 J

Therefore, the energy transferred to the oil is 1837.5 J.

26. At 10:30 AM a car started traveling from Town A to Town B at an average speed of 65 km/h. Another car left Town B at the same time and traveled towards Town A at an average speed of 70 km/h. The distance between Town A and Town B is 810 km. At what time would the two cars meet?​


Answer:

The two cars would meet at approximately 6:22 PM, 7 hours and 52 minutes after they started their journey. This is calculated by dividing the total distance between Town A and Town B (810 km) by the difference in the speeds of the two cars (65 km/h minus 70 km/h). That result is then multiplied by 60 to convert it to minutes, and 10:30 AM is added to that to get the time at which the two cars will meet.
