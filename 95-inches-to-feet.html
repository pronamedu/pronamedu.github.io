11. 1. A researcher wishes to estimate the proportion of adults males who are less than 5 feet 5 inches tall. She wants to be 95% confident that her estimate is within 5% of the true proportion. If no estimate of the sample proportion available, how large should be the sample size?


Answer:

Sample size estimation

To determine the necessary sample size, we can use the formula:

n = [(z*σ/E)^2]*p(1-p)

where:

n is the sample size

z is the z-score associated with the desired confidence level (in this case, 1.96 for 95% confidence)

σ is the estimated standard deviation of the population proportion (since we don't have an estimate of the sample proportion, we can use 0.5 as a conservative estimate for p(1-p))

E is the desired margin of error (in this case, 0.05)

p is the estimated proportion of males who are less than 5 feet 5 inches tall (since we don't have an estimate, we can use 0.5 as a conservative estimate)

Plugging in the values, we get:

n = [(1.96*0.5/0.05)^2]*0.5(1-0.5) ≈ 384.16

Therefore, the researcher should sample at least 385 adult males to be 95% confident that her estimate of the proportion of males who are less than 5 feet 5 inches tall is within 5% of the true proportion.

15. The distribution of heights of adult american men is approximately normal with mean 69 inches and standard deviation 2.5 inches. between what heights do the middle 95% of men fall?


Standard Deviation

The standard deviation reveals how far data deviates from the mean. While a high standard deviation indicates that the data are more spread, a low standard deviation suggests that the data are clustered around the mean.

Problem:

American adult men's height distribution is roughly typical, with a mean of 69 inches and a standard variation of 2.5 inches. What range of heights does the middle 95% of males inhabit?

Solution:

Step 1: 95%/2= 47.5%.

Step 2: Track down the z score that equals 47.5%. That comes out to 1.96.

Step 3. Determine the 95% confidence interval using the formula: 69±2.5*1.96 = 69±4.9.

Step 4: 69+4.9=73.9. 69–4.9= 64.1.

Therefore, the majority of 95% of men fall within the range of 64.1 and 73.9 or in the range of 64 – 74 inches in height.

Additional information:

Contrary to distance and average true range, variance is a measure of distribution that takes into consideration the distribution of all the data points in a data collection. It is the dispersion index that is most usually used, along with the confidence interval, which is simply the measure of dispersion.

20. A given set of data with normal distribution has a mean height of 70 inches and a standard deviation of 3 inches. What would be an appropriate height range for 95% of all people in the sample?a) 66.5 inches to 73.5 inchesb) 64 inches to 76 inchesc) 61 inches to 79 inchesd) 36.75 inches to 103.25 inches​


MULTIPLE CHOICES: DISTRIBUTION

In a normal distribution, 95% of the population is within 2 standard deviations of the mean.

Given values:

[tex]\textsf{Mean}\; \mu=70\; \sf inches[/tex][tex]\textsf{Standard deviation}\; \sigma=3\; \sf inches[/tex]

Given that the mean height is 70 inches and the standard deviation is 3 inches:

[tex]\implies \mu -2 \sigma = 70-2(3)=64[/tex]

[tex]\implies \mu+2 \sigma = 70+2(3)=76[/tex]

Therefore, an appropriate height range for 95% of all people in the sample would be:

[tex] \textsf{64 to 76 inches.}[/tex]

Where:
