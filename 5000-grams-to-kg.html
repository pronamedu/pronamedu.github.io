13. how much biomass of chicken can 5000 kg of corns support?


Good Day...

Question:

How much biomass of chicken can 5000 kg of corns support?

Answer:

5000 Kg of corns can support 500 Kg of chicken

In the pyramid of biomass, only 10 percent of the biomass can be transferred to next trophic level. Corns occupies the first trophic level since it is considered as the producers. Corns has the ability to make their own foods by the process known as photosynthesis. Chicken belongs to the second trophic level as the primary consumer of corns. Corns can only transfer 10% of its biomass to the chicken since 90 % of the biomass of corns are used in metabolic purposes like growth. Which means, chicken can only get 500 Kg out of 5000 Kg biomass of corn.

5000 Kg × 0.1 (10%) = 500 Kg

Corns 5000 Kg  →  can support 500 kg of chicken

22. How much biomass of chicken can 5000 kg of corn support...


Corn   (10%)    →    Chicken

  ↓                            ↓

5000 kg         →    500 kg

In the diagram above (biomass pyramid), the corn can only  support 500 kg of biomass to the chicken.

Explanation:

The amount of biomass every time it passes a certain trophic level in biomass pyramid will be 10% only from the actual amount in the lower trophic level. If the corn have 5000 kg biomass, 10% of it will only proceed to the next trophic level which is now the chicken. The remaining 90% are utilized during growth and development and also for metabolic purposes. The amount and competition is quite intense on the upper trophic level since the amount of energy, biomass, food are limited.
