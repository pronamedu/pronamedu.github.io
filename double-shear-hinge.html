13. Bent-Handled Shears/Dressmaker Shear.


Answer:

Dressmaker's shears are heavy duty scissors that are designed specifically with the needs of seamstresses in mind. These shears are designed to cut efficiently and smoothly through a wide variety of materials, and they are typically forged so that they can withstand a lifetime of use. Many craft stores sell them in varying qualities, and they can also be ordered directly from manufacturers; when ordered from the manufacturer, they may come with a warranty or sharpening service.

The distinguishing feature of dressmaker's shears is that the handle is offset from the blades, allowing them to be used to cut fabric against a flat surface without distortion. Like other shears, one of the finger rings is typically larger than the other. In industry terms, the finger rings on scissors and shears are known as “bows,” and on this type of shears, the large bow is usually on the side of the blade that points towards the floor when the shears are in use.

16. what is the relationship of butterfly hinges to Hinge theorem ​


butterfly hinges and the hinge theorem are not directly related concepts.

butterfly hinges are a type of hinge used in woodworking to attach a lid or flap to a box or chest. these hinges have two rectangular wings that are attached to the lid and box, and a pivot pin that joins the wings together. when the lid is opened, the wings of the hinge fold together like a butterfly's wings.

the hinge theorem, on the other hand, is a theorem in geometry that relates the side lengths of two triangles that share a common side and have two pairs of congruent angles. specifically, the theorem states that if two triangles have congruent angles adjacent to the same side, then the ratio of the lengths of the sides opposite those angles is the same for both triangles.

therefore, while both butterfly hinges and the hinge theorem involve the concept of hinges, they are not directly related to each other.
