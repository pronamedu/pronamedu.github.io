Not long ago, plain old SPF 30 seemed like all we needed, the formula de rigueur. Now SPF 50, 70, even 100 are everywhere. The SPF rating system made its debut in the early 1960s, but the numbers game has since become something of an arms race; the Food and Drug Administration even proposed in 2007 to cap the SPF system at 50 to keep things from spiraling out of control. What happened to the sunscreen industry, and what does it mean that SPF values have inflated over time?It's hard to know what to make of SPF inflation without first going into how the system works. The number that makes up the SPF rating is based on a ratio. If you take an average person with untreated skin and calculate how long it takes for them to develop a sunburn -- about 15 minutes -- then the SPF value tells you how many times weaker that person's UV exposure would be after applying the sunscreen. So, using sunscreen with an SPF of 15 means that an average person would be protected 15 times more than if they'd gone without.

Answer:

1.The newsprint in a newspaper can vary in a few different ways, depending on the particular newspaper. Generally, the newsprint used in the front section of a newspaper is usually a heavier, thicker paper than what is used in the other sections. This is because the front section contains the most important news stories and needs to last longer, whereas the other sections may contain less important stories or stories of more general interest.

2.We use newspaper when screening the UV out because newspaper is a natural source of shade and it is relatively inexpensive. Newspaper also blocks out a significant amount of UV radiation, which helps to protect us from the harmful effects of the sun.

3.The purpose of sunscreen is to protect the skin from the harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation of the sun. Sunscreen works by absorbing, reflecting, and/or scattering UV radiation, thereby preventing it from reaching the skin. Sunscreen also helps to prevent sunburn, skin cancer, and premature aging.

24. what property is this If UV=KL and KL=6,then UV=6​


Answer:

The situation is described as the transitive property of Equality

Step-by-step explanation:

Transitive property states that if one value is equal to the second value, and the second value is equal to the third value, then the first value should be equal to the third value.

Different Properties of Equality Numbers

1. Commutative Property

2. Distributive Property

3. Associative Property

4. Reflexive Property

5. Symmetric Property

6. Transitive Property

In notation form, Transitive property is  "if [tex]a=b[/tex] and [tex]b=c[/tex], then [tex]a=c[/tex]".

So the situation "If [tex]UV=KL[/tex] and [tex]KL=6[/tex],then [tex]UV=6[/tex]" is justified by the Transitive property of Equality.
