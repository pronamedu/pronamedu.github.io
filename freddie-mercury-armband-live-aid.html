6. A professor is three times as old as one of his pupils, Freddie. Four years ago, he was four times as old as Freddie was at the time. How old is Freddie?


Answer:

Freddie is 12 years old

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us represent the age of the professor as P and of Freddie as F at present. We can then represent the given in the following equations:

P = 3F

(P - 4) = 4(F - 4)

Since the first equation expresses P in terms of F already, we can substitute it into the second equation,

3F - 4 = 4(F - 4)

3F - 4 = 4F - 16

-4 + 16 = 4F - 3F

12 = F

Therefore, Freddie is 12 years old at present.
