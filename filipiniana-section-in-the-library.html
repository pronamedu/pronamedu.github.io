What is a library?

— a library is a collection of sources, resources, and services, and the structure in which it is housed it is organized for use and maintained by a public body,an institution, or a private individual.

What are the sections in the library?

— There are various sections in the library although big libraries have more sections. what I'm going to give you are just basic sections commonly found even in small libraries:

1. General Reference - where you can find general encyclopedia works like dictionaries, almanacs, atlases, directories, handbooks, manual, indexes and abstracts, etc.

2. Periodicals section - where you can find local and foreign journals, magazines, newspapers, government publications, and other forms of serial publication, vertical files such as pamphlets, brochures and newspaper clippings.

3. General Collection/Circulation Section - Where you can find the main collection of the library like books on philosophy, Religion, social sciences, language, arts, pure science, applied sciences, literature, history, and geography.

4. Filipiniana Section - Where you can find books published within bthe Philippines and written about the Philippines, it's history, people, government, and culture.

21. why we need to consider the different sections in a library?​


They’re all important because they all perform different functions.

Circulation — Allows you to check out and return books. May serve the function of the Reserve section. Makes sure books are returned to their proper place on the shelves. Picks up books from the floor(s) that were used during the day and returns them to the shelves.

Reference — Maintains books that are frequently consulted, is staffed by specialized librarians who will help answer questions and occasionally do specialized research for you.

Acquisitions — Purchases new books and magazines, makes sure they’re paid for.

Cataloging — Prepares books for addition to the library’s collections. This is likely the department responsible for repairing damaged books as well as bindery functions. May also be responsible for withdrawing books from the collection.

Serials — Receives and maintains newspapers and magazines and other publications that are issued on a regular (and sometimes irregular) schedule.

Documents — Maintains government publications and map collections.

Children's/Young Adult — May be two separate departments; specializes in books for younger readers. Often holds story times and other programs of special interest to young readers.

Special Collections/Archives — Books on a theme of significance to the area where the library is located. May be historical in nature and/or specific to a certain author.

Friends of the Library — May or may not have their own section for sales of gift items and donated books.

There may also be sections specific to various subjects which may or may not have their own librarians in charge of them. The people in charge of the business of the library will have offices as well.

30. what the filipiniana present​


Answer:

based on the definition by Isagani Medina from "Collection Building: Filipiniana", in his In Developing Special Library Collections, Filipiniana: Proceedings (November 1992), refers to Philippine-related books and non-book materials (such as Culture; figurines, games, fashion and others). The materials may be produced inside or outside the Philippines by Filipino or non-Filipino authors. The product could be literature written in any of the languages and dialects in the Philippines or a foreign language.
