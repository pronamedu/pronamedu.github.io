8. what are 3 adaptive advantages of mammalian anatomy


Answer:

What are 3 adaptive advantages of mammalian anatomy

Mammals are animals that have distinct characteristics that set them apart from others. They belong to class Mammalia which indicates that they are the only group of animals that have mammary glands which are responsible for producing milk. This milk is used during the lactation period which serves as the primary food for the offspring. Here are some of the characteristics of mammals which serve as their advantage over the other classes of animals in the animal kingdom:

They give birth to a live young - most mammals give birth to a live young. The least is one but some mammals can give birth to up to 10 offspring. This is one of their advantages because of the assurance that their genetic line will continue. Unlike other animals, they lay many eggs because of the high mortality rate after they hatch. There are mammals who give birth in eggs but they are still considered mammals because of the presence of mammary glands in their body. In line with this, the mammary glands of all mammals serve their purpose of providing primary food for the young ones. These glands produce milk during the gestation so that when the young one is lactating, the body has already adapted to its new purpose. They are warm-blooded animals - this is one of the good things about mammals. Regardless of their species and families, all mammals are warm blooded and can easily survive anywhere. They can still be active in cold places, that is why you can see mammals in any part of the world. They also have high metabolism because of their activities that makes them search for food both day and night with varying temperature levels. Unlike other classes of animals which are nocturnal too, they don't search for food at daytime because their body cannot withstand the heat from the sun. They have four-chambered hearts - this is one of the adaptations of mammals. Because of being warm blooded that causes them to maintain body heat at a certain level by eating and utilizing nutrients, their heart has four chambers that deliver oxygen from lungs through our blood.

Examples of mammals according to body structurePlacental mammals - they bear their offspring in their placenta inside their body for a period of time and gives birth with their functional parts. Most mammals are placental ones like dogs, cats, pigs, horses, whales, and more. Marsupials - have a special pouch that enables them to carry their young ones in going places. They are placental mammals too but their difference is that they have this pouch. Examples are koalas, kangaroos, opossum, and more. Monotremes - are mammals who have mammary glands but gives birth to their offspring in an egg.

Contribution of mammalsThey are important to maintain the balance in the ecosystem. Some mammals serve as hunter and predators while others are the prey. According to their food preferences, a mammal can be herbivore, insectivore, carnivore, or omnivore. The nature also benefits from their symbiotic relationship to others. Plants continue to grow because of the fruits and seeds that fall in the ground after a mammal eat it. They serve as pets for relaxation and therapy. Some mammals can live harmoniously among humans and can adapt easily. Some of them are used for protection, while others are for clinical therapy. Dogs and cats are the common animals that serve these purposes. They contribute to the welfare of the society - many products nowadays also came from animals. One of the most common products is the milk. Dairy products came from the milk production of mammals like cows and goats. Others provide meat for food like pigs, cows, cattle, sheep, and more. Others contribute by their fur and skin that is used for making clothes and accessories.
