27. How many gallons of paint are required to paint the walls of a building 33 ft. long, 31 ft. wide, and 47 ft. high, if 1 gallon of paint will cover 500 ft² of the wall?


Answer:

12.032 gallons of paint are required to paint the walls of the building.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Write the given

Length of the room= 33 ft Width of the room= 31 ftHeight of the room= 47 ft

Step 2: Find the surface area of the wall

The building is considered as rectangular prism, which has six faces. The area of the six faces are as follows: two 31 ft by 33 ft "rooftop" and base, and two 33 ft by 47 ft and two 31 ft by 47 ft walls. [tex]surface area of wall= (2)(33 ft)(47 ft) +(2)(31 ft)(47 ft)[/tex][tex]surface area of wall=6016 ft^{2}[/tex]

Step 3: Find the number of gallons necessary to paint the walls.

Use the relationship: 1 gallon of paint covers 500 square ft of wall[tex]Gallons of paint=surface area of wall*(\frac{gallon of paint}{surface area of wall})[/tex][tex]Gallons of paint=(6016ft^{2} )*(\frac{1 gallon}{500ft^{2} })[/tex][tex]Gallons of paint=12.032 gallons[/tex]

Therefore, 12.032 gallons of paint will be needed to paint the walls of the building.
