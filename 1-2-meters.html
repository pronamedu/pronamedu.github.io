Answer:

First, we'll convert the mixed numbers to fractions.

1 1/2 meters = 3/2 meters

1 2/5 meters = 7/5 meters

Now we can add the lengths:

3/2 + 7/5 + 5/2 = (35 + 72 + 52)/(25) = 38/10 = 19/5 meters

Now we can divide by the number of lengths to find the average:

19/5 meters / 3 = 19/15 meters

So the average length of 1 1/2 meters, 1 2/5 meters, and 2 1/2 meters is 19/15 meters.

12. ACTIVITY #21. DUPLE METER 2. TRIPLE METER3. QUADRUPLE METER PASAGOT PO​


Answer:

In meter, the meter signature signifies the grouping of notes/rests in every measure. The time signature is consist of two numbers, the upper one and the  lower one. The upper number tells the number of beats to a measure, while the lower number tells the kind of beat that receives the beat (for a simple meter, and the subdivided meats for compound meter)

Explanation:

1. Duple meter is a kind of  meter where the primary division of beats in a measure is two. An example of this is 2/4 (duple-simple) and 6/8 (duple-compound). In duple-simple meter, the upper number in the time signature is as is. There are two quarter notes in a measure. While in 6/8 compound meter, there are six eight notes in every measure, where one eight note receives the division of one beat. In compound meter, a dotted note receives one basic beat. So in 6/8, a dotted quarter note receives one basic beat, while the subdivided beats of six is received by the eight note. Since 6 is to be equally grouped into three, resulting to two basic beats, making it a duple  meter.

2. Triple meter is a kind of meter where the primary division of beats in a measure is three. An example is 3/4 (triple-simple) and 9/4 (triple-compound. In 3/4, there are 3 quarter notes in a measure. In 9/4, there are 9 quarter notes in a measure, a dotted half note receives the subdivision of  9 beats where a dotted half note receives one basic beat. The nine is equally grouped by three's resulting to three, making it a triple meter.

3. Quadruple meter is a kind of meter where the primary division of beats in a measure in four. An example is 4/4 (quadruple-simple) and 12/4. In 4/4, there are 4 quarter notes in a measure where one quarter note receives one beat. In 12/4, there are 12 subdivisions of beats received by a quarter note. 12 when equally grouped with three will result to four, making it a quadruple compound.

18. A rectangular water tank is 1/2 meter wide, 1 meter long and 1 1/2 meters high. If is half filled, how water does it contain?


Answer:

The volume of water at the tank is 0.375 cubic meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Write the given

Rectangular water tank is 1/2 meter wide (0.5 m)Rectangular water tank is 1 meter longRectangular water tank is 1 1/2 meters high (1.5 m)

Step 2: Identify the formula/s that will be utilized

Volume of rectangular prism is equal to (length)(width)(height)

Step 3: Find the volume of the water tank

The volume of the water tank is (1 m)(0.5 m)(1.5 m) which is equal to 0.75 cubic meters.

Step 4: Solve for the volume of water the tank contains

The tank is half-filled, which means the water is only half of the volume of the tank. Volume of water is (0.5)(0.75 cubic meters) is equal to 0.375 cubic meters.

Therefore, the volume of water at the tank is 0.375 cubic meters.
