14. a square pyramid has 10cm base and 10cm height..what is the surface area of the square pyramid..​


Answer:

surface area of the square pyramid is approximately 313.1 square centimeters.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the surface area of a square pyramid, we need to find the area of each face and add them up.

Since the base of the pyramid is a square with 10 cm sides, the area of the base is:

Area of base = (side length)^2 = 10^2 = 100 cm^2

To find the area of each triangular face, we first need to find the slant height of the pyramid. We can use the Pythagorean theorem to do this:

slant height = √(height^2 + (1/2 × base)^2)

slant height = √(10^2 + (1/2 × 10)^2)

slant height = √(100 + 25)

slant height = √125

slant height = 5√5

Now that we know the slant height, we can find the area of each triangular face using the formula:

Area of triangular face = (1/2 × base × slant height)

Area of each triangular face = (1/2 × 10 × 5√5) = 25√5

Finally, we can find the total surface area by adding up the area of the base and the area of the four triangular faces:

Total surface area = Area of base + 4 × Area of triangular face

Total surface area = 100 + 4 × 25√5

Total surface area = 100 + 100√5

Total surface area ≈ 313.1 cm^2

Therefore, the surface area of the square pyramid is approximately 313.1 square centimeters.
