9. Jermaine buys T-shirts for $6 each and marks up the price by 45% How much profit does Jermaine make from each T-shirt sold


Answer:

The profit for each T-shirt sold is [tex]\$2.7[/tex].

To solve the profit, construct a mathematical model to represent the situation.

Mathematical  modelling is the process of translating the situation in a mathematical contexts.

Advantages of mathematical modelling

1. It is easier to formulate ideas and identify the unknown variables.

2. Mathematics is a precise language, with well-defined process of manipulation.

3. All the results have been proved over time.

Steps in constructing the mathematical model

1. Assume a variable for the unknown.

2. Formulate a mathematical model that best describes the problem.

3. Solve the model analytically.

4. Interpret the resulting value.

For additional information about constructing a mathematical model, check the link https://brainly.ph/question/1769208.

In this case, let [tex]C[/tex] be the profit cost. The statement "marks up the price by 45%" means that new price has increased by 45% of the original price. In the context of mathematical modeling, the new price has an additional amount of [tex]45\%\times{6}[/tex].

To know more examples about mark up prices, go to https://brainly.ph/question/2480346.

Then the mathematical model should be [tex]C=45\%\times 6[/tex].

To simplify the right side of the equation, rewrite [tex]45\%[/tex] in decimal. There are two possible ways to do this, either we divide 45 by 100 or we move the percent symbol two places to the left and change it to a point. Lastly, simplify the result.

                                             [tex]\begin{aligned}C&=.45\times6\\C&=2.7\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that the new price is [tex]\$2.7[/tex].

If an event has m possible outcomes, and when it has been done, a second event has n possible outcomes, and when it has been done, a third event has p possible outcome, and so forth, then the total number of outcomes is the product of m, n, P, and so forth.
