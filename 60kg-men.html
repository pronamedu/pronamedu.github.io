6. what is the age of person if he/she has 60kg​


Answer:

The weight is 588 N and its equivalent to pounds is 132.18 lbs.

Explanation:

The weight force is the force of gravity acting on a body or object. Here, we are going to get the weight force or gravity acting upon a person with a mass of 60 kg.

The weight force has a formula of:

Fw = mg

where

Fw     is the weight force, unit is in Newtons (N)

m       is the mass, unit is in kg

g        is the acceleration due to gravity, equal to 9.8  

For the given information:

m = 60 kg

g = 9.8  

Solving for the weight force:

Using the information above, substitute it to the formula we get:

Fw = mg

Fw = (60kg)(9.8 )

Fw = 588 Newtons

Using the conversion: 1N = 0.2248 lb, convert 588 Newtons to pounds, we get:

588  N x  = 132.18 lbs

Therefore, the weight force of the person with a mass of 60 kg is 588 N which is equivalent to 132.18 lbs.

15. what is the weight of 60kg at sea level?​


Explanation:

Ok physics 101. 60 kg is Mass. So if you weighed 60kg on surface, you don't actually lose mass as you go in space.

here is the math:

On surface of earth, you get g=9.81m/s² acceleration pulling you down. That gave you 60kg reading on the weighing scale. you would need to go high enough to get 0.5g of the gravitational pull. The gravitational force is calculated as

Force = ( G * M1 * M2 )/d²

where G is the gravitational constant, d is the distance between the two objects weighing M1 & M2.

Now if earth is M1, the acceleration would be (G * M1)/d²

You need that to be halved.

distance has to be (1.414 * d) where d is the radius of the earth.average radius of earth is 6371km. So you would need to go about 2637.6km above earth's surface to halve your weight.
