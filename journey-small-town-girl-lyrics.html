12. A truck traveled 168 kilometers from Town A to Town B at an averagespeed of 56 kilometers per hour. It then traveled back from Town B toTown A at an average speed of 42 kilometers per hour. What time willits journey end if it started from Town A at 5:30 p.m.?​


Answer:

The train will arrive back at Town A at 12:30 a.m.

Step-by-step explanation:

From Town A to Town B

Knowing the distance between town A and town B (168 km) and the speed it travels from town A to B (56 km/hr), we can solve for the time it take the train to reach town B from town A. Using the speed formula, we can derive the formula to get the time it took the train to transfer from one town to another.

From the speed formula we can derive the formula for time as follows:

speed = [tex]\frac{distance}{time}[/tex]    ⇒  time = [tex]\frac{distance}{speed}[/tex]

Using the derived formula for time, we just input the given that we have.

time = [tex]\frac{168 km}{56 km/hr}[/tex] = 3 hours

It took the train to travel from town A to town B 3 hours at the speed of 56 km/hr.

From Town B to Town A

Knowing the distance between town B and town A (168 km) and the speed it travels from town B to A (42 km/hr), we can solve for the time it take the train to go back to town A from town B. We can use the derived formula we had awhile ago.

Using the derived formula for time, we just input the given that we have.

time = [tex]\frac{168 km}{42 km/hr}[/tex]= 4 hours

It took the train to travel from town B to town A 4 hours at the speed of 42 km/hr.

Total Travel Time

We know from above that the total travel time of the train is 7 hours by putting together the travel time from Town A to Town B and then back to Town A. Since it started it's journey at 5:30 p.m., we just have to add 7 hours to it which will give us 12:30 a.m. as the train's arrival time back to town A.

23. A van and a car left Town A for Town B at the same time. The car traveled at an average speed of 56 kilometers per hour and reached Town B in 3 hours. The van was 3 kilometers away from Town B when the car completed the journey. What was the average speed of the van for the journey made in 3 hours.​


Answer:

The car was traveling at an average speed of 55 hours

56 km times 3 hours= 168 km

55 km times 3 hours= 165 km

168 km - 165 km = 3 km

Step-by-step explanation:

Answer:

55 kph

Step-by-step explanation:

The average speed is not merely the average of two speeds but is computed by the total distance and time,

Average speed = Total distance / Total time

To compute for the total distance of the van, we subtract 3 from the total distance of the car,

Total distance = 56(3) + 3 = 168 - 3 = 165

We can then substitute it to the formula,

Average speed = 165 / 3 = 55

Therefore, the van's average speed is 55 kph.
