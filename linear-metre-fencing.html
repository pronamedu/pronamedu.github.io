3. Charles needs enough fencing to enclose a rectangular garden with a perimeter of 140 metres. If the width of his garden is to be 30 metres, write the equation that can be used to solve for the length of the garden.


perimeter= 2L + 2w
    where L is the length and w is the width
given the perimeter and the width, we can now calculate the length

140 m = 2L + 2 (30 m)
140 m = 2 L + 60 m

isolate the variable L to one side of the equation by transposing the constant to the other side. we get:

140 m - 60 m = 2L
80 m = 2L

divide both side by 2 to get L

40 m = L
Length is equal to 40 meters

11. (5) The breadth of a rectangular field is 15 m. The length of the rectangular field is 3 times as long as its breadth. Each metre of fence costs $4. How much will it cost to fence the field?


Answer: $240.00

Step-by-step explanation:

Given the breadth of the rectangular field which is 15 m, and the length, which is 3 (15m) = 45m, a metre of fence costs $4.

Cost to fence the field = cost of each meter of fence (breadth + length)

Cost = $4 (15 m + 45 m)

        = $4 (60 m)

        = $240.00

Therefore, it will cost $240.00 to fence the rectangular field.

20. In three minutes Gabrielle can walk 300 metres. In five minutes Mara can walk 600 metres. They started walking at the same time along the same track. How far apart were they after 4 minutes? A 80 metres B 100 metres C 120 metres D 300 metres


The Answer is A 80 metres


Gabriel's Speed = 300m/3min = 100m/min

Mara's Speed = 600m/5min = 120m/min

Speed Difference = 120 - 100 = 20m/min

Distance = Speed Difference X Time = 20m/min X 4min = 80m

Distance each walked in 4 minutes:

Gabrielle:

3:300 = 4:x

3x/3 = 1,200/3

x = 400 meters

Mara:

5:600 = 4:x

5x/5 = 2,400/5

x = 480 meters

Difference of distance walked in 4 minutes:

480 - 400 = 80

ANSWER: (A) 80 meters

26. Ten workers are fencing a 25 m by 28 m stadium. They had finished fencing 95 m. Howmany more metres of fence do they need to build?A. 11 mC. 148 mB. 106 mD. 201 m​


THE METRES OF FENCE THEY NEED TO BUILD

a. 11 m

   The workers must construct an additional 11 meters of barrier.

SOLUTION:

  To find the problem: How many more metres of fence do they need to build?

   So, if the workers are erecting a fence around a stadium, we must first determine the stadium's perimeter.

Formula of the perimeter:

 P stands for perimeter

L stands for Length

W stands for  W

 P=2(L+W)

 P=2(25m+28m)

 P=2(53m)

 P=106 m

The workers were able to complete 95 meters of fencing. As a result, the remaining fence is 11 meter.

  106-95=11 meter

As a result, workers will need to construct 11 extra meters of fencing.

PERIMETERThe route or border that surrounds a shape in geometry is referred to as the perimeter. It can also be described as the length of a shape's outline.When putting up Christmas lights or fencing the rear garden, we frequently come across the boundary. Another example is determining the length of a soccer field's boundary or the amount of crochet or ribbon required to cover the border of a table mat.PERIMETER FORMULA

    P = (L + W) 2 is the perimeter formula for a rectangle, where P stands for perimeter, L for length, and W for width. When given the dimensions of a rectangular shape, all you have to do is input the values of L and W into the formula to find the perimeter.

What is the best method for determining the perimeter? The sides of a little regular shape are measured with a ruler. By adding the lengths of the shape's sides/edges, the perimeter may be calculated.We can use a string of thread to create little irregular shapes by placing it once exactly at the shape's boundary. The shape's of the perimeter is equal to the whole length of the string used along the boundary.
