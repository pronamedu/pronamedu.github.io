What is a streak?

The streak of a mineral refers to the color of its powder produced when it is scraped or scratched across an unweathered surface.

The surface across which the mineral is scraped is known as the "streak plate", this is usually made of unglazed porcelain tile.

Pyrite is called “Fool’s Gold” because it resembles gold to the untrained eye. While pyrite has a brass-yellow color and metallic luster similar to gold, pyrite is brittle and will break rather than bend as gold does. Gold leaves a yellow streak, while pyrite’s streak is brownish black.

30. what is the similarities of color and streak


Answer:

Streak is closely related to color, but is a different property because the color of the mineral may be different than the color of the streak. Streak is actually the color of the powder of a mineral. It is called streak because the proper way to test for streak is to rub a mineral across a tile of white unglazed porcelain and to examine the color of the "streak" left behind. It has proven to be a powerful property because it is generally very consistent from specimen to specimen for a given mineral.
