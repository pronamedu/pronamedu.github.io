17. a chord 18 cm long is perpendicular to a radius of a circle. the distance frome the intersection of chord and radius to the outer end of the radius is 3 cm. Find the length of the radius.​


Answer:

3√10 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

A chord, 18 cm long, is perpendicular to radius of a circle. If the radius is perpendicular to the chord, then the radius bisects the chord equally into two parts.

Since the radius is perpendicular to the chord, the angle of their intersection is 90°. Labeling the chord AB, the center of the circle C, and their intersection D, we can make two right triangles ADC and BDC.

Considering only ADC, the length of AD is 9 cm and the length of the 'cut radius' is 3 cm. To get the hypothenus (radius), use Gougu's theorem a²+b²=c².

9²+3²=81+9=90

90 is the value of c². We're only getting c.

√90=3√10

22. If radius OD is perpendicular to chord AB at C, AC=2(6.5-x) AB=15, find x. ​


Answer;

Step-by-step explanation:

If radius OD is perpendicular to chord AB at point C, then we know that OC (the radius) is equal to the distance from the center of the circle to the midpoint of chord AB (let's call this midpoint point M).

We can use the Pythagorean Theorem to solve for x:

OC = √(AC^2 + (1/2) AB^2)

We know that AC = 2(6.5-x) and AB = 15. So we can substitute these values into the equation:

√(4(6.5-x)^2 + (1/2)(15)^2) = OD = OC = x

Solving for x, we get:

x = √(4(6.5-x)^2 + 56.25)

Squaring both sides of the equation to remove the square root:

x^2 = 4(6.5-x)^2 + 56.25

Expanding the square on the right side of the equation:

x^2 = 4(42.25 - 13x + x^2) + 56.25

Simplifying the equation and solving for x:

x^2 - 13x + 8.5 = 0

(x - 8.5)(x - 1) = 0

So x can be either 1 or 8.5

But given information that OC is radius, it should be a positive value and greater than 0, that means x is 8.5

x = 8.5

So the value of x is 8.5.
