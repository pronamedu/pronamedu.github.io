18. A truck was initially traveling at 25 m/s, NE and accelerated to 32 m/s on a straight path for 69 seconds. Find its acceleration? ​


ACCELERATION

Problem: A truck was initially traveling at 25 m/s, NE and accelerated to 32 m/s on a straight path for 69 seconds. Find its acceleration?

Answer: The acceleration is 0.101 m/s².

[tex]\Large\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

To calculate the acceleration, we must use the formula below:

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \rm \: { \: Acceleration = \dfrac{V_f - \: V_i}{T} \: } }[/tex]

Here, we are given that:

[tex]\tt{(32 \: m/s) =V_{f} = (final \: velocity)}[/tex][tex]\tt{(25 \: m/s) =V_{i} = (initial \: velocity)}[/tex][tex]\tt{(69 \: sec) = T = (elapsed \: time)}[/tex]

Substitute the given values into the formula and solve for the acceleration:

[tex]\sf:\implies{Acceleration= \dfrac{V_f - V_i}{T}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Acceleration= \dfrac{32 \: m/s - 25 \: m/s}{69 \: sec}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Acceleration= \dfrac{7 \: m/s}{69 \: sec}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies \bold{Acceleration= 0.101 \: m/s^2} \\[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration is 0.101 m/s².
