6. For the reaction of 3Na2CO3 (aq) + 2AICI2 (aq) Al2 (CO3) (s) + 6NaCl(aq) what volume of 1.2 M Na2CO3 is needed to react with 5.0 L of 0.30 M AICI3?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of Na₂CO₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 2 moles of AlCl₃.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of} \: \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3 & = 5.0 \: \cancel{\text{L soln}} \times \frac{0.30 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{AlCl}_3}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{L soln}}} \times \frac{3 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3}}{2 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{AlCl}_3}} \times \frac{\text{1 L soln}}{1.2 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{1.9 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of 1.2 M Na₂CO₃ needed is 1.9 L.

13. Calculate the mass of the solid obtained when 16.8 g of sodiumhydrogen carbonate is heated strongly until there is no furtherchange. The equation for the reaction is:2NaHCO3(8) → Na2CO3(s) + CO2(g) + H2O(1).bg​


Answer:

10.6 g

Explanation:

Molecular weights

NaHCO3 = 84.01 g/mol

Na2CO3 = 105.99 g/mol

Starting with 16.8 g NaHCO3

mass Na2CO3 = 16.8 g NaHCO3 × (1 mol NaHCO3 / 84.01 g NaHCO3) × (1 mol Na2CO3 / 2 mol NaHCO3) × (105.99 g Na2CO3 / 1 mol Na2CO3)

mass Na2CO3 = 10.6 g

30. What is the percent by mass of water in Na2SO4 • 10H2O?


The percent by mass of H₂O in Na₂SO₄ • 10H₂O : 55.9%

Further explanation

Proust states the Comparative Law that compounds are formed from elements with the same Mass Comparison so that compounds have a constant composition of elements

The empirical formula is the mole ratio of compounds forming elements

In the same compound, although from different sources and formed by different processes, it will still have the same composition/comparison

With this law, we can calculate how many grams an element is needed to make a compound with a certain mass, as desired

While the percentage:

[tex]\rm \%~A~in~A_xB_y=\dfrac{x\times Ar~A}{Mw~A_xB_y}\times 100\%[/tex]

[tex]\rm \%~B~in~A_xB_y=\dfrac{y\times Ar~B}{Mw~A_xB_y}\times 100\%[/tex]

Na₂SO₄ • 10H₂O

Mw Na₂SO₄ • 10H₂O = 2.Ar Na + Ar S + 4. Ar O + 20.Ar H + 10.Ar O

Mw Na₂SO₄ • 10H₂O = 2.23 + 32 + 4. 16 + 20.1 + 10.16

Mw Na₂SO₄ • 10H₂O = 322 g/mol

10H₂O

Mw = 20.Ar H + 10. Ar O

Mw = 20.1 + 10.16

Mw = 180

The percent by mass of water(H₂O) :

[tex]\rm \%H_2O=\dfrac{10H_2O}{Na_2SO_4.10H_2O}\times 100\%\\\\\%H_2O=\dfrac{180}{322}\times 100\%\\\\\%H_2O=\boxed{\bold{55.9\%}}[/tex]
