15. An aqueous solution of NaOH is 40% (w/w) concentrated. Calculate the mole fraction of NaOH and H2O. (NaOH = 40 g/mol, H2O = 18 g/mol)​


Strategy:

The mole fraction is a dimensionless quantity that expresses the ratio of the number of moles of one component to the number of moles of all components present. In general, the mole fraction of component [tex]i[/tex] in a mixture or solution is given by

[tex]X_{i} = \frac{n_{i}}{n_{\text{T}}}[/tex]

where [tex]n_{i}[/tex] and [tex]n_{\text{T}}[/tex] are the number of moles of component [tex]i[/tex] and the total number of moles present, respectively.

Pointers to Remember:

Mole fraction is dimensionless, meaning, it has no unit of measurement.Mole fraction is always less than one.The sum of mole fractions of all components is always equal to one.If the given quantity is mass, convert it to moles using the molar mass of the component of interest.If the given quantity is percent by mass, always assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Then, determine the mass of each component.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components present.

Since the given is in terms of percent by mass, we assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Therefore, 40% aqueous NaOH solution contains 40 g of NaOH in 60 g of water.

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For NaOH

[tex]n_{\text{NaOH}} = \frac{\text{mass}_{\text{NaOH}}}{\text{molar mass}_{\text{NaOH}}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{NaOH}} = \frac{\text{40 g}}{\text{40 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{NaOH}} = \text{1.0 mol}[/tex]

For water

[tex]n_{water} = \frac{\text{mass}_{water}}{\text{molar mass}_{water}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{water} = \frac{\text{60 g}}{\text{18 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{water} = \text{3.333 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mole fraction of each component.

For NaOH

[tex]X_{\text{NaOH}} = \frac{n_{\text{NaOH}}}{n_{\text{NaOH}} \: + \: n_{water}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{NaOH}} = \frac{\text{1.0 mol}}{\text{1.0 mol + 3.333 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{\text{NaOH}} = 0.23}[/tex]

For water

[tex]X_{water} = \frac{n_{water}}{n_{\text{NaOH}} \: + \: n_{water}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{water} = \frac{\text{3.333 mol}}{\text{1.0 mol + 3.333 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{X_{water} = 0.77}[/tex]

29. A 33 - ml solution of sulfuric acid (H2SO4) is completely neutralized by 28 ml of 1.5 M NaOH solution. What is the concentration of H2SO4 solution ?​


The concentration of H2SO4 solution : 0.63 M

Further explanation  

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution (analyte) by reacting with another solution whose known concentration (usually a standard solution) is called the titrant. Determination of the endpoint /equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range  

Titrations can be acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. An acid-base titration is the principle of neutralization of acids and bases  

Acid-base titration formula  

Ma Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb  

Ma, Mb = acid base concentration  

Va, Vb = acid base volume  

na, nb = acid base valence  

Given

Va = acid = 33 ml

Vb = base = 28 ml

Mb = 1.5 M

Required

Ma = the concentration of H2SO4 solution

Solution

na = 2 (valence 2 because it releases 2 H⁺)

nb = 1 (valence 1 because it releases 2 OH⁻)

Input the value :

Ma x 33 ml x 2 = 1.5 M x 28 ml x 1

Ma = 0.63 M
