11. I had a stronger claim to Ruth than Boaz a. Chilion b. "Next of Kin" c. Moab d. Jesse


The correct answer is b. "Next of Kin".

In the biblical tale of Ruth, the phrase "Next of Kin" refers to the nearest relative who is tasked with marrying the widow of a deceased husband in order to carry on the family line. In the tale, Boaz and Chilion were interested in Ruth, a widowed Moabite woman.

Chilion was referred to as Ruth's "next of kin" because, according to the levirate marriage laws, he had a stronger claim to Ruth. By demonstrating that he was a closer relative, Boaz was able to convince Ruth to become his wife.
