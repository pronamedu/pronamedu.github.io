20. Tate a cross involving dominance pattern of inheritance. Materials Needed: Activity sheets Pen/pencil Procedure: Read and analyze the given problem: Rose flowers (Rosa gallica) are incompletely dominant for color, the red flowers are homozygous dominant and the alleles for red color is RR. The white flowers are homozygous recessive, and the alleles for white color is WW. When you cross a pure red rose to a pure white one, the offspring will be pink rose, and pink flowers are heterozygous and the allele for pink color is RW. Guide Questions: 1. What is the genotype of red rose? 2. What is the phenotype of RR alleles? 3. What is the genotype of white rose? 4. How about the phenotype of WW alleles in rose flower? 5. What is the genotype of rose-pink flower? 9​


Answer:

1. The genotype of the red rose flower is RR.

2. The phenotype of RR alleles is Red.

3. The genotype of the white rose flower is WW.

4. The phenotype of WW alleles in rose flower is White.

5. The genotype of the pink rose flower is RW.
