18. A bell rings every 20 seconds, another bell rings every 60 seconds. At 4:00 p.m., the two bells ring simultaneously. At what time will the bells ring again at the same time?​


Answer:

We want the Least Common Multiple of 18 and 60.

Using our least common multiple of 18 and 60 is 180

180/18 = 10 (18 second periods)

180/60 = 3 (60 second periods)

180 seconds = 3 minutes

So the next time the bells ring simultaneously is 5:00 + 3 = 5:03 pm

Hence the next time the bells ring is 5:03 pm

30. Two bells ring at different intervals. Bell A rings every 20 minutes. Bell B rings every 30 minutes. If both bells ring together at 8:30 a.m., at what time will the two bells ring together again?


Answer:

5:00 pm

Step-by-step explanation:

So first you find the LCM, or least common multiple, (can be called LCD, least common denominator).

The LCM of 20, 30, and 50 is basically 10⋅2⋅3⋅5 because you factor out the 10 since that is a common factor.

10⋅2⋅3⋅5=300

This is the number of minutes. To find the number of hours, you simply divide by 60 and get 5 hours. Then you count 5 more hours from 12:00 pm and get 5:00 pm
