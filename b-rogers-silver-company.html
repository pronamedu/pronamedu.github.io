8. Sterling silver is 92.5℅ pure silver. How many grams of sterling silver must be mixed to a 90℅ silver alloy to obtain a 500g of 91℅ silver alloy?a.200gb.400gc.300gd.100g​


Answer:

x = 200g

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x and y be the weights, in grams, of sterling silver and of the 90% alloy to make the 500 grams at 91%. Hence

x + y =500

The number of grams of pure silver in x plus the number of grams of pure silver in y is equal to the number of grams of pure silver in the 500 grams. The pure silver is given in percentage forms. Hence

92.5% x + 90% y = 91% 500

Substitute y by 500 - x in the last equation to write

92.5% x + 90% (500 - x) = 91% 500

Simplify and solve

92.5 x + 45000 - 90 x = 45500

x = 200 grams.

200 grams of Sterling Silver is needed to make the 91% alloy.

21. roger statement true to all integers a and b? is a-b= a+(-b)


Proof:

For all integers number a and b, a-b = a +(-b). Integers (Z) includes + and - signs. If a ∈ Z and b ∈ Z then a-b ∈ Z. Thus a+(-b) ∈ Z. Hence, a-b = a+(-b).

Illustration:

a = -1 and b = 3

a-b = a+(-b)

-1-3 = -1 +(-3)

-4 = -4

Therefore, All integers a and b, a-b = a+(-b) is true to all.
