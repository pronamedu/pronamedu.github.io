Answer:

In The Midst of Hardship - Analysis of the Poem

Synopsis

This poem tells of the hardship that a family in a village faces after a big flood. The elders return at dawn in soaking-wet clothes. They go straight to the kitchen. They are probably hungry. Their hands and legs are bruised but they do not show any sign of despair or of losing hope. After braving the dreadful flood for the last 24 hours, they still can not find their son’s albino buffalo. Despite all the adversities and suffering, the people in the poem do not complain or lament on their misfortunes. They spend time together, enjoying each other’s company. They are grateful for the fact what they still have instead of what is lost. Life goes on with their daily chores of preparing food and habit of rolling their cigarette leaves. They are still able to joke with one another.

Meaning of the Poem (According to Stanza)

Stanza 1

They returned home at day break and headed for the stove. Their clothes were soaking wet and tattered. Their bodies were covered with scratches and wounds. Yet, they did not display any signs of being worried.

Stanza 2

They were out in the flood the whole day and night. They were surrounded by dead animals and parts of trees that had been destroyed by the flood. They searched desperately for their son’s albino buffalo but were unable to find it.

Stanza 3

They were born into poverty and difficulty, but they do not complain about their suffer. Instead, they sit in the kitchen, cracking jokes while smoking cigarettes.

1. Give three things that symbolize Africa in the poem.

Ancestral Savannahs

Savanna is grassy vegetation overgrown with trees or a scattered group of trees. The largest savanna ecosystem in the world is in Africa, savanna has become the identity of the African people and symbolizes the wild life in Africa

Black Blood

Black blood symbolizes the blood of the African nations. Black is the color of the greatness of the African nation. Even the identity of the continent of Africa is a black continent because the majority of the population has black skin. So that the identity of the black color has been united in the blood of all African peoples.

Impetuous child that tree, young and strong

A young and strong child. Symbolizes the strong African youth. The African race or negroid race is a nation known for having a solid body structure and denser muscle mass than any other race on earth. So strong is the right word to represent Africa.

2. Give two things that describe the hardships of Africa in the poem

The blood of your sweat.

The blood on the sweat symbolizes the difficult situation of the African nations where they were often forced to become workers and slaves for the white people during their dark times. Not infrequently they died while working to serve them during African slavery.

And saying no to the whip under the midday sun?

This phrase also describes the suffering of Africans during the slavery period, where they worked under the sun's rays which increasingly blackened their skin.

Answer:

Hardship in the poem mother and son.

Explanation:

The poem "Mother to Son" uses a staircase to symbolize the hardship of the mother's existence as a black woman. Her metaphorical "staircase" of life has been difficult to climb: it has exposed tacks, splinters, gaps, and places where the carpet is completely worn down. The mother contrasts this rough staircase with a crystal staircase, saying, "Life for me ain't been no crystal stair." The crystal staircase symbolizes an easy, affluent, and sparkling life that is out of reach for most black people, while the rickety staircase the mother speaks of symbolizes a difficult life.
