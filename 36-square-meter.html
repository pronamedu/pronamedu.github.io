2. A wire x meters long was bent to form a square. It was determined that the area of the square formed is 36 square meters. Do you agree that wire is exactly 36 meters long?


✎ Problem:

A wire x meters long was bent to form a square. It was determined that the area of the square formed is 36 square meters.

Do you agree that wire is exactly 36 meters long?

✎ Answer and explanation:✘ No.

The perimeter of the square is not always assumed to be equal to the square's area.

However, this usually depends on the length of the side of the square, aside from the fact that the perimeter and area are equal if the length of the side is equal to 4.

If the length of the side < 4, the perimeter of the square is greater than the area. However, if the length of the side > 4, the perimeter is less than the area.

Solution to your problem:

Let's find x.

Given that the area of a square is equal to the square of the sides, the length of the sides are equal to the square root of the area of the square. So the length of the side is equal to √36 = 6 m.The wire, which is x meters long, is bent to form a square. Therefore, we can say that x is the perimeter of the square, equal to four times the length of the side. So x = 4 × length of side = 24 m.
