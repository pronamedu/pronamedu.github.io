2. superstitious beliefs about eclipse and scientific explanation


Superstitious Beliefs about Eclipse and Scientific Explanation

This paper will examine superstitious beliefs held by Nigerian which can be scientifically explained.  ECLIPSE

Early humans looked to the sky and invented stories explaining the intricate machination of gods and monsters, heroes and heroines, warriors and poets . They created myths, stories and legends to account for the workings of this mysterious brilliant point of light. This made them came up with stories to explain everyday occurrences like the rising and setting of the sun, moon phases, tides, weather patterns and sky.Eclipse is one area of a serious misconceptions or superstitious beliefs held by many Nigerians; either eclipse of the moon or of the sun. Most people believed that at a particular time of the year there used to be a conflict between the sun and the moon resulting into changes in the climate of the world. In some part of the country sacrifices are made for gods because of this perceived conflict while in some communities human movement and activities are restricted at this period of perceived conflict. Some people will come out singing in their dialect that "Ekilo forum ko fosupa le". The meaning of this is, warns the Sunto leave Moon alone.Scientifically, eclipse occur when one celestial body is obscured by another especially the sun or a planetary satellite. When the earth is between the Sun and the Moon and its shadow darkens the moon, this is called lunar eclipse. It could be when the moon is between the sun and the earth, its shadow moves across the face of the earth, this is solar eclipse.

SICKLE CELL

 

ANAEMIA

Sickle cell anaemia is a killer disease that has been associated to witches and wizards insome communities in Nigeria. In some instances any child born as a sickle cell, known as"sickler" is erroneously believed to have been possessed by a familiar spirit through the irancestral spirit or a witch in the family.Scientifically, sickle cell is caused by a variant type of hemoglobin. This is due to a defective gene that produces an abnormal form of hemoglobin, the component of red blood cells that transport oxygen from the lungs to the tissues.The abnormal hemoglobin, known as hemoglobin , distorts red blood cells after the release of oxygen from the tissues. Because of the resemblance of the sickle, the distorted cells makes it difficult to pass through the tiny blood vessels, leading to serious painful blockages that disallow vital oxygen and nutrients in the blood from reaching organs and tissues impairing their functions. This makes sickle cell patients vulnerable to a number of infections. Sickle cell anaemia is caused by the inheritance of a variant hemoglobin  gene from both parents. This inheritance is known as the homologous state.

5. superstitious beliefs about the moon in the philippines


Answer:

1. No marriage should take place except during the period of the full moon

2. Giving birth to a child on a full moon is a miracle

3. Monsters will come out every full moon

4. Spiritual doings in full moon may lead to danger

1. One of the most common moon-eating monsters is the Bakunawa. It was very attracted to the Moon and would rise from the sea to swallow it whole. The sign that the Bakunawa was attempting to eat the Moon was what we now call a lunar eclipse.

2. People used to believe that the phases of the moon were somehow linked to the menstrual cycle. This idea came from the fact that the lunar cycle is about the same length of a women's average menstrual cycle.

3. The full moon makes you crazy. The ancient Roman goddess, Luna, was the divine representative for the moon. She also became the root word for "lunacy". The superstitious have always been trying to prove that people behave differently when the moon is full or new or crescent.

4. The full moon will keep you from sleeping. In various studies and reports, trial sleep patients actually were recorded as sleeping less during the full moon. This is significant because in the studies, the patients were kept in windowless rooms and made unaware of the current stage of the moon. So, it wasn't the light or the psychological expectation. This remains a significant yet unsolved mystery.

5. The full moon will make you go into labor. There's no significant proof that shows that pregnant, full-term women are any more likely to go into labor during a full moon. Sure, it might happen by coincidence, but just in the same way that some babies are born on New Year's and others are born on random Tuesdays.

12. 10 superstitious beliefs in the philippines


Answer:

Superstitious Beliefs in the Philippines

Superstition is a belief or practice based upon one's trust in luck or supernatural forces. It is known as pamahiin.

Superstitious beliefs are inherited from our ancestors. Not all Filipinos believe in superstitious beliefs because they have no scientific explanation. However, Philippines has countless common superstitions we still believe in.

Here are some of the examples of superstitious beliefs in the Philippines:

1. Fitting your wedding dress prior to your wedding day will cost you your life.

2. When someone sees you and you appear to be headless, it means you’re about to die.

3. Crossing paths with a black cat is a bad omen.

4. Bringing home food served at a wake or a funeral will bring bad luck.

5. If you have to leave on a trip before finishing your meal, turn the plates on the table so that your trip will be safe.

6. Do not place money on the table while eating.

7. When three people pose for a picture, the one in the middle will be the first to die.

8. Siblings should not marry within the same year. This superstition is called “sukob”.

9. Don’t go straight home after attending a wake. Go somewhere before going home to shake off the spirit of the deceased lest it follows you home. This superstition is known as “pagpag”.

10. When a woman sings while cooking, she will end up a spinster.

5. If you get wounded on a Good Friday, it will heal for a long time since Jesus is dead on that day.
6. If you sweep the floor at night, you are also letting the blessings out the house.
7. If you want to get taller, you need to jump high and higher on New Year's eve.
8. It is good luck to the family if there is a bee inside the house.
9. Guests should not open the door while the family is eating because the family's good fortune will fly out the door.
10. If you have to leave on a trip before finishing your meal, turn the plates on the table so that your trip will be safe.

These are just some of the Filipino superstitious beliefs. It's still up to them if they'll believe it or not. After all, luck is just within their hands.
