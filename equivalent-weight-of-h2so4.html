12. What is the approximate molarity and normality of a 13.0 per cent by weight solution of H2SO4 (SG = 1.090)? To what volume should 100 mL of the acid be diluted in order to prepare a 1.50 N solution?


Molarity : 1.45 M

Normality : 2.9 N

Dilution volume : 193 ml

Further explanation

Molarity is a way to express the concentration of the solution  

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solute or mmol in each ml of solution  

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{M=\dfrac{n}{V}}}[/tex]  

Where  

M = Molarity  

n = Number of moles of solute  

V = Volume of solution  

Normality = the number of moles equivalent of solute in 1 liter of solution

Relationship between normality and molarity

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{N = M \times n}}~, n=valence (amount~of~H^+~or~OH^-)[/tex]

1.Molarity

13.0 per cent by weight (13% w/w)solution of H₂SO₄ (SG = 1.090)

Assuming there is 100 grams of solution, then

H₂SO₄ = 13 gr

mol H₂SO₄

[tex]\rm n=\dfrac{13}{98\Rightarrow molar~mass~of~H_2SO_4}=0.133[/tex]

Volume of solution =

[tex]\rm =\dfrac{mass~of~solution}{SG}\\\\=\dfrac{100}{1.09}=91.74~ml=0.09174~L[/tex]

Then :

[tex]\rm Molarity=\dfrac{n}{V}=\dfrac{0.133}{0.09174}=1.45~M[/tex]

Or we can use simple formula to find Molarity from %weight :

[tex]\rm M=\%weight\times SG\times \dfrac{1000}{Mw}\\\\M=0.13\times 1.09\times \dfrac{1000}{98}=1.45~M[/tex]

2. Normality

Because H₂SO₄ produce 2 H⁺, so valence = 2, then

Normality = Molarity x 2

[tex]\rm N=M\times 2\\\\N=1.45\times 2=2.9~N[/tex]

3. Dilution

Formula :

[tex]\rm V_1.N_1-V_2.N_2[/tex]

V₁ = 100 ml

N₁ = 2.9 N

N₂ = 1.5 N

Then :

[tex]\rm 100\times 2.9=V_2\times 1.5\\\\V_2=\boxed{\bold{193~ml}}[/tex]

18. 1. Calculate the mole fraction of sulphuric acid (H2SO4) in 8% (w/w0 aqueous H2SO4 solution. (molar masses: H2SO4 = 98 g/mol; H2O = 18 g/mol)​


The mole fraction of Sulphuric acid H₂SO₄ : = 0.016

Further explanation  

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm), or mole fraction.  

Percent mass of the component shows the total mass of the component in the overall mass of a compound/mixture  

[tex]\tt \%mass=\dfrac{mass~solute}{mass~solution}\times 100\%[/tex]

Solution = Solute + Solvent  

The mole fraction shows the mole ratio of a substance to the mole of solution /mixture  

Can be formulated :

[tex]\tt xa=\dfrac{na}{na+nb}[/tex]  

xa + xb = 1

Given

8% (w/w) aqueous  H₂SO₄ solution

Required

the mole fraction

Solution

For 100 g solution :

H₂SO₄ = 8% x 100 g = 8 g

H₂O = 92% x 100 g = 92 g

mol H₂SO₄ :

= mass : molar mass

= 8 g : 98 g/mol

= 0.082

mol H₂O :

= 92 g : 18 g/mol

= 5.1

total mol :

= 0.082 + 5.1

= 5.182

mol fraction of H₂SO₄ :

= 0.082 : 5.182

= 0.016

26. A 33 - ml solution of sulfuric acid (H2SO4) is completely neutralized by 28 ml of 1.5 M NaOH solution. What is the concentration of H2SO4 solution ?​


The concentration of H2SO4 solution : 0.63 M

Further explanation  

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution (analyte) by reacting with another solution whose known concentration (usually a standard solution) is called the titrant. Determination of the endpoint /equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range  

Titrations can be acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. An acid-base titration is the principle of neutralization of acids and bases  

Acid-base titration formula  

Ma Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb  

Ma, Mb = acid base concentration  

Va, Vb = acid base volume  

na, nb = acid base valence  

Given

Va = acid = 33 ml

Vb = base = 28 ml

Mb = 1.5 M

Required

Ma = the concentration of H2SO4 solution

Solution

na = 2 (valence 2 because it releases 2 H⁺)

nb = 1 (valence 1 because it releases 2 OH⁻)

Input the value :

Ma x 33 ml x 2 = 1.5 M x 28 ml x 1

Ma = 0.63 M
