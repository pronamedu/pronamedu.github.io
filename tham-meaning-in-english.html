Adam Ansel was referring to Photography when he quoted “There is nothing worse than a sharp image of a fuzzy concept.”

What Adam meant is that a 'sharp and crispy' photo that contains a lot of details doesn't automatically make a picture look good. He wanted to teach Photographers that you don't need High-end cameras to make a good photograph. For example, this shot taken by British photographer Pennie Smith is low-light, blurry, and grainy.
