2. Is it safe to take stresstabs and myra e at the same time?


It is generally safe to take StressTabs and Myra-E at the same time. StressTabs is a multivitamin while Myra-E is a Vitamin E supplement. So long as there is no overdose of either of the two then you should be safe. Also consider if you have any allergic to the components of any of the two mentioned drugs. Should you experience any difficulty in breathing, rashes or any inflammation, stop the drug and consult a doctor right away.  

Why do we take vitamin supplements?

We take vitamin supplements because there are certain nutrients that are not produced by the body nor can they be easily sourced from food. Eating a healthy diet of fruits and green leafy vegetables is still the best way to get our vitamins and minerals but in many instances, we are not able to maintain this, hence we take vitamin supplements.

Take note that before taking any kinds of drugs, including vitamin and food supplements, it is best to consult a doctor for guidance. Also let the doctor know of any other medicines you are taking as certain medicines are known to interact with each other, causing harm to the body. So far, by experience, StressTabs and Myra-E have no negative effects--but you can never be too careful.

Further explanation:Rational Equation

A rational equation is a particular kind of equation that contains at least one rational expression, which is just another way of saying a fraction. The ideal method for solving this kind of equation is to use the LCD principle to get rid of all the denominators (the least common denominator). By doing this, a linear or quadratic equation is typically left over to be solved.

Recognize when it is okay to use the lowest common denominator. Rational equations can be made simpler using the lowest common denominators (LCDs), allowing their variables to be solved. When your rational equation can't be written in a way that contains one (and only one) fraction or rational expression on each side of the equals sign, finding an LCD is a good idea.Check each fraction's denominator. The lowest number that each denominator evenly divides into has to be found. This is your equation's LCD.In the rational equation, multiply each fraction by 1. Each phrase being multiplied by one could appear meaningless. But there is a trick. Any number over itself can be defined as 1, therefore "1" can alternatively be written as 2/2 or 3/3, for example. This approach makes use of the alternative definition. To give the LCD over itself, multiply each fraction in your rational equation by 1, noting 1 each time as the number or term that multiplies with each denominator.Simplify, then find x. You may now remove the denominators from your rational equation and solve the numerators because every term has the same denominator now. To get your numerators by themselves, simply multiply both sides of the equation. Once you have x (or whatever other variable you are solving for) alone by itself on one side of the equals sign, utilize algebraic procedures to bring it there.
