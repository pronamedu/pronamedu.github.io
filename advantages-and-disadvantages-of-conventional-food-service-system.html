18. What are the advantages and disadvantages of conventional farming?


Answer:

Advantages of Conventional Farming:

1. Cost-Effective: Conventional farming is often more cost-effective than organic farming. Traditional farming methods are usually less labor-intensive, require fewer inputs and use less expensive equipment.

2. Productivity: Conventional farming is highly productive and allows farmers to produce large amounts of food at a relatively low cost.

3. Accessibility: Traditional farming methods are widely available and accessible to most farmers, making it easier for them to get started.

Disadvantages of Conventional Farming:

1. Environmental Impact: Conventional farming often involves the use of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides, which can have an adverse effect on the environment.

2. Soil Degradation: Overuse of chemical fertilizers and pesticides can cause soil erosion and degradation, leading to lower yields and reduced soil quality.

3. Risk of Contamination: The use of synthetic chemicals can also lead to the contamination of water supplies and food with chemical residues.
