20. a classroom has a length of 20 feet and a width of 30 feet. the head master decided that tiles will look good in that class. if each tiles has a length of 24 inches and a width of 36 inches, how many times are needed to fill the classroom?


Answer:

100 tiles.

Computatation:

Converting dimensions of a tile to feet.

[tex]24\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!in*\frac{ft}{12\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!in}=\frac{24\:ft}{12}=2\:ft\\36\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!in*\frac{ft}{12\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!in}=\frac{36\:ft}{12}=3\:ft[/tex]

Solving for the area of a tile.

[tex]Area=2\:ft*3\:ft=6\:ft^{2}[/tex]

Solving for the area of the classroom.

[tex]Area=20\:ft*30\:ft=600\:ft^{2}[/tex]

Dividing the area of the classroom by the area of a tile to determine the number of tiles needed to fill the classroom.

[tex]600\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!ft^{2}/6\:\diagdown\!\!\!\!\!ft^{2}=\boxed{100}[/tex]
