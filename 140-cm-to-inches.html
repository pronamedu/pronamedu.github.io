21. The perimeter of a rectangle is 48 cm with the area of 140 cm². Find the dimensions if the length is 4 cm longer than the width?​


p = 48 cm

area = 140 cm²

l = 4 + x

let x = width

p = 2(l + w)

48 = 2(4 + x) + x

48 = 2(4 + 2x)

48 = 8 + 4x

48 - 8 = 4x

x = 10

l = 4 + 10 = 14

Thus, the dimensions are 10 cm for width and 14 cm for length.

checking:

p = 2(l + w)

48 = 2 (10 + 14)

48 = 2(24)

48 = 48

a = lw

140 = (10)(14)

140 = 140

23. The length of a rectangular frame is 20 inches. longer than its width. if the perimeter of the frame is 140 inches, what are the frame's dimensions?​


Answer:

Length = 45 inches

Width = 20 inches

Step-by-step explanation:

P = 2(L + W)

Given,

L = W + 20P = 140

Substitute the given value of P.

140 = 2(L+W)

Replace L in terms of W.

140 = 2[(W+20) + W]

140 = 2(2W + 20)

140 = 4W + 40

Subtract 40 to both sides.

100 = 4W

100/4 = W

25 = W

Now lets find the length of the frame

L = W + 20

Substitute the value of W.

L = 25 + 20

L = 45

∴ The frame is 45 inches long and 20 inches wide.
