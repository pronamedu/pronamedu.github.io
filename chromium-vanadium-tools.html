13. 50g of Vanadium is mixed with 50g of Ozone (O3) to produce 65 g ofVanadium (V) oxide. (a) What is the theoretical yield of this reaction? (b)What is the percent yield? (c) How much Vanadium (V) oxide would beproduced if this process was 90% efficient?6V + 503 → 3V2O5​


Answer:

a) theoretical yield = 89 g

b) percent yield = 73%

c) actual yield = 80 g

Explanation:

Step 1: Solve for the mass of V2O5 produced from each reactants.

Using 50 g of V

mass V2O5 = 50 g V × (1 mol V / 50.94 g V) × (3 mol V2O5 / 6 mol V) × (181.9 g V2O5 / 1 mol V2O5)

mass V2O5 = 89 g

Using 50 g of O3

mass V2O5 = 50 g O3 × (1 mol O3 / 48.00 g O3) × (3 mol V2O5 / 5 mol O3) × (181.9 g V2O5 / 1 mol V2O5)

mass V2O5 = 1.14 × 10² g

Step 2: Determine the limiting reagent.

Since V produces less amount of V2O5 than O3, V is the limiting reagent.

a) Determine the theoretical yield.

Since V is the limiting reagent

theoretical yield = 89 g

b) Solve for the percent yield.

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

percent yield = (65 g / 89 g) × 100

percent yield = 73%

c) Solve for the actual yield.

actual yield = (theoretical yield × percent yield) / 100

actual yield = (89 g × 90) / 100

actual yield = 80 g

14. Chromium forms a body centered cubic unit cell. If the radius of a chromium atom is 125 pm, what is the density of chromium in g/cm3?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the edge length of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}r \\ & = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}(\text{125 pm}) \\ & = \text{288.68 pm} \\ & = \text{288.68 pm} \times \frac{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}}{\text{1 pm}} \times \frac{\text{100 cm}}{\text{1 m}} \\ & = 2.8868 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = a^3 \\ & = (2.8868 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm})^3 \\ & = 2.4057 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass the unit cell.

Note that in a body-centered cubic crystal structure, there are 2 atoms in a unit cell.

The molar mass of chromium is 51.996 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \text{2 atoms} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \times \frac{\text{51.996 g}}{\text{1 mol}} \\ & = 1.7268 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the density of chromium.

[tex]\begin{aligned} d & = \frac{m}{V} \\ & = \frac{1.7268 \times 10^{-22} \: \text{g}}{2.4057 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{7.18 g/cm}^3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the density of chromium is 7.18 g/cm³.
