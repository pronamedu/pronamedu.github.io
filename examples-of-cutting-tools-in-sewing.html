Tools for handsewing: Needle, thread, scissors, fabric.

Tools for sewing in general:

Apart from a machine, tools come in very handy for making certain sewing tasks easier and more professional looking. You need to have good shears (scissors for cutting fabric) and small pointed end scissors for snipping threads.

Naturally you’ll need pins. I try to always make the smallest hole possible in the fabric so I usually use extra fine pins — unless I am sewing with jeans or canvas, then I use just the basic pins. I do however, always choose longer pins. You can keep pins near your machine by using a pin cushion or a magnetized pin holder.

One handy tool I would never want to be without is a tool for pressing out corners. This is very useful for turning out items that have pointy corners like collars, sashes, belts, ties, pillowcases, front edges on jackets, and so on.

26. measuring toolscutting tools marking tools sewing tools​


Answer:

Here are some examples of measuring tools:

Ruler: A flat, straight tool with marked increments used for measuring lengths.

Caliper: A tool with two adjustable arms used for measuring the dimensions of an object.

Tape measure: A flexible, retractable tool with marked increments used for measuring lengths and distances.

Protractor: A tool with a circular or semicircular head and marked degrees used for measuring angles.

Level: A tool with a bubble vial used for determining if a surface is horizontal or vertical.

Here are some examples of cutting tools:

Knife: A tool with a sharp blade used for cutting or slicing.

Scissors: A pair of cutting tools with two pivoted blades used for cutting paper or other materials.

Shears: A pair of cutting tools with long, curved blades used for cutting cloth or other materials.

Saw: A tool with a handle and a thin, sharp blade used for cutting wood or other materials.

Chisel: A tool with a handle and a sharp, flat blade used for carving or shaping wood or other materials.

Here are some examples of marking tools:

Pencil: A writing tool with a graphite core used for drawing or writing on paper.

Pen: A writing tool with a metal or plastic tip and a container for ink used for writing on paper.

Marker: A writing tool with a felt or foam tip and a container for ink or pigment used for making bold marks on paper or other surfaces.

Stencil: A template with cut-out shapes used for creating repeated patterns or designs.

Compass: A tool with a pointed end and a pivot used for drawing circles or arcs.

Here are some examples of sewing tools:

Needle: A thin, pointed tool with a hole at one end used for sewing.

Thread: A thin, flexible material used for sewing or weaving.

Thimble: A small, finger-shaped tool with a raised end used for pushing a needle through fabric.

Scissors: A pair of cutting tools with two pivoted blades used for cutting thread or fabric.

Sewing machine: A machine with a needle and a motor used for stitching fabric or other materials.
