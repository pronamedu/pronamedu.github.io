2. find the area of rectangle whose lenght is (2m-5n) cm and whose width is (5n+2m) cm


[tex]\bold{Hello}[/tex]


We know tha Area of rectangle is :-


=> Length*Width

→ Length = 2m-5n

→ Width = 5n + 2m


Area = (2m-5n)(2m+5n)


This is an identity = [tex]\bold{a^2-b^2}[/tex]


Area = [tex]\bold{4m^2 - 25n^2}[/tex]





__________________________

Area of rectangle = (length) (width)

Length: (2m-5n)

Width: (5n + 2m) or (2m + 5n)

In special products, the product of sum and difference of binomial is the difference of squares.

(x + y) (x - y) = x² - y²

(2m + 5n) (2m - 5n) = 4m² - 25n²

ANSWER: The expression for the area is 4m² - 25n².

In finding the area of a rectangle, we are going to use the formula
A=LW

So All you need to do was substitution.

A=LW
=(2m-5n)(5n+2m)

As you can notice, this is a difference of two squares. You can simply solve it by squaring the first term (2m) then add a negative sign and then square the last term (5n). You can also use the foil method. Both would do.

The area was: 4m²-25n²
