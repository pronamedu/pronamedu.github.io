1. There are two brands of isopropyl alcohol. Brand X contains 350 mL of isopropyl alcohol in 500mL solution while Brand Y contains 200 mL of isopropyl alcohol in 250mL solution. Which brand is more concentrated, brand X or brand Y?​


Comparing Concentrations of Solutions

Brand Y is more concentrated than Brand X. Brand Y is 80% isopropyl alcohol while Brand X is 70% isopropyl alcohol.

Explanation:

The percent by volume is one way to determine the concentration of the solution. It specifically tells the ratio of solute in a given solution multiplied by 100%. This is used if the solution is in a liquid or gaseous phase wherein the volume of solute and solution is given. If the volume of solution is not provided, it can be obtained by getting the sum of the volumes of solute and solvent.

Comparing the percent by volume of brand X and brand Y, we make use of of the formula,  % volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%. Substituting the formula will give us the percent by volume of Brand Y which is 80% isopropyl alcohol while Brand X is only 70% isopropyl alcohol . Please refer to the computation below:

Brand X

  volume of solute (isopropyl alcohol) - 350 mL

  volume of solution - 500 mL

Formula

    % volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

Solution

    % volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

    % volume = [tex]\frac{350mL}{500 mL}[/tex] x 100%

                     = 0.7 x100%

                     = 70%

Brand Y

  volume of solute (isopropyl alcohol) - 200 mL

  volume of solution - 250 mL

Formula

    % volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

Solution

    % volume = [tex]\frac{volume of solute}{volume of solution}[/tex] x 100%

    % volume = [tex]\frac{200mL}{250 mL}[/tex] x 100%

                     = 0.8 x100%

                     = 80%
