1. The energy of a photon that has a wavelength of 12.3 nm is __________ J.


Given:

[tex]\lambda = \text{12.3 nm} = 12.3 × 10^{-9} \: \text{m}[/tex]

[tex]\text{particle = photon}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]E[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the frequency of a photon.

[tex]f = \frac{c}{\lambda}[/tex]

[tex]f = \frac{2.998 × 10^{8} \: \text{m/s}}{12.3 × 10^{-9} \: \text{m}}[/tex]

[tex]f = 2.4374 × 10^{16} \: \text{Hz}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the energy of a photon.

[tex]E = hf[/tex]

[tex]E = (6.626 × 10^{-34} \: \text{J•s})(2.4374 × 10^{16} \: \text{s}^{-1})[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{E = 1.62 × 10^{-17} \: \text{J}}[/tex]
