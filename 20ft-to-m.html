1. 15ft 20ft 40ft 20ft find the composite area​


To find the composite area of the figures with lengths of 15ft, 20ft, 40ft, and 20ft, we need to add up the individual areas of each figure.

Assuming all figures are rectangles, the formula for the area of a rectangle is:

area = length x width

For the 15ft x 20ft rectangle, the area would be:

15ft x 20ft = 300 square feet

For the 20ft x 40ft rectangle, the area would be:

20ft x 40ft = 800 square feet

For the second 20ft x 20ft rectangle, the area would be:

20ft x 20ft = 400 square feet

Now we can add up the areas of all three rectangles to get the composite area:

300 + 800 + 400 = 1500 square feet

Therefore, the composite area of the figures with lengths of 15ft, 20ft, 40ft, and 20ft is 1500 square feet

We are given the Density: 0.5 g / mL and the Volume: (100 m^2)(20 ft)(12 in / 1 ft)(2.54 cm / 1 in)(1 m / 100 cm) = 609.6 m^3. We find the mass using the formula: D = M/V --> 0.5 g/mL = M / (609.6 m^3) --> M = 304.8 g.

22. A swimming pool is 20ft long 12ft wide and 8ft high. what is the volume of the pool?​


Step-by-step explanation:

A rectangular pool is 60 feet long by 30 feet wide. The shallow end is 4 feet deep and the deep end is 8 feet deep. Find the surface area and the volume.

2) A circular spa has a 10 foot diameter and a constant depth of 4 feet deep. Find the surface area and the volume.

3) A rectangular pool has a swimming area and a diving well. The swimming area is 75 feet long by 25 feet wide and is 3 feet deep to 5 feet deep. The diving well is 25 feet long by 25 feet wide and has a constant depth of 10 feet deep. Find the total volume. (Hint: Split the pools, then find the volume of each and add totals.)

FORMULAS

Surface Area (Circle): Radius x Radius x 3.14 (Radius = Diameter / 2)

Surface Area (Rectangle/Square): Length x Width

Volume (Circle/Rectangle/Square): Surface Area x Average Depth x 7.5

Average Depth: (Shallow Depth + Deep Depth) ÷ 2

(Note: Constant Depth = Average Depth)

EXAMPLE

A rectangular pool is 50 feet long by 20 feet wide. The shallow end is 3 feet deep and the deep end is 7 feet deep. Find the surface area and the volume.

Surface Area = Length x Width

Surface Area = 50 feet x 20 feet

Surface Area = 1,000 square feet

Volume = Surface Area x Average Depth x 7.5

Volume = 1,000 x Average Depth x 7.5

Average Depth = (Shallow Depth + Deep Depth) ÷ 2

Average Depth = (3 + 7) ÷ 2

Average Depth = 10 ÷ 2

Average Depth = 5

Volume = 1,000 x 5 x 7.5

Volume = 37,500 Gallons

ANSWERS

1. Surface Area = 1,800 square feet Volume = 81,000 Gallons

2. Surface Area = 78.5 square feet Volume = 2,355 Gallons

3. Total Volume = 103,125 Gallons
