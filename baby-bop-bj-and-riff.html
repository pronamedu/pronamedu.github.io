Explanation:

27. A plastic ball on a pool is bopping up and down. Is it continous or a pulse?


Answer: Pulse

Explanation: The motion of the plastic ball on a pool that is bopping up and down can be classified as a pulse.

A pulse is a single disturbance that travels through a medium, causing a brief disturbance or oscillation in that medium. In the case of the plastic ball, it creates a disturbance or a wave on the surface of the water as it bounces up and down. This wave moves through the water as a single disturbance or pulse, and it is not continuous since it is created by the motion of the ball and only lasts for a brief period of time.

In contrast, a continuous wave is one that has a constant frequency and amplitude and travels through a medium without any interruption. Examples of continuous waves include sound waves and light waves.
