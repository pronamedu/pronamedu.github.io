24. Measures of Variability The average daily rainfall in Seattle for the month of June has a mean absolute deviation of 1.3in. What concludes can you make about the average daily rainfall in Seattle for the month of June?


Answer:

The mean absolute deviation (MAD) is a measure of variability that tells us how much the data values deviate from the mean. In this case, the MAD of the average daily rainfall in Seattle for the month of June is 1.3 inches.

This means that on average, the daily rainfall in Seattle for the month of June is about 1.3 inches away from the mean rainfall for that month. A low MAD indicates that the data values are tightly clustered around the mean, while a high MAD indicates that the data values are more spread out.

Based on the MAD of 1.3 inches, we can conclude that the average daily rainfall in Seattle for the month of June is relatively consistent, with most days having rainfall amounts that are close to the mean. However, we cannot make any conclusions about the actual amount of rainfall, only about the variability of the data.
