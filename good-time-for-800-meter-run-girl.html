6. Ashley is on the track team and is running an 800-meter race. throughout the race, her respiratory rate increases rapidly. does this represent negative or positive feedback


Answer:

Yes, a negative feedback loop is operated in Ashley because its brain setpoint reduces the temperature from hypothalamus. Her breathing level is decreased so that a decrease in blood pressure is observed due to her 800-meter race. throughout the race. The activation of the vasomotor center in the brain is followed by a positive feedback loop to maintain homeostasis in order to avoid circulatory shock.

Circulatory shock is reduced blood flow throughout the body. During hypotensive conditions, the cardiac output decreases and all body organs are not supplied.

In the course of maintenance of normal homeostatic conditions, our body works through various hormones and enzymes.If the blood pressure falls below the normal levels, a hormone called erythropoietin (EPO) is released from the endocrine cells of the kidney and renin is released from the juxtra glomerular apparatus of the kidney. The activation of renin-angiotensin system (RAS) causes the secretion of aldosterone and anti diuretic hormone (ADH), which together causes increased thirst and fluid intake that in turn causes increase in blood volume and pressure. Activation of RAS also causes increased red cell production that contributes for increasing the blood volume.

erythropoietin released -----> renin released --------> increased red blood cell production -------> aldosterone secreted-----------> ADH secreted -----------> Increased fluid intake and retension ---------> rising blood pressure
