24. Which organism has large, smooth, glistening colonies with a lavender pigment and smells like ammonia?


The organism, which has large, smooth, and glistening colonies with a lavender pigment and smells like ammonia, is Stenotrophomonas maltophilia.

What is Stenotrophomonas maltophilia?

Stenotrophomonas maltophilia is an organism commonly found in respiratory tract infections. This type of environmental bacterium can also be found in aqueous habitats, including plant rhizospheres, animals, foods, and water sources. The Infections of Stenotrophomonas maltophilia can occur in several organs and tissues.

What is the history of S. maltophilia?

In the past, this organism has had multiple different names. The first time this organism was found was in 1943 in the pleural effusion; its name was bacterium bookeri.

In 1961 it was renamed Pseudomonas maltophilia. Then it was moved to become the genus Xanthomonas in 1983. In the end, it changed to Stenotrophomonas in 1993 as we know it until now.

Pathogenesis (How can it be developed)?

S. maltophilia often colonize moist surfaces such as tubes used in mechanical ventilation as well as fixed urinary catheters, along with medical devices such as suction catheters and endoscopes. Infection with these organisms is usually facilitated by the presence of a prosthetic material such as plastic or metal.
