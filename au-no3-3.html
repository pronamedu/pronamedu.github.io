The astronomical unit (symbol: au,[1][2][3] or AU) is a unit of length, roughly the distance from Earth to the Sun and equal to about 150 million kilometres (93 million miles). 19. PERFORMANCE TASKS: 10 PTS.Using the Five steps in Nitrogen cycle, discuss the process of Nitrogen cycle1. Nitrogen Fixation (N2 to NH3 / NH4+ or NO3-)2. Nitrification (NH3 to NO3-)3. Assimilation (incorporation of NH3 and NO3- into biological tissues)4. Ammonification (organic nitrogen compounds to NH3)5. Denitrification (NO3- to N2)​


Answer:

1.Nitrogen fixation is the process by which gaseous nitrogen (N2) is converted to ammonia (NH3 or NH4+) via biological fixation or nitrate (NO3-) through high-energy physical processes. N2 is extremely stable and a great deal of energy is required to break the bonds that join the two N atoms.

2.Nitrification is a two-step process in which NH3/ NH4+ is converted to NO3-. First, the soil bacteria Nitrosomonas and Nitrococcus convert NH3 to NO2-, and then another soil bacterium, Nitrobacter, oxidizes NO2- to NO3-. These bacteria gain energy through these conversions, both of which require oxygen to occur.

3.Assimilation is the process by which plants and animals incorporate the NO3- and ammonia formed through nitrogen fixation and nitrification. Plants take up these forms of nitrogen through their roots, and incorporate them into plant proteins and nucleic acids.

4.Ammonification is the conversion of organic nitrogen into ammonia. The ammonia produced by this process is excreted into the environment and is then available for either nitrification or assimilation.

5.Denitrification. Denitrification is the process that converts nitrate to nitrogen gas, thus removing bioavailable nitrogen and returning it to the atmosphere. Dinitrogen gas (N2) is the ultimate end product of denitrification, but other intermediate gaseous forms of nitrogen exist (Figure 7).

We balance the chemical equation since atoms are not destroyed in the process and the mass is conserved. The mass of the reactant is equal to the mass of the product which also means, the number of atoms of elements in both the product and reactant are equal.

23. 1. What volume 0.300M NaOH is required to react completely with 2.50g al(no3)33NaOH(aq) + Al(NO3)3 = 3NaNO3(aq) + Al(OH)3(aq)2. Using the same equation in no.1, What mass of Al(OH)3 os produced from 25ml 1.0M Al(NO3)3 pa turo din sa solution pano kunin hehe​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{1. \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of NaOH by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of NaOH is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al(NO₃)₃. Note that the molar mass of Al(NO₃)₃ is 213.0 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NaOH} & = 2.50 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}}{213.0 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \times \frac{\text{3 mol NaOH}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \\ & = \text{0.0352 mol NaOH} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the volume of NaOH solution.

Note that 1 molar (M) is equal to 1 mole per liter (mol/L).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of NaOH solution} & = \frac{\text{moles of NaOH}}{\text{molarity of NaOH solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0352 mol}}{0.300 \: M} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0352 mol}}{\text{0.300 mol/L}} \\ & = \text{0.117 L} \\ & = 0.117 \: \cancel{\text{L}} \times \frac{\text{1000 mL}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{117 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of NaOH needed is 117 mL.

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{2. \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of Al(NO₃)₃.

Note that 25 mL is equal to 0.025 L.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 & = \text{molarity of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 \: \text{solution} \times \text{volume of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 \: \text{solution} \\ & = 1.0 \: M \times \text{0.025 L} \\ & = \text{1.0 mol/L} \times \text{0.025 L} \\ & = \text{0.025 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the mass of Al(OH)₃ produced using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Al(NO₃)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al(OH)₃. Note that the molar mass of Al(OH)₃ is 78.00 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 & = 0.025 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \times \frac{\text{78.00 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{2.0 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of Al(OH)₃ produced is 2.0 g.

26. Calculate the freezing and boiling points of a solution prepared by dissolving 15.5 g of AI(NO3)3 in 200.0 g of water. (molar mass of AI(NO3)3 is 212.996 g/mol).Answer plssss.​


Freezing Point Depression:

Formula: Tsoln = Th20 + Kf • m • i

Boiling Point Elevation:

Formula: Tsoln = Th2o + Kb • m • i

where in,

Tsoln = Temperature of Solution

Th2o = Temperature of Water

Kb = Molal Boiling Point Elevation Constant

Kf = Molal Freezing Point Depression Constant

m = Molality

i = Van't Hoff Factor

________________________________

Freezing Point:

Given: Th2o = 0°C

Kf = -1.86 °C/m

Msolute = 15.5g

Msolvent = 200.0g

Molar Mass = 212.966g/mol

Unknown: m = ?

i = ?

Tsoln = ?

Solution:

a) m = ?

solve first for moles by using,

formula: mass of solute / molar mass

15.5g Al(NO3)3 × 1mol Al(NO3)3 / 212.996g/mol Al(NO3)3 = 0.073 mol Al(NO3)3

now, compute for the molality by using,

formula: moles of solute / kg of solvent

g ------> kg

200g × 1kg / 1000kg = 0.2kg

0.073mol Al(NO3)3 / 0.2kg H2O = 0.365m

m = 0.365m

b) i = ?

Al(NO3)3 ------> Al+ 3NO3- = 4

i = 4

c) Tsoln = ?

Formula: Tsoln = Th20 + Kf • m • i

Solution:

Tsoln = 0°C - 1.86°C/m (0.365m)(4)

= 0°C - 1.86°C/m (1.46m)

= -2.72°C

Final Answer: Tsoln = -2.72°C

________________________________

Boiling Point Elevation:

Given: Th2o = 100°C

Kb = 0.512 °C/m

m = 0.365m

i = 4

Unknown: Tsoln = ?

Formula: Tsoln = Th2o + Kb • m • i

Solution:

Tsoln = Th2o + Kb • m • i

= 100°C + 0.512 °C/m (0.365)(4)

= 100°C + 0.512°C/m (1.46m)

= 100°C + 0.75°C

= 100.75°C

Final Answer: Tsoln = 100.75°C
