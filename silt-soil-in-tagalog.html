10. What are the similarities between Sandy soil and Silt soil​


Answer:

All soils are similar in that they are made up of inorganic particles, usually derived from weathered rock or airfall volcanic material, and include organic matter from decayed plant and maybe animal material. The inclusion of the organic matter differentiates the soils from the simple deposits of raw materials, e.g. sand, silt and clay.

The soil texture relates to the particle sizes in the soil. Most soils include a range of particle sizes dependent upon their parent material. The predominance of one particle size, large for sand, smaller for silt and microscopic for clay, determines the classification of the soil, which may include descriptors from two of these categories, e.g. sandy silt, silty clay, sandy clay, etc.

The soil structure relates to the chemical bonding within the soil matrix. Sandy soils, having larger particles, have a far lower particle surface area to volume ratio meaning that there is little chemical bonding within the soil and hence it has a weak structure. At the opposite end is the clay soil that has a very high surface area to volume ratio and hence is very strongly bonded, resulting in almost impermeable soils. In between are the soils which are useful for agriculture because they have sufficient structure to remain stable under cultivation and to hold water.

The age of soils is an important factor, young soils tending to have relatively weak structures while older soils are well structured. Very old soils may have most of the nutrients washed out of them and effectively be an inorganic matrix of silicates and feldspars.

That is a very brief summary of soils; for more details consult any of the many available books on soil formation and soil physics - there may also be online references.

Answer:

To calculate the soil texture using a soil texture triangle, you need to plot the percentage of sand, silt, and clay on the triangle and then determine which texture the point falls within. For example, for the combination 25% sand, 30% silt, and 45% clay, you would plot the point on the triangle at the 25% sand, 30% silt location, and then draw a line to the 45% clay location. The point at which this line intersects with one of the texture boundaries on the triangle will determine the soil texture.

For the combination of 25% sand, 30% silt, and 45% clay, the point would fall within the clay texture area.

For the combination of 40% sand, 30% silt, and 30% clay, the point would fall within the loam texture area.

For the combination of 60% sand, 10% silt, and 30% clay, the point would fall within the sandy loam texture area.

For the combination of 70% sand, 12% silt, and 18% clay, the point would fall within the loamy sand texture area.

For the combination of 90% sand, 5% silt, and 5% clay, the point would fall within the sandy texture area.

For the combination of 80% sand, 15% silt, and 5% clay, the point would fall within the sandy loam texture area.

For the combination of 10% sand, 85% silt, and 5% clay, the point would fall within the silt texture area.

25. What materials can be found in sandy soil, loamy soil, silt soil, and clay soil?


Answer:

Different soil types contain different proportions of minerals, organic matter, and water, which affect their physical and chemical properties. Here are some common materials found in sandy soil, loamy soil, silt soil, and clay soil:

Sandy soil: Sandy soil is composed of large particles that are loosely packed together. It is generally low in nutrients and organic matter, and it has poor water-holding capacity. Sandy soil may contain minerals such as quartz, feldspar, mica, and calcite.Loamy soil: Loamy soil is a mix of sand, silt, and clay particles. It has a good balance of drainage and water-holding capacity, and it is generally rich in nutrients and organic matter. Loamy soil may contain minerals such as quartz, feldspar, mica, and clay minerals such as kaolinite, illite, and smectite.Silt soil: Silt soil is composed of fine particles that are smaller than sand but larger than clay. It has good water-holding capacity, but it can be easily compacted and may be prone to erosion. Silt soil may contain minerals such as quartz, feldspar, mica, and clay minerals.Clay soil: Clay soil is composed of very small particles that are tightly packed together. It has high water-holding capacity, but it can be poorly drained and prone to compaction. Clay soil may contain minerals such as quartz, feldspar, mica, and clay minerals such as kaolinite, illite, and smectite.

It is important to note that the mineral composition of soil can vary greatly depending on the specific location and geological history. Additionally, soil can also contain various microorganisms, plant roots, and other organic matter that can affect its properties.

Answer:

Sandy soil is composed of larger particles, typically ranging in size from 0.05 to 2 mm. This type of soil tends to be well-drained and droughty, and doesn't hold onto water or nutrients as well as other types of soil. Sandy soil is typically composed of a mix of minerals, including quartz, feldspar, and mica.

Loamy soil is a mixture of sand, silt, and clay particles, with each particle type making up roughly one-third of the soil. Loamy soil is often considered to be ideal for gardening and agriculture, as it retains moisture and nutrients well, while also providing good drainage. Loamy soil typically contains a mix of minerals, organic matter, and microorganisms.

Silt soil is made up of smaller particles than sandy soil, ranging in size from 0.002 to 0.05 mm. This type of soil is typically composed of minerals such as quartz, feldspar, and clay, as well as organic matter. Silt soil tends to be well-drained, but it can become compacted and hard to work with.

Clay soil is composed of very fine particles, less than 0.002 mm in size. This type of soil is often dense, poorly-drained, and difficult to work with, but it is also highly fertile and can hold onto water and nutrients very well. Clay soil typically contains minerals such as mica, feldspar, and quartz, as well as organic matter.

29. why is sandy loam silt loam and clay soil good


Answer:

Considered the most fertile of soil type, loamy soils are a combination of sandy, clay and silt particles. The clay and silt particles improve moisture retention while the sand minimizes compaction and improves drainage. Loamy soils don't get dried out in the summer, but also don't get water-logged in winter.
