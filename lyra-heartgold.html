Lyra, the Harp

It's easy to find Lyra, the harp, by first finding Vega — one of the brightest stars in Earth's night sky. Look for Vega high overhead in mid-summer. Lyra looks like a small, lopsided square, with Vega just beside one of the corners of the square.

Lyra, the Harp

The legend of Lyra tells the story of Orpheus, who was given a harp by the god Apollo. Orpheus' music was sweeter than that of any other mortal man. It could soothe any savage, bring joy to the heart of the weary. It was even said that rivers changed course to stay near its beauty.

Orpheus married the lovely maiden Eurydice. But after their wedding, as she walked with her bridesmaids, she was bitten by a snake and died.

19. Lyra is given Php72.00 by her mother as her daily allowance. She spends 5/8 of her daily allowance on fare and 2/3of the remaining money for food. How muchdoes she spend from food?​


63

72÷8= 9

To get the 5/8 of 72 multiply the 1/8 of 72 to 5, 9×5=45, so the 5/8 of 72 is equal to 45. Next is to deduct the 45 from 72, 72 - 45= 27. To get the 2/3 of the remaining money which is 27, is to divide 27 into 3, 27÷3= 9, next is to multiply the 1/3 of 27 to 2, to get the 2/3 of 27, 9×2= 18. Finally add the 5/8 and 2/3 of the remaining money to get the amount of how much money was spend for food, 18+45= 63.
