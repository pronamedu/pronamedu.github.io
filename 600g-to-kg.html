7. Problem: If concentration by mass of 600g salt solution is 40%, find amount of solue and sobem by mas in this solution Solution:​


Answer:

So first we will calculate the amount of NaCl in the first solution:

w(NaCl) = m(NaCl)/ m(solution)

40% = m(NaCl)/ 60g

40/100 = m(NaCl)/ 60g

m(NaCl) = 60*40/ 100

m(NaCl) = 24g

After this we will calculate the amount of NaCl in the second solution:

w(NaCl) = m(NaCl)/ m(solution)

15% = m(NaCl)/ 100g

15/100 = m(NaCl)/ 100g

m(NaCl) = 15*100/ 100

m(NaCl) = 15g

Now when we now the amounts of sodium chloride in each of the solutions, we can say that the final solution will be 160g containing 39g of sodium chloride.

To find the mass percent of NaCl in this final solution, we will use the formula, we used above:

w(NaCl) = m(NaCl)/ m(solution)

w(NaCl) = 39g/ 160g

w(NaCl) = 0.24375

w(NaCl) = 24.375%

w(NaCl) = 24.4%

So the mass percent of sodium chloride in the final solution is 24.4%

26. how many grams of no are produced when 25 moles of oxygen gas react with an excess of ammonia? 600g ​


✏️STOICHIOMETRY

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to solve for the mass of NO formed when 25 mol of O₂ reacts with an excess of NH₃. First, we need to write the balanced chemical equation. The balanced chemical equation is

4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O

Second, we need to find the molar mass of NO. The molar mass of NO is

molar mass of NO = (14 g/mol × 1) + (16 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of NO = 30 g/mol

Finally, we can now solve for the mass of NO formed. Therefore, the mass of NO formed is

[tex]\text{mass of NO} = \text{25 mol} \: \text{O}_{2} \times \dfrac{\text{4 mol NO}}{\text{5 mol} \: \text{O}_{2}} \times \dfrac{\text{30 g NO}}{\text{1 mol NO}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of NO = 600 g}}[/tex]
