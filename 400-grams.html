Given

Molarity (M) = 0.200 M NaClVolume of Solution (v) = 400 ml or 0.400 LMass of Solute (m) = ?

m = Mv

m = (0.200)(0.400)

m = 0.08 mol NaCl

1 mol NaCl = 58.44 g/mol

(0.08 mol NaCl)(58.44 g NaCl / 1 mol NaCl)

25. Calculate the mass in grams of sucrose and the mass in grams of water that must be used to prepare 400 g of 45% by mass of sucrose solution.


Given:

percent by mass = 45%

mass of solution = 400 g

Required:

mass of solute

mass of solvent

Equations:

[tex]\text{mass of solute} = \dfrac{\text{percent by mass}}{100} \times \text{mass of solution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent} = \dfrac{100 - \text{percent by mass}}{100} \times \text{mass of solution}[/tex]

Solutions:

[tex]\text{mass of solute} = \dfrac{45}{100} \times \text{400 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent} = \dfrac{100 - 45}{100} \times \text{400 g}[/tex]

Answers:

mass of solute = 180 g

mass of solvent = 220 g
