4. Solve the following problems involving concentration of solutions. Problem 1 Calculate the % by mass and volume of the following solutions: A. 2 grams of sucrose in 80grams water B. 20 grams sodium hydroxide (NaOH) contained in 500mL solution C. 10 mL ethanol in 50mL solution D. 20mL of NaCl in 180mL water nem Problem 2 a How many grams of NaCl are present in a 250g salt solution containing 9% Naci? Problem 1:A.

Given: 2g C12H22O11

80g H2O

Unknown: % by mass = ?

Formula: M% = Msol / Msoln × 100%

where in, M% = percentage by mass

Msol = mass of solute

Msoln = mass of solution

Solution:

Msoln = mass of solute + mass of solvent

= 2g C12H22O11 + 80g H2O

= 82g

M% = Msol / Msoln × 100%

= 2g C12H22O11 / 82g × 100%

= 0.0244g × 100%

= 2.44%

Final Answer: The percentage by mass of the solution is 2.44%

B.

Given: 20g NaOH

500mL solution

Unknown: % by volume = ?

But the problem is, NaOH is in grams!!

Note: To convert the mass of NaOH to volume, we must know it’s density first. (Density of a certain mixture or compound can be found in Periodic Table of Elements, or by using the formula D = m/V, m = mass, and V = volume)

NaOH (d = 2.13g/cm³)

Now, we will derive the formula “D = m/V” so we can convert NaOH mass into volume, by following these steps:

• D = m/V ← divide both sides by m

• m/D = m/V/m ← cancel like terms

• m/D = V ← final formula

Solution of NaOH mass to volume:

V = m/D

= 20g / 2.13g/cm³

= 9.39cm³

Note: 1cm³ = 1mL, therefore, 9.39cm³ = 9.39mL

Now, we have the volume for NaOH, so let’s continue!

Formula: V% = Vsol / Vsoln × 100%

where in, V% = percentage by volume

Vsol = volume of solute

Vsoln = volume of solution

Solution:

V% = Vsol / Vsoln × 100%

= 9.39mL / 500mL × 100%

= 0.01878mL × 100%

= 1.878%

Final Answer: The percentage by volume of the solution is 1.878%

7. What is the final temperature of a 180ml gas with a temperature of 47Â°c if the volume is increased to 300ml


Problem:

What is the final temperature of a 180 ml gas with a temperature of 47°c if the volume is increased to 300 ml.

Note:

Convert degree celsius to kelvin

k = °C + 273

Given Data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 180 ml

V₂ (final volume) = 300 ml

T₁ (initial pressure) =  47°c + 273  = 320 K        

T₂ (final pressure) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use Charles's Law which states that volume and temperature are directly proportional when the pressure and other variable are constant. when temperature increases, the volume of the gas also increases and vise versa.

T₂ =  V₂T₁ / V₁

    = 300 ml × 320 K  / 180 ml

    = 533.33 K

Answer:

the final temperature of the gas in Kelvin temperature is 533.33 K

which is also equal to 260.33°C
