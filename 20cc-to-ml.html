1. The density of methanol at 20oC is 0.791 g/mL. what is the mass in grams, of 250 mL sample?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & d = \text{0.791 g/mL} \\ & V = \text{250 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = dV \\ & = \text{(0.791 g/mL)(250 mL)} \\ & = \text{197.75 g} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{198 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of the sample of methanol is 198 g.

4. SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{700 mmHg} \\ & V_1 = \text{18.0 L} \\ & T_1 = 25^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{298 K} \\ & V_2 = \text{30.0 L} \\ & T_2 = 15^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{288 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure using combined gas law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1T_2}{V_2T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{700 mmHg})(\text{18.0 L})(\text{288 K})}{(\text{30.0 L})(\text{298 K})} \\ & = \boxed{\text{406 mmHg}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pressure of the gas at 15°C and 30.0 L is 406 mmHg.

There are several gas equations used, with various conditions

Boyle's Law

At a constant temperature, the gas volume is inversely proportional to the pressure applied

Gay Lussac's Law

When the volume is not changed, the gas pressure in the tube is proportional to its absolute temperature

Boyle-Gay-Lussac Law

Combined with Boyle's law and Gay Lussac's law

P1 = initial gas pressure (N / m² or Pa)

V1 = initial gas volume (m³)

P2 = final gas pressure

V2 = final gas volume

T1 = initial gas temperature (K)

T2 = final gas temperature

volume of 600 mL

the pressure 700 mmHg

temperature 27 ° C = 27 + 273 = 300 K

the pressure 500 mmHg

temperature 20 ° C = 20 +273 = 293 K

28. The temperature of 578 ml chlorine is 20oC. What will be its volume when the temperature changes to 10oC? Show your complete solutions. Thank you po! :)


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_1 = \text{578 mL} \\ & T_1 = 20^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{293 K} \\ & T_2 = 10^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{283 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Charles' law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{V_1}{T_1} & = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \\ V_2T_1 & = V_1T_2 \\ \frac{V_2T_1}{T_1} & = \frac{V_1T_2}{T_1} \\ V_2 & = \frac{V_1T_2}{T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{578 mL})(\text{283 K})}{\text{293 K}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{558 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of the gas will be 558 mL.
