20. The area of a rectangular rug is 66 square feet. If the length of the rug is 5 ft longer than the width, what is the width of the rug?


Answer:

width = 6ft

Step-by-step explanation:

Area = length × width

66 ft² = (5+w) × w

66 ft² = 5w + w²

w² + 5w = 66

Subtract both sides by 66

w² + 5w - 66 = 66-66

w² + 5w - 66 = 0 -> in form of quadratic equation

By using conpleting the square:

w² + 5w +(5/2×1)² = 66 +(5/2×1)²

Take the half of the coefficient of the second variable which is 5, and square itAdd Step 1 to the other side of the equation

w² + 5w +(2.5)² = 66 + 6.25

w² + 5w +6.25 = 72.25

Tip: Take the half of the second term's coeficient, that half would be the second term included on the parenthesis as shown below.

(w + 2.5)² = 72.25

take the square root of both sides

√(w +2.5 )² = √72.5

w + 2.5 = 8.5

Subtract both side of the equation by 2.5 to isolate w

w + 2.5 - 2.5 = 8.5 - 2.5

w + 0 = 6

w = 6

The width of the rug is 6 ft

Check:

Area = 5w + w²

Area = 5(6) + (6)²

Area = 30 + 36

Area = 66 ft²

Therefore the width of the rug is indeed 6 ft
