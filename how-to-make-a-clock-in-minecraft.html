8. the second hand of a clock 15 cm long has a mass of 30 grams. how much is the centripetal force that makes it move? ​


Answer:

To calculate the centripetal force acting on the second hand of a clock, we need to know the speed at which it is moving and the radius of its circular path.

Assuming that the second hand completes one revolution every 60 seconds (i.e., one minute), the speed of the tip of the second hand can be calculated as follows:

circumference of the circle = 2πr

where r = length of the second hand = 15 cm

So, the circumference = 2π(15 cm) = 30π cm

The time taken to complete one revolution (period) = 60 seconds

Therefore, the speed of the tip of the second hand = distance / time = (30π cm) / (60 seconds) = 0.5π cm/s

Now, we can calculate the centripetal force using the following formula:

centripetal force = (mass x velocity^2) / radius

where mass = 30 grams = 0.03 kg, velocity = 0.5π cm/s, and radius = 15 cm

Substituting the values, we get:

centripetal force = (0.03 kg x (0.5π cm/s)^2) / 15 cm

= 0.0037 N (approx)

Therefore, the centripetal force acting on the second hand of the clock is approximately 0.0037 Newtons.
