5. Carlo found a big box filled with equal sizes of smaller boxes. If the big box measures 12 cm by 6 cm by 10 cm, how many smaller boxes has Carlo found if each of the smaller boxes measured 5 cm × 4 cm × 3 cm?​


Answer:

Carlo found 12 smaller boxes inside the big box.

Step-by-step explanation:

Since the big box contained smaller boxes, we need to know how big the volume of the big box is. For us to do so, we need to use the formula for the area of a rectangular prism. We know that the big box is a rectangular prism because it's dimensions or sides are different from one another. We just need to multiply the length, width, and height of the big box. We will now have:

12 cm × 6 cm × 10 cm = 720 [tex]cm^{3}[/tex]

We now know that the volume of the big box is 720 [tex]cm^{3}[/tex]

We then find the volume of the smaller box. The smaller boxes are also rectangular prisms. Hence, we use the same formula. We just need to multiply the length, width, and height of the small box. We will now have:

5 cm × 4 cm × 3 cm = 60 [tex]cm^{3}[/tex]

We now know that the volume of one small box is 60 [tex]cm^{3}[/tex]

We just need to find out how many small boxes can fit in the big box. We can do so by dividing the volume of the big box by the volume of the small box.

720 ÷ 60 = 12

This means that we can fit 12 smaller boxes inside the big box.

[tex]V_b=lwh=12(5)(2)=120cm^3[/tex]
