6. The time (t) it takes to travel from North Vancouver to Hope varies inversely as the speed (v) at which one travels. If it takes 3 hours to travel this distance at an average speed of 140 km/h, find the constant (k) and the amount of time it would take to drive back if you were only able to travel at 70 km/h due to an engine problem. plz answer this asap ​


Answer:

The time it takes to travel from North Vancouver to Hope varies inversely as the speed of travel, meaning that:

t = k / v

where t is the time, v is the speed, and k is a constant.

Since we know that it takes 3 hours to travel this distance at an average speed of 140 km/h, we can use this information to find the value of k:

3 = k / 140

k = 3 x 140

k = 420

Now, to find the amount of time it would take to drive back if you were only able to travel at 70 km/h due to an engine problem, we can use the equation we have and substitute 70 km/h for v:

t = k / v

t = 420 / 70

t = 6 hours

So, it would take 6 hours to drive back if you were only able to travel at 70 km/h due to an engine problem.

13. You are diving in Vancouver and you pass through the George Massey Tunnel on your way from Delta to Richmond. You are travelling at 90 km/h and the tunnel is 629 m long. Assuming your speed remains constant, how long are you in the tunnel for?​


To solve this problem, we can use the formula:

time = distance / speed

where:
distance = 629 m
speed = 90 km/h

First, we need to convert the speed from km/h to m/s:

90 km/h = (90 x 1000 m) / (60 x 60 s) = 25 m/s

Now we can plug in the values and solve for time:

time = 629 m / 25 m/s = 25.16 s

Therefore, you are in the tunnel for approximately 25.16 seconds.

Answer:

k = 180

t = 3 hours

Step-by-step explanation:

A.

Given: Time(t) = 1.5 hours , Speed(v) = 120 km/h

Find: Constant(k) of variation [inverse].

t = [tex]\frac{k}{v}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1.5}{1} = \frac{k}{120}[/tex]

k = 180

The constant of variation is equal to 180.

B.

Given: Speed(v) = 60 km/h , Constant(k) = 180

Find: Time(t) it would take to drive back.

t = [tex]\frac{k}{v}[/tex]

t =  [tex]\frac{180}{60}[/tex]

t = 3

Therefore, the amount of time it would take to drive back if you were only able to travel at 60 km/ h due to an engine problem is 3 hours.
