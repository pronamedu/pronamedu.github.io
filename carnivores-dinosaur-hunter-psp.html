Answer:

Herbivore is a term for plant-eating animals. Carnivore is a term for meat-eating animals. Animals that eat both plants and meat are called omnivores.

Herbivore dinosaurs:

1. Ammosaurus

2. Brachiosaurus

3. Chasmosaurus

4. Dacentrurus

5. Leptoceratops

Carnivore dinosaurs:

1. Tyrannosaurus Rex

2. Allosaurus

3. Megalosaurus

4. Giganotosaurus

5. Spinosaurus

Details of explanation:

Herbivores mostly eat plants by munching fruits, seeds, grass, aquatic vegetation, and other plants. They have a unique digestive system to handle different types of plants. Usually, herbivores have big front teeth to grasp and cut plants. The size of herbivore animals often comes from medium to big-sized animals.

Carnivores hunt other animals for food to eat. They usually have strong jaws and sharp teeth to cut and tear up their food. They also often have long and sharp claws. Carnivore animals have an important role to control the population of other animals.

Herbivore animal lives depend on the number of plant growth. When the number of plants decreased, it leads to declining in herbivore numbers. It also affects the number of carnivore animals, because carnivores lives depend on the number of herbivore animals.
