16. John got a bag at a cost of P2,400 and wants to sell it at 40% mark up based on retail price. How much will she sell the bag for ?​


Answer:

A 40% markup on the retail price means that the cost of the item will be increased by 40% when it is sold. To find out the retail price, we can use the formula:

Retail Price = Cost Price / (1 - markup percentage)

In this case, the cost price is P2,400 and the markup percentage is 40%, so we can plug these values into the formula:

Retail Price = P2,400 / (1 - 0.40)

Retail Price = P2,400 / 0.6

Retail Price = P4,000

So, John will sell the bag for P4,000
