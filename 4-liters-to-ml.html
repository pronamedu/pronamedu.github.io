12. The tetraethyl lead [Pb(C2H5)4] in a 25.00 mL sample of aviation gasoline was shaken with 15.00 mL of 0.02095 M I2. The reaction is Pb(C2H5)4 + I2 → Pb(C2H5)3I + C2H5I After the reaction was complete, the unused I2 was titrated with 6.09 mL of 0.03465 M Na2S2O3. Calculate the weight (in milligrams) of Pb(C2H5)4 (323.4 g/mol) in each liter of the gasoline.


The weight (in milligrams) of Pb (C₂H₅)₄ in each liter of the gasoline = 2700 mg/L

Further explanation

Iodometric titration is one of the titrations using a redox reaction iodine/iodide to determine the concentration of oxidation potential compounds smaller than the iodine-iodide system

In the above reaction there are two stages of reaction:

1. The reaction of TEL with iodine

Pb (C₂H₅)₄ + I₂ → Pb(C₂H₅)₃I + C₂H₅I

2. The excess iodine with thiosulfate reaction

I₂ + 2S₂O₃²⁻ → 2I⁻ + S₄O₆²⁻

Then to find the weight (in milligrams) of Pb (C₂H₅)₄ then we determine the calculation of the reaction stage 2 then proceed to the reaction stage 1

1. Reaction stage 2

I₂ + 2S₂O₃²⁻ → 2I⁻ + S₄O₆²⁻

The redox reaction coefficient above shows the ratio of mol I₂ to mol thiosulfate = 1: 2

mol thiosulfate = 6.09 mL of 0.03465 M = 0.211 mlmol

so that mole I₂ = 1/2 x 0.211 mlmol = 0.1055 mlmol

Reaction stage 1

Initial mole I₂ = 15.00 mL of 0.02095 M = 0.3142 mlmol

So that mole I₂ which reacts perfectly with Pb (C₂H₅)₄ = 0.3142 - 0.1055

= 0.2087 mlmol

Because the mole ratio of Pb (C₂H₅)₄ and I₂ in the reaction stage 1 = 1: 1, then mole Pb (C₂H₅)₄ = mole I₂ which reacts completely = 0.2087 mlmol = 0.0002087 mole

Then the mass of Pb (C₂H₅)₄

= mlmol x molar mass Pb (C₂H₅)₄

= 0,0002087 x 323.4

= 0.0675 g

= 67.5 mg

In 25 ml of the sample becomes:

[tex]\rm =\dfrac{67.5~mg}{0.025~L}\\\\=\boxed{\bold{2700~mg/L}}[/tex]
