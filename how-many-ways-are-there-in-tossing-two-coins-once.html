Answer:

1.4 different ways

Because each coin can land in two possible ways (heads or tails), the two coins can land in 2 × 2 = 4 different ways.

2.eight

The number of different outcomes when three coins are tossed is 2 × 2 × 2 = 8. All eight possible outcome are HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, and TTT. Step 2. There are four possible events: 0 heads, 1 head, 2 heads, and 3 heads.

3.The dice can fall in 6x9x12 = 648 ways. There is only one way that all of them fall 1. From here on, we will not count all 1's again. If the 6-sided and 9-sided are 1's, there are 11 ways for the 12-sided to fall.

4.36 possibilities

Note that there are 36 possibilities for (a,b). This total number of possibilities can be obtained from the multiplication principle: there are 6 possibilities for a, and for each outcome for a, there are 6 possibilities for b.

5.12 possible

When a die is rolled and penny flipped, there are 6 × 2 = 12 possible outcomes
