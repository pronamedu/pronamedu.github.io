10. V = 331 m/s + (0.6 m/s/c) (90°)???V = 331 m/s + (0.6 m/s/c) (180°)???V = 331 m/s + (0.6 m/s/c) (360°)???​


The expression "(0.6 m/s/c) (90°)" doesn't make sense, as it appears to be multiplying a unit of speed (meters per second) by an angle (degrees). Similarly, "(0.6 m/s/c) (180°)" and "(0.6 m/s/c) (360°)" also do not make sense.

If we assume that the intended units for the angular velocity are radians per second, we can calculate the resulting velocities as follows:

- V = 331 m/s + (0.6 rad/s) * (90 degrees * pi/180) = 331.38 m/s

- V = 331 m/s + (0.6 rad/s) * (180 degrees * pi/180) = 332.16 m/s

- V = 331 m/s + (0.6 rad/s) * (360 degrees * pi/180) = 334.72 m/s

where pi/180 is the conversion factor from degrees to radians. Note that these calculations assume that the angular velocity is constant over the given time interval.
