4. sino Nagpalaganap ng Division of Labor?


Answer:

The French scholar Émile Durkheim first used the phrase division of labour in a sociological sense in his discussion of social evolution. Rather than viewing division of labour as a consequence of a desire for material abundance, Durkheim stated that specialization arose from changes in social structure caused by an assumed natural increase in the size and density of population and a corresponding increase in competition for survival. Division of labour functioned to keep societies from breaking apart under these conditions.

18. why dos division of labor increase productivity? ​


Answer:

As labor is divided amongst workers, workers are able to focus on a few or even one task. The more they focus on one task, the more efficient they become at this task, which means that less time and less money is involved in producing a good.

20. some advantages and disadvantages of having labor unions?


Advantages:
1. Labor unions promote higher wages.
2. Labor unions help workers get better benefits.
3. Labor unions help families receive better benefits.
4. Labor unions provide better access to a funded retirement.
5. Labor unions provider worker protections.

Disadvantages:
1. Labor unions can discount worker education and experience.
2. Labor unions require ongoing dues and may require initiation fees.
3. Labor unions may participate in activities that workers disagree upon.
4. Labor unions discourage individuality.
5. Labor unions offer job bumping arrangements.

26. differentiate of division of labor and specialization​


Answer:

There is no significant difference between division of labour and specialization of labour as these are synonyms. Both these concepts involve dividing the main process into different tasks, assigning each task to individual workers or group of workers.
