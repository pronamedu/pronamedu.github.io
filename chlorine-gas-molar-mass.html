1. What is the molar mass of chlorine gas?


 Whenever you are referring to molar mass, the correct unit is in expressed in terms of grams. Since there are two atoms per molecule of chlorine, this would yield 2 Cl atoms per Cl molecule x 35.5 amu per Cl atom = 71.0 amu. 

This the molecular weight of chlorine molecule. To obtain the molar mass, simply replace amu with grams. Therefore, the correct answer for the molar mass of chlorine gas is 71.0 grams.

12. In an experiment to measure the molar mass of a gas, 250 cm3 of the gas was confined in a glass vessel. The pressure was 152 Torr at 298 K and, after correcting for buoyancy effects, the mass of the gas was 33.5 mg. What is the molar mass of the gas?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{152 torr = 0.200 atm} \\ & V = \text{250 cm}^3 = \text{0.250 L} \\ & T = \text{298 K} \\ & mass = \text{33.5 mg = 0.0335 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.200 atm})(\text{0.250 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{298 K})} \\ & = \text{0.0020447 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molar mass of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} MM & = \frac{mass}{n} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0335 g}}{\text{0.0020447 mol}} \\ & = \text{16.384 g/mol} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{16.4 g/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar mass of the gas is 16.4 g/mol.

16. An unknown gas effuses 1.73 times faster than krypton. what is the molar mass of the gas?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & r_{\text{gas}} = 1.73r_{\text{Kr}} \\ & MM_{\text{Kr}} = \text{83.8 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Use Graham's law of diffusion/effusion to calculate the rate of effusion of a gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \sqrt{\frac{MM_{\text{gas}}}{MM_{\text{Kr}}}} & = \frac{r_{\text{Kr}}}{r_{\text{gas}}} \\ \sqrt{\frac{MM_{\text{gas}}}{\text{83.8 g/mol}}} & = \frac{r_{\text{Kr}}}{1.73r_{\text{Kr}}} \\ \frac{MM_{\text{gas}}}{\text{83.8 g/mol}} & = \left(\frac{1}{1.73}\right)^2 \\ MM_{\text{gas}} & = (\text{83.8 g/mol})\left(\frac{1}{1.73}\right)^2 \\ & = \boxed{\text{28.0 g/mol}} \\ \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar mass of the unknown gas is 28.0 g/mol.

19. A 1.00-L sample of a gas has a mass of 1.92 g at STP. What is the molar mass (g/mol) of the gas?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

The temperature and pressure of an ideal gas at STP are 273.15 K and 1.00 atm, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{1.00 atm} \\ & V = \text{1.00 L} \\ & T = \text{273.15 K} \\ & mass = \text{1.92 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{1.00 atm})(\text{1.00 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{273.15 K})} \\ & = \text{0.044615 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molar mass of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} MM & = \frac{mass}{n} \\ & = \frac{\text{1.92 g}}{\text{0.044615 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{43.0 g/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar mass of the gas is 43.0 g/mol.

20. = Get the empirical and molecular formulas of a refrigerant whose mass is composed of 13.85% carbon, 41.89% chlorine, and 44.06% fluorine and has a molar mass of 315.1 mol​


Explanation:

First, calculate the moles of each element present in one mole of the refrigerant:

Carbon: 13.85 g C / (12.01 g/mol) = 1.157 mol C

Chlorine: 41.89 g Cl / (35.45 g/mol) = 1.185 mol Cl

Fluorine: 44.06 g F / (19.00 g/mol) = 2.315 mol F

Next, divide each of these values by the smallest one to get the empirical formula ratios:

Carbon: 1.157 mol / 1.157 mol = 1

Chlorine: 1.185 mol / 1.157 mol = 1.024

Fluorine: 2.315 mol / 1.157 mol = 2

So, the empirical formula is CCl2F2.

Finally, divide the molar mass of the refrigerant (315.1 g/mol) by the molar mass of the empirical formula (1 x 12.01 g/mol + 2 x 35.45 g/mol + 2 x 19.00 g/mol = 126.08 g/mol) to get the empirical formula mass ratio:

315.1 g/mol / 126.08 g/mol = 2.5

So, the molecular formula is (CCl2F2) * 2.5 = C5Cl10F10.
