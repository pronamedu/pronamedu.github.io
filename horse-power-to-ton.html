16. A horse performs 15000 joules Of work pupunningawagon for 20 seconds.what is the horse power.​


Formula:

[tex]\boxed{\textsf{P}=\dfrac{\textsf{W}}{\textsf{t}} }[/tex]

where

P = power

W = work

t = time

Solution:

[tex]\textsf{P}=\dfrac{\textsf{15000 J}}{\textsf{20 s}} =\textsf{750 W}\\\\\textsf{P}=\textsf{750 W}\times\dfrac{\textsf{1 hp}}{\textsf{746 W}}\\\\\boxed{\textsf{P = 1.01 hp}}[/tex]

Note: [tex]\textsf{1 hp}=\textsf{746 W}[/tex]

22. A horse moves a sleigh 1.00 kilometer by applying a horizontal 2,000-newton force on its harness for 45 minutes. What is the power of the horse? (Hint: Convert time to seconds.)


Answer:

≈740.7 Watts

Explanation:

Given: horse moves a sleigh a distance =1.00 km with force =2000 N for 45 minutes

Power= Work/Time with units of [N⋅m/s = J/s = Watt]

Work=Force×distance with units of [N⋅m = Joule = J]

Work of the given horse: W = 2000 N × 1000 m = 2,000,000J

Power = 2,000,000J/45min × 1min/60s ≈740.7 Watts

1 hp ≈ 745.7W
