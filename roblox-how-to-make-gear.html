8. Two gears of radii 3.5 cm and 5.8 cm are adjusted so that the smaller gear drives the larger one, as shown. If the smaller gear rotates counterclockwise through 225°, through how many degrees will the larger gear rotate?


To find the answer, we need to first find the ratio of the radii of the two gears. The radius of the smaller gear is 3.5 cm and the radius of the larger gear is 5.8 cm, so the ratio of their radii is 3.5 cm / 5.8 cm = 0.603.

Next, we need to find the ratio of the number of teeth on the two gears. Since the smaller gear is driving the larger gear, the number of teeth on the smaller gear must be greater than the number of teeth on the larger gear. Let's say the number of teeth on the smaller gear is x and the number of teeth on the larger gear is y. The ratio of the number of teeth on the two gears is x / y.

Since the radii of the two gears are in a 0.603:1 ratio and the number of teeth on the two gears are in a x:y ratio, the ratio of the number of teeth on the smaller gear to the number of teeth on the larger gear must be 0.603:1. This means that the ratio of the number of teeth on the two gears is 0.603:1 = x:y.

Next, we need to find the ratio of the angular velocities of the two gears. Since the smaller gear is driving the larger gear, the angular velocity of the smaller gear must be greater than the angular velocity of the larger gear. Let's say the angular velocity of the smaller gear is w and the angular velocity of the larger gear is z. The ratio of the angular velocities of the two gears is w / z.

Since the ratio of the radii of the two gears is 0.603:1 and the ratio of the number of teeth on the two gears is x:y, the ratio of the angular velocities of the two gears must be the same as the ratio of the radii of the two gears, or 0.603:1 = w:z.

Finally, we can use the ratio of the angular velocities of the two gears to find the number of degrees through which the larger gear will rotate. Since the smaller gear rotates through 225°, and the ratio of the angular velocities of the two gears is 0.603:1 = w:z, the larger gear will rotate through a total of 225° * (1 / 0.603) = 375°.

Therefore, if the smaller gear rotates counterclockwise through 225°, the larger gear will rotate through 375°.

If that happens, the gears can seize, locking up the rear wheels and potentially causing a lot of damage or even an accident.

20. Learning how to drive a car with a stick is difficult. The location of each other gear and the best speed to drive for each gear must be best speed


Answer:

1.Understand the different pedals

2.Understand the gear shifter

3.Start the car

Explanation:

Before starting the car, take a look at the gear shifter. Most cars with manual transmissions have a gear shifter in the middle of the passenger and driver seats. First gear is typically located at the top left corner and the remaining four or five gears rotate from top to bottom, left to right. The gear locations will be clearly marked on most stick shift vehicles. If the gear shift is located in the center, the car will be in neutral, at which point you should be able to easily move the gear shifter back and forth.

Before you begin to drive a stick shift, you need to understand how this type of car differs from cars with automatic transmissions. While seated in the driver's seat, take a look at the three pedals at your feet. The first pedal on the left is the clutch. This pedal appears only in cars with manual transmissions. The middle pedal is the brake. The right pedal is the accelerator. You will use your left foot for the clutch and your right foot for the brake and accelerator. When you push in the clutch, or step down with your left foot on the clutch pedal, you are disengaging the assembly. When you are lifting your foot off the clutch pedal, the friction of the assembly starts moving, causing your vehicle to move.

To start a stick shift, first press the clutch all the way to the floor board with your left foot. Ensure that the gear shifter is in the neutral position then turn the ignition with the key. If you are certain that the car is in neutral, you can remove your foot from the clutch. Ensure that the parking or emergency brake is not in use.
