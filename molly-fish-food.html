4. Your little sister Molly is one third the age of your mom. In 12years,Molly will be half the age of your mom.how old is Molly and your mom now?solution


Present ages:
Mom: x
Molly: 1 (x)
          3

In 12 years (+12):
Mom:  x + 12
Molly: 1 (x) + 12
          3

Equation: In 12 years, Molly will be half age of Mom:

[tex] \frac{x + 12}{2} = \frac{1}{3} x +12[/tex]

[tex]2( \frac{1}{3}) (x)+ 2(12) = x + 12[/tex]

[tex] \frac{2}{3} x+24 = x+12[/tex]

[tex] \frac{3}{3} x- \frac{2}{3} x = 24 - 12[/tex]

[tex] \frac{1}{3} x = 12[/tex]

[tex]( \frac{3}{1} )( \frac{1}{3} )x = 12( \frac{3}{1} )[/tex]

x= 36

Present ages:
Mom: x = 36 years old
Molly: 1/3 (36) = 12 years old

12. Chris and Molly receive prize money. this split it 2:3. molly has 240$. how much does Chris receive.


Answer:

Chris receives $160.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Ratio of Chris' prize money to Molly's prize money  ⇒  2:3Molly's prize:  $240Chris' prize money:  x

Find the prize money Chris receives:

2 : 3 = x : 240  ⇒  Direct proportion

3x = 2(240)   ⇒   Product of the means is equal to product of extremes

3x = 480

3x/3 = 480/3

x = 160

Chris receives $160.
