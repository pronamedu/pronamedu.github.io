18. the base is a polygon if faces are triangles ​


Answer:

If the faces of a polygonal solid are triangles, then the base of the solid must also be a polygon.

To understand why, we need to consider the definition of a polygonal solid. A polygonal solid is a three-dimensional figure that has flat faces that are polygons, and the faces are joined along their edges.

In the case where the faces are triangles, each face is a polygon with three sides. The edges of the triangles are joined to form the sides of the solid. The base of the solid is the face on which the solid rests, and since the faces are triangles, the base must also be a polygon with three sides.

Therefore, the base of a polygonal solid with triangular faces must be a polygon.
