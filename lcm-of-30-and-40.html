25. what is the lcm of 30 40 and 50​


What is the LCM of 30 and 50?

Answer: LCM of 30 and 50 is 150.

Explanation:

The LCM of two non-zero integers, x(30) and y(50), is the smallest positive integer m(150) that is divisible by both x(30) and y(50) without any remainder.

Methods to Find LCM of 30 and 50

The methods to find the LCM of 30 and 50 are explained below.

By Division Method

By Listing Multiples

By Prime Factorization Method

LCM of 30 and 50 by Division Method

To calculate the LCM of 30 and 50 by the division method, we will divide the numbers(30, 50) by their prime factors (preferably common). The product of these divisors gives the LCM of 30 and 50.

Step 1: Find the smallest prime number that is a factor of at least one of the numbers, 30 and 50. Write this prime number(2) on the left of the given numbers(30 and 50), separated as per the ladder arrangement.

Step 2: If any of the given numbers (30, 50) is a multiple of 2, divide it by 2 and write the quotient below it. Bring down any number that is not divisible by the prime number.

Step 3: Continue the steps until only 1s are left in the last row.

The LCM of 30 and 50 is the product of all prime numbers on the left, i.e. LCM(30, 50) by division method = 2 × 3 × 5 × 5 = 150.

LCM of 30 and 50 by Listing Multiples

LCM of 30 and 50 by Listing Multiples Method

To calculate the LCM of 30 and 50 by listing out the common multiples, we can follow the given below steps:

Step 1: List a few multiples of 30 (30, 60, 90, 120, . . . ) and 50 (50, 100, 150, 200, . . . . )

Step 2: The common multiples from the multiples of 30 and 50 are 150, 300, . . .

Step 3: The smallest common multiple of 30 and 50 is 150.

∴ The least common multiple of 30 and 50 = 150.

LCM of 30 and 50 by Prime Factorization

Prime factorization of 30 and 50 is (2 × 3 × 5) = 21 × 31 × 51 and (2 × 5 × 5) = 21 × 52 respectively. LCM of 30 and 50 can be obtained by multiplying prime factors raised to their respective highest power, i.e. 21 × 31 × 52 = 150.

Hence, the LCM of 30 and 50 by prime factorization is 150.

LCM of 30 and 50 Examples

Example 1: The GCD and LCM of two numbers are 10 and 150 respectively. If one number is 30, find the other number.

Solution:

Let the other number be p.

∵ GCD × LCM = 30 × p

⇒ p = (GCD × LCM)/30

⇒ p = (10 × 150)/30

⇒ p = 50

Therefore, the other number is 50.

Example 2: Find the smallest number that is divisible by 30 and 50 exactly.

Solution:

The smallest number that is divisible by 30 and 50 exactly is their LCM.

⇒ Multiples of 30 and 50:

Multiples of 30 = 30, 60, 90, 120, 150, 180, 210, . . . .

Multiples of 50 = 50, 100, 150, 200, 250, 300, 350, . . . .

Therefore, the LCM of 30 and 50 is 150.

Example 3: The product of two numbers is 1500. If their GCD is 10, what is their LCM?

Solution:

Given: GCD = 10

product of numbers = 1500

∵ LCM × GCD = product of numbers

⇒ LCM = Product/GCD = 1500/10

Therefore, the LCM is 150.

The probable combination for the given case is LCM(30, 50) = 150.
