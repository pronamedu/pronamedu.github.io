ADDITION AND SUBTRACTION OF FRACTIONS

Answers with Step-by-step explanation:

1. Mrs. Romero bought 4/5 kilogram of cabbage and 2/10 kilograms of carrots. How many kilograms of cabbage did Mrs. Romero buy than carrots?

A. 2/3 kg B. 3/5 kg C. 4/7 kg D. 5/8 kg

Solution:

We can solve for the difference between the quantity of cabbages and carrots that Mrs. Romero bought by subtracting them. So:

4/5 - 2/10

We can still convert 2/10 to its simplest form by dividing the GCF of the numerator and denominator which is 2. Thus, 2/10 is converted to 1/5.

Since 1/5 and 4/5 are already similar fractions, we can directly subtract the numerators. So:

4/5 - 1/5 = 3/5

Therefore, there is 3/5 kilogram more cabbages than carrots that Mrs. Romero bought. The correct answer is letter B.

2. Mr. Escobar has 4/8 box of nails to make a cabinet. He used 3/8 box of nails to make a cabinet. How much does he have left?

A. 4/8 B. 3/8 C. 2/8 D. 1/8

Solution:

Since 4/8 and 3/8 are already similar fractions, simply subtract the numerators:

4/8 - 3/8 = 1/8

Therefore, there is still 1/8 box of nails that Mr. Escobar have. The correct answer is letter D.

3. A cake requires 2/5 cup of sugar for frosting and 1/5 cup of sugar for the cake. What is the total amount of sugar needed?

A. 3/5 B. 1/5 C. 2/5 D. 4/5​

Solution:

This word problem requires addition of similar fractions. Just simply add the numerators:

2/5 + 1/5 = 3/5

Therefore, the cake needs a total of 3/5 cup of sugar.

28. Mr. Escobar has 7/8 box of nails to make a cabinet. He used 3/8 box of nails to make a cabinet How Much does he have left?​


Answer:

[tex]\frac{4}{8}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

[tex]\frac{7}{8} - \frac{3}{8} = \frac{4}{8}[/tex]

#CarryonLearning

Problem:

Mr. Escobar has 7/8 box of nails to make a cabinet. He used 3/8 box of nails to make a cabinet How Much does he have left?

What is Asked?

How Much does he have left of a box of nails?

The operation to be used:

Subtraction

Number Sentence:

[tex] \frac{7}{8} - \frac{3}{8} = n[/tex]

Answer:

[tex] \frac{1}{2} [/tex]

Solution:

[tex] \frac{7}{8} - \frac{3}{8} = n \\ \\ 7 - 3 = 4 \\ \\ \frac{4}{8} \\ \\ 4 \div 4 = 1 \\ 8 \div 4 = 2 \\ \\ \\ = \frac{1}{2} [/tex]

( The answer is in the lowest term )
