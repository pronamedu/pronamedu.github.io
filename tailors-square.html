26. 1. Tailor's chalk2. L-square3. Seam ripper4. Tape measure5. Hand needle​


Explanation:

1. Tailor's chalk -- is a thin piece of hard chalk for making the cloth, it is available in a range colors and is removed by brushing.

2. L-square -- is also known as tailor's square, made if wood or alluminum and is calibrated on both sides used to make perpendicular lines and to break down measurements into 6ths, 8ths, 12ths, etc.

3. Seam ripper -- seam rippers are specifically designed for ripping out stitches from seams, either as a result of an error or during alterations. They should be used carefully to prevent damage to the fabric.

4. Tape measure -- is a flexible measuring device usually marked with inches on one side and centimeter on the other use to take body measurements as well as any measuring task.

5. Hand needle -- a small, slender rod-like instrument with a sharp point on one and an eye or hole for thread on the other used to pass thread through cloth to make stitches in sewing.
