21. The distance a body falls from rest varies directly as the square of the time it falls (ignoring air resistance). If a ball falls 126 feet in three seconds, how far will the ball fall in seven seconds?a.686 feetb.784 feetc.812 feetd.896 feet​


APPLICATION OF DIRECT PROPORTION

Problem:

The distance a body falls from rest varies directly as the square of the time it falls (ignoring air resistance). If a ball falls 126 feet in three seconds, how far will the ball fall in seven seconds?

a. 686 feet

b. 784 feet

c. 812 feet

d. 896 feet​

Answer: The correct answer is letter A.

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve this, let us formulate first the equation of this direct variation. Let us represent D as the distance of the ball from rest and t as the time it will take for it to reach the ground. So:

D = kt^2

where k is the proportionality constant

By direct substitution:

126 = (3^2)k

126 = 9k

k = 14

Let us use the value of k that we obtained here to solve for D.

D = 14(7^2)

D = 14(49)

D = 686 feet

Therefore, the ball can reach 686 feet as it falls in 7 seconds.
