13. What is the approximate molarity and normality of a 13.0 per cent by weight solution of H2SO4 (SG = 1.090)? To what volume should 100 mL of the acid be diluted in order to prepare a 1.50 N solution?


Molarity : 1.45 M

Normality : 2.9 N

Dilution volume : 193 ml

Further explanation

Molarity is a way to express the concentration of the solution  

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solute or mmol in each ml of solution  

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{M=\dfrac{n}{V}}}[/tex]  

Where  

M = Molarity  

n = Number of moles of solute  

V = Volume of solution  

Normality = the number of moles equivalent of solute in 1 liter of solution

Relationship between normality and molarity

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{N = M \times n}}~, n=valence (amount~of~H^+~or~OH^-)[/tex]

1.Molarity

13.0 per cent by weight (13% w/w)solution of H₂SO₄ (SG = 1.090)

Assuming there is 100 grams of solution, then

H₂SO₄ = 13 gr

mol H₂SO₄

[tex]\rm n=\dfrac{13}{98\Rightarrow molar~mass~of~H_2SO_4}=0.133[/tex]

Volume of solution =

[tex]\rm =\dfrac{mass~of~solution}{SG}\\\\=\dfrac{100}{1.09}=91.74~ml=0.09174~L[/tex]

Then :

[tex]\rm Molarity=\dfrac{n}{V}=\dfrac{0.133}{0.09174}=1.45~M[/tex]

Or we can use simple formula to find Molarity from %weight :

[tex]\rm M=\%weight\times SG\times \dfrac{1000}{Mw}\\\\M=0.13\times 1.09\times \dfrac{1000}{98}=1.45~M[/tex]

2. Normality

Because H₂SO₄ produce 2 H⁺, so valence = 2, then

Normality = Molarity x 2

[tex]\rm N=M\times 2\\\\N=1.45\times 2=2.9~N[/tex]

3. Dilution

Formula :

[tex]\rm V_1.N_1-V_2.N_2[/tex]

V₁ = 100 ml

N₁ = 2.9 N

N₂ = 1.5 N

Then :

[tex]\rm 100\times 2.9=V_2\times 1.5\\\\V_2=\boxed{\bold{193~ml}}[/tex]

15. how many grams are there in 7.50*10 ^23 molecules of h2so4?


Answer:

122.08 grams of sulfuric acid

Explanation:

Determining Mass from Number of Particles

To determine the number of particles (molecules or atoms) from a given mass of any compound, you need first to calculate the molar mass.

The molar mass is calculated by obtain the sum of the atomic mass of the individual atoms in a compound.

The molar mass of sulfuric acid ([tex]\mathrm{H_2SO_4}[/tex]) is:

2 H × 1.01  = 2.02

1 S × 32.0 = 32.0

4 O × 16.0 = 64.0

Adding 2.02 + 32.0 + 64.0, you will get 98.02 g/mol as the molar mass of sulfuric acid.

Now, since you have the number of molecules and molar mass, you need to remember that 1 mol = [tex]6.022\times 10^{23}[/tex] molecules (also known as Avogadro's number)

To calculate for mass of sulfuric acid,

[tex]\displaystyle 7.50\times 10^{23}\;\mathrm{molecules}\;\times\; \frac{1\;\mathrm{mole}}{6.022\times10^{23}\;\mathrm{molecules}}\;\times\; \frac{98.02\;\mathrm{g}}{1\;\mathrm{mol}} = 122.077382929\;\mathrm{grams}[/tex]

Thus, there are 122.08 grams of sulfuric acid.

To recall, in order to get the mass in grams from number of particles:

1. Obtain the molar mass.

2. Divide the given number of particles by Avogadro's number.

3. Multiply the answer in step 2 by the molar mass obtained in step 1.

21. Calculate the number of grams of sulfuric acid, H2SO4, necessary to prepare 225 mL of 0.150 N sulfuric acid solutions in reactions that replace both hydrogen ions.


Solution:

Note: H₂SO₄ is the solute and water is the solvent.

Step 1: Calculate the number of equivalents of solute.

[tex]\text{normality} = \frac{\text{equivalents of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalents of solute = normality × volume of solution (L)}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalents of solute = 0.150 N × 0.225 L}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalent of solute = 0.0338 eq}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 98.09 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the equivalent weight of solute.

[tex]\text{equivalent weight} = \frac{\text{molar mass}}{\text{no. of replaceable ions}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalent weight} = \frac{\text{98.09 g/mol}}{\text{2 eq/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{equivalent weight = 49.045 g/eq}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mass of solute.

[tex]\text{mass of solute = equivalents of solute × equivalent weight}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 0.0338 eq × 49.045 g/eq}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 1.66 g}}[/tex]

30. A dilute sulfuric acid (H2SO4) solution is being prepared by the workers of an oil refinery. They had 105 grams of sulfuric acid used to prepare 175 L of solution. What is the molar concentration or molarity of the solution they are preparing? solve this problem.​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (H₂SO₄).

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (32.07 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 98.086 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 105 g H₂SO₄} × \frac{\text{1 mol H₂SO₄}}{\text{98.086 g H₂SO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 1.0705 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{1.0705 mol}}{\text{175 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 0.00612 M}}[/tex]
