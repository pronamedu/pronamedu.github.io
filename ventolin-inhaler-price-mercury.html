12. Mercury has a density of 13.6 g/cm³. What is the mass of 95mL of mercury in grams?


The mass of Mercury : 1292 grams

Further explanation

Density is a quantity derived from the mass and volume

Density is the ratio of mass per unit volume

With the same mass, the volume of objects that have a high density will be smaller than objects with low density

The unit of density can be expressed in g / cm³ or kg / m³

Density formula:

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\rho ~ = ~ \dfrac {m} {V}}}}[/tex]

ρ = density

m = mass

v = volume

A common example is the water density of 1 gr / cm³

Given

density of Mercury: 13.6 g / cm³

volume = 95 ml

Asked

the mass

Solution

[tex]\rm mass=\rho\times v\\\\mass=13.6\times 95\\\\mass=\boxed{1292\:grams}[/tex]

21. Which statement describes inhalation? a. Inhalation is breathing in carbon dioxide. b. Inhalation is making the lungs shrink and lose air. c. Inhalation is breathing in air that contains oxygen. d. Inhalation is removing the waste products from the body.


c. Inhalation is breathing in air that contains oxygen.﻿

﻿Inhalation, also known as inspiration, is the act of breathing in air through the nose or mouth. It brings oxygen into the body and removes waste gases such as carbon dioxide. This process occurs during respiration, which is how the body obtains oxygen from the air and removes carbon dioxide. During inhalation, the chest and diaphragm muscles contract and the rib cage expands, causing the lungs to expand and draw in air. The air is then brought into the alveoli, small air sacs in the lungs, where oxygen is absorbed into the bloodstream and waste gases are exhaled.
