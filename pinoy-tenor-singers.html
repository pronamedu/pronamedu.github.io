1. Local singers with tenor voice


Local singers with tenor voice

The tenor is a kind of male voice singing, and is the highest male voice in the modal register. The standard tenor voice lies down to high C (C5) between C3, the C one octave below mid C. For tenors the low extreme is  around A2 (two octaves below middle C). Some tenors will sing up to F one octave above mid C (F5) at the  highest extreme. The word tenor has been established in relation to classical and operatic voices, where the classification is  based not only on the vocal range of the singer but also on the vocal tessitura and timbre. Their voice style  dictates the roles they will play for classical and operatic singers, which is a key form of categorization. Singers in non-classical music are characterized mainly by their genre and gender, and not by their vocal range.  If the terms soprano, mezzo-soprano, contralto, tenor, baritone, and bass are used as descriptors of non-classical voices, they are applied to those of classical singers more broadly than they should be, and usually  apply only to the perceived vocal range of the singer.
