1. Instruments that are blown on the mouth piece to produce sounds belong to Aerophone.

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Answer:}}[/tex] True

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Explanation:}}[/tex]

An aerophone is a musical instrument that produces sound primarily by causing a body of air to vibrate, without the use of strings or membranes.

──────────────────────

2. Instruments that uses electronic circuits are belong to Chordophone.

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Answer:}}[/tex] False

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Explanation:}}[/tex]

Instruments that use electronic circuits belong to Electrophon.

──────────────────────

3. Instruments like guitar and violin are belong to Membranophone.

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Answer:}}[/tex] False

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Explanation:}}[/tex]

A membranophone is an instrument that produces sound through the vibrations of a flat, stretched material. Therefore, instruments like the violin and guitar do not belong to the Membranophone. 

──────────────────────

4. Instruments like gong, xylophone, cymbals and kulintang are belong to Idiophone.

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Answer:}}[/tex] True

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Explanation:}}[/tex]

An idiophone is a class of musical instruments in which a resonant solid material—such as wood, metal, or stone—vibrates to produce the initial sound. Therefore, instruments like gongs, xylophones, cymbals, and kulintang belong to the idiophone. 

──────────────────────

5. Instruments like keyboard, electric guitar and electric drums are belong to Electrophon.

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Answer:}}[/tex] True

[tex]\LARGE\blue{\tt{Explanation:}}[/tex]

Instruments like keyboard, electrical guitar, and electrical drums belong to Electrophon.
