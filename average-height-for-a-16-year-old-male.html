9. the 1st quartile of the ages of 250 fourth year students is 16 years old. which of the following statements is true? A. most of the students are below 16 years old B. 75% of the students are 16 years old and above C. 25% of the students are 16 years old D. 150 students are younger than 16 years


Quartile:

The 1st quartile of the ages of 250 fourth year students is 16 years old. This also means that 25% of the students are 16 years old.

Quartile is the value that divide a list of numbers into quarters. In this case, a whole is divided into 4 equal parts which is 1/4. Thus, a quartile is 1/4 of any given number. Taking the quotient of 1/4 will give its equivalent in decimal which is 1/4 = .25. To find its percentage equivalent, multiply .25 by 100 such that .25 = 25%.

Using the given example, since it is stated that the 1st quartile of the ages of 240 fourth year students is 16 years old, then 1/4 or 25% of the fourth year students are 16 years old.

Other Examples:

What are the quartiles for the following set of numbers?  

        8, 11, 20, 10, 2, 17, 15, 5, 16, 15, 25, 6

Solution:

Arrange the numbers from least to greatest.

       2, 5, 6, 8, 10, 11, 15, 15, 16, 17, 20, 25

   2. Divide the numbers into 4 groups.

       2, 5, 6, I 8, 10, 11, I 15, 15, 16, I 17, 20, 25

   3. Identify the quartiles.

       Q1 = 2, 5, 6      Q2 = 8, 10, 11      Q3 = 15, 15, 16     Q4 = 17, 20, 25
