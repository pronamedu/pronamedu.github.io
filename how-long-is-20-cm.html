15. How much gift wrappers is needed to wrap a box 45 cm long 30 cm wide and 20 cm high


To calculate the amount of gift wrapper needed to wrap a box, you need to calculate the surface area of the box first.

The formula for the surface area of a rectangular box is:

Surface Area = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

where l is the length, w is the width, and h is the height of the box.

So, for the given box with dimensions 45 cm x 30 cm x 20 cm, the surface area would be:

Surface Area = 2(45 x 30) + 2(45 x 20) + 2(30 x 20)

Surface Area = 2700 + 1800 + 1200

Surface Area = 5700 square cm

Assuming a standard gift wrapper size of 60 cm x 60 cm, you would need at least 96 gift wrappers to wrap the entire box.

Number of gift wrappers = Surface Area / Area of one gift wrapper

Number of gift wrappers = 5700 / (60 x 60)

Number of gift wrappers = 5700 / 3600

Number of gift wrappers = 1.58

Since you can't use only a part of a gift wrapper, you would need to round up to the nearest whole number. Therefore, you would need at least 96 gift wrappers to wrap the box.
