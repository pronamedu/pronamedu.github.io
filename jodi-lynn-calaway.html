1. Josh and Jodie are nutricula jellyfish (a form of jellyfish that have no natural limit to their lifespan). Josh is 4 times as old as Jodie is today. Sixty years ago, Josh was 6 times as old as Jodie. How old are they today?


Let's start by assigning variables to their current ages. Let Jodie's current age be represented by J, and Josh's current age be represented by 4J (since Josh is 4 times as old as Jodie).

We also know that 60 years ago, Josh was 6 times as old as Jodie, so we can write an equation based on this information:

4J - 60 = 6(J - 60)

Simplifying this equation, we get:

4J - 60 = 6J - 360

2J = 300

J = 150

So Jodie is currently 150 years old.

And since Josh is 4 times as old as Jodie, Josh's current age is:

4J = 4(150) = 600

Therefore, Josh is currently 600 years old.

5. Jamie and Lynn each worked a different number of days, but each earned the same amount of money. Jamie earned ₱150 per day and Lynn earned ₱100 per day. Lynn worked 5 more days than Jamie. How much did each earn?


Answer:

Jamie and Lynn each earned 1500 pesos.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's assume Jamie worked for x number of days.

Therefore, Lynn worked for x+5 days.

Jamie earned 150 pesos per day, so his total earnings would be:

150x

Lynn earned 100 pesos per day, so her total earnings would be:

100(x+5)

Since they both earned the same amount of money, we can set these two expressions equal to each other and solve for x:

150x = 100(x+5)

150x = 100x + 500

50x = 500

x = 10

So Jamie worked for 10 days, and Lynn worked for 15 days (5 more than Jamie).

Jamie earned 150 pesos per day for 10 days, so his total earnings are:

150 x 10 = 1500 pesos

Lynn earned 100 pesos per day for 15 days, so her total earnings are:

100 x 15 = 1500 pesos

Therefore, Jamie and Lynn each earned 1500 pesos.

18. lynne jogs daily around a circular track of diameter 25 m for a total distance of 942 m in 5.0 minutes. (a) How many times did lynne jog around the track? (b) what is Lynne's average angular speed in radian/s?


(a) Lynne jogged about 12 times around the track

(b) Lynne's average angular speed is about 0.25 rad/s

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Further explanation

Given:

Diameter of circular track = d = 25 m

Total Distance = D = 942 m

Time Taken = t = 5.0 minutes = 300 seconds

Asked:

(a) N = ?

(b) ω = ?

Solution:

Question (a):

[tex]N = \texttt{Total Distance} \div \texttt{Circumference of Track}[/tex]

[tex]N = D \div ( \pi d )[/tex]

[tex]N = 942 \div ( \pi \times 25 )[/tex]

[tex]N = 37.68 \div \pi[/tex]

[tex]N \approx 12[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Question (b):

[tex]\omega = 2\pi \div T[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi \div ( \frac{t}{N} )[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi N \div t[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi(12) \div 300[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 24\pi \div 300[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi \div 25[/tex]

[tex]\omega \approx 0.25 \texttt{ rad/s}[/tex]
