28. example of prefixes and the meaning of the prefix​


Answer:

A short list of prefixes:

Prefix\Examples

de-  decode, decrease

dis-  disagree, disappear

ex-  exhale, explosion

il-  illegal, illogical

im-  impossible, improper

in-  inaction, invisible

mis-  mislead, misplace

non- nonfiction, nonsense

pre- prefix, prehistory

pro- proactive, profess, program

re- react, reappear

un- undo, unequal, unusual

Explanation:

A prefix is a group of letters placed before the root of a word. For example, the word “unhappy” consists of the prefix “un-” [which means “not”] combined with the root (or stem) word “happy”; the word “unhappy” means “not happy.”
