3. Milk solution and calamansi extract What is appearance before mixing


Answer:

When adding lemon juice or vinegar to hot milk, it will curdle almost immediately, but adding it to cold milk will not produce a reaction for quite some time. This is the very same reaction used to create fresh cheeses like ricotta or paneer.

When lemon juice is added, it increases milk''s acidity as lemon contains citric acid. Due to this lowering of pH, calcium causes precipitation of the casein and thus milk gets curdled.

When milk becomes too acidic, like when we add lemon juice or when it goes sour, the negative charge on the casein groupings becomes neutralized. Now instead of pushing each other apart, the casein starts to clump together. Eventually large enough clumps are formed that we can actually see the separation, and then we have curdled milk.

19. What is the after appearance after mixing milk and calamansi extract


#BrainlyFast

When mixing milk solution and calamansi extract, the acidity of the solution increases as calamansi is made of citric acid. When milk solution increases its acidity, the protein molecule of milk (casein) will stop repelling each other. As a result, the protein molecule will be attracted or stick to each other and coagulate making the appearance of curdled milk (coagulation of milk into clumps).

Casein molecule of milk will be evenly distributed when the ph is high. But when milk becomes acidic or when we add a solution that contribute to the acidity of the milk, the casein molecule will clump and stick to each other making curdled milk.
