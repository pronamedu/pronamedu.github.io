11. mass(g) of calcium fluoride​


Answer:

The mass of calcium fluoride depends on the amount or quantity of the substance. Calcium fluoride has a molar mass of 78.075 g/mol, which means that one mole of calcium fluoride has a mass of 78.075 grams. Therefore, to determine the mass of calcium fluoride, you need to know the amount (in moles) of the substance.

For example, if you have 0.5 moles of calcium fluoride, you can calculate the mass using the following formula:

mass = number of moles x molar mass

mass = 0.5 moles x 78.075 g/mol

mass = 39.04 grams

Therefore, the mass of 0.5 moles of calcium fluoride is 39.04 grams.


12. what kind of chemical bond for calcium fluoride


The kind of chemical bond in Calcium fluoride is an ionic bond.

Ionic bond happens when a metal and non-metal reacted with one another forming a new compound.

As explained deeply, ionic bonding is the absolute exchange of valence electron(s) between atoms. It is a type of chemical bond that produces two ions charged in opposite directions. The metal loses electrons in ionic bonds to become a positively charged cation, while the nonmetal absorbs those electrons to become a negatively charged anion.

Analyzing the given compound, we can see the elements involved are calcium and fluorine. Calcium is a metal and fluorine is a non-metal. The only possible chemical bond for a metal and non-metal is an ionic bond, so the kind of chemical bond for calcium chloride is ionic bond.

12÷180×100 = 6.66

96÷180×100 = 53.33

40+6.66+53.33= 99.99 g/mole

F2= Fluorine 19 (Atomic Weight) 19×2=38

N2= Nitrogen 14 (Atomic Weight) 14×2=28

40÷106×100=37.73

28÷106×100=26.42
