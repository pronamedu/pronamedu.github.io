6. Turn this Into Improper Fraction1) 9 17/232) 10 15/33​


Steps in Turning Mix Fractions to Improper Fractions:First, you multiply the whole number by its denominator (If you have the mix fraction, [tex]a\frac{b}{c}\\[/tex], [tex]a[/tex] is your whole number, [tex]c[/tex] is your denominator and [tex]b[/tex] is your numerator). For fractions 1, we have [tex]9 \times 23[/tex] and it's 207.Then you add the product you got from the first step to its numerator. For fraction 1, we have [tex]207 +17[/tex] then we have [tex]224[/tex].The sum you got from step 2 is your new numerator while the denominator is just the same. From fraction 1, its improper fraction will be written as [tex]\frac{224}{23}\\[/tex].If possible, you must write it in its lowest term. For [tex]\frac{224}{23}\\[/tex], it's already its Lowest Term

If you have the mix fraction, [tex]a\frac{b}{c}\\[/tex], you can just evaluate [tex]\frac{(a \times c) +b}{c}\\[/tex] to find its improper form. Then reduce to the lowest term if possible.

The same process for Fraction 2.

[tex]10 \times 33 [/tex] is [tex]330[/tex].[tex]330 +15[/tex] is [tex]345[/tex].Fraction 2 written in its improper form is [tex]\frac{345}{33}\\[/tex].Reducing [tex]\frac{345}{33}\\[/tex] to its lowest is [tex]\frac{115}{11}\\[/tex].

[tex]\frac{(10 \times 33) +15}{33} \\ \frac{330 +15}{33} \\ \frac{345}{33} \\ \frac{115}{11}[/tex]
