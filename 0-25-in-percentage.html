13. Using the given genotypes below, illustrate a cross between a violet and a white eggplant. What is the genotypic percentage of the offspring will be having a heterozygous fruit?A. 0% EeB. 25% EeC. 50% EeD. 50% ee​


Answer:

C. 50% Ee

Explanation:

The gene for the color of the eggplant in the question involves the alleles for purple eggplant (E) and white eggplant (e). A homozygous dominant (EE) produces purple eggplant, homozygous recessive (ee) produces white eggplant, and the heterozygous genotype (Ee) produces violet eggplant. The mode of inheritance here is incomplete dominance, wherein there is an intermediate phenotype by the heterozygous genotype. In this inheritance, none of the alleles is completely dominant. Thus, the heterozygous genotype will produce an intermediate phenotype which is a blend of the two alleles.

If there is a cross between a violet (Ee) eggplant and a white (ee) eggplant, the resulting genotypic percentage of the offspring will be 50% Ee and 50% ee. The phenotypes will be 50% violet eggplant and 50% white eggplant.

28. A farmer has brown cows with white spots (genotype BBss) and brown cows with no spots (genotype BBSS). If the farmer crossed a brown spotted bull with a brown spotted cow, what percentage of the cows would be black? 25% 0% 50% 100%


If the farmer crossed a brown spotted bull with a brown spotted cow, the percentage of the cows would be black is 0%.  

To explain this, let us clarify some concepts first.

Having a genotype of BB means that brown is a dominant allele giving black a genotype of bb which is a recessive allele.  

In this question, we are only be concerned with a single allele, the cow color and pattern is already two alleles. Thus, we will not be concerned about the cow coat pattern whether it would be spotted or not since the question is only concerned with cow color.  

Looking at the genotype of the brown cow, we can say that brown cow, having the genotype of BB is dominant over a black cow that should be having the genotype of bb.

Thus if you were going to cross breed a brown spotted bull with a brown spotted cow the probability of having a black is zero percent.  

In this problem, we assume that the cow and the bull has a homozygous dominant allele for their coat color which is brown. In this case, the probability of having black is zero. If we cross them, we have

BB x BB = 4 BB or 4 brown

However, if they were both heterozygous dominant Bb, the chance is 25%. If we cross them, we have

Bb x Bb = 1 BB, 2 Bb, 1 bb (3 brown, 1 black)
