2. What is library? What are the sections in the library?


[tex] \huge\boxed{ \colorbox{lightblue}{Answer}}[/tex]

What is a library?

— a library is a collection of sources, resources, and services, and the structure in which it is housed it is organized for use and maintained by a public body,an institution, or a private individual.

What are the sections in the library?

— There are various sections in the library although big libraries have more sections. what I'm going to give you are just basic sections commonly found even in small libraries:

1. General Reference - where you can find general encyclopedia works like dictionaries, almanacs, atlases, directories, handbooks, manual, indexes and abstracts, etc.

2. Periodicals section - where you can find local and foreign journals, magazines, newspapers, government publications, and other forms of serial publication, vertical files such as pamphlets, brochures and newspaper clippings.

3. General Collection/Circulation Section - Where you can find the main collection of the library like books on philosophy, Religion, social sciences, language, arts, pure science, applied sciences, literature, history, and geography.

4. Filipiniana Section - Where you can find books published within bthe Philippines and written about the Philippines, it's history, people, government, and culture.

21. multimedia definition 60 words​


Explanation:

As the name implies, multimedia is the integration of multiple forms of media. This includes text, graphics, audio, video, etc. For example, a presentation involving audio and video clips would be considered a "multimedia presentation." Educational software that involves animations, sound, and text is called "multimedia software." CDs and DVDs are often considered to be "multimedia formats" since they can store a lot of data and most forms of multimedia require a lot of disk space.

Due to the advancements in computer speeds and storage space, multimedia is commonplace today. Therefore, the term doesn't produce the same excitement is once did. This also means it is not as overused as it was back in the late '90s. Thank goodness.
