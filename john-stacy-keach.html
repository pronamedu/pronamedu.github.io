12. In consecutive turns of a monopoly game, stacy first paid 800 for a hotel . Since then , she lost half of her money when she landed on boardwalk. Next she collected 200 for passing go. She the lost half of her remaining money when she landed on illinois avenue. Stacy now has 2500. How much did she have just before she purchased the hotel?


Let's represent her original money as x.

stacy paid 800: x - 800

lost half her money: (x - 800)/2

collected 200: [tex]\frac{x-800}{2} + 200[/tex]

lost half her money (again): [tex]\frac{\frac{x-800}{2} + 200}{2}[/tex]

After her turns, she has 2,500.

[tex]\frac{\frac{x-800}{2} + 200}{2}[/tex] = 2500

[tex]\frac{x-800}{2} + 200[/tex] = 5000

(x - 800)/2 = 4800

x - 800 = 9600

x = 10400

Stacy originally had 10,400.

MATH PUZZLE

Problem:

Jackie is older than Martha. Martha and Stacy are both older than Maya. Darlyn is not the youngest. Who is the youngest of the five?

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

Let us use the process of elimination in order to determine whom of the following is the youngest.

So we have five persons: Jackie, Martha, Stacy, Maya and Darlyn.The first clue tells us that it is not Jackie since she is older than Martha.The second clue tells us that Martha and Stacy are also eliminated from our list of possible answers since it was stated that they are both older than Maya. So are choices are narrowed down into 2 possible choices which are Maya and Daryln.The last clue tells us that Darlyn is not the youngest so it is Maya.

Therefore, Maya is the youngest of the five ladies.
