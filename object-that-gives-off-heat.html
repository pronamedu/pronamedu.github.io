2. which object gives off heats​


Anything that gives off heat is a heat source.

Here is a list of things that give off significant amounts of heat: Stove/oven/microwave/convection-oven, clothes dryer, refrigerator, computer(s), TV, air conditioner, dehumidifier, hot water from (shower, dishwasher, washing machine), high wattage lights, stereo receiver/amplifier.

Answer:

Anything that gives off heat is a heat source. The sun is the main source of heat for Earth.

There are three basic ways to transfer heat energy: convection, conduction, and radiation. Convection transfers heat energy through gases or liquids. Conduction transfers heat energy from one solid to another. Radiation transfers heat in the form of waves or particles through places where there are no molecules.In the end, if you're wondering where the heat in your home comes from, it's almost certainly one of the three main sources: combustion, electric resistance, or the outdoor air, ground, or water.

28. situation the object that gives off the heat the object that receives the heat. method of heat transfer taking place​


Answer:

Here are a few examples of situations involving objects that give off heat, objects that receive heat, and the method of heat transfer taking place:

A cup of hot coffee is placed on a table.

The cup gives off heat, and the table receives the heat. The method of heat transfer taking place is conduction, as the heat moves from the cup to the table through direct contact.

A pot of boiling water is placed on a stove.

The pot gives off heat, and the air around the pot receives the heat. The method of heat transfer taking place is convection, as the hot water heats up the air molecules, causing them to rise and be replaced by cooler air.

A person holds a warm towel after it has been heated in a dryer.

The towel gives off heat, and the person's skin receives the heat. The method of heat transfer taking place is conduction, as the heat moves from the towel to the person's skin through direct contact.

A campfire is burning in a fire pit.

The fire gives off heat, and the surrounding air and ground receive the heat. The method of heat transfer taking place is radiation, as the heat travels through space from the fire to the surrounding environment without any direct contact.
