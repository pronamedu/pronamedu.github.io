10. what is cost plus pricing?


what is cost plus pricing?

Answer:

Cost plus pricing enable to compute all the amounts plus the percentage to get the final product price or the selling price. It is done by simply adding a markup to the cost of goods and services. To be more specific, in this method, you will add the three types of costs the direct material, direct labor, and the overhead costs for a product. After which, you add the mark up percentage in order to derive the final price or selling price of the product. In addition, Cost plus pricing can also be applicable to a customer contract, wherein the customer refund the seller for all the cost incurred and give money as payment for the agreed profit as additional to the cost incurred during the transaction.

16. A company, using cost-plus pricing, has an ROI of 24%, total sales of 20,000 units, and a desired ROI per unit of $30. What was the amount of investment?a. $144,000b. $2,500,000c. $456,000d. $789,475


The amount of investment is B. $2,500,000

Explanation

ROI (Return On Investment)

ROI or Return On Investment is a ratio used to calculate the effectiveness of a given investment. ROI is a calculation of net profit obtained from the nominal investment money that has been spent. ROI calculations are commonly used by investors to be able to determine the next business strategy with the results of the ROI that has been done.

We know a company, using cost-plus pricing, has an ROI of 24% and total sales of 20,000 units, and a desired ROI per unit of $30. We will find the amount of investment.

Given:

Total sales = 20,000 units

ROI per unit = $30

Net Income = 20,000 x $30 = $600,000

ROI = 24%

Ask: Amount of Investment

Answer:

ROI = Net Income / Cost of Investment x 100%

Cost of Investment = $600,000 / 24% x 100%

Cost of Investment = $2,500,000

So, we get the amount of investment is $2,500,000 (B)
