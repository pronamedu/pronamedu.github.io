1. simple future tense of fly​


Answer:

will fly OR shall fly

Explanation:

The simple future tense is composed of two parts: will / shall + the infinitive without to. Subject. +will. +infinitive without "to" Affirmative. So, the simple future tense of fly is will/shall fly.

24. write your own sentences in the past, present and future tense.1.read2.buy3.fly4.wash​


Answer:

1. Read

Past: Read

Sentence: Ana read a pocketbook yesterday.

Present: Read

Sentence: Ana reads the pocketbook she bought yesterday.

Future: Will/shall read

Sentence: Ana will read the pocketbook on Saturday.

2. Buy

Past: Bought

Sentence: We bought ice cream in 7-eleven lately.

Present: Buy

Sentence: Ricky buy a set of art materials.

Future: Will Buy

Sentence: We will buy set of materials tomorrow at National Book Store.

3. Fly

Past: Flew

Sentence: The birds flew far away.

Present: Fly

Sentence: Birds fly freely up in the sky.

Future: Will fly

Sentence: We will fly to America the next month.

4. Wash

Past: Washed

Sentence: I washed my clothes yesterday as it was holiday.

Present: Wash/washes

Sentence: Anna washes the dishes after they have eaten their lunch.

Future: Will wash

Sentence: Since tomorrow is Saturday, we will wash our clothes at the river.

Explanation:

There are three main verb tenses in English: present, past and future.
