3. A 200 kg boulder is 1000m above the ground. what is the potential energy when it is 1000m above the ground


Potential Energy

[tex]_______________________________________[/tex]

A 200 kg boulder is 1000m above the ground. what is the potential energy when it is 1000m above the ground

===============================

Given data:

Mass (m) - 200 kgHeight (h) - 1 000 m

Formula to be used:

PE = mgh

where;

m - mass

g - gravity

h - height

Solution:

PE = mgh

= (200 kg)(1 000 m)(9.8 m/s²)

= (200 000 kg.m)(9.8 m/s²)

PE = 1 960 000 J

___________________________

Therefore, the potential energy of the boulder is 1 960 000 J

22. What is appropriate symbol of 1km to 1000m


Answer:

The appropriate symbol to represent 1 kilometer to 1000 meters is "1 km = 1000 m".

Step-by-step explanation:

The appropriate symbol to represent 1 kilometer to 1000 meters is "1 km = 1000 m". This is the conversion factor that relates kilometers to meters.

To convert kilometers to meters, we multiply the number of kilometers by 1000. For example, if we have 2 kilometers, we can convert to meters as follows:

2 km = 2 x 1000 m = 2000 m

Therefore, 2 kilometers is equivalent to 2000 meters. Similarly, to convert meters to kilometers, we divide the number of meters by 1000. For example, if we have 5000 meters, we can convert to kilometers as follows:

5000 m = 5000 / 1000 km = 5 km

Therefore, 5000 meters is equivalent to 5 kilometers.
