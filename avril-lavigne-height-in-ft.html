25. The rectangular solid has a length of 3 ft, a height of 8 ft, and a width of 5ft. While, cylinder a diameter of 2 ft the has has and a height of 10 ft. What is the volume of the cylinder and the rectangular solid?​


The volume of a rectangular solid is given by the formula:

V = l × w × h

where l is the length, w is the width, and h is the height.

Substituting the given values, we have:

V = 3 ft × 5 ft × 8 ft = 120 ft³

Therefore, the volume of the rectangular solid is 120 cubic feet.

The volume of a cylinder is given by the formula:

V = πr²h

where r is the radius and h is the height.

We are given the diameter of the cylinder, which is 2 ft. The radius is half the diameter, so:

r = 2 ft ÷ 2 = 1 ft

Substituting the given values, we have:

V = π × (1 ft)² × 10 ft = 31.42 ft³ (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the volume of the cylinder is approximately 31.42 cubic feet.
