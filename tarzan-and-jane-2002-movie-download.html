21. should people be punished for illegally downloading movies and music​


Answer:

The short answer is, ‘no’.

The longer answer is more complicated and ensconced in the word — ‘intent’.

If your intent is to download material you know to be meant for a singular audience and to then redistribute it, then you are morally in the wrong. Furthermore, if your intent is to download copyrighted material, replicate it and then redistribute it for PROFIT, you are crossing explicit legal lines. The implicit moral concerns are stamped on the deed on itself — ‘you are selling stolen goods’.

Conversely, if your intent is to download copyrighted digital material that is otherwise available to the public for you personal consumption then the lines take on a more grayish tone.

It is, by all definitions ILLEGAL to engage in this practice. However, very few entities will actually pursue single use downloaders. It’s not cost effective. There have been cases in the past where with content holders have attempted to make ‘examples’ out of people who were caught with downloaded music on their hard drives. However, most if not all of these cases were either dropped or settled out of court with not more than a warning of ‘not-to-do-again’.

I’ve been downloading music, movies etc since the late 80s. I do however, have developed a personal ethical code — if there is such a thing in this case. For example, I never download music from a band who is signed to an ‘independent’ label. I will instead choose to stream them. I won’t save their music to my hard drives until I pay for it. However, the artist(s) are signed to major labels then I will save it.

Most importantly are FILMS. I have ONE rule and ONE rule only.. I will never stream or download any visual media that has not been released for retail. This has been a rule of mine since the earliest days of Bittorrent. I have never broken this self imposed rule, even though I have been tempted.

As you can see, it really comes down to one’s personal ethics and understanding of how the economy of the entertainment industry works.

26. summary of the movie entitled "Spirit Stallion of the Cimarron (2002)​


Answer:

"Spirit: Stallion of the Cimarron" is an animated movie released in 2002, produced by DreamWorks Animation and directed by Kelly Asbury and Lorna Cook. The film tells the story of a wild mustang named Spirit, who lives in the American West during the late 19th century.

Spirit is a strong and independent horse, who is fiercely protective of his herd and territory. He is captured by a group of American soldiers, who are trying to tame and break the wild horses to use them for transportation and farming. Spirit refuses to be broken and repeatedly tries to escape, but is eventually tamed by a Native American boy named Little Creek.

Together, Spirit and Little Creek embark on a journey to find a way for Spirit to return to his herd and freedom. Along the way, they face many challenges, including dangerous predators, harsh weather conditions, and encounters with the American soldiers. They also form a deep bond and friendship that transcends their differences.

The film is unique in that it features very little dialogue, instead relying on the narration of Spirit's thoughts and emotions. The soundtrack includes songs by Bryan Adams, who also co-wrote and performed the movie's theme song, "Here I Am."

Overall, "Spirit: Stallion of the Cimarron" is a beautifully animated film with a powerful message about freedom, loyalty, and the importance of preserving the natural world.
