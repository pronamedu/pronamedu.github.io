14. A 200mm x 200mm x 400mm block weights 50 n when fully submerged in water what is the unit weight of the block​


[tex]\huge\mathbb\pink{\underline{{\colorbox{in}{ANSWER}}}}[/tex]

The weight of the block in water is less than the weight of the block in air because of the buoyant force of water in the upward direction and the weight is in the downward direction. The apparent loss of weight in water is the weight of the water displaced by the block when totally immersed in water. The apparent loss of weight is equal to the product of the volume of displaced water, its density, and the acceleration due to gravity g.

In formula form the apparent loss of weight = B.F = Vdg where g = 9.8 m/s^2.

Solving for the value of the volume V

V = lwh = 2 dm * 2 dm * 4 dm = 16 dm^3 = 16 liters of water.

Since the density of water = 1.0 kg / L, the mass of the displaced water = 16 kg

Solving for the buoyant force of water

B.F = 16 dm^3 * 1.0 kg / dm^3 * 9.8 m/s^2 = 156.8 N

The weight in air is equal to the product of the mass and the acceleration due to gravity

Weight in air = 16 kg * 9.8 m/s^2 = 156.8 N

Weight in air = B.F + weight in water

Weight in air = 156.8 N + 50 N = 206.8 N
