Solution (Problem 1)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of MgO.

molar mass = (24.31 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 40.31 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of MgO.

For (a)

[tex]\text{moles of MgO = 160 g MgO} × \frac{\text{1 mol MgO}}{\text{40.31 g MgO}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of MgO = 3.97 mol}}[/tex]

For (b)

[tex]\text{moles of MgO = 400 g MgO} × \frac{\text{1 mol MgO}}{\text{40.31 g MgO}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of MgO = 9.92 mol}}[/tex]

Solution (Problem 2)

[tex]\text{mass of P = 7 mol P} × \frac{\text{30.97 g P}}{\text{1 mol P}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of P = 217 g}}[/tex]

Solution (Problem 3)

[tex]\text{number of BaCl₂ formula units = 0.25 mol BaCl₂} × \frac{\text{6.022 × 10²³ BaCl₂ formula units}}{\text{1 mol BaCl₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{number of BaCl₂ formula units = 1.51 × 10²³ formula units}}[/tex]
