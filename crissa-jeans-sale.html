3. which is a cheaper- pair of jeans worth 1 200 pesos that is on sale at 10% or pair of jeans worth 1,800 that is on sale at 20%? why?​


Assuming that both pairs of jeans are on sale at the same store and the sale prices are the final prices, the pair of jeans that is cheaper is the one that is originally priced at 1,800 pesos and is on sale at 20%. This is because the final price of this pair of jeans would be 1,800 x (1 - 20/100) = 1,440 pesos, which is cheaper than the pair of jeans originally priced at 1,200 pesos and on sale at 10%, which would have a final price of 1,200 x (1 - 10/100) = 1,080 pesos.

10. Juan has no more than $50 to spend at the mall. He wants to buy a pair of jeans and some juice. If the sales tax on the jeans is 4% and the juice with tax costs $2, what is the maximum price of jeans Juan can afford?​


Given :-Juan has no more than $50 to spend at the mall .He wants to buy a pair of jeans and some juice .If the sales tax on the jeans is 4% and the juice with tax costs $2 .

To Find :-What is the maximum price of jeans Juan can afford ?

Solution :-

Given information :-

Maximum spend = $50Cost of juice = $2 (inc tax)Sales tax on jeans = 4%

Define the variable :-

Let x be the price of the jeans (without tax) .

Convert 4% into a decimal :-

[tex]\sf\implies 4\%=\dfrac{4}{100}=0.04[/tex]

Therefore, the sum of the cost of the juice, the price of the jeans and the sales tax on the jeans must be equal to or less than $50 :-

[tex]\sf\implies 2+x+0.04x\leq 50[/tex]

[tex]\sf\implies 2+1.04x\leq 50[/tex]

Solving the inequality :-

[tex]\sf\implies 2+1.04x\leq 50[/tex]

[tex]\sf\implies 2+1.04x-2\leq 50-2[/tex]

[tex]\sf\implies 1.04x\leq 48[/tex]

[tex]\sf\implies \dfrac{1.04x}{1.04}\leq \dfrac{48}{1.04} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered}\sf\implies x\leq 46.15384615\\\end{gathered} [/tex]

Therefore, the maximum price of the jeans Juan can afford is $46.15 (not including sales tax) .

26. after a 15% discount , a pair of jeans can be bought at only 442 pesos how much were the jeans before the sale?​


ORIGINAL PRICE

PROBLEM

After a 15% discount, a pair of jeans can be bought for only 442 pesos how much were the jeans before the sale?

SOLUTION

To decipher this problem first, analyze the problem and observe that the one that is missing or what's being asked on the problem was the original price of the jeans before the discount if they weren't given a 15% discount that makes the cost be ₱442.

That is exactly the meaning of the original price. Let's discuss the others.

Original Price: The actual amount of the product before any reductions or accumulations have been done.

Discount: The amount of expenditure of an item from the original price.

Discount rate: The (%) percent of the discount for the item.

Going back to the problem, to solve that, let 100% be the original price of the product and we'll have to minus the original price from the discount rate.

[tex] \large{100 \% \: - 15 \% \: = \: 85 \%}[/tex]

100% represents the original price. If there is a 15% discount, then you are paying 100% - 15% = 85% of the original price

To make it easier:

442 is 85% of what number?

Let n be the missing price or number for the problem.

[tex] \large {\frac{442}{0.85} = \frac{0.85}{0.85} } \: = \sf \: n \\ \\ \large {\frac{ \red{442}}{ { \orange{0.85}}} = \frac{\cancel{0.85}}{ \cancel{0.85}} } \: = \sf \: n[/tex]

Now divide 442 by 0.85

But since the given isn't applicable for division because of the decimal, let's change both of the numbers for a more applicable manual long division.

[tex] \large\: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: 52 0\\ 85 \: | \: \overline{ \: 44200} \\ - \: 425 \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \overline{ \: \: \: \: 170\: \: \: } \\ - \: \: \: \: \: 170 \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: 0\: \: \: \: } \\ \: \: \: - \: \: \: \: {0} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \overline { \: \: \: \: \: \: 0 \: \: \: \: \: } \\ \\ \\ \sf {n = 520}[/tex]

Checking:

[tex] \sf{520 \times 85 } = \: 44200[/tex]

ANSWER:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \sf \: n = 520}[/tex]

The original price or the price of the jeans before the discount was[tex]\boxed{ 520}[/tex]
