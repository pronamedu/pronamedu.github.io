POWER==============================Statement:

» In a classroom there are four 25-watt fluorescent lamps and four 60-watt electric fans operating for 8 hours in a regular school day.

[tex] \: [/tex]

Question #1

• How many kilowatt-hours are consumed by the four 25-watt flourescent lamps each school day?

Solve for Q #1

• Identify how many working florescent lamps are there in a classroom that can be multiplied to its power. In this case, there are four.

25 watts × 4 = 100 watts

• Using the formula, identify the total consumption of four 25-watt florescent lamps 8 hours daily in a form of kWh.

[tex] \sf kWh = \frac{watt \: \cdot \: hours}{1000} [/tex]

[tex] \sf kWh = \frac{100 \: \cdot \: 8}{1000} [/tex]

[tex] \sf kWh = \frac{800}{1000} [/tex]

[tex] \sf kWh = 0.8 [/tex]

[tex] \boxed{\sf Final Answer: \purple{0.8 \: kWh \: each \: school \: day}} [/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

Question #2

• How many kilowatt-hours are consumed by the four 60-watt electric fans each school day?

Solve for Q #2

• Identify how many working electric fans are there in a classroom that can be multiplied to its power. In this case, there are also four.

60 watts × 4 = 240 watts

• Using the formula, identify the total consumption of four 60-watt electric fans 8 hours daily in a form of kWh.

[tex] \sf kWh = \frac{watt \: \cdot \: hours}{1000} [/tex]

[tex] \sf kWh = \frac{240 \: \cdot \: 8}{1000} [/tex]

[tex] \sf kWh = \frac{1920}{1000} [/tex]

[tex] \sf kWh = 1.92 [/tex]

[tex] \boxed{\sf Final \: Answer: \purple{1.92 \: kWh \: each \: school \: day}} [/tex]
