13. NO to Water Shortage! 'The gross water consumption of the rapidly growing human population and climate change are perhaps the main causes of water shortage in many dry regions in the world. However, there are some ways you can do to help prevent or solve the problem. J 2First, you can save just a little bit of water each time you turn on the tap and add up to water bill savings over time. While waiting for the shower water to warm up, catch the cold water in a jug or pail. You can use it later to water the plants, fill the kettle, or mop the floor. Turning off the tap while soaping or shampooing will save you about nine liters. Your swift action in fixing a leaky faucet can save you up to 2000 liters per month. 3If you are buying a new washing machine, consider the front-loading machine because it normally uses less water, energy, and detergent. Always wait until you have a full load before starting on the wash, choosing the shortest cycle for regular washing. You can avoid using running water to defrost food or wash fruits or vegetables-use a basin instead. 4If you and I work on these and more countless ways of saving water, water shortage will not be a problem in our homes. Clean water will stay in our tap.​


Answer:

There are several ways to help prevent or solve the problem of water shortage, including:

Saving water by turning off the tap while soaping or shampooing, catching cold shower water in a jug to use later, and fixing leaky faucets.

Choosing water-efficient appliances, such as front-loading washing machines, and only running full loads of laundry with the shortest cycle.

Avoiding using running water to defrost food or wash fruits and vegetables and instead using a basin.

By working together to implement these and other water-saving measures, we can help prevent water shortage and ensure a clean water supply in our homes.
