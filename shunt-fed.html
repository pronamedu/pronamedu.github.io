12. A 230 v shunt generator has an armature circuit resistance of 0.05 ohm and shunt field resistance of 57.5 ohms. when the generator operates at rated voltage it has a total core and mechanical losses of 1880 w. at what load does the generator operate so as to achieve maximum efficiency


The load at which a shunt generator with 230V, armature circuit resistance of 0.05 ohms, shunt field resistance of 57.5 ohms, and total core and mechanical losses of 1880 Watts will also achieve the maximum efficiency at the same time is 53.51 kW.

To solve this problem, we first have to find the field current.

[tex]I_{field}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{230V}{57.5 ohms}[/tex] = 4A

At maximum efficiency, the variable losses are equal to the constant losses.

[tex](I_{armature})^2R_{armature} = (I_{field})^2 R_{field}[/tex] + 1880 Watts

[tex](I_{armature})^2R_{armature} =[/tex] ( [tex]4^{2}[/tex] x 57.5 ) + 1880 Watts

[tex]I_{armature}[/tex] = 236.64 A

Then, we have to get the value of the load current.

[tex]I_{load}= I_{armature} - I_{field}[/tex] = 236.64-4 = 232.64 A

Now, we can get the load in kW by multiplying the load current by the given rated voltage.

P= 232.64 A x 230 V = 53.5072 x [tex]10^{3}[/tex] Watts

16. The shunt field winding of a shunt generator has a resistance of 80 ohms at 20°C. After several hours of continuous operation, the winding temperature rises to 50°C. How much is the winding resistance under this condition? Assume the resistance temperature coefficient of copper to be 0.004 ohm per degree at 0°C.​


Answer: 80.12 ohms

Explanation: Shunt Generator Resistance Calculation

Carlo Abad

The shunt field winding of a shunt generator has a resistance of 80 ohms at 20°C. After several hours of continuous operation, the winding temperature rises to 50°C. How much is the winding resistance under this condition? Assume the resistance temperature coefficient of copper to be 0.004 ohm per degree at 0°C.​

The resistance temperature coefficient of copper is used to determine how the resistance of a material changes as its temperature changes. At 0°C, the resistance temperature coefficient of copper is 0.004 ohm per degree.

To calculate the winding resistance of the shunt generator at 50°C, we can use the formula:

R50 = R20 + (RTCopper * (T50 - T20))

where:

R50 is the resistance of the winding at 50°C

R20 is the resistance of the winding at 20°C

RTCopper is the resistance temperature coefficient of copper (0.004 ohm per degree)

T50 is the temperature of the winding at 50°C

T20 is the temperature of the winding at 20°C

Given that R20 = 80 ohms, T20 = 20°C and T50 = 50°C, we can substitute these values into the formula:

R50 = 80 + (0.004 * (50 - 20))

R50 = 80 + (0.004 * 30)

R50 = 80 + 0.12

R50 = 80.12 ohms

So the winding resistance of the shunt generator at 50°C is 80.12 ohms.

Find the speed at which it must be driven as a shunt generator to supply a power output of 25 kW at a terminal voltage of 500 volts. ​


Please see the figure for the configuration.

Let's solve first and collect the following parameters on the shunt motor.

Field resistance:

[tex]I_{f}=\dfrac{500\text{ V}}{200\:\Omega}=2.5\text{ A}[/tex]

Current drawn by supply:

[tex]I_{L}=\dfrac{25000\text{ W}}{\text{500 V}}=50\text{ A}[/tex]

Armature current:

[tex]I_{a}=50{\text{ A}}-2.5{\text{ A}}=47.5{\text{ A}}[/tex]

Back emf:

[tex]E_{b}=500\text{ V}-(47.5\text{ A})(0.5\: \Omega)=476.25\text{ V}[/tex]

Motor speed:

[tex]N_{1}=400\text{ rpm}[/tex]

Field resistance:

[tex]I_{f}=\dfrac{500\text{ V}}{200\:\Omega}=2.5\text{ A}[/tex]

[tex]I_{L}=\dfrac{25000\text{ W}}{\text{500 V}}=50\text{ A}[/tex]

[tex]I_{a}=50{\text{ A}}+2.5{\text{ A}}=52.5{\text{ A}}[/tex]

[tex]N_{2}=\:?[/tex]
