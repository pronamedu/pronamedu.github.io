7. Passengers on airplane are allowed a maximum of 66 lbs of luggage. Overweight luggage are charged P800 per kg excess. How much would a nurse going to Canada have to pay if her luggage weighs 215.65 kg? ​


Answer:

When travelling by aircraft, passengers have a maximum allowable weight for their luggage. They are then charged £10 for every kilogram overweight. If a passenger carrying 40 kg of luggage is charged £50, how much would a passenger carrying 80 kg be charged?

Solution

A £50 charge represents 5 kg overweight (5 times £10 = £50).
So 40 minus 5 = 35 kg must be the maximum allowable weight for baggage.

Hence 80 kg is 80 minus 35 = 45 kg overweight and the passenger would be charged 45 × £10 = £450.

20. A luggage set with a regular price of P1 255.00 is on sale for P735.00. Find the discount rate


Answer:

The discount rate for the luggage set is 42%. To calculate this, divide the amount of the sale price by the regular price and then subtract it from 1. This gives you the discount rate expressed as a decimal, which you can then convert to a percentage. In this case, (735/1 255) - 1 = 0.42 or 42%.
