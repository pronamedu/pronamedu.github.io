14. A car is travelling at a constant velocity of 100.0km/h on the sky way. What is the power output of the car engine if the total air resistance and friction between road and tires is 750N? Calculate your answers in watts, horsepower and kilowatts?​


Answer:

20,835W or 27.94 hp or 20.835 kW

Explanation:

To calculate the power output of the car engine, we need to know the force acting on the car and the velocity of the car.

Work done = force x distance = force x velocity

The power output in watts is:

P(W) = F(N) x v(m/s)

Since we have a velocity in km/h, we need to convert it to m/s:

v = 100.0 x 1000 / 3600 = 27.78 m/s

Plugging in the values we have:

P(W) = 750 N x 27.78 m/s = 20,835W

1 horsepower = 746 W

1 kW = 1000 W

So, the power output in horsepower is:

P(hp) = 20835 W / 746 W/hp = 27.94 hp

And the power output in kilowatts is:

P(kW) = 20835 W / 1000 W/kW = 20.835 kW

So the power output of the car engine is 20,835W or 27.94 hp or 20.835 kW.

22. the hammer of a pile driver has a mass of 500kg and must be lifted a vertical distance of 2meter in 3 seconds. what horsepower engine is required?


Answer:

The power required is 4.38 hp

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the power of the engine in terms of horsepower. To do this, we will use the formula:

Power = Work / time

Work = Force x distance

Force = mass x acceleration due to gravity (weight force)

Solving the problem

1. First, let us solve for the force

Force = mass x acceleration due to gravity

Force = (500 kg)(9.8 m/s²)

Force = 4900 N

2. Next, let us solve for work

Work = Force x distance

Work = (4900 N)(2 m)

Work = 9800 Nm or 9800 J

3. Power = Work / time

Power = 9800 J/ 3s

Power = 3266.67 Watts

Converting Watts to horsepower

Power = 4.38 hp
