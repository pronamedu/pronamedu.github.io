10. faucet A can fill a tank 1.25 times as fast as faucet b. When both faucets are opened they fill the tank in 4 hours. How long it will take for faucet b to fill the tank by itself​


Answer:

The faucet B can fill the tank by itself in 9 hours.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Assume the amount of water delivered by each faucet per hour

Assume that faucet B delivers 100 liters of water per hour. Since it says that faucet A delivers 1.25 times faster than faucet B, we can safely assume that faucet A can deliver 125 liters of water per hour.

Step 2: Calculate the total amount of water that the 2 faucets can deliver in the given number of hours.

Faucet A can deliver 125 L/hr and faucet B can deliver 100 L/hr, since they are both open they can deliver a total of 225 L/hr. We can then multiply 225 L/hr to the total number of hours that they are open which is 4 hrs. So,

(225L/hr) x (4 hrs) = 900 Liters

Step 3: Calculate the time it can take faucet B to fill the tank

Since the total amount of water is 900 liters and the rate of flow of faucet B is 100L/hr. We can solve it by dividing the total amount of water by the rate of flow of faucet B;

(900L) / (100L/Hr) = 9 Hrs.

Answer: The faucet B can fill the tank by itself in 9 hours.

(additional: Faucet A can fill the tank in 7.2 hrs)
