Percent by Mass

Given:

   mass of solute (cocoa powder) = 1.2 g

   total mass of solution = 8 g

Required: percent by mass

Solution:

    % mass = [tex]\frac{mass of solute}{total mass of solution}[/tex] x 100%

    % mass = [tex]\frac{1.2 g}{8 g}[/tex] x 100%

    % mass = 0.15 x 100%

    % mass = 15%

Explanation:

A solution is made up of solute that is dissolved in a solvent. The percent by mass, percent by volume, percent by mass/volume are different ways to describe the concentration of a solution.  A percent by mass is the ratio of the mass of solute in a given total mass of solution. Below is the formula in getting percent by mass:

        % mass = [tex]\frac{mass of solute}{total mass of solution}[/tex] x 100%

It is used when the mass of solute and mass of the solution is given. We just need to substitute the values to the above formula. The higher the percent by mass, the more concentrated the solution is. Thus, in the given problem, brand X (65%) is more concentrated than brand Y (15%).
