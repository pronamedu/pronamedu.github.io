29. A ream of bond paper contains 500 sheets.if a bond paper is 0.05 mm thick,how thick is 1 ream in centimeters?(with solution po thx0


To solve this, multiply the number of bond papers in the stack/ream to the thickness of each.

each bond paper = 0.05 mm thick

You can either convert mm to cm immediately, or get the product before converting.

0.05 mm → cm

0.05 mm/10 = 0.005 cm

0.005 cm x 500 = 2.5 cm

or you can do it this way:

0.05 mm x 500 = 25 mm

25 mm/10 = 2.5 cm

Either way, the answer should be the same. The ream of bond paper is 2.5 centimeters thick.


30. equivalent of 1 ream of bond paper​


Answer:

1 ream bond paper is defined as 500 identical sheets (or 20 quires of 25 sheets) according to international standards organizations. A ream is also a quantity for the number of sheets of the same size and quality.

Answer:

500

Explanation:

International standards organizations define the ream as 500 identical sheets. This ream of 500 sheets (20 quires of 25 sheets) is also known as a 'long' ream, and is gradually replacing the old value of 480 sheets, now known as a 'short' ream.
