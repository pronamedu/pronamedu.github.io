Answer:

The speaker is the voice or "persona" of a poem. One should not assume that the poet is the speaker, because the poet may be writing from a perspective entirely different from his own, even with the voice of another gender, race or species, or even of a material object. The reader or listener must do more than just hear the voice of the poem to identify the speaker. It is important to examine the other elements of the poem, such as the situation, structure, descriptive details, figurative language and rhythms to help determine the speaker’s identity.

30. who is the speaker/persona in the poem mother to son


Poem: Mother to Son

Answer:

The poem Mother to Son by Langston Hughes was a mother’s plea to his son. The speaker or persona in the poem was the mother. The mother explained that the path she has been through wasn’t made of crystal stairs, in other words she had difficulties here and there. But amidst those difficulties she didn’t remain in the painful part of the road, she continued to climb-she continued to reach lengths. And even in the dark where there are no lights seen, she still kept going.

The Message of the Mother to her Son was:

To not remain standing Keep going even it is difficult Don’t turn back, don’t back down

The mother was sure that her life wasn’t crystal stairs, it isn’t beautiful as she might have perceived it would be. So, through her Son she is determined to make him strong and to continue living on. This poem was somewhat tearful, it is like a mother saying farewell to her son but even though that is the case she still guides him throughout his life.
