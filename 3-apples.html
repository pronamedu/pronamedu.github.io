4. 3. the potential energy of an apple is 6 joules. the apple is 3m high. what is the mass of the apple?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The mass of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{m = \frac{PE}{gh}}[/tex]

where:

m = mass

PE = potential energy

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

[tex]m = \dfrac{PE}{gh}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{6 J}}{(\text{9.8 m/}\text{s}^{2})(\text{3 m})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{0.204 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of the apple is 0.204 kg.

[tex]\huge{ \colorbox{lightblue}{\tt \: \: \: PROBABILITY \: \: \: }} [/tex]

FORMULA:

[tex]\tt P(E)= \frac{number \: of \: ways \: E \:\: can \: occur}{number \: of \: possible \: outcomes} [/tex]

A basket contains 10 green apples, 15 red and 25 Fuji apples. Find the probability of getting:

green applesFuji applesred applesnot red apples

Note: In the given problem, the number of possible outcomes is 50 since there are 50 total apples in the basket (10+15+25=50)

[tex] \\ [/tex]

1. green apples

[tex] \tt \: P(g)= \frac{10}{50} \\ \\ \large \blue{ \boxed{\tt P(g)= \frac{1}{5}}} [/tex]

∴ The probability of getting red apples is [tex]\tt \frac{1}{5} [/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

2. Fuji apples

[tex] \tt \: P(f)= \frac{25}{50} \\ \\ \large \blue{ \boxed{\tt P(f)= \frac{1}{2}}} [/tex]

∴ The probability of getting red apples is [tex]\tt \frac{1}{2} [/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

3. red apples

[tex]\tt \: P(r)= \frac{15}{50} \\ \\ \large \blue{ \boxed{\tt P(r)= \frac{3}{10}}} [/tex]

∴ The probability of getting red apples is [tex]\tt \frac{3}{10} [/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

4. not red apples

[tex]\tt \: P(n)= \frac{35}{50} \\ \\ \large \blue{ \boxed{\tt P(n)= \frac{7}{10} }} [/tex]
