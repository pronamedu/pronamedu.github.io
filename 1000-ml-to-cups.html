10. What is the final temperature of 25 g of ice at -5 °C added to a styrofoam cup filled with 1000 mL of water at 35 "C?​


Answer:

What is the final temperature of 100 g of ice at -5 °C added to a styrofoam cup filled with 2500 ml of water at 35 °C?

Let’s start by assuming that all of the ice will melt and the resulting ice water will rise in temperature to meet the falling temperature of the original water. Thus, energy lost by water as it cools to some temp = energy needed to raise temp of ice to 0 °C + energy needed to melt ice + energy needed to raise temp resulting ice water to equalize temp with that of falling temp of original water.

Some constants you need:

specific heat of ice = 2.03 J/(g•°C)

heat of fusion of ice at 0 °C = 334 J/g

specific heat of water = 4.18 J/(g•°C)

-[2500 g • 4.18 J/(g•°C) • (tf - 35)] = 100 g • 2.03 J/(g•°C) • 100 g •334 J/g 100 g • 4.18 J/(g•°C) • (tf - 0) °C

where tf is the final temperature of the “mixture”. Note the “-” is necessary to indicate direction of heat flow. One side loses energy, other side gains energy.

-[2500 g • 4.18 J/(g•°C) • (tf - 35)] = 34415 J + 100 g • 4.18 J/(g•°C) • (tf ) °C

tf = 30.5 °C

So you will end up with 2600 g of water at 30.5 °C.
