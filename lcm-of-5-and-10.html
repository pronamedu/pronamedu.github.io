27. what is the LCM of 5 and 10​


Answer:

Formula of L C M is L C M (a,b) = ( a × b) / G C F (a,b)

G C F (5,10) = 5

L C M (5,10) = ( 5 × 10) / 5

L C M (5,10) = 50 / 5

L C M (5,10) = 10

Prime factors of 5 are 5. Prime factorization of 5 in exponential form is:

5 = 5 ^1

Prime factors of 10 are 2, 5. Prime factorization of 10 in exponential form is:

10 = 2 ^1 × 5 ^1

Now multiplying the highest exponent prime factors to calculate the L C M of 5 and 10.

L C M (5,10) = 5 ^1 × 2 ^1

L C M (5,10) = 10

Answer:

10

How?

LCM or the least common denominator is the smallest common multiple of 2 integers

In getting the LCM of a number you can use prime factorization, listing method and continuous division

here I used continuous division
