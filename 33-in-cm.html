11. a rectangular tank 50 cm long, 20 cm wide, and 33 cm high is filled with water to a depth​


Answer:

21 liters:)

Step-by-step explanation:

hope it helps lol

Answer:

21 litres

Step-by-step explanation:

1 litre = 1000 cubic cm

The rectangular tank is 50 cm long, 20 cm wide, and 33 cm high.

The tank is in the shape of a rectangular prism.

The volume of a rectangular prism is:

V = l * w * h

where l = length

w = width

h = height

Therefor, its volume is:

V = 50 * 20 * 33 = 33000 cubic cm = 33 litres

The tank is filled to a depth of 12 cm.

The volume of water in the tank is:

V = 50 * 20 * 12 = 12000 cubic cm = 12 litres

Therefore, the number of litres left to fill the tank is:

33 - 12 = 21 litres
