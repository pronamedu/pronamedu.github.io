20. A roulette wheel contains 30 numbers: 20 are blue and 10 are green. The roulette is fair which means that it is equally likely to land on any of the 30 numbers. You win P300 if the roulette lands on a blue number, you lose P200. What is the expected value or payoff of the game?.


Answer:

P133.33

Step-by-step explanation:

The expected value or payoff of the game can be calculated by taking the sum of the product of the possible outcomes and their probabilities.

Let X be the random variable representing the payoff of the game.

If the roulette lands on a blue number, the payoff is P300. This happens with probability 20/30 = 2/3.

If the roulette lands on a green number, the payoff is -P200. This happens with probability 10/30 = 1/3.

Therefore, the expected value or payoff of the game is:

E(X) = (2/3) * P300 + (1/3) * (-P200)

= (2/3) * 300 - (1/3) * 200

= 200 - 66.67

= P133.33

So the expected value or payoff of the game is P133.33. This means that on average, over the long run of playing the game, a player can expect to win P133.33 per game.
