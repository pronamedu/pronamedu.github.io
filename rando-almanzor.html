2. Age problemRando is 33 years older than has son Juan eight years ago, Rando's age is five more than trice Juan's present age. How old is Rando now?​


Step-by-step explanation:

Let's begin by using variables to represent the ages of Rando and his son Juan.

Let R = Rando's current age

Let J = Juan's current age

We know from the problem that Rando is 33 years older than his son Juan was eight years ago. This can be expressed as:

R = J + 33

We also know that Rando's current age is five more than three times Juan's present age. This can be expressed as:

R = 3J + 5

Now we can set these two equations equal to each other and solve for J, Juan's current age:

J + 33 = 3J + 5

2J = 28

J = 14

So Juan is currently 14 years old. We can use this information to find Rando's current age:

R = J + 33

R = 14 + 33

R = 47

Therefore, Rando is currently 47 years old.

5. A math teacher claims that the mean score of students who to ok a calculus test 76 with a standard deviation of 5.2. A rando m sample of 50 students took the test, and the results showe d that the mean grade of the students is 78.5. Calculate the te st statistic.​


Answer:

To calculate the test statistic, we need to use the formula for the z-score:

z = (x - μ) / (σ / sqrt(n))

where x is the sample mean, μ is the population mean, σ is the population standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

In this case, we have:

x = 78.5 (sample mean) μ = 76 (population mean) σ = 5.2 (population standard deviation) n = 50 (sample size)

Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

z = (78.5 - 76) / (5.2 / sqrt(50)) z = 2.5 / 0.7348 z ≈ 3.408

Therefore, the test statistic is approximately 3.408.
