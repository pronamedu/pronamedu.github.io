There are 110 people at a meeting. They each shake hands with everyone else. How many handshakes were there?

If there are n people in a room and each person shakes hands with every other person exactly once, the total number of handshakes can be calculated using the following formula:

Total handshakes = (n * (n-1)) / 2

In this case, there are 110 people at the meeting, so we can substitute n = 110 into the formula:

Total handshakes = (110 * (110-1)) / 2

Total handshakes = (110 * 109) / 2

Total handshakes = 11,990

Therefore, there were 11,990 handshakes at the meeting.

25. she felt as if she were being unfriended by everyone A. first person B.second person C.third person ​


Answer:

C. Third Person

"She felt as if she were being unfriended by everyone" is an example of third person point of view. The speaker narrates or describes what is happening in the person the speaker is referring to. Our clue in the sentence is she which means it is in third person point of view and not first person or second person.

_________________

What is first person? First person refers to the speaker himself. The words used are we, us, I, our, and more.

What is second person? Second person refers to the speaker referring to who he is talking to. The clue here is "you".
