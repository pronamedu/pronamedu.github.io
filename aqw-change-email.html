22. The digital age has brought many years ways to communicate such as email and facebook.How has communication changed? In what way is it helpful to us? ​


Social media and other digital technologies have made it possible for us to share and communicate in real time beyond conventional cultural barriers and across the globe.

The phrase "digital age" has a fairly broad definition, but over the past ten years, the usage of digital technology in our daily lives has grown significantly. Online communication between people can take many different forms. Online gaming, social media, and online dating will all be covered in this essay.

Although it has been around for a while, online dating is not a recent phenomenon. But since its inception, how we use it has significantly evolved. Online dating was viewed as a bit shady and desperate in the late 1990s and early 2000s. Nowadays, meeting someone through an app like Tinder or OkCupid has become regular and routine. This movement in my opinion reflects a larger cultural change in attitudes toward gender equality and the acceptance of technology in daily life.

Another significant, relatively new development in our digital communication with one another is social media. Because Fasebook has become such a crucial component of how many people engage with one another on a daily basis, it is difficult to fathom life before it. Although social media can be used to promote false information, it also makes it possible for individuals to rapidly and easily share significant information about their life.

In the past century, communication has undergone a significant transformation, but it hasn't been until recently that we've started to fully grasp its long-term effects. Since the invention of the telegraph, we've had to devise fresh methods for creating a powerful and trustworthy signal that can successfully transmit messages. As a result, we frequently have to choose between delivering complicated messages and using all of our available bandwidth. When you use emoticons, for instance, you are simply reducing your ideas into something you know will be seen clearly on a little computer screen.

Today's world is becoming more and more controlled by digital communication. As technology develops and new communication methods appear, the way we interact with one another has radically altered and will likely continue to do so. Asynchronous communication (communication that happens over time), synchronous communication (communication that happens in real-time), and face-to-face communication are the three basic types of communication in the digital age.

Users who engage in asynchronous communication can answer whenever they are able. Email and messaging are two examples, while email often results in more productive communication than texting because it allows for lengthier conversations. Email is frequently favored in professional interactions because it gives you more time to consider your response before you send it. Real-time synchronous communication takes place during phone calls or video conferences. This is frequently the go-to approach in today's digital world for contacting someone who lives far away without having to make a costly long-distance call. Gorgle Hangouts and Skype are two examples of this.

However, despite all these technological advancements, many people are discovering that the level of their communication is declining. Instead of spending less time conversing with one another, we are simply filling that time with less significant exchanges. I believe we are losing out on real connections because we are being diverted by digital communication devices that offer very little in the way of value. Throwing technology out the window won't solve this problem; instead, I think it offers an opportunity to focus on forging deeper relationships with people who are close to us.
