1. Movie analysis of Rapunzel By the Brother Grimm. the original Rapunzel the Grimms fairly tailsthank you po​


Answer: The Grimm Brothers' version of "Rapunzel" drew heavily upon the French version of the "Persinette," written by Charlotte-Rose de Caumont La Force in her Tale of Tales book. The most prevalent version was in Joachim Christoph Fredrich Schulz's 1790 book Kleine Romane, which included substituting the parsley herb from La Force's version and replacing it with the famous rapunzel (in the English translation, variously defined as 'rampion,' 'field salad,' 'corn salad,' or 'lamb's lettuce'). Giambattista Basile's 1634 "Petrosinella" also has a similar structure to the tale, with the pregnant mother character's craving for her witch neighbor's parsley inspiring the name of the child herself.

Explanation:

"Rapunzel" was one of the 200-some stories published as part of the Grimm Brothers' "Kinder- und Hausmärchen" ("Children's and Household Tales"), which later became referred to simply as Grimm's Fairy Tales. The book was published in German originally but within the next decade (1823) had been translated into English as German Popular Tales. Besides "Rapunzel," this collection includes some of the most recognizable folk and fairy tales of Western culture, such as "Hansel and Gretel," "Snow White," "Sleeping Beauty," "Little Red Riding Hood," and more.

As noted above, the Grimm Brothers took inspiration for many of their tales from various sources, and "Rapunzel" is no exception. Their immediate start would have been Friedrich Schultz's 1790 Kleine Romane, v. 5, but they also would have understood the tale as having a longer literary and oral tradition.

The version readers encountered in Grimm's Fairy Tales did have some modifications to match the style of their other tales. The brothers also edited the stories slightly between editions.

With "Rapunzel," the 1819 version saw the following changes:

The tale is slightly longer.Gothel is now not a fairy but a 'sorceress.'The prince discovers Rapunzel not by seeing her directly but by hearing her singing from the tower.Rapunzel's pregnancy is no longer directly alluded to (the mention of her "too-tight clothes" has been removed in this edition).

"Rapunzel" tells the story of a couple struggling to conceive a child, their miracle child Rapunzel, and the wicked witch (Mother/Frau Goethel) who kidnaps and imprisons the young and beautiful Rapunzel in a lonely tower.

21. who is Caleb in the bible​


Answer:

Caleb, in the Old Testament, one of the spies sent by Moses from Kadesh in southern Palestine to spy out the land of Canaan. Only Caleb and Joshua advised the Hebrews to proceed immediately to take the land; for his faith Caleb was rewarded with the promise that he and his descendants should possess it (Numbers 13–14).

Meaning of caleb

The Hebrew meaning offered for Caleb is "faithful, devotion, whole hearted, bold, brave". This is on the basis of its being actually a compound word, a phenomenon quite common in ancient Hebrew.

The first criterion must be a critical event that triggered the transaction process, and the amount that is to be collected from that transaction is measurable within a certain degree of reliability.
