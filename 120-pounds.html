25. Find the cost per pound of chocolate coated corn flakes made from 40 pounds of chocolate which costs php200,00 per pound and 120 pounds of cornflakes which costs php88,00 per pound ​


Answer:

The cost per pound of chocolate coated corn flakes is Php116.00

Step-by-step explanation:

Cost of chocolate = 40 pounds x Php200.00/pound = Php8,000.00

Cost of cornflakes = 120 pounds x Php88.00/pound = Php10,560.00

Total cost of chocolate coated corn flakes = Cost of chocolate + Cost of cornflakes

= Php8,000.00 + Php10,560.00

= Php18,560.00

divide the total cost by the total weight of the chocolate coated corn flakes:

Total weight of chocolate coated corn flakes = 40 pounds + 120 pounds = 160 pounds

Cost per pound = Total cost of chocolate coated corn flakes / Total weight of chocolate coated corn flakes

= Php18,560.00 / 160 pounds

= Php116.00/pound

Therefore, the cost per pound of chocolate coated corn flakes is Php116.00.

30. what is the momentum of a 120 pound bicyclist that is traveling at 25 mph?​


Answer:

The momentum is 21,120 lb.ft/s

Explanation:

In our case, we are to solve for the momentum of a traveling bicyclist. In momentum, this is possible when an object is moving.

For the formula, we will use:

p = mv

where

p     is the momentum of the object, unit is in kg.m/s (metric system) or

      lb.ft/s (British system)

m    is the mass of the object, unit is in kg or lb

v     is the velocity of the object, unit is in m/s or ft/s

For the given information

m = 120 lb

v = 25 mph or miles per hour

p = ?

Solving the problem

1. First, let us convert the velocity of the bicyclist to feet per second, we have:

[tex]v=120 \frac{mi}{h} x \frac{5280ft}{1mile} x\frac{1hour}{3600s}[/tex]

v = 176 ft/s

Now, we are ready to solve for the momentum.

2. Let us use the formula for momentum, then substitute the given information, we have:

p = mv

p = (120 lb)(176 ft/s)

p = 21,120 lb.ft/s          (unit in British system)

If you want the unit of momentum in metric system, just convert 120 lb to kg and 25 mph to m/s. Then substitute the converted units to the formula of momentum and the answer for momentum in metric unit is 609.47 kg.m/s.

Therefore, the momentum of the bicyclist is 21,120 lb.ft/s
