4. what is the brief definition or description of a leading term?​


Answer:

The leading term is the term with the highest power of x.

Answer:

In a polynomial, the leading term is the term with the highest power of x. For example, the leading term of 7+x−3x2 is −3x2. The leading coefficient of a polynomial is the coefficient of the leading term.

Answer:

As a doctor in the locality, it is important to inform the people about the leading causes of morbidity and mortality, as well as how to prevent them. Here are some common diseases in the Philippines, their causes, and the mortality and morbidity rates:

1.Cardiovascular Diseases - These are diseases of the heart and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease, stroke, and hypertension. They are caused by risk factors such as smoking, unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, obesity, and stress. Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of mortality in the Philippines, accounting for 24.8% of all deaths. The mortality rate is 246.1 deaths per 100,000 population, while the morbidity rate is 2,716 cases per 100,000 population.

Prevention: Lifestyle modifications such as regular exercise, healthy diet, stress reduction, smoking cessation, and maintaining a healthy weight can help prevent cardiovascular diseases. Proper management of hypertension and diabetes is also important.

2.Cancer - This refers to a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells. The most common types of cancer in the Philippines are lung cancer, breast cancer, and liver cancer. The main risk factors include tobacco use, unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, and exposure to radiation and certain chemicals. Cancer is the second leading cause of mortality in the Philippines, accounting for 22.3% of all deaths. The mortality rate is 115.3 deaths per 100,000 population, while the morbidity rate is 126.6 cases per 100,000 population.

Prevention: Avoiding tobacco use, adopting a healthy diet and lifestyle, reducing alcohol consumption, and getting screened for cancer regularly can help prevent the disease. Vaccines for hepatitis B and human papillomavirus (HPV) can also help prevent liver and cervical cancers, respectively.

3.Respiratory Diseases - These include diseases of the lungs and airways, such as pneumonia, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and tuberculosis (TB). They are caused by risk factors such as smoking, air pollution, and exposure to occupational hazards. Respiratory diseases are the third leading cause of mortality in the Philippines, accounting for 8.8% of all deaths. The mortality rate is 46.4 deaths per 100,000 population, while the morbidity rate is 3,044 cases per 100,000 population.

Prevention: Quitting smoking, reducing exposure to air pollution, and improving indoor air quality can help prevent respiratory diseases. Vaccination against influenza and pneumococcal disease is also recommended, particularly for those with underlying respiratory conditions.

It is important for healthcare professionals like doctors to educate the public about the causes, prevention, and treatment of these common diseases to help reduce the mortality and morbidity rates in the community. Regular check-ups, healthy lifestyle choices, and proper management of chronic conditions can go a long way in promoting overall health and wellness.

9. cause and effect of invasive species with a short description that explains how each process leads to loss of biodiversity​


Answer:

Invasive species can harm both the natural resources in an ecosystem as well as threaten human use of these resources. An invasive species can be introduced to a new area via the ballast water of oceangoing ships, intentional and accidental releases of aquaculture species, aquarium specimens or bait, and other means.

Invasive species are capable of causing extinctions of native plants and animals, reducing biodiversity, competing with native organisms for limited resources, and altering habitats. This can result in huge economic impacts and fundamental disruptions of coastal and Great Lakes ecosystems.
