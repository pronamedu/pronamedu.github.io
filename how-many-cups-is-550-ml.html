16. Calculate the number of moles contained in 550.mL of carbon dioxide at STP


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: List the given values.

Note that at STP, the temperature and pressure of an ideal gas are 273.15 K and 1.00 atm, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{1.00 atm} \\ & V = \text{550. mL = 0.550 L} \\ & T = \text{273.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{1.00 atm})(\text{0.550 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{273.15 K})} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.0245 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the number of moles contained in 550. mL of carbon dioxide at STP is 0.0245 mol.
