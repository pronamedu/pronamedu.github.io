8. 3. The price of Mangoes in the market rises from Php90.00 per kilo to Php130.00 in one week. Does the demand for mangoes rise or fall? Why?​


THE PRICE OF MANGO IN THE MARKET

In economics, demand is the quantity of a good or service that consumers are willing and able to buy at a given price, over a specific time period. It is represented by a downward sloping curve, where an increase in price leads to a decrease in quantity demanded, and vice versa.

DOES DEMAND FOR MANGOES RISE OR FALL

Now, let's consider the scenario given. The price of mangoes has increased from Php90.00 per kilo to Php130.00 per kilo in one week. This price increase could be due to a number of factors, including a decrease in supply, an increase in production or transportation costs, or an increase in demand.

However, the price increase alone does not tell us if the demand for mangoes has risen or fallen. It is possible that the demand for mangoes has remained the same, while the supply has decreased, leading to an increase in price.

On the other hand, it is also possible that the demand for mangoes has increased, leading to a higher price equilibrium. For example, if the weather conditions are favorable for mango growth and consumption, or if there is a sudden surge in popularity for mango-based products, it could cause an increase in demand for mangoes.

To determine if the demand for mangoes has risen or fallen, we need to look at other factors, such as changes in consumer behavior or market trends. For example, we could gather data on the quantity of mangoes sold before and after the price increase, conduct surveys to understand consumer preferences and spending habits, and analyze market trends to see if there are any external factors that could be impacting the demand for mangoes.

In summary, while an increase in the price of mangoes could suggest a decrease in demand, it is important to consider other factors before making any conclusions about changes in demand.
