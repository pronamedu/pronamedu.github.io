19. How many molecules of water are needed to make a 25% 350g solution of NaCl?​


Answer:

no. of molecules H2O = 8.77 × 10²⁴ molecules

Explanation:

Calculating the mass of solute (NaCl)

mass solute = (percent by mass / 100) • (mass solution)

mass solute = (25 / 100) • (350 g)

mass solute = 87.5 g

Calculating the mass of solvent (H2O)

mass solvent = mass solution - mass solvent

mass solvent = 350 g - 87.5 g

mass solvent = 262.5 g

Calculating the number of molecules of H2O

no. of molecules H2O = 262.5 g × (1 mol / 18.02 g) × (6.022 × 10²³ molecules / 1 mol)

no. of molecules H2O = 8.77 × 10²⁴ molecules

30. Ethane, C2H, contains 79.9% carbon and 20,1% hydrogen, Calculate the amount of hydrogen in350g sample of this compourd,​


To calculate the amount of hydrogen in the compound Ethane, we will re-arrange the formula:

[tex]\%\:mass\:of\:element=\frac{mass\:of\:element\:in\:the\:compound}{mass\:of\:the\:compound} *100\\ mass\:of\:element\:in\:the\:compound=\frac{\%\:mass\:of\:the\:element}{100} *mass\:of\:the\:compound[/tex]

The mass of elements in the compound:

carbon = 79.9%

hydrogen = 20.1%

Mass of the compound:

350 g

Now we can solve as we have the formula and the needed information.

[tex]mass\:of\:element\:in\:the\:compound=\frac{\%\:mass\:of\:the\:element}{100} *mass\:of\:the\:compound\\ mass\:of\:element\:in\:the\:compound=\frac{20.1\%}{100\%} *350\:g\\ mass\:of\:element\:in\:the\:compound=70.35\:g[/tex]

∴ The amount of hydrogen in 350 g sample of Ethane is 70.35 g.
