9. In fruit flies the allele for long wings (L) is dominant and the allele for short wings (l) is recessive. What is the likely genotype of a fruit fly with short wings? A. Either LL or Ll B. Either Ll or ll C. Only LL D. Only ll


Answer:

D.

Explanation:

An allele that expresses itself even in the presence of an alternative allele is known as a dominant allele. An allele that is not expressed in the presence of an alternative allele is called a recessive allele.

Mendel's Law of Inheritance

Law of dominance- It is the first law of Mendel.- It states that in a cross between two organisms that are pure for any pair of contrasting characters, the character that expresses in the first generation is dominant and the one which is suppressed is recessive. Law of segregation- It is the second law of Mendel. It states that members of allelic pairs in a hybrid remain together without mixing with each other and they segregate during gamete formation

In fruit flies, the allele for long wings (L) is dominant and the allele for short wing (l) is recessive. The likely genotype of the fruit fly with short wings is only ll.

A. Either LL or Ll- According to the law of dominance allele L will be dominant over allele l. Hence the fruit fly with genotype LL or Ll will have long wings.  

B. Either Ll or ll- According to the law of dominance allele L will be dominant over allele l. Hence the fruit fly with genotype Ll will have long wings. The recessive allele is expressed only in the absence of other alleles. The allele for short wing (l) is recessive and is present in homozygous condition, There is an absence of another allele Hence the genotype of the fruit fly with short wing will be ll.  

C. Only LL- According to the law of dominance allele L will be dominant over allele l. Hence the fruit fly with genotype LL will have long wings.  

D. Only ll- The recessive allele is expressed only in the absence of other alleles. The allele for short wing (l) is recessive and is present in homozygous condition, There is an absence of another allele.

Hence the genotype of the fruit fly with short wing will be (D.) only ll.

21. In fruit flies, the allele for vestigial wings is recessive to the allele for normal wings. In a generation of fruit flies, 18 males and 15 females with vestigial wings are produced, and 43 males and 44 females with normal wings are produced. Which of the following is the best conclusion you can make about the parents of this generation?


Answer:

Both parents phenotypically have normal wings. Both also have the genotype Ww .

Explanation:

Let us say that the two types of alleles that can make up the gene locus for wing type of fruit flies are represented by a capital W (normal wings for the dominant allele) and a lowercase w (vestigial wings for the recessive allele).

To have an idea about the genotype of the parents, we can get clues by knowing the phenotypic ratio within the generation. Although the question gives us the number of males and females with normal and vestigial wings, the trait does not seem to be sex-linked because the occurrences of normal and vestigial wings between male and female are not quite far off.

Frequency of fruit flies with vestigial wings = (males with vestigial wings + females with vestigial wings) / total population = (18 + 15) / (18 + 15 + 43 + 44) = 33 / 120 or approximately 25% of the generation.

Frequency of fruit flies with normal wings = (males with normal wings + females with normal wings) / total population = (43 + 44) / (18 + 15 + 43 + 44) = 87 / 120 or approximately 75% of the generation.

We therefore conclude that 25% of the generation has vestigial wings with genotype ww, and 75% have normal wings with genotype WW or Ww. The phenotypic ratio of fruit flies with vestigial wings to fruit flies with normal wings is 25:75 or 1:3.

If each of the two parents have 2 alleles for wing type, this means that during reproduction 4 alleles in total can do recombination. What combination of alleles will produce 25% ww and 75% WW or Ww? The only way to get 25% ww is if both parents have the recessive allele. Thus, both parents phenotypically have normal wings, but both also have the genotype Ww .

Checking our answer: Ww x Ww = 25% WW, 50% Ww, and 25% ww. Our genotypic ratio is 1:2:1, and our phenotypic ratio is 1:3
