13. Which of the following traits is not controlled by multiple genes?a. color blindnessb. eye colorc. frecklesd. hair texturw​


Answer:

D.hair texture

Explanation:

The major difference between the two is that pleiotropy is when one gene affects multiple characteristics (e.g. Marfan syndrome) and polygenic inheritance is when one trait is controlled by multiple genes (e.g. skin pigmentation). Codominance and incomplete dominance are not the same.

In humans, traits include such things as the color of a person's hair, skin, and eyes, the blood group, the shape of nose and lips, and the tendancy to be short-sighted or to become bald. Some of the traits that humans acquire through inheritance can be modified by the influence of the environment, while others cannot.

17. An example of multiple allele inheritance is ___________. A. the ABO blood type. B. the appearance of freckles on the skin. C. the appearance of birthmarks on the skin. D. hair that seems to have several shades of a color.


Answer:

A. the ABO blood type.

Explanation:

An excellent example of multiple allele inheritance is human blood type. Blood type exists as four possible phenotypes: A, B, AB, & O. There are 3 alleles for the gene that determines blood type. (Remember: You have just 2 of the 3 in your genotype --- 1 from mom & 1 from dad).

What is ABO blood type:

ABO(type A, type B, and type O) blood group literally is the process of grouping blood according to the presence or absence of both A and B antigens on the RBC membrane or a and b antibodies in plasma in a for all intents and purposes big way. The theory for the most part is the presence of "A antigen marks absence\" of an antibody in blood and the same for B antigen and b antibody.  

ABO blood group system:Group A has A antigen.Group B has B antigen.Group AB has both A & B antigens.Group O have no antigens.

30. Which of the following traits is not controlled by multiple genes? a. eye color b. hair texture c. color blindness d. freckles


The answer is b.

Hair texture is not a trait so it is not controlled by the genes of your mother and father. An individual's hair texture depends on the care of an individual, not the biological side.

Some traits can be passed down from one generation to other. These traits include eye color, color blindness, and freckles. If both parents are carriers of these traits, the higher the chance of having offspring with the features mentioned above.

Here are the reasons why hair texture is not controlled by multiple genes.

Hair texture is can be manipulated personally
Some people go to the salon to fix their hair or use hair products to take care of it. It is someone's actions that determine the health of his or her hair which affects its texture.

Hair can be affected by the location of an individual
Scientists have observed that some individuals living in tropical areas tend to have dry hair textures. This made them conclude that the humidity and climate of a particular location can affect the texture of an individual's hair.

Hair traits that can be controlled by genes
Wavy and curly hair, hair color, and hair diseases are some of the traits that can be connected to genes relating to someone's hair. Although some of these traits may affect the texture of someone's hair, but the texture alone cannot be passed by parents.
