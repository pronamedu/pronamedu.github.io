9. How does Pitcher plant catch insects


The pitcher plant are attached to the cavity formed by the cupped leaf. They contain mutualistic insect larvae, so if the fly is there, they will eat it and trapped the prey. This plant is called insectivorous plant.

As the name suggests, pitcher plant leaves are shaped like pitchers. When it rains these modified leaves hold water inside it. When insects fall in these leaves, they drown in the water.
