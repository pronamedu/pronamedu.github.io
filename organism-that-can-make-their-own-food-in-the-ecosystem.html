1. What are the two feeding relationships in an intertidal zone or estuarine ecosystem?

Answer:

Food Webs and Interdependency

The feeding relationship, known as the food web, is vital to the interdependence of life in estuaries. The estuary food web contains phytoplankton, which are microscopic plants that produce food through photosynthesis and absorb nutrients from the water.

2. What are the three biotic (living) factors in any ecosystem?

Answer: The living components of an ecosystem are referred to as biotic factors. They are classified into three groups: producers (autotrophs), consumers (heterotrophs), and decomposers (detritivores).

3. What will happen to the other living things if producers are removed from their

environment?

Answer: The removal of the producers would result in the collapse of the entire food web. Primary consumers or herbivores, which feed directly on producers, would become extinct. Higher level consumers would suffer if lower trophic level organisms began to die off.
