4. A 200 item aptitude test was given to BSED and BEED college students. 350 BSED students has a mean of 140 and standard deviation of 25 while 420 BEED has mean of 120 and standard deviation of 30. How many of the BSED students are better than the average BEED students?


To find out how many of the BSED students are better than the average BEED student, we need to first standardize the scores for both groups using z-scores. The formula for z-score is:

z = (x - μ) / σ

where x is the raw score, μ is the mean, and σ is the standard deviation.

For the BSED students:

z = (x - μ) / σ

z = (x - 140) / 25

For the BEED students:

z = (x - μ) / σ

z = (x - 120) / 30

To compare the two groups, we need to find the z-score for the average BEED student, which is:

z = (x - μ) / σ

z = (120 - 120) / 30

z = 0

This means that the average BEED student has a z-score of 0.

To find out how many BSED students have a higher score than the average BEED student, we need to find the proportion of BSED students with a z-score greater than 0. We can use the standard normal distribution table or calculator to find this proportion.

Using the table, we can look up the area under the curve to the right of z = 0, which is 0.5000. This means that 50% of the BSED students have a z-score greater than 0.

To find the actual number of BSED students who are better than the average BEED student, we need to multiply the proportion by the total number of BSED students:

Number of BSED students better than average BEED student = 0.5 x 350

Number of BSED students better than average BEED student = 175

Therefore, there are 175 BSED students who are better than the average BEED student.


5. To calculate the average height of the students in PHINMA COC 50 students are randomly selected. The mean height of the sample is 168 cm. The presumption shows a standard deviation of 54 cm. Find the interval estimate of the population mean u using 90% confidence level.​


To find the interval estimate of the population mean u using a 90% confidence level, we can use the following formula:

CI = x̄ ± z* (σ/√n)

where CI is the confidence interval, x̄ is the sample mean, z is the z-score corresponding to the desired level of confidence (0.90 in this case), σ is the population standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

Substituting the given values, we get:

CI = 168 ± 1.645*(54/√50)

CI = 168 ± 13.38

CI = (154.62, 181.38)

Therefore, with 90% confidence, the interval estimate of the population mean height of the students in PHINMA COC is (154.62 cm, 181.38 cm). If we repeat the sampling process many times and calculate the confidence interval each time, we would expect about 90% of those intervals to contain the true population means height.

The z-score is calculated as follows: z = (x - μ) / σ, where x is the score of the BSED student, μ is the mean score for BSED students, and σ is the standard deviation of scores for BSED students.

Substituting the given values into the formula, we get: z = (120 - 140) / 25 = -0.8. This means that a BSED student who scored 120 is 0.8 standard deviations below the mean score for BSED students.

Using a z-table, we can find that the proportion of BSED students who scored better than 120 is approximately 0.7881. Multiplying this proportion by the total number of BSED students (350), we get that approximately 276 BSED students scored better than the average BEED student.

24. the mean score for a class of 30 students was 75. fifteen male students in the class average 70. what is the mean of fifteen female?​


Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Mean score of 30 students = 75

Mean score of 15 male students = 70

Required: Mean score of 15 female students

Solution:

First, get the total score of all students by using the average formula below:

Mean score or Average = Total score of all students / Total no. of students

Hence,

Total score of all students = Mean score * Total no. of students

= 75 * 30

= 2,250

Thus, the total score of all students is 2,250.

Next, get the total score of 15 male students following above formula:

Total score of 15 male students = Mean score of 15 male students * 15 male students

= 70 * 15

= 1,050

Thus, the total score of 15 male students is 1,050.

Then, derive the total score of 15 female students by subtracting the total score of 15 male studens from the total score of the class:

Total score of 15 female students = Total score of all students - Total score of 15 male students

= 2,250 - 1,050

= 1,200

Lastly, divide the total score of female students with 15 to get the mean score, hence,

Mean score of 15 female students = Total score of 15 female students / 15 female students

= 1,200 / 15

= 80

Therefore, the mean score of 15 female students is 80.

How To Calculate Semester Grade

Class grades are calculated based on weighted average of your quarterly grades and final grades.

1) Calculate my Semester grade: If you already have your finals grade handy, then you can calculate your semester grade by factoring in your weighted quarter grades and final grades.

2) What do I have to get on the exam?: Because your final exam grades aren’t available until the very end, it isn’t included when calculating your current overall grade. So, all the other categories are factored in to calculate the minimum grade you need to score on the final exam.

Determining your semester grade

To determine your semester grade in a course, we must first explore how the parts of the semester are weighted for a course. If we have a semester grade that is weighted 40% for first quarter, 40% for second quarter, and 20% for a final exam, then we need to begin by changing the percentages into decimals:

40%= .4 (Quarter one)

40%= .4 (Quarter two)

20%= .2 (Final Exam)

When the decimals are added together, they should equal 1.

.4 + .4 + .2=1

This indicates that 100% of the semester grade (40% + 40% + 20%=100%) is represented.

Once we have renamed the percentages as decimals, the math is a little easier. We now take each weight and multiply it by the grade for each category:

weight of a part of the semester (as a decimal) X grade in that same part of the semester

In the Philippines, some universities follow a 4-Point Scale, which resembles or is equivalent to the U.S. grading system. This system uses a grade between 0.00 to 4.00 wherein 4.00 is the highest and 0.00 being a failing mark.

Other universities follow a 5-Point Scale, wherein the highest grade is a 1.00 and the lowest is a 5.00 (failing mark). The lowest passing mark is actually a 3.00. Although usually not depicted, a grade of 4.00 is equivalent to a grade of incomplete. If the school does not use the grade point "4.00", it will use "INC" instead.

Most colleges and universities will use either the 4-Point or 5-Point Scales when presenting final grades. When scoring individual coursework, they will use the Percent Grade, Letter Grade, or both. More importantly, Philippine universities do not have standard grade equivalence. Different universities have varied equivalence range, while passing grades are subject to imposed academic quality of an institution.

K–12 (Kindergarten and Basic Education) always uses the Percent Grade, Letter Grade, or both.

GWA (general weighted average; similar to GPA) is a representation (often numerical) of the overall scholastic standing of students used for evaluation. GWA is based on the grades in all subjects taken at a particular level including subjects taken outside of the curriculum. Representation of the subjects taken only in a specific curriculum is called CWA, or curriculum weighted average.
