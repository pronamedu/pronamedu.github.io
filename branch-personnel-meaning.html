5. advance study of the branches of science and give meaning of each branches


Branches of Science

There are two broad divisions of science-the physical sciences and the biological sciences and I will gonna explain to you of what are the branches.

Physical Sciences

Physical Sciences deal with the study of nonliving matter. The two main branches of physical science are chemistry and physics. Some of the branches of science that under this category are the following:

Chemistry - It deals with the study of the composition, properties, form, structure, and changes in matter.

Physics - It deals with the study of matter and energy including light, electicity, sound, magnetism, motion, and radiations.

Earth Sciences - These deal with the scientific study of Earth, its composition, structure, and other astronomical objects, interacting with it. The branches of Earth Science include geology, astronomy, seismology, and meteorology.

Geology - This branch of science involves the study of the origin, structure, history, and evolution of the crust of Earth. It also involves the examination of rocks and soil.

Astronomy - This is the study of celestial bodies, such as stars, galaxies, comets, and planets, and some phenomena which originate outside the atmosphere of our Earth.

Seismology - This is the study of earthquakes and the movement of waves and artificially produced vibrations of Earth.

Meteorology - This is the study of the atmosphere that focuses on weather processes and forecasting.

Biological Sciences or Life Sciences

Biological Sciences deal with the study of living organisms. The sciences under this category focus on the study of internal body functions and the structure of body cells and tissues. These are the branches of science under life sciences:

Biology - This deals with the study of living things or living organisms. It encompasses all the various aspects like the structure and function of the various organs, physiology, taxonomy, morphology and etc.

Zoology - This deals with the study of animals - like their form and structure.

Ecology - This deals with the study of the relationship of living organisms among one another and their interaction with their surroundings.

Botany - This deals with the study of plants.

Genetics - This deals with the study of heredity—the ways in which characteristics are passed from parent to offspring.

26. 10 branches of science and its meaning​


Answer:

The Physical Sciences

Physics: The study of matter and energy and the interactions between them. Physicists study such subjects as gravity, light, and time. Albert Einstein, a famous physicist, developed the Theory of Relativity.

The Life Sciences (Biology)

Botany: The study of plants.

Zoology: The science that covers animals and animal life.

Genetics: The study of heredity.

Medicine: The science of diagnosing, treating, and preventing illness, disease and injury.
