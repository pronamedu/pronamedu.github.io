10. What is the compound Cu2SO4 called?


Cu₂SO₄ is called Copper (I) sulfate

Further explanation

Chemical formulas are compositional instructions of the constituent particles of a substance. Expressed in the form of symbols of the constituent elements and their comparisons.

The chemical formula itself can be divided into 2 namely MF = molecular formula and EF = empirical formula

Empirical formula (EF) is the smallest atomic ratio of the constituent elements.

Molecular formula (MF) is a formula that shows the number of elemental atoms that make up a compound.

(EF) n = MF

A chemical formula is related to the rules for naming chemical compounds.

The nomenclature of the salt compound is not much different from the naming of the ion compound.

The name of the ion compound is a combination of the name of the positive ion which is mentioned first then followed by the name of the negative ion

Cations are mainly metals such as Na⁺, Fe²⁺ and there are some non-metals such as NH₄⁺

While anions can be formed from a single atom (monatomic) or polyatom. Usually for polyatomic ions that contain oxygen are named from non-oxygen atoms and then given the suffix -ate or -it.

In salts which are ionic compounds, they consist of cations of bases and acid residual anions

1) A cation that has only one oxidation number, its name is to write the first cation (initial capital letter) followed by the residual acid anion (lower case)

Example:

K₂SO₄ = Potassium sulfate

Mg₂SO₄ = Magnesium sulfate

 

2) cations that have more than one oxidation number, the naming is to write the name of the cation along with its oxidation number (Roman number) in parentheses followed by the name of the residual acid anion.

Example

SnSO₄ = Tin (II) sulfate (Sn has an oxidation number + 2, + 4)

FeSO₄ = Iron (II) sulfate (Fe has an oxidation number + 2, + 3)

Cu₂SO₄ includes salt formed from Cu (monoatom cation) which have 2 possible oxidation numbers,  +1 and +2 (in these compounds the oxidation number is +1)

While the anion is an anion of residual polyatomic acid containing oxygen with the -ate suffix
