The classic French vinaigrette dressing is excellent on its own, but it's also a great starting point for additional embellishments.

Vinaigrette is produced by combining oil with an acidic ingredient like vinegar or lemon juice. Salt, herbs, and/or spices can be added to the mixture of vinaigrette to make it more flavorful. Vinaigrette is typically used as a salad dressing, but it also works well as a marinade.

French dressing is a creamy dressing that ranges in color from pale orange to brilliant crimson and is found in consumer-facing American cuisine and store-bought products in the United States. It's created using oil, vinegar, sugar, and other flavorings, with tomato and paprika as the coloring. It's somewhere in the middle of Russian and Catalina dressings.

French dressing was synonymous with vinaigrette in the nineteenth century, and the American professional culinary sector still uses that term today.
