14. A ship sails 13 miles due east from a port, then turns south and sail 9 miles. What is the distance and bearing from the port


Answer:

√277 miles or 16.65 miles

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use a cartesian plane to solve this. We can say that the port lies on (0,0), we can say that the ship that sails east 13 miles and south 9 miles lies on (13,9). Now we can use the distance formula to solve for the distance between the port (0,0) and the current location of the ship (13,9).

Solution :

(0,0) and (13,9) are the coordiantes

d = √[(x_2 - x_1)² + (y_2 - y_1)²]

d = √[(13 - 0)² + (9 - 0)²]

d = √[(13)² + (9)²]

d = √(196 + 81)

d = √277

d = √277 miles or 16.65 miles
