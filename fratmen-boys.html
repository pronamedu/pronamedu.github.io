14. 2. A class contains 6 boys and 5 girls. In how many ways can a committee of five be formed if: a.) All members are boys b.) 2boys and 3 girls ​


a.) Since all members must be boys, we simply need to choose 5 boys from the 6 available. We can do this in ${6 \choose 5} = 6$ ways.

b.) To form a committee of 2 boys and 3 girls, we need to choose 2 boys from the 6 available and 3 girls from the 5 available. We can do this in ${6 \choose 2} \cdot {5 \choose 3} = 15 \cdot 10 = 150$ ways.

Therefore, there are 6 ways to form a committee of all boys, and 150 ways to form a committee of 2 boys and 3 girls.

22. 2. A class contains 6 boys and 5 girls. In how many ways can a committee of five be formed if: a.) All members are boys b.) 2boys and 3 girls solution pls...​


Answer:

a.) Since we need to form a committee of 5 with all boys, we can choose 5 boys from the 6 boys in the class in 6 choose 5 ways, which is:

6C5 = 6

Therefore, there are 6 ways to form a committee of 5 boys from a class containing 6 boys and 5 girls.

b.) For a committee of 2 boys and 3 girls, we can choose 2 boys from the 6 boys in the class and 3 girls from the 5 girls in the class. This can be done in 6 choose 2 ways for boys and 5 choose 3 ways for girls, which is:

6C2 × 5C3 = (6 × 5) ÷ (2 × 1) × (5 × 4 × 3) ÷ (3 × 2 × 1) = 15 × 10 = 150

Therefore, there are 150 ways to form a committee of 2 boys and 3 girls from a class containing 6 boys and 5 girls

Answer:

a. 56

b. 220

3. 1848

Step-by-step explanation:

a. To select a cleaning committee consisting of 3 boys, we need to choose 3 boys from the 8 boys in the class. We can do this using combinations:

C(8,3) = 8! / (3! * (8-3)!) = 56

Therefore, there are 56 ways to select a cleaning committee consisting of 3 boys.

b. To select a cleaning committee consisting of 3 girls, we need to choose 3 girls from the 12 girls in the class. We can do this using combinations:

C(12,3) = 12! / (3! * (12-3)!) = 220

Therefore, there are 220 ways to select a cleaning committee consisting of 3 girls.

c. To select a cleaning committee consisting of 2 boys and 2 girls, we need to choose 2 boys from the 8 boys in the class and 2 girls from the 12 girls in the class. We can do this using combinations:

C(8,2) * C(12,2) = (8! / (2! * (8-2)!)) * (12! / (2! * (12-2)!)) = 28 * 66 = 1848

Therefore, there are 1848 ways to select a cleaning committee consisting of 2 boys and 2 girls.
