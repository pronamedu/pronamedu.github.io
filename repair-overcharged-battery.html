2. Describe the possible cause and trouble from battery overcharging?


Answer:

Overcharging a battery causes excessive gassing the electrolyte gets hot and both hydrogen and oxygen gas are generated. On older vented batteries, the electrolyte could cook away, leaving the plates exposed and ruining them. On sealed batteries, the buildup of gases could cause the battery to burst.

10. 4. Rechargeable batteries are called nickel-cadmium batteries a. b. d. primary batteries secondary batteries storage batteries​


Answer:

Secondary batteries

Explanation:

Rechargeable batteries, sometimes known as secondary batteries, are intended to be recharged and reused several times. They differ from primary batteries, which are intended to be used once and then destroyed, and storage batteries, which are often huge and used to generate electricity in off-grid applications. Nickel-cadmium (NiCd) batteries are a type of secondary battery.
