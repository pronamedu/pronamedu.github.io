x = distance = m

river wide = wr = x = 300 m

v boat = vb = 10 m/s

30. A body is projected with a speed of 30ms-1 at an angle of 300 with the vertical. Find the maximum height, time of flight and the horizontal range of the motion. [Take = 10 m/s2]​


Answer:

the maximum height is 11.25 m

the time of flight is 3 seconds

and the horizontal range is 77.94 m.

Explanation:

Given:

Initial velocity, u = 30 m/s

Angle with the vertical, θ = 30°

Acceleration due to gravity, g = 10 m/s²

We can break the initial velocity into its horizontal and vertical components:

Initial horizontal velocity, uₓ = u cos θ

uₓ = 30 cos 30°

uₓ = 30 × 0.866

uₓ = 25.98 m/s

Initial vertical velocity, uᵧ = u sin θ

uᵧ = 30 sin 30°

uᵧ = 30 × 0.5

uᵧ = 15 m/s

Maximum height:

At the highest point, the vertical velocity is zero (vᵧ = 0).

Using the kinematic equation v² = u² + 2as, where v = 0, u = 15 m/s, and a = -10 m/s² (taking upwards as positive), we get:

0² = 15² + 2(-10)s

s = 11.25 m

Time of flight:

Using the kinematic equation s = ut + 0.5at², where s = 0 (since the projectile returns to the same height), u = 15 m/s, and a = -10 m/s² (taking upwards as positive), we get:

0 = 15t + 0.5(-10)t²

t = 3 seconds

Horizontal range:

Using the kinematic equation s = ut + 0.5at², where s is the horizontal range, u = 25.98 m/s (initial horizontal velocity), and a = 0 (since there is no acceleration in the horizontal direction), we get:

s = 25.98 × 3

s = 77.94 m

Therefore, the maximum height is 11.25 m, the time of flight is 3 seconds, and the horizontal range is 77.94 m.
