16. what is the formula of fahrenheit to celcius and celcius to fahrenheit ?​


MEASURING TEMPERATURE

Gabriel Fahrenheit (1686-1736) devised a scale for the mercury thermometer such that the freezing point of water is 32° F and the boiling point of water is 212° F.

Shortly after Fahrenheit developed his scale, Anders Celsius (1701-1744), developed another scale. Celsius developed his scale such that the freezing point of water was 0° and the boiling point was 100°. We are familiar with the Celsius thermometer as the centigrade thermometer. Remember that centi means 1/100 or 0.01. In the Celsius thermometer, there are 100 intervals between 0° centigrade and 100° centigrade. Since the scale that Celsius developed was so convenient to use, the centigrade thermometer was adopted by scientists. All the countries using the metric system have also adopted this thermometer which was formerly known as the centigrade thermometer. It is now officially known as the Celsius thermometer in honor of the man who developed the scale. Each unit in the Celsius thermometer is called a degree Celsius (°C).

This are the formula to convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius and vice versa.

1. Fahrenheit to Celsius

C = 5/9 (F - 32)

2. Celcius to Fahrenheit

F = 9/5C +32

21. 2. It takes 12 minutes for an object at 100 degree celcius to cool to80 degree celcius in a room at 50 degree celcius - How much lingerwill it takefor its temperature decrease to 70 degree celcius?​


[tex]\mathbb{FORMULA:}[/tex]

Newton's Law of Cooling:

[tex]\mathsf{T-T_s=(T_o-T_s)e^{-kt}}[/tex]

T = temperature of the body at any time t

Ts = ambient temperature

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

At t = 12 mins, T = 80  °C, k = ?

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{T-T_s=(T_o-T_s)e^{-kt}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{80-50=(100-50)e^{-12k}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{30=50e^{-12k}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{e^{-12k}=\dfrac{30}{50}} \longleftarrow \textsf{take the natural log on both sides}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{ln\left(e^{-12k}\right)=ln\left(\dfrac{30}{50}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{-12k=ln\left(\dfrac{30}{50}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{k=-\dfrac{ln\left(\dfrac{30}{50}\right)}{12}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{k=0.04256880198}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

At T = 70 °C, t = ?

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{T-T_s=(T_o-T_s)e^{-kt}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{70-50=(100-50)e^{-0.04256880198t}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{20=50e^{-0.04256880198t}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{e^{-0.04256880198t}=\dfrac{20}{50}} \longleftarrow \textsf{take the natural log on both sides}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{ln\left(e^{-0.04256880198t}\right)=ln\left(\dfrac{20}{50}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{-0.04256880198t=ln\left(\dfrac{20}{50}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{t=\dfrac{ln\left(\dfrac{20}{50}\right)}{-0.04256880198}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{t=21.52}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The temperature of the body will cool from 100°C to 70 °C at:

[tex]\large{\boxed{\mathsf{t=21.52 \: \: mins}}}[/tex]
