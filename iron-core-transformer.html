10. try to investigate the effect of using a people core instead of an iron core for an electromagnet​


Answer:

Soft iron is commonly used in the manufacture of electromagnets due to its high magnetic permeability, which means it can easily obtain magnetic properties when current is conducted through the core and quickly lose them when current is stopped. This is especially crucial in the case of electromagnets, as they are commonly employed for taking up or carrying iron loads, as well as in electric bells and speakers, where rapid transformation of properties is required. Steel may be used as the core in circumstances when such rapid changes are not required. Steel has a low magnetic permeability, so once it acquires magnetic properties, it retains them for a long period. As a result, steel is frequently employed in the fabrication of permanent magnets.

Answer:

Core, mantle, and crust are divisions based on composition. The crust makes up less than 1 percent of Earth by mass, consisting of oceanic crust and continental crust is often more felsic rock. The mantle is hot and represents about 68 percent of Earth’s mass. Finally, the core is mostly iron metal. The core makes up about 31% of the Earth. Lithosphere and asthenosphere are divisions based on mechanical properties. The lithosphere is composed of both the crust and the portion of the upper mantle that behaves as a brittle, rigid solid. The asthenosphere is partially molten upper mantle material that behaves plastically and can flow. This animation by Earthquide shows the layers by composition and by mechanical properties.
