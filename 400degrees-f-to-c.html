3. A ray of light is reflected from a plane mirror. The angle of incidence is 20degrees. The angle between the incident and the reflected ray is _______ a. 10degrees b. 30degrees c. 20degrees d. 40degrees


Explanation:

Since angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection, we can also say that angle between the incident ray and reflected ray is twice the angle of reflection or incidence.


Thus, as per above case the angle between the incident and the reflected ray is

= (20*2) 

= 40 degrees.

Therefore, d. 40 degrees is the angle between the incident and the reflected ray.


Hello Mam.

Here is the answer---

→→→→→→→→→→→→→

Given, the angle of the incidence(i) = 20°

As per as the second law of Reflection, the angle of incidence is always equals to the angle of Reflection.

Thus, angle of Reflection(r) = 20°

The angle between the incident ray and the reflected ray = i + r
                                                                                           = 20° + 20°
                                                                                           = 40°

Thus the option(d) i.e., 40° is the correct answer.
