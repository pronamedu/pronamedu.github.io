1. a 1000kg car is hoisted up to a 15 meter height. calculate the potential energy gained by the car


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The potential energy of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{PE = mgh}[/tex]

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (1000 kg)(9.8 m/s²)(15 m)

[tex]\boxed{PE = \text{147,000 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the potential energy of the car is 147,000 J.

Height of tower = 97 m

Step-by-step explanation:

Angle of depression of the red car = 40°

Angle of depression of the blue car = 55°

Distance from the tower to the red card = 115 m

Find the height of the tower:

tan(40) = height/115

height = 115 x tan(40)

height = 97 m (nearest m)

Find the distance between the tower and the blue car:

tan(55) = 96.5/distance

distance = 96.5/tan(55)

distance = 68 m (nearest m)
