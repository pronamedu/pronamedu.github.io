Explanation:

Hydrochloric acid is a mixture. Although we use HCl as the formula for hydrochloric acid, HCl is, in fact, the formula of hydrogen chloride, which is a compound. Hydrogen chloride is a gas which readily reacts with water. There are many different concentrations of hydrochloric acid - it must be a mixture.

Examples of Mixtures

Mixtures are everywhere. The definition of a mixture is a combination of different things that are not chemically bonded. For example, when we bake a cake, it's a result of a mixture of eggs, flour, sugar, and other ingredients. Mixtures can also be much simpler than that.

Any time two or more items are combined, a mixture is formed. Sometimes, the different parts of a mixture can be separated into individual entities. Other times, they're married for as long as they exist. An example of a mixture is adding loose leaf tea to hot water, creating a simple kind of mixture that we call tea. Let's explore more examples of mixtures.

Common Mixtures of Food

Most of the food we eat is a combination of different things. Rarely do we eat only one ingredient. For example, we can eat plain chicken, but why not mix it with a little seasoning? Here are more examples of mixtures as they relate to one of American's favorite pastimes: eating.

27. State several intrinsic properties can be used to distinguish between these pairs of substances and mixtures. A. Water and Gasoline B. Water and rubbing alcohol


The following are the several intrinsic properties that can be used to distinguish between these pairs of substances and mixtures: A. water and gasoline; B. water and rubbing alcohol

A. Water and gasoline are both fluids which means they both have the internal resistance to flow. This resistance is called viscosity which measures the friction of any fluid based on its thickness or thinness. In this case, water is thinner than gasoline which means that the former has a lower viscosity than the latter. With that, water, being less viscous, has greater fluidity than gasoline.

B. Water and rubbing alcohol both can vaporize. This is called volatility. Rubbing alcohol (methanol or ethanol) is considered a volatile liquid because it easily turns into vapor. Example if you leave an alcohol on a container for some time, you will notice that it was gone when you get back or has lessened its amount. This is because it has 70-800C boiling point. On the other hand, water can vaporize only when it reaches the temperature of 1000C. Hence, since alcohol has a lower boiling point, it is said to have greater volatility than water.
