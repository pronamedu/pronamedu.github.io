4. what meaning of Ranat Ek instruments​


Answer:

The ranat ek is a Thai musical instrument in the percussion family that consists of 21 wooden bars suspended by cords over a boat-shaped trough resonator and struck by two mallets. It is used as a leading instrument in the piphat ensemble.

If NO = 19 and LI = 13, find EK.4. If NO = 21.5 and EK = 28, find LI.5. If LI = 3x + 8, EK = 6x + 4 and NO = 24, find LI and EK.​


[tex]\underline{\mathcal{SOLUTION}}:[/tex]

1. If LI = 23 and EK = 36, find NO.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{gathered}\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\boxed{\begin{array}{l}\textsf { \small LI = 23, EK = 36} \\ \ \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{LI \: + \: EK}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{23 \: + \: 6}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \small{NO = \frac{59}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \boxed{{ \sf \small NO = 29.5}} \end{array}} \end{gathered}\end{gathered}[/tex]

2. If LI = 15.5 and EK = 22.5, find NO.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{gathered}\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\boxed{\begin{array}{l}\textsf { \small LI = 15.5, EK = 22.5} \\ \ \: \: \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{LI \: + \: EK}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{12.5 \: + \: 22.5}{2}}\\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{{ \sf \small NO = 19}} \end{array}} \end{gathered}\end{gathered}[/tex]

3. If NO = 19 and LI = 13, find EK.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{gathered}\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\boxed{\begin{array}{l}\textsf { \small NO = 19, LI = 13} \\ \ \: \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{LI \: + \: EK}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{13 \: + \: EK}{2}} \\ \: \: \textsf {\small 38 = 13 \: + \: EK} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{{ \sf \small EK = 25}} \end{array}} \end{gathered}\end{gathered}[/tex]

4. If NO = 21.5 and EK = 28, find LI.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{gathered}\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\:\boxed{\begin{array}{l}\textsf { \small NO = 21.5, EK = 28} \\ \ \: \: \: \: \: \sf \small {NO = \frac{LI \: + \: EK}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \sf \small {21.5 = \frac{LI \: + \: 28}{2}} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \textsf {\small 43 = LI \: + \: 28} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{{ \sf \small LI = 15}} \end{array}} \end{gathered}\end{gathered}[/tex]

OT = (1/2) * (AC + EK)

Using the formula for the length of the median of a trapezoid, we get:

OT = (1/2) * (AC + EK)
