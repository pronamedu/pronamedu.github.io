8. 1. Find the area of a square and a triangle. The side of the square is 7cm. The base of the triangle is the same as the side of the square and has a height of 5cm.​


Answer:

The area of the square is 49 cm²

The area of the triangle is 17.5 cm²

Step-by-step explanation:

The formula in calculating the area of a square is:

A = s²

Here, the side is 7 cm, so s = 7; substitute the value of s:

A = 7² cm²

A = 49 cm² (area of the square)

The formula in calculating the area of a triangle is:

A = 1/2(b)(h)

Given that the base is equal to the side of the square (which is 7 cm), and the height is 5 cm; substitute:

A = 1/2(7 cm)(5 cm)

A = 1/2(35 cm²)

A = 17.5 cm² (area of the triangle)

The triangle inequality theorem states that the sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle must be greater than the length of the third side. This means that if the length of one side of a triangle is longer than the sum of the lengths of the other two sides, then the triangle must be an obtuse triangle.

To prove this, let's consider a triangle with sides of lengths a, b, and c, where c is the longest side. According to the triangle inequality theorem, we have:

a + b > c

If we square both sides of this inequality, we get:

a^2 + 2ab + b^2 > c^2

This inequality can be rearranged as follows:

a^2 + b^2 > c^2 - 2ab

If we take the square of the length of the longest side c and subtract the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides a and b, we get:

c^2 - (a^2 + b^2) > 0

Since this inequality is always true, we can conclude that if the square of the length of the longest side of a triangle is greater than the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides, then the triangle is an obtuse triangle

Answer:

Let's first find the perimeter of the square. Since all sides of a square are equal, we can divide the area by one of the sides to get the length of one side.

Area of square = side x side = 144 square meters

Side of square = √144 = 12 meters

Perimeter of square = 4 x side = 4 x 12 = 48 meters

Since the equilateral triangle also has the same perimeter, each side must be 48/3 = 16 meters.

To find the area of an equilateral triangle, we can use the formula:

Area = (sqrt(3)/4) x side^2

Plugging in the value of the side, we get:

Area = (sqrt(3)/4) x 16^2

Area = (sqrt(3)/4) x 256

Area = 64sqrt(3) square meters

Therefore, the area of the equilateral triangle is 64sqrt(3) square meters.
