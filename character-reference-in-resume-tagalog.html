17. importance of resume


Importance of Resume

A resume is a type of paper work or document that would tell about a person's professional experience for the job that they are applying to. This would sum up all your work experiences, job titles, name of the Company and year which would give idea to your potential employers about your working experiences. Normally, it would start of with your previous or current work experiences followed by older jobs that you've got. Managers and hiring staff would be the first to get and read your resume. Most of their questions during the job interview will be from your resume so it is important to present your resume in a way that would impress and attract your employers in terms of professionally speaking.

Creation of resume is important so you can find the job which is ideal for you. When you emphasize your desired job in your resume, clients with the exact needs based on your listed skills and chosen field could easily evaluate and hire you. In a way that you would also know if you are really what the company needs as an employee.

Make use of a formal email address. As much as possible, use an email address which indicating your full name rather than your gaming email or others. Relate your resume to the position that you are applying to. Choose a simple and professional template.

21. give atleast 3 differences of chronological resume and functional resume.and give atleast 3 similarities ​


Chronological resumes and functional resumes are two common formats for presenting one's work experience and skills to potential employers. Here are some differences and similarities between the two:

Differences:

1. Format: Chronological resumes list work experience in reverse chronological order, starting with the most recent job, while functional resumes emphasize skills and accomplishments rather than specific jobs held.

2. Focus: Chronological resumes focus on job titles and employment history, while functional resumes emphasize the skills and accomplishments that are relevant to the position being applied for.

3. Suitability: Chronological resumes are best suited for job seekers with a consistent employment history, while functional resumes are better for those with gaps in their work history or those looking to make a career change.

Similarities:

1. Basic information: Both formats include standard information such as contact details, educational background, and work experience.

2. Customization: Both formats can be customized to highlight specific skills or experiences that are relevant to the job being applied for.

3. Clarity: Both formats should be easy to read and clearly present the job seeker's qualifications and accomplishments.

Overall, choosing between a chronological and functional resume depends on individual circumstances and career goals. Job seekers should consider their work history and the position they are applying for when deciding which format to use.
