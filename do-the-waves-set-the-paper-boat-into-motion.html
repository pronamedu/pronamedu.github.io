6. do the waves set the paper boat into motion?What is required to set an object into motion?


Let me answer these two questions separately.

Do the waves set the paper boat into motion?

The answer to this question is YES.

Explanation:

According to the first law of motion, an object will remain at rest or in motion unless there is an external unbalanced force that acts upon the object. This first law of motion is also called the law of inertia.

If we were going to analyze the situation, the waves of the water are exerting a force into the boat. Since there is an, external force that acts upon the boat, therefore the boat will move thus settings the paper boat into motion.

What is required to set an object into motion?

To set an object into motion, it will require an external unbalance force.

Explanation:

This is still according to the first law of motion. We can say that the force that will act upon the object should be external. This means that it will come from another object. The term unbalanced force means that the force that acts upon the object should not equal to the force that the object is exerting.  

Let us have an example.

Object A has a force of 10N and object B has a force of 20N. When the two objects collide, object A will move since the force of object B is greater than the force of object A. This is what we called unbalanced force.  

However, If object A has a force of 10 N and object B had a force of also 10N neither of the two will move since they will cancel out each others force. Since the force is balanced.

15. How does the boat's motion relate to the direction the water waves travel?​


The motion of a boat is related to the direction the water waves travel in several ways. The most obvious way is that the motion of the boat is affected by the water waves, which means that the boat may rise and fall as it moves over the waves. This is because the waves create an up-and-down motion in the water, which is then transferred to the boat.

Another way that the boat's motion is related to the direction the water waves travel is through the concept of wave direction. If the waves are traveling in the same direction as the boat, then the boat will experience a smoother ride because it will be moving along with the waves. However, if the waves are traveling perpendicular or at an angle to the boat, then the boat will experience a more turbulent ride because it will be moving against the waves.

Overall, the direction of the water waves has a significant impact on the motion of a boat. The boat's motion is affected by the up-and-down motion of the waves, as well as the direction in which the waves are traveling. Skilled boat operators can use this knowledge to navigate more efficiently and avoid rough water conditions.
