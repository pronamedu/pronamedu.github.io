5. the wavelength of a wave is 2.5 cm and its frequency is 60 Hz. Determine the speed of the wave


To determine the speed of the wave, we can use the formula:

v = λf

where:

v = velocity or speed of the wave

λ = wavelength of the wave

f = frequency of the wave

In this problem, we are given that the wavelength (λ) of the wave is 2.5 cm and the frequency (f) is 60 Hz. Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

v = λf

v = 2.5 cm x 60 Hz

v = 150 cm/s

Therefore, the speed of the wave is 150 cm/s.

3.) What is the wavelength of a wave with a speed of 12 m/s and a period of 0.25 s?

Answer:

The wavelength of a wave with a speed of 12 m/s and a period of 0.25 s is 3 meters.

Solution:

The formula for wavelength is:

wavelength = speed / frequency

We can find the frequency using the formula:

frequency = 1 / period

Substituting the given values, we get:

frequency = 1 / 0.25 sfrequency = 4 Hz

Using the formula for wavelength, we get:

wavelength = speed / frequencywavelength = 12 m/s / 4 Hzwavelength = 3 m

Therefore, the wavelength of the wave is 3 meters.

4.) Two mechanical waves travelling at the same speed with equal amplitudes have frequencies 60 Hz and 40 Hz, respectively. Which wave has more energy and by how much?

Answer:

The wave with a frequency of 60 Hz has 1.5 times more energy than the wave with a frequency of 40 Hz.

Solution:

The energy of a wave is directly proportional to the square of its amplitude and frequency. The formula for energy is:

energy = (amplitude)^2 * frequency

Since the two waves have equal amplitudes and travel at the same speed, we just need to compare their frequencies to determine which one has more energy.

Using the formula for energy, we get:

energy1 = (amplitude)^2 * frequency1energy2 = (amplitude)^2 * frequency2

Substituting the given values, we get:

energy1 = (amplitude)^2 * 60 Hzenergy2 = (amplitude)^2 * 40 Hz

Since the two waves have equal amplitudes, we can compare their energies by comparing their frequencies. The wave with the higher frequency (60 Hz) has more energy than the wave with the lower frequency (40 Hz).

To determine the difference in energy, we can calculate the ratio of the energies:

energy1 / energy2 = (amplitude)^2 * 60 Hz / (amplitude)^2 * 40 Hzenergy1 / energy2 = 60 Hz / 40 Hzenergy1 / energy2 = 3/2

Therefore, the wave with a frequency of 60 Hz has 1.5 times more energy than the wave with a frequency of 40 Hz.
