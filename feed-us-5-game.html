17. A. Define the following concept: 1. Ad libitum feeding 2. Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) 3. Restricted feeding 4. Feeding 5. Rationpls answr thisâ€‹


Answer:

Ad libitum feeding: Ad libitum feeding is a feeding method in which the animal has access to feed at all times, and can eat as much as it wants whenever it wants. This feeding method is also known as free-choice feeding and is commonly used in research settings to ensure that animals are consuming sufficient nutrients.

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR): Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is a measure of how efficiently animals convert feed into body mass or product. It is calculated by dividing the amount of feed consumed by the animal by the amount of weight gain achieved during the same period. A lower FCR indicates that the animal is converting feed more efficiently.

Restricted feeding: Restricted feeding is a feeding method in which the animal's access to feed is restricted to a certain amount or time period each day. This method is commonly used in commercial animal production to optimize growth and minimize feed costs.

Feeding: Feeding is the process of providing animals with food to meet their nutritional requirements. It involves selecting appropriate types and amounts of feed, as well as providing the feed in a manner that the animal can easily consume.

Ration: A ration is a specific amount of feed provided to an animal over a given period. Rations are designed to meet the animal's nutritional needs and can be adjusted depending on the animal's stage of development, activity level, and other factors

Explanation:

In summary, ad libitum feeding refers to animals having access to feed at all times, feed conversion ratio is a measure of efficiency in converting feed to body mass, restricted feeding involves limiting an animal's access to feed, feeding involves providing animals with food to meet their nutritional requirements, and a ration is a specific amount of feed provided to an animal over a given period, designed to meet their nutritional needs.
