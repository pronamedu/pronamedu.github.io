A motherboard, also known as the "main board," is the central circuit hub that allows connection between all components and peripherals attached to the computer. It also allows all the components such as CPU, graphics card, hard drive and the memory to receive power from the attached power supply.

Because it's the Main Circuit Board in a Computer

Because it's the Main Circuit Board in a ComputerA computer can have more than one circuit board for several different functions. 19. What is a motherboard? Explain why the motherboard is important in a computer?​


Answer:

A printed circuit board containing the principal components of a computer or other device, with connectors into which other circuit boards can be slotted.

Explanation:

The motherboard provides a socket into which a CPU can be fitted along with slots for the RAM, internal ports for storage devices and external ports for peripherals. Expansion ports such as PCI/PCI-e are also provided which allow expansion cards to be fitted for additional functionality.

22. why motherboard called motherboard why its not fatherboard?


It's referred to with a female pronoun (motherboard) because of the tradition where people would refer to ships or cars as she/her or give them female names. This practice carried on to other inanimate objects as well with people giving female pronouns/names to other objects, not just ships and cars. Hence why the motherboard is referred to as such and not as a "fatherboard".
